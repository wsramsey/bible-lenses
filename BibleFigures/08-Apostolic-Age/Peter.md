# Simon Peter
*The Gospels and Acts · The Rock / First Among the Apostles*

## In the Biblical Story
Simon (called Peter/Cephas = rock) is the most prominent of the Twelve and the most human:

- **Matthew 16:13–19** — "Who do you say I am?" / "You are the Christ, the Son of the living God" / "On this rock I will build my church" / "I will give you the keys of the kingdom"
- **Matthew 17** — the Transfiguration; "Let us build three tabernacles"
- **John 13** — the foot washing; "You shall never wash my feet!" / "Unless I wash you, you have no part with me"
- **Luke 22** — "I will never disown you!" / the rooster crows three times; "Peter went out and wept bitterly"
- **John 21** — the restoration; "Do you love me?" / "Yes, Lord, you know that I love you" / "Feed my sheep" — three times, one for each denial
- **Acts 2** — Pentecost sermon; 3,000 baptized
- **Acts 10** — Cornelius; the vision of clean and unclean; "God shows no partiality"
- **Galatians 2** — Paul confronts Peter face-to-face for separating from Gentile Christians

## What the Theologians Have Said
**[[Roman Catholic Church]]** — Matthew 16:18–19 establishes Peter as the rock on which the church is built; the keys indicate jurisdictional primacy; this primacy is inherited by his successors as bishops of Rome (the papacy).

**[[Eastern Orthodox Church]]** — Peter has primacy of honor among the apostles but not jurisdictional supremacy over other bishops; the Roman bishop is *first among equals* (*primus inter pares*), not supreme.

**[[Martin Luther]]** — "the rock" in Matthew 16 is Peter's *faith*, not Peter himself; the papacy's claim to Petrine succession is not supported by Scripture.

**[[John Calvin]]** — same as Luther; Peter has no special authority beyond the other apostles; his role in Acts is prominent but not monarchical.

**[[Oscar Cullmann]]** — *Peter: Disciple, Apostle, Martyr* (1952): the most careful NT study; Peter's role is unique in the NT but the Catholic doctrine of ongoing succession goes beyond the evidence.

**[[N.T. Wright]]** — Peter's primacy is real but pastoral, not juridical; the "keys" language refers to the Davidic steward in Isaiah 22; binding and loosing are rabbinic terms for teaching authority.

**[[Hans Urs von Balthasar]]** — the "Petrine" and "Marian" principles in the church; Peter represents institutional authority; Mary represents receptive faith; both are needed, but the Marian principle is more fundamental.

## Key Theological Debates
- **Matthew 16:18** — "On this rock (*petra*) I will build my church": is the rock Peter (Catholic), Peter's faith (Protestant), or Peter's confession (some Protestant)?
- **Papal succession**: Does Peter's role transfer to Roman bishops? The most contested question in ecumenical theology
- **Peter's death in Rome**: Strong tradition (1 Clement, Ignatius); no NT proof; confirmed by archaeology under St. Peter's Basilica to some degree
