# John the Baptist
*The Gospels · The Voice in the Wilderness / Forerunner of Christ*

## In the Biblical Story
John (son of Zechariah and Elizabeth, cousin of Jesus) occupies a unique hinge position in redemptive history:

- **Luke 1** — the Annunciation to Zechariah; born 6 months before Jesus; filled with the Spirit from the womb
- **Matthew 3, Mark 1** — preaching in the wilderness; "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is near"; baptizing in the Jordan; "I baptize you with water... he will baptize you with fire"
- **Matthew 3:13–17** — Jesus's baptism; the voice from heaven; the Spirit as a dove
- **Matthew 11:11** — Jesus: "Among those born of women there has not risen anyone greater than John the Baptist; yet whoever is least in the kingdom of heaven is greater than he"
- **Mark 6** — John's imprisonment and beheading by Herod Antipas; Salome and the head on a platter
- **John 1:29** — "Look, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!"

## Historical Attestation
**Josephus** (*Antiquities* 18.5.2) confirms John's existence, his baptizing, and his execution by Herod Antipas — independently of the Gospels. This is one of the most significant extrabiblical confirmations of a NT figure.

## What the Theologians Have Said
**[[Origen of Alexandria]]** — John as the typological fulfillment of Elijah (Malachi 4:5); the Spirit of Elijah resting on him; his beheading as a foreshadowing of Christ's crucifixion

**[[Augustine of Hippo]]** — John's "voice" (John 1:23) vs. Jesus as the "Word"; John's role is to prepare and then decrease; "He must increase; I must decrease" as the model of Christian service

**[[Thomas Aquinas]]** — John's baptism and Christian baptism; John's baptism was not sacramental (didn't confer grace) but preparatory; the transition from old to new covenant

**[[N.T. Wright]]** — John stands at the hinge of Israel's story; his baptism represents a new Exodus through the Jordan; Jesus's acceptance of John's baptism is his identification with Israel's penitence and judgment

**[[Bart Ehrman]]** — the historical Jesus was almost certainly a disciple of John; Jesus's movement grew out of John's; the differences (Jesus's more positive message about the kingdom) represent development

## Key Theological Debates
- **John as Elijah**: Is John *literally* Elijah reincarnated (John 1:21 says no) or *typologically* Elijah (Matthew 11:14 says yes)?
- **Jesus's baptism**: Why was the sinless Jesus baptized? Early Christians wrestled with this — Mark doesn't even mention John baptizing Jesus explicitly
- **John's doubts** (Matthew 11:3): "Are you the one who was to come, or should we expect someone else?" — the forerunner's crisis of faith
