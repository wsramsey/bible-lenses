# Mary of Nazareth
*The Gospels · Theotokos / Second Eve / Mother of God*

## In the Biblical Story
Mary appears at the key moments of Jesus's life:
- **Luke 1:26–38** — the Annunciation; "Let it be to me according to your word" (*fiat*); the *Magnificat* (1:46–55) — one of the great theological songs of the Bible
- **Luke 2** — the birth at Bethlehem; Simeon's prophecy: "a sword will pierce your own soul"
- **John 2** — Cana; "Do whatever he tells you" — her last recorded words
- **John 19:25–27** — at the foot of the cross; "Woman, here is your son"
- **Acts 1:14** — present in the upper room at Pentecost; part of the praying community

## The Magnificat (Luke 1:46–55)
One of the most theologically dense passages in the Gospels:
> "He has scattered those who are proud... He has brought down rulers from their thrones but has lifted up the humble. He has filled the hungry with good things but has sent the rich away empty."

[[Dietrich Bonhoeffer]] called it "the most passionate, wild, revolutionary hymn ever sung." [[Liberation theology]] has made it central.

## What the Theologians Have Said

**[[Irenaeus of Lyon]]** — the Eve-Mary typology: "The knot of Eve's disobedience was loosed by the obedience of Mary." Mary's *fiat* reverses Eve's disobedience.

**[[Council of Ephesus (431)]]** — *Theotokos* (God-bearer, Mother of God) — the council's Christological point: the child Mary bore *is* God; denying this (Nestorius) would divide Christ.

**[[Augustine of Hippo]]** — Mary's perpetual virginity; her freedom from personal sin (though not from original sin — that distinction came later).

**[[Thomas Aquinas]]** — Mary as the model of faith and contemplation; her cooperation in the Incarnation as the paradigm of human cooperation with grace.

**[[John Calvin]]** — Great respect for Mary as the most blessed of women; the *Theotokos* title is legitimate as a statement about Christ; but veneration and intercession rejected as unscriptural.

**[[Martin Luther]]** — Initially retained more Marian devotion than later Lutheranism; his *Magnificat* commentary is one of his most beautiful works; gradually the veneration became Protestant's primary point of rejection.

**[[Karol Wojtyła / John Paul II]]** — *Redemptoris Mater* (1987): Mary as model of the church; her receptive femininity as the paradigm for all creatures before God; "Marian" vs. "Petrine" principles in the church.

**[[Hans Urs von Balthasar]]** — Mary as the perfect creaturely response to God; her immaculate conception not as privilege but as grace; the *fiat* as the paradigm of faith; the church is constitutively Marian before it is Petrine.

**[[Phyllis Trible]]** / feminist theologians — the *Magnificat* as a liberation text; Mary's agency; but also the problematic tradition of using Mary to impose passive submission on women.

## Key Theological Debates
- **The *Theotokos***: All major Christian traditions affirm it as Christological; only becomes controversial when it shifts to Mariological devotion
- **Perpetual virginity**: Catholic/Orthodox tradition; Protestant rejection (James and Joseph's other sons mentioned in NT)
- **Immaculate Conception** (Catholic dogma, 1854): Mary was conceived without original sin; Protestant and Orthodox rejection
- **Assumption** (Catholic dogma, 1950): Mary was taken bodily into heaven; not in Scripture explicitly; Protestant and Orthodox rejection
- **Mediatrix/Co-Redemptrix**: Proposed Catholic dogma; extremely controversial even within Catholicism
