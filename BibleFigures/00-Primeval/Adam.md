# Adam
*Genesis 1–5 · The First Human / Type of Christ*

## In the Biblical Story
Adam (Hebrew: *adamah* = ground/earth) is the first human in Genesis 1–2:
- **Genesis 1:26–27** — created in the *imago Dei* (image of God); male and female together
- **Genesis 2** — formed from the dust; given the garden; the woman formed from his side
- **Genesis 3** — the Fall; the serpent; the fruit; exile from Eden; "in Adam all die"
- **Genesis 4–5** — father of Cain, Abel, Seth; genealogy linking primeval history to the patriarchs

## The Bigger Story
Adam is the hinge of the entire biblical narrative. Everything that follows — the covenant history, the Law, the sacrificial system, the prophets, the Incarnation — is the story of God undoing and redoing what happened in Genesis 3.

Paul makes this explicit in Romans 5 and 1 Corinthians 15:
- "As in Adam all die, so in Christ all shall be made alive" (1 Cor 15:22)
- "The first man Adam became a living being; the last Adam became a life-giving Spirit" (1 Cor 15:45)

Adam and Christ are typologically paired. Christ is the "second Adam" who does what Adam failed to do.

## Historical/Archaeological Context
- **"Adam" is also the Hebrew word for "humanity"** — the name is a pun; Adam *is* humanity
- **No archaeological evidence** for a specific individual Adam; the question of historicity is vigorously debated
- **Ancient Near Eastern parallels** — the *Enuma Elish* (Babylonian), *Gilgamesh*, and *Atrahasis* all have creation/flood figures; Genesis engages and corrects them
- **The image of God** (*imago Dei*) is a unique biblical claim — in Mesopotamian thought, only the king bore the divine image; Genesis democratizes it

## What the Theologians Have Said
**[[Augustine of Hippo]]** — Adam's sin was the original sin transmitted to all humanity; free will corrupted; the will in bondage; sexuality post-Fall tainted. His reading shaped Western Christianity for 1,500 years.

**[[Thomas Aquinas]]** — Adam possessed *sanctifying grace* (donum superadditum) and *preternatural gifts* (impassibility, immortality) before the Fall; these were lost, wounding human nature but not destroying it.

**[[John Calvin]]** — Total depravity: the Fall corrupted every aspect of human nature; the *imago Dei* is defaced but not destroyed; restoration only through Christ.

**[[Karl Barth]]** — Adam is not a historical figure but an *actualistic* type; humanity in its determination before God; the Fall is the presupposition of the covenant; Jesus Christ is the true Adam in whom humanity finds its identity.

**[[N.T. Wright]]** — Adam and Eve represent Israel in miniature; their story is Israel's story; the Garden is a temple; the commission to "fill and subdue" is priestly and royal; Christ as the new Adam completes the commission.

**[[Karl Rahner]]** — "Monogenism vs. polygenism" — whether all humans descend from a single couple; Rahner argued the theological point (solidarity in sin) doesn't require biological monogenism.

**[[René Girard]]** — The expulsion from Eden as the primal scapegoat event; exile as the mechanism by which the community excludes the transgressor.

## Key Theological Debates
- **Historical Adam**: young-earth (literal individual) → old-earth (real individual in evolutionary context) → literary/theological figure (BioLogos)
- **Original sin**: Is it inherited guilt? Inherited nature? Imitation? Solidarity?
- **Imago Dei**: Reason? Moral capacity? Relationality? Dominion?
- **The two creation accounts**: Genesis 1 (priestly, orderly) vs. Genesis 2 (Yahwist, intimate) — contradiction or complementary?
