# Eve
*Genesis 2–3 · Mother of All Living / Type of Mary and the Church*

## In the Biblical Story
Eve (*Hawwah* = living, or life-giver) is:
- **Genesis 2:18–25** — formed from Adam's side (*tsela* = rib/side); the *ezer kenegdo* (helper corresponding to him); "bone of my bones"
- **Genesis 3** — deceived by the serpent; eats the fruit; gives to Adam; first to receive the proto-Gospel (*protoevangelium*): "I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and hers; he will crush your head" (Gen 3:15)
- **Genesis 4:1** — "I have produced a man with the help of the LORD"

## The Bigger Story
Eve is the first person to receive the promise of a Redeemer (Gen 3:15). The "offspring of the woman" who will crush the serpent is interpreted by the entire Christian tradition as pointing to Christ, born of a woman.

**The Mary typology:** Eve believed a lie and brought death; Mary believed a promise and brought life. This parallel is developed by every major Church Father.

## What the Theologians Have Said
**[[Irenaeus of Lyon]]** — the Eve-Mary typology: "The knot of Eve's disobedience was loosed by Mary's obedience." This is the earliest and most influential reading.

**[[Augustine of Hippo]]** — Eve's sin was spiritual pride; she desired to be "like God knowing good and evil"; her vulnerability to deception reflects the disorder of reason by passion.

**[[Thomas Aquinas]]** — Eve was created as Adam's "helpmate" specifically for reproduction; Aquinas follows Aristotle on female biology in ways that are now recognized as wrong and have been used to subordinate women.

**[[Phyllis Trible]]** — *God and the Rhetoric of Sexuality* (1978): a feminist rereading; Eve is the more active, intellectually engaged figure in Genesis 3; the traditional reading has been used to oppress women.

**[[N.T. Wright]]** — the *ezer kenegdo* is a term used for military allies and God himself in the Psalms; "helper" connotes strength, not subordination.

**[[John Paul II / Karol Wojtyła]]** — *Theology of the Body*: Eve's creation from Adam's side reveals the spousal meaning of the body; man and woman are a communion of persons imaging the Trinity.

## Key Theological Debates
- **Subordination vs. equality**: Does Eve's creation "after" Adam and "from" Adam imply hierarchy?
- **The "weaker sex" tradition**: 1 Peter 3:7, 1 Timothy 2:13 — misreading or inspired instruction?
- **The protoevangelium**: Is Genesis 3:15 a messianic prophecy? Who is the "offspring"?
- **Mary as second Eve**: Catholic/Orthodox tradition vs. Protestant caution
