# Denominational Health — The Complete Data

## The Overall Picture

### Protestant Denominations — Membership Trends
**Declining** (all mainline Protestant; most evangelical):
- United Methodist: 11M (1965) → ~5M (2023)
- ELCA Lutheran: 5.3M (1987) → 3.1M (2023)
- Presbyterian USA: 4.2M (1965) → 1.1M (2023)
- Episcopal: 3.4M (1965) → 1.6M (2023)
- American Baptist: 1.6M → ~1.0M
- Southern Baptist Convention: 16.3M (2006) → 13.2M (2022) — **critical: this is the first sustained decline**

**Growing** (or stable):
- Assemblies of God: 2.5M → 3.3M (2023)
- Church of God in Christ: 5.5M (stable/growing)
- Nondenominational evangelical: growing; no denominational tracking
- Seventh-day Adventist: ~1.1M (stable)
- Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints: 6.9M US (stable)

### Catholic Data (CARA — Center for Applied Research in the Apostolate)
- 2000: 62.4 million Catholics
- 2023: 52-55 million Catholics
- Weekly Mass attendance: 75% (1955) → 17% (2023)
- Priests: 58,000 (1965) → 35,000 (2023); average age 70+
- Seminarians: 49,000 (1965) → 3,100 (2023) — **the pipeline crisis**
- Parish closures: ~4,000 parishes closed since 1965
- The US has more Catholics than priests can serve — leading to merged parishes, lay leadership, deacons

### Orthodox Data (OCA, Antiochian, Greek, etc.)
- Total US Orthodox: ~1.5 million (undercounted; many immigrant communities not fully counted)
- Unique pattern: attracting converts from evangelical Protestantism and Catholicism
- Convert communities: ~15% of US Orthodoxy is converts
- **Relatively stable** because immigration (from Eastern Europe, Middle East) offset some losses

## The Southern Baptist Crisis (Lifeway/ACP Data)
The SBC is the canary in the coal mine for evangelical Protestantism:

| Metric | 2006 (peak) | 2022 | Change |
|--------|-------------|------|--------|
| Total membership | 16.3M | 13.2M | -19% |
| Weekly worship attendance | 6.0M | 4.5M | -25% |
| Baptisms | 345,000 | 177,000 | -49% |
| Churches | 44,696 | 46,706 | +2% (but smaller) |

**The baptism decline is the most alarming**: baptisms are the leading indicator; membership declines follow. A 49% drop in baptisms in 16 years means the SBC's future is in serious question.

## Nondenominational Evangelical Churches
The fastest-growing segment of US Protestantism — but poorly tracked:
- Approximately 20-25% of churchgoing Americans now attend nondenominational churches
- Megachurches (2,000+ weekly attenders): ~1,650 in the US (Hartford Institute)
- Combined weekly attendance of the 100 largest megachurches: ~2 million
- **But**: megachurch attendance growth often comes at the expense of smaller churches, not unchurched people

## The Megachurch Phenomenon
**US Megachurches** (Hartford Institute 2020):
- Total: ~1,750 churches with 2,000+ weekly attendance
- Total weekly attendance: ~9.3 million
- Average annual budget: $6.7 million
- Average staff: 38
- **Geographic concentration**: Texas (most), California, Georgia, Florida
- **Predominantly evangelical/nondenominational**
- **Racially**: majority white (about 60%); but growing diversity

**The 10 largest US churches** (approximate weekly attendance, 2023):

## Retention and Switching Data (Pew 2015)

### Who's Switching Where?
Christianity's **net losers** (more people leaving than arriving):
- Mainline Protestant: -12.9% (net)
- Catholic: -5.5% (net)

Christianity's **net gainers** (more people arriving than leaving):
- Evangelical Protestant: +1.2% (net) — but barely, and shrinking
- Nondenominational: +4.2% (net)

**Biggest gainer**:
- No religion: +4.7% (net)

### Where Do Catholics Go When They Leave?
- 53% become unaffiliated
- 16% become evangelical Protestant
- 10% become mainline Protestant
- 8% become nondenominational

### Where Do Evangelicals Go When They Leave?
- 48% become unaffiliated
- 22% become mainline Protestant
- 15% become Catholic
- 15% other

## The Seminary Pipeline Crisis

### Protestant Seminary Enrollment (ATS data)
| Year | Total Enrollment |
|------|-----------------|
| 1995 | 72,000 |
| 2005 | 74,000 |
| 2015 | 70,000 |
| 2020 | 64,000 |
| 2023 | ~58,000 |

**The concerning shift**: More students are pursuing non-residential/online degrees; fewer are pursuing ordained ministry specifically.

**Average age of seminary student**: 34 (vs. 25 in 1980) — people are coming to ministry as second careers, not primary vocations.

### Catholic Seminary Crisis (CARA)
- 1965: 49,000 seminarians
- 2023: 3,100 seminarians
- The US Catholic church cannot sustain its current parish structure with this pipeline
- Response: married permanent deacons (now 18,000 in the US); lay ecclesial ministers

## International Church Growth Data (CSLI 2023)

### Countries Where Christianity Is Growing Fastest (% annual growth)
1. China: ~5% annual (from estimated base of 70-130M)
2. Nepal: ~3.5%
3. Cambodia: ~3%
4. Bhutan: ~2.8%
5. Algeria: ~2.5% (from a very small base)
6. Sub-Saharan Africa: ~2.5% average

### Countries With Fastest Absolute Growth (New Christians per Year)
1. Nigeria: ~1.5 million/year
2. DRC: ~1 million/year
3. Ethiopia: ~800,000/year
4. China: ~600,000/year (estimate; very uncertain)
5. Brazil: ~500,000/year

## The Giving Data

### Church Giving in the US (Giving USA 2023)
- Total to religious organizations: ~$145 billion (2022)
- Per-member giving (evangelical Protestant): ~$1,200/year average
- Tithing (giving 10%+): 5% of Christians tithe consistently (Barna)
- The top 20% of church members give ~80% of total church income

### Global Mission Funding
- Total Protestant/evangelical mission spending: ~$17 billion/year
- US sources provide approximately 35%
- Per-capita giving to missions has declined in real terms since 1990
