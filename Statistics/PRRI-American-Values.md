# PRRI — American Values and Religious Change
*The intersection of religion, politics, and culture*

## About PRRI
Public Religion Research Institute, founded 2009 by Robert P. Jones. Rigorous; large samples (40,000+ for the American Values Atlas); focus on religion-politics-culture intersection. Robert Jones's *The End of White Christian America* (2016) used PRRI data.

## The End of White Christian America

### White Christian Share of the US Population
| Year | White Christian % |
|------|------------------|
| 1976 | 81% |
| 1996 | 65% |
| 2006 | 54% |
| 2016 | 43% |
| 2023 | 42% |

**"White Christian America" — the dominant cultural majority for most of US history — is no longer a majority.**

This is Robert Jones's central thesis: the loss of white Christian cultural dominance explains much of American political anxiety — including the appeal of "Make America Great Again."

## Religion and Politics (PRRI 2023)

### Party Identification by Religion
**Predominantly Republican**:
- White evangelical Protestant: 70% Republican/lean Republican
- White Catholic: 55% Republican
- White mainline Protestant: 51% Republican

**Predominantly Democratic**:
- Black Protestant: 80% Democrat
- Hispanic Catholic: 56% Democrat
- Jewish: 67% Democrat
- Religiously unaffiliated: 65% Democrat
- Muslim: 63% Democrat

### How Political Views Have Sorted by Religion
The partisan sorting is nearly complete:
- White evangelicals have moved from 50/50 in 1980 to 70-30 Republican today
- The unaffiliated have moved from roughly even to 65% Democratic
- Religious affiliation now predicts political affiliation almost as well as race

## Christian Nationalism (PRRI 2023)

PRRI's Christian nationalism index — views on whether America should be a "Christian nation":

**"Ambassadors"** (strongly agree with CN statements): 10% of Americans
**"Adherents"** (agree with most): 19% of Americans
**"Sympathizers"** (agree with some): 39% of Americans
**"Skeptics"** (disagree with most): 19% of Americans
**"Rejecters"** (strongly disagree): 13% of Americans

**Christian nationalism correlates strongly with**:
- White evangelical Protestant identity
- Distrust of immigrants
- Distrust of mainstream media
- Support for January 6 (24% of CN ambassadors say it was justified)
- Belief that "real Americans" must be Christian
