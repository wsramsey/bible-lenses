# Barna Group Research — Key Findings
*Evangelical-adjacent research organization; most focused on Christian practice and engagement*

## About Barna
Founded by George Barna in 1984. The most widely cited Christian research organization in evangelical circles. Methodology: telephone and online surveys; typically 1,000-2,000 respondents; approximately 8-10 studies per year. Has some bias toward evangelical frame but consistently rigorous.

## Church Engagement

### The "Churchless" Study (2014, updated 2023)
- **1993**: 24% of Americans were "unchurched" (hadn't attended church in 6+ months)
- **2014**: 33% unchurched
- **2023**: 38-43% unchurched (post-COVID spike never fully recovered)

### Who Is Leaving the Church?
Most significant finding: **the "dechurched" are the fastest growing group** — not people who were never Christian, but people who used to attend and have stopped.

Estimated 40 million Americans are "dechurched" — formerly regular church attenders who have left.

**Why they left** (Barna/Lifeway joint study 2023):
- Life changes (moved, job, family): 27%
- Work schedule conflicts: 22%
- No longer feel church is important: 21%
- Church not welcoming: 20%
- Didn't feel connection: 17%
- Disagreement with church's political positions: 14%
- Abuse or negative experience: 8%

## The Six Reasons Young Adults Leave (Barna 2011 — "You Lost Me")

David Kinnaman's landmark research:
1. **Overprotective**: the church is a place of fear, not exploration; anti-science posture
2. **Shallow**: faith formation failed; never learned to think theologically
3. **Anti-doubt**: questions were suppressed; faith required certainty
4. **Sexual**: purity culture; LGBTQ+ issues; unrealistic expectations
5. **Exclusive**: "our way is the only way" with no engagement with the complexity
6. **Hostile to doubt**: the church never addressed intellectual challenges seriously

## Worldview Research

### "Biblical Worldview" (Barna's Definition)
Barna defines a "biblical worldview" as agreeing with:
- Absolute moral truth exists
- The Bible is completely accurate
- Satan is a real being
- A person cannot earn salvation through works
- Jesus lived a sinless life
- God is omnipotent and omniscient

**Who holds this view**:
- 2003: 9% of American adults; 19% of "born again" Christians
- 2017: 17% of Americans; 46% of evangelical Christians
- 2023: ~9% of American adults; ~21% of those who identify as Christian

**The "worldview gap"**: Most self-identified Christians don't hold what Barna considers a biblical worldview.

### Syncretic Beliefs Among Christians (Barna 2020-2023)
Percentage of Americans who identify as Christian AND hold these beliefs:
- Karma is a real principle: 72%
- Multiple paths to heaven: 56%
- All religions teach essentially the same things: 52%
- The Holy Spirit is not a personal being but a symbol: 58%
- "A good person can earn a place in heaven by being good enough": 48%
- Satan is not a living being but just a symbol of evil: 59%

**Interpretation**: Many Americans hold a syncretic "moralistic therapeutic deism" (Christian Smith's term) rather than orthodox Christianity.

## The Spiritual but Not Religious (SBNR)

Barna data on the SBNR population:
- 37% of Americans describe themselves as "spiritual but not religious" (up from 19% in 2009)
- They pray (50%+ regularly)
- They believe in God or a higher power (70%+)
- They attend church rarely or never (90%+)
- They pick-and-choose beliefs from multiple traditions

## Pastoral Research

### Pastor Wellbeing Study (Barna/Pastors.com 2022)
During COVID and its aftermath:
- 42% of pastors seriously considered quitting ministry in the past year
- 38% say their mental health is worse than before COVID
- The top reason: "the immense burden of leadership during crisis"
- Second reason: political division in their congregation (46% experienced significant political conflict)
- Church attendance revenue declined: 43% of churches had budget shortfalls

### What Pastors Believe
- 97% of pastors believe in the resurrection
- 95% believe in the Trinity
- 63% are concerned their congregation has insufficient theological knowledge
- 44% feel their congregation is "spiritually immature"

## Millennials and Gen Z

### Gen Z Religious Profile (Barna 2018, 2021)
- **"Atheist"**: 13% of Gen Z identify as atheist (vs. 6% of all adults)
- **No religious preference**: 35% of Gen Z
- **Christian**: 36% of Gen Z (down from 55% of Boomers)

### What Gen Z Believes
- Less likely to believe in God: 58% vs. 70% of Boomers
- More likely to question sexuality and gender
- Most common concern about Christians: "hypocritical" (85%), "judgmental" (87%), "anti-homosexual" (91%) — from Barna's earlier "unChristian" study

### Gen Z Spiritual Hunger
- 69% believe "God is real and anyone can have a relationship with him"
- 78% believe "a relationship with God is something people can have today"
- Interest in spirituality/meaning-making is high — just not institutional religion

## Racial Dynamics

### The Black Church (Barna)
- Black Americans are the most religiously active racial group in the US
- 53% say religion is "the most important thing" in their life (vs. 28% of white Americans)
- 65% attend church weekly (vs. 40% of white Americans)
- More theologically conservative on gender and sexuality than white mainline Protestants
- More politically progressive on economics and justice

### Hispanic Christianity (Barna)
- The most rapidly shifting demographic
- 1990: 67% Catholic; 23% Protestant
- 2023: ~47% Catholic; ~26% Protestant (heavily Pentecostal)
- Hispanic millennials leave religion at similar rates to white millennials
- But: those who remain are often more religiously engaged than white peers
