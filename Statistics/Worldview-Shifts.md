# Worldview Shifts — The Data on Belief Change
*What Americans believe — and how it's changing*

## Belief in God (Long-Term Trend)

### Gallup Data (1944–2023)
| Year | Believe in God | Don't believe | No opinion |
|------|---------------|---------------|------------|
| 1944 | 96% | 1% | 3% |
| 1967 | 97% | 1% | 2% |
| 1981 | 95% | 2% | 3% |
| 2011 | 92% | 6% | 2% |
| 2017 | 87% | 10% | 3% |
| 2022 | 81% | 17% | 2% |

**The acceleration**: The 17-point drop from 97% (1967) to 80% (2022) took 55 years. The steepest portion was 2011-2022.

### What Kind of God? (Pew 2023)
- 45% believe in the God of the Bible
- 27% believe in a higher power or spiritual force but not the biblical God
- 17% don't believe in any higher power
- 11% unsure

### Absolute vs. Relative Morality (Pew 2002-2023)
- "Standards of right and wrong are absolute": 2002: 52%; 2023: 38%
- "Standards of right and wrong depend on the situation": 2002: 42%; 2023: 55%

**The relativism shift is real — but it applies inside the church as much as outside.**

## The Spiritual but Not Religious (Gallup/Pew)

- "Spiritual but not religious": 27% of Americans (Gallup 2017), 31% (Pew 2023)
- This group:
  - Prays (50% regularly)
  - Believes in some higher power (70%)
  - Does not attend organized religion
  - Practices meditation, yoga, or other spiritual disciplines
  - Is not going away — it's a stable and growing category

## The "Moralistic Therapeutic Deism" Finding (Christian Smith, 2005)
National Study of Youth and Religion surveyed American teenagers. Found not atheism or secularism but a default religion across all denominational identifications:

1. A God exists who created the world and watches over life
2. God wants people to be good, nice, and fair
3. The goal of life is to be happy and feel good about yourself
4. God doesn't need to be involved in your life unless you have a problem
5. Good people go to heaven when they die

**"MTD"** has become the most discussed concept in American religious sociology — it describes the practical religion of most Americans who still identify as Christian.

## Prayer and Spiritual Practice (Pew 2023)

### Prayer
- 55% of Americans say they pray daily
- 22% say they pray weekly
- 23% say they rarely or never pray
- Among Christians: 74% pray daily
- Among "nones": 20% still pray regularly

### Meditation
- 40% of Americans meditate at least occasionally
- 17% meditate daily
- Up from 12% in 2012 (Barna)

### Reading Scripture
- 14% of Americans read the Bible daily (Barna 2023)
- Down from 19% in 2011
- Among evangelicals: 27% daily (down from 35% in 2011)

## Religious Experience

### Have You Had a Religious Experience? (Gallup)
- "A religious or mystical experience": 49% of Americans (2009) — up from 22% in 1962
- The rise in claimed mystical experience even as institutional religion declines is one of the most striking data points in American religion

### Near-Death Experiences
- 8 million Americans report having a near-death experience (IANDS estimate)
- 57% of NDE experiencers say their religious beliefs changed
- The data suggests spiritual experience is rising while institutional religion declines
