# Christian Statistics and Demographics
*What the data actually shows about Christianity globally and in the United States*

## Major Research Organizations
- **[[Pew Research Center]]** — the gold standard; rigorous methodology; large samples; religious landscape surveys
- **[[Barna Group]]** — evangelical-adjacent; focuses on Christian practice and belief; church attendance and engagement
- **[[PRRI (Public Religion Research Institute)]]** — American religious identity; politics and religion intersection
- **[[Gallup]]** — long-running trends; church membership and attendance
- **[[Lifeway Research]]** — Southern Baptist-adjacent; evangelical church statistics
- **[[Gordon-Conwell CSLI]]** — World Christian Database; global Christianity statistics
- **[[World Religion Database]]** — comprehensive global data

## The Major Surveys
- [[US Religious Landscape Survey (Pew, 2007, 2014, 2023)]]
- [[State of the Church (Barna)]]
- [[American Values Atlas (PRRI)]]
- [[Global Christianity Report (Pew, 2011)]]
- [[Religious Nones in America]]

## Key Trend Notes
1. **The United States** is the most intensively surveyed country; treat US data with care when drawing global conclusions
2. **Self-identification ≠ belief ≠ practice** — surveys measure different things
3. **Global Christianity** is growing rapidly in the Global South even as it declines in the West
4. **Generational differences** are the most significant predictor of all trends
