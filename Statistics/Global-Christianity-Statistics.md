## The Top Line: Christianity's Geographic Revolution

### Global Christian Population

### The Geographic Revolution
**1900**: 80% of Christians lived in Europe and North America
**2023**: 67% of Christians live in Africa, Asia, and Latin America
**2050 (projected)**: ~75% in the Global South

## Regional Breakdown (2023, Pew/CSLI)

### Sub-Saharan Africa
- **Christian population**: ~700 million (2023)
- **1900**: ~10 million Christians; ~9% of population
- **2023**: ~63% of sub-Saharan Africa is Christian
- **Growth rate**: among the fastest in the world
- **Dominant tradition**: Pentecostal/charismatic and African Independent Churches
- **Projection 2050**: Africa will have more Christians than any other continent

**Country snapshots**:
- Nigeria: 105 million Christians (~53% of population); fastest-growing Christian population
- Ethiopia: 60 million Christians (Ethiopian Orthodox + evangelical)
- DRC: 90% Christian
- Kenya: 85% Christian
- Rwanda: 90%+ Christian (including Catholic majority)

### Latin America
- **Christian population**: ~565 million (~90% of population)
- **Historically**: overwhelmingly Catholic
- **The shift**: Catholic → Protestant (especially Pentecostal) at massive scale
  - 1910: 96% Catholic; <1% Protestant
  - 1970: 90% Catholic; 7% Protestant
  - 2023: ~68% Catholic; ~20% Protestant/Pentecostal
- Brazil: 185 million Christians; ~42% now identify as evangelical (up from 8% in 1960)
- Guatemala: now majority Protestant

### Asia
- **Christian population**: ~360 million (~8.5% of Asia's population)
- **South Korea**: 30% Christian (1% in 1900); 12 million Protestants + 5 million Catholics
- **Philippines**: 87% Catholic (the only predominantly Catholic country in Asia)
- **China**: estimates range from 70-130 million Christians (government: 38 million); explosive growth despite (or because of) restrictions
- **India**: 28-60 million Christians (2.3-5% of population); Mar Thoma + Catholic + evangelical
- **Indonesia**: 28 million Christians (~10%)

### Europe
- **Christian identification**: dropping rapidly
- **UK**: 59% identify as Christian (2021 census, down from 72% in 2011)
- **Germany**: 54% Christian (falling 2% per year)
- **France**: 41% Christian (majority Catholic in name only)
- **Czech Republic**: 12% — one of the least religious countries in the world
- **Weekly church attendance, Western Europe**: 5-10% in most countries
- **Eastern Europe**: more religious; Poland 90%+ Catholic identification; Romania 80%+ Orthodox

### North America
- See [[US Religious Landscape]] for US data
- **Canada**: 54% Christian (2021 census, down from 77% in 1991)
- **Mexico**: 85% Christian (77% Catholic)

### Middle East / North Africa (MENA)
- **Christians as % of population**: falling dramatically due to emigration after wars and persecution
- **Iraq**: 1.5 million Christians in 2003 → ~250,000 now (ISIS and instability)
- **Syria**: 2 million Christians in 2010 → ~600,000 now
- **Egypt (Coptic)**: 8-10 million (10% of population); stable but under pressure
- **Lebanon**: Christians now ~32% (down from 50%+ in 1932)

## Fastest Growing and Fastest Declining (CSLI Data)

### Fastest Growing
1. Sub-Saharan Africa (esp. Nigeria, Ethiopia, DRC)
2. China (underground house churches)
3. Iran (despite persecution; estimated fastest per-capita growth)
4. Nepal (from ~1% in 1950 to ~1.5 million today)
5. Global Pentecostalism overall

### Fastest Declining
1. Western Europe (UK, Germany, France, Netherlands)
2. United States (especially mainline Protestant)
3. Australia and Canada
4. Middle East (emigration/persecution)

## The Bible Translation Milestone
- Languages with the complete Bible: 736
- Languages with the NT: 1,658
- Languages with at least some Scripture: 3,589
- Languages with no Scripture yet: ~4,000 (mostly small)
- **Wycliffe goal**: "Scripture access for all people by 2033"

## Christianity and Poverty
- 50% of the world's Christians live on less than $10/day
- The "prosperity gospel" is most prevalent in the communities with least prosperity
- Catholic and mainline Protestant social services remain the largest non-governmental safety net globally
