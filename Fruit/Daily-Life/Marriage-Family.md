# Marriage, Family, and Sexual Ethics
*Christianity transformed the ancient family — for better and worse*

## What Christianity Changed
1. **Consent** — Christian marriage required the consent of both parties; Roman marriage was a property arrangement between families; canon law made consent central
2. **Monogamy** — enforced strictly across all social classes; the emperor couldn't have concubines; this was revolutionary
3. **Prohibition of infanticide** — the Christian prohibition on exposing unwanted babies (especially girls) changed demographics; more women survived
4. **Elevation of celibacy** — unprecedented in the ancient world; celibacy as a higher calling (1 Cor 7); created monasticism and a life-path outside marriage for women
5. **Prohibition of divorce** — Jesus's prohibition (Matt 19:6) was stricter than both Roman and Jewish law; this protected women (who couldn't survive economically after divorce) but also trapped people in abusive marriages

## By Tradition
- **Catholic** — marriage as a sacrament; indissoluble; no contraception (Humanae Vitae, 1968); annulment as the only way out; celibate priesthood
- **Orthodox** — allows divorce and remarriage (up to 3 marriages); married priests (but celibate bishops)
- **Protestant** — marriage as a civil covenant, not a sacrament (Luther); divorce permitted (reluctantly); contraception permitted (after 1930 Lambeth Conference); married clergy
- **Evangelical** — complementarianism vs. egalitarianism debate; purity culture (Joshua Harris, *I Kissed Dating Goodbye*); the "Billy Graham Rule" (never be alone with a woman not your wife)

## The Rotten Fruit
- Purity culture — shame-based sexual ethics that damaged generations; *I Kissed Dating Goodbye* author later deconstructed his own teaching
- Coverture — Christian legal tradition that made wives property of husbands
- Abuse enabled by "submission" theology
- Clergy sexual abuse — the Catholic sex abuse scandal is the most devastating institutional failure in modern Christian history
- LGBTQ+ exclusion — ongoing; causes real harm
