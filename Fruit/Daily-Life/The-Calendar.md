# The Calendar, the Weekend, and Time Itself
*Christianity reorganized how humanity experiences time*

> **The claim:** The seven-day week, the weekend, the annual calendar (Christmas, Easter, holidays), the dating system (AD/BC → CE/BCE), and the very structure of the Western year are Christian impositions on time.

## What Christianity Did to Time
1. **Sunday** — Constantine made Sunday a day of rest (321 AD); the concept of a weekly day off is Judeo-Christian; the pagan world had market days but no universal rest day
2. **The weekend** — the two-day weekend (Saturday + Sunday) is a Jewish-Christian hybrid; Saturday (Jewish Sabbath) + Sunday (Christian Lord's Day)
3. **The calendar year** — Christmas (Dec 25), Easter (moveable), Lent, Advent, Pentecost — the church year structures the Western calendar; most public holidays in Western countries are Christian in origin
4. **AD/BC** — the entire dating system is pegged to the birth of Christ; even the "neutral" CE/BCE system merely relabels the same pivot point
5. **Monasteries invented the clock** — Benedictine monasteries structured the day around the canonical hours (Matins, Lauds, Prime, Terce, Sext, None, Vespers, Compline); this regularity drove the development of mechanical clocks
6. **Hospital visiting hours, school terms, fiscal years** — all shaped by the church calendar

## Beer
Yes, beer. Monks were the master brewers of medieval Europe. Belgian Trappist beer is still brewed in monasteries. The reason pubs in England close on Sunday (or did) is the Lord's Day.

## By Tradition
- **Catholic/Orthodox** — the liturgical calendar; saints' days structure the year
- **Protestant** — simplified the calendar but kept Sunday; Puritans tried to abolish Christmas (too Catholic) but failed
- **Secular inheritance** — even post-Christian societies operate on the Christian calendar
