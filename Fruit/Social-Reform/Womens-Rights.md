# Christianity and Women's Rights
*The tradition that both liberated and oppressed women*

## The Good Fruit
- **Early church** — women held significant roles: Phoebe (deacon), Priscilla (teacher), Junia (apostle?); women were among the first converts and the first witnesses to the resurrection
- **Monasticism** — convents gave women an alternative to marriage; education, leadership, intellectual life; Hildegard, Teresa, Clare
- **Quakers** — full equality from the beginning; women preached from the 1650s
- **Suffrage** — Frances Willard (Methodist) and the WCTU were major forces for women's suffrage; most suffragists were devout Christians
- **Abolitionist women** — the Grimké sisters, Sojourner Truth; women's rights grew directly from abolitionist Christianity
- **Modern egalitarian movement** — Christians for Biblical Equality; Scot McKnight, N.T. Wright argue for women's ordination from Scripture

## The Rotten Fruit
- **1 Timothy 2:12** — "I do not permit a woman to teach or to assume authority over a man"; used for 2,000 years to exclude women
- **Augustine on women** — women bear the image of God only jointly with their husbands; alone, they do not
- **Coverture** — Christian legal tradition making wives their husbands' property
- **Complementarianism** — the Council on Biblical Manhood and Womanhood; John Piper, Wayne Grudem; women are equal in dignity but subordinate in role
- **Catholic** — no women's ordination; recent popes have declared this infallibly settled
- **Orthodox** — same; no women's ordination
- **Purity culture** — disproportionately burdens women with responsibility for male lust
