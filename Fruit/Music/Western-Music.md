# Christianity and Western Music
*The church invented musical notation, polyphony, and the Western harmonic system*

> **The claim:** Western music as we know it — from notation to harmony to the symphony — was invented in the church, by the church, for the church. Music theory, music education, and musical institutions are Christian fruit.

## The Evidence
1. **Gregorian chant (6th c.)** — Pope Gregory I standardized the chant tradition; this is the root of all Western music
2. **Musical notation** — Guido of Arezzo (991–1033), a Benedictine monk, invented the staff notation system; the names of the notes (do, re, mi...) come from a Latin hymn to St. John; *without notation, complex music cannot exist*
3. **Polyphony (9th–12th c.)** — multiple simultaneous melodies; developed in monasteries and cathedrals; the *Musica Enchiriadis* (9th c.) is the first treatise
4. **Bach (1685–1750)** — "The aim and final end of all music should be none other than the glory of God and the refreshment of the soul"; signed his scores *Soli Deo Gloria*; virtually all major works were for the Lutheran church
5. **Mozart, Haydn, Beethoven** — all wrote major sacred works; the Mass and Requiem forms are among the pinnacles of Western music
6. **Hymns** — Luther ("A Mighty Fortress"), Watts ("Joy to the World"), Wesley ("Hark! The Herald Angels Sing"); the hymn tradition created congregational singing
7. **African-American spirituals and gospel** — born in slavery; the most emotionally powerful Christian music ever created; influenced blues, jazz, soul, rock, and hip-hop

## By Tradition
- **Catholic** — chant, polyphony, the Mass as musical form; Palestrina, Victoria, Mozart's Requiem
- **Lutheran** — Bach; chorale tradition; congregational singing
- **Anglican** — choral evensong; Handel's *Messiah*; Vaughan Williams, Britten
- **Methodist** — the Wesleys wrote 6,000+ hymns
- **African-American** — spirituals, gospel, Kirk Franklin; the most globally influential Christian musical tradition today
- **Pentecostal** — contemporary worship music; Hillsong, Bethel, Elevation; the dominant musical form in global Christianity
