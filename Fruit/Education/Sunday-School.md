# Sunday Schools and Mass Education
*1780 · Anglican / Methodist*

> **The claim:** The Sunday School movement — started to teach literacy to working-class children who worked six days a week — was the precursor to universal public education in Britain and America.

## The Story
**Robert Raikes** (1735–1811), an Anglican newspaper publisher in Gloucester, started Sunday Schools in 1780 to teach poor children to read. The children worked in factories Monday–Saturday; Sunday was the only day available. By 1831, Sunday Schools in Britain enrolled 1.25 million children — in a country with no public education system.

## The Impact
- Provided the organizational model for the public school movement
- The first mass education of working-class children in history
- Spread globally through Methodist and evangelical missions
- Became the template for Christian education programs that persist today

## By Tradition
- **Anglican** — Raikes founded it
- **Methodist** — John Wesley enthusiastically promoted it; Methodists became the primary operators
- **American evangelicals** — the American Sunday School Union (1824) standardized curriculum
