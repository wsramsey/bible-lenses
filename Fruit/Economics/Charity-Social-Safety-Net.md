# Charity and the Social Safety Net
*Christians invented organized charity*

> **The claim:** Organized, systematic care for the poor — beyond individual generosity — is a Christian invention. The Roman world had *patronage* (rich men buying loyalty) but not *charity* (care for the poor because they are poor).

## The Evidence
- **Acts 2:44–45** — "All who believed were together and had all things in common"
- **The deacon's role** — from Acts 6; the first Christian office was specifically for food distribution to the poor
- **Emperor Julian the Apostate** complained that "the Galileans" fed not only their own poor but pagan poor as well — and this was making Christianity irresistible
- **Basil of Caesarea's Basiliad** — hospital + poorhouse + hospice
- **Monastic hospitality** — Benedictine Rule required receiving every guest "as Christ"
- **Catholic Relief Services**, **Lutheran World Relief**, **World Vision**, **Salvation Army** — the largest non-governmental aid organizations in the world are all Christian

## By Tradition
- **Catholic** — Catholic Charities is the largest private social service provider in the US
- **Evangelical** — World Vision is the largest Christian humanitarian organization globally ($3.1 billion budget)
- **Salvation Army** — founded by William and Catherine Booth (Methodist) in 1865; serves 23 million Americans per year
- **Orthodox** — IOCC (International Orthodox Christian Charities)
