# Christianity and the Scientific Revolution
*Did Christianity help or hinder science?*

> **The claim (Stark, Harrison, Hannam):** The Scientific Revolution was not a triumph of reason over religion but a product of specifically Christian assumptions about creation — that the universe is rational, orderly, contingent, and comprehensible because it was made by a rational God. No other civilization produced experimental science because no other theology provided these conditions.

## The Argument For
1. **Creation is rational** — because God is rational (Logos, John 1:1); therefore creation can be understood by reason
2. **Creation is contingent** — it didn't have to be this way; therefore you must *observe* to learn how it actually is (against Greek armchair deduction)
3. **Creation is good** — matter is not evil (against Gnosticism); studying it is dignified, even holy
4. **Natural law** — the idea that nature follows regular, comprehensible laws is a theological concept before it is scientific
5. **The medieval university** — provided the institutional setting for sustained inquiry; see Education notes

## The Scientists
The founders of modern science were almost all Christians:
- **Copernicus** — Catholic canon
- **Galileo** — devout Catholic (his conflict with the church was political, not fundamentally about religion vs. science)
- **Kepler** — Lutheran; believed he was "thinking God's thoughts after him"
- **Newton** — wrote more theology than physics; Arian Christian
- **Boyle** — devout Anglican; founded the Boyle Lectures for proving Christianity
- **Faraday** — Sandemanian (obscure Christian sect)
- **Mendel** — Augustinian monk; genetics
- **Lemaitre** — Catholic priest; proposed the Big Bang theory

## The Conflict Thesis (Draper-White)
John William Draper's *History of the Conflict Between Religion and Science* (1874) and Andrew Dickson White's *A History of the Warfare of Science with Theology in Christendom* (1896) — the "conflict thesis" — has been demolished by historians of science. No serious historian of science holds it anymore. But it persists in popular culture.

## The Genuine Tensions
- **Galileo affair** — a real case of institutional overreach; but the science was also legitimately uncertain at the time
- **Darwin** — forced genuine theological rethinking; young-earth creationism is a 20th century invention (not the historical Christian position)
- **Stem cell research, climate change** — ongoing tensions between certain Christian communities and scientific consensus
