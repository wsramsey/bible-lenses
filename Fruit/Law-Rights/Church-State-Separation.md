# Church-State Separation
*A Christian invention against Christian theocracy*

> **The claim:** Church-state separation was invented by Christians who were persecuted by other Christians. It is not a secular idea imposed on Christianity but a Christian idea born from intra-Christian conflict.

## The Key Figures
1. **Roger Williams (1603–1683)** — Puritan turned Baptist; founded Rhode Island as a "shelter for persons distressed of conscience"; coined the metaphor "wall of separation between the garden of the church and the wilderness of the world"
2. **William Penn (1644–1718)** — Quaker; Pennsylvania's religious toleration was the most progressive in the colonial world
3. **John Locke (1632–1704)** — deeply influenced by Christian arguments; *A Letter Concerning Toleration* (1689)
4. **Isaac Backus (1724–1806)** — Baptist minister; argued that established churches corrupt both church and state
5. **James Madison** — influenced by Baptist arguments for disestablishment; the First Amendment

## The Irony
The people who most demanded church-state separation were Christians (Baptists, Quakers, Mennonites) who had been persecuted by *other* Christians (Anglicans, Puritans, Catholics). The wall was built by the persecuted to protect themselves.
