# The Crusades
*1095–1291 · Catholic*

> **The damage:** Holy war, massacres of Jews and Muslims, permanent damage to Christian-Muslim relations, the sack of Constantinople (1204, Fourth Crusade) which destroyed Christian unity.

## What Happened
- **Pope Urban II** called the First Crusade at Clermont (1095); promised remission of sins for participants
- **First Crusade (1096–1099)** — captured Jerusalem; massacred the Muslim and Jewish population
- **Rhineland massacres (1096)** — Crusaders murdered Jewish communities in Germany on the way to the Holy Land; the first major European pogrom
- **Fourth Crusade (1204)** — intended for Egypt; diverted to sack Constantinople; Catholic soldiers looted Orthodox churches; permanent wound in Catholic-Orthodox relations
- **Children's Crusade (1212)** — thousands of children marched toward the Holy Land; most died or were enslaved

## The Honest Assessment
- The Crusades were not purely aggressive; they were partly a response to Muslim conquest of formerly Christian territory
- But the form of the response — holy war, indulgences, massacres — was a betrayal of the Gospel by any measure
- The theological justification (Augustine's just war theory, stretched beyond recognition) corrupted both the theory and the church
