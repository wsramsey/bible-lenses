# Christian Antisemitism
*The longest hatred*

> **The damage:** Christianity fostered, justified, and perpetuated hatred of Jews for 1,900 years. The Holocaust was not a Christian act, but it was unthinkable without the 19 centuries of Christian antisemitism that preceded it.

## The Arc
1. **The "Christ-killer" charge** — Matthew 27:25 ("His blood be on us and our children"); used for centuries to blame all Jews for the crucifixion
2. **John Chrysostom's sermons** — *Adversus Judaeos* (Against the Jews, 386–387); "the synagogue is a brothel... a den of thieves"; among the most toxic texts in Christian history
3. **Crusader pogroms (1096)** — massacres of Rhineland Jewish communities on the way to Jerusalem
4. **Expulsions** — England (1290), France (1306, 1394), Spain (1492)
5. **Blood libel** — the accusation that Jews murdered Christian children; originated in England (1144); persisted for centuries
6. **Luther's *On the Jews and Their Lies* (1543)** — recommended burning synagogues, confiscating property, forced labor; the Nazis reprinted it
7. **Russian pogroms (1881–1921)** — Orthodox-majority Russia; blessed by the church
8. **The Holocaust** — Hitler was not motivated by Christianity, but Christian antisemitism created the cultural conditions; "Pius XII's silence" remains debated
9. **Nostra Aetate (1965)** — Vatican II officially repudiated the deicide charge and affirmed the ongoing validity of God's covenant with the Jewish people

## The Honest Assessment
Christian antisemitism is not a side effect or a distortion; it is rooted in the New Testament itself (or at least in how it was read). The Gospel of John's references to "the Jews" as a hostile group, the Matthean blood curse, Paul's complex relationship with Judaism — these texts were weaponized for 19 centuries. Undoing this requires not just apologies but hermeneutical transformation — which is part of what the [[New Perspective on Paul]] is doing.
