# Clergy Sexual Abuse
*The catastrophic institutional failure*

> **The damage:** Systematic sexual abuse of children and vulnerable adults by clergy, covered up by institutional leadership to protect the institution's reputation.

## Catholic
- **Scale** — John Jay Report (2004): 4,392 priests accused of abusing 10,667 minors in the US (1950–2002); the real numbers are certainly higher
- **Cover-up** — bishops systematically moved abusive priests to new parishes rather than reporting to police; Cardinal Bernard Law (Boston) is the paradigm case
- **Global** — Ireland (Ryan Report, Murphy Report), Australia (Royal Commission), Germany, France, Chile — the pattern is universal
- **Financial cost** — $4 billion+ in settlements in the US alone
- **Trust cost** — the single greatest cause of Catholic Church decline in the developed world

## Protestant
- **Southern Baptist Convention** — independent investigation (2022) revealed decades of abuse and cover-up; 700+ abusers identified
- **Jean Vanier** — founder of L'Arche communities; revealed after death to have abused at least 25 women
- **Ravi Zacharias** — evangelical apologist; posthumously revealed to have been a serial abuser
- **Bill Hybels** — founder of Willow Creek; resigned amid allegations
- **Mars Hill / Mark Driscoll** — spiritual abuse and authoritarian leadership

## The Pattern
Across all traditions:
1. Authority figure has unsupervised access to vulnerable people
2. Institutional culture discourages questioning authority
3. Reporting mechanisms are internal (to the institution) not external (to police)
4. When abuse is reported, institutional survival takes priority over victims
5. Theological language is used to silence victims ("forgive," "submit to authority," "don't cause scandal")

## What It Means for the Vault
This is not a footnote. This is the rotten core of institutional Christianity's failure to practice what it preaches. Any honest account of Christianity's impact must put this alongside the hospitals and universities.
