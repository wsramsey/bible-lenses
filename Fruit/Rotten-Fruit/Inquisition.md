# The Inquisition
*1184–1834 · Catholic*

> **The damage:** Institutional persecution of heresy through torture, forced confession, imprisonment, and execution; the Spanish Inquisition specifically targeted converted Jews (*conversos*) and Muslims (*moriscos*).

## What Happened
- **Episcopal Inquisition (1184)** — Pope Lucius III; bishops required to investigate heresy
- **Papal Inquisition (1231)** — Gregory IX; Dominican friars as inquisitors
- **Spanish Inquisition (1478)** — Ferdinand and Isabella; primarily targeted Jewish and Muslim converts suspected of secret practice; estimated 3,000–5,000 executions over 350 years
- **Roman Inquisition (1542)** — established to combat Protestantism; tried Galileo; maintained the Index of Forbidden Books until 1966

## The Honest Assessment
- Death tolls were lower than popular imagination (not millions; thousands over centuries)
- Inquisitorial courts were actually more procedurally fair than secular courts of the same era
- But the principle — that the church has the right to torture people for their beliefs — is indefensible
- Pope John Paul II apologized in 2000 for "the use of violence that some have committed in the service of the truth"
