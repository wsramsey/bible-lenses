# Colonialism, Forced Conversion, and Mission
*Christianity's most damaging global legacy*

> **The damage:** Christianity was the ideological partner of European colonialism for 500 years. Missionaries sometimes preceded soldiers, sometimes followed them, and sometimes were indistinguishable from them.

## The Evidence
1. **Doctrine of Discovery** — papal bulls (1452, 1455, 1493) authorizing Christian nations to claim non-Christian lands and enslave non-Christian peoples; not formally repudiated by the Vatican until 2023
2. **Requerimiento (1513)** — Spanish legal document read aloud (in Spanish) to indigenous peoples before attacking them; claimed the Pope had given their land to Spain
3. **Residential schools** — Canada and the US; Christian-run schools designed to "kill the Indian, save the man"; children forcibly removed from families; physical, sexual, and cultural abuse; graves still being discovered
4. **Missionary destruction of indigenous culture** — languages suppressed, sacred sites destroyed, traditional practices banned
5. **Belgian Congo** — Leopold II's brutality was blessed by Catholic missions; missionaries received land grants in exchange for "civilizing" the population

## The Counter-Evidence
Not all mission was colonial:
- **Matteo Ricci** — Jesuit; learned Chinese, wore Mandarin robes, engaged Chinese philosophy respectfully; the inculturation model
- **Bartolomé de las Casas** — the first European to defend indigenous rights
- **The Jesuit Reductions (Paraguay)** — protected indigenous peoples from slavers; autonomous communities; eventually destroyed by colonial powers
- **African Christianity** — the explosive growth of Christianity in Africa in the 20th century was largely led by Africans, not missionaries; African Christianity is now the most dynamic in the world
- **Bible translation** — preserved hundreds of languages that might otherwise have been lost

## The Honest Assessment
Mission and colonialism were intertwined but not identical. The same tradition that produced the Doctrine of Discovery also produced Las Casas. The question is not "was it all good or all bad" but "how do you hold both realities at the same time."
