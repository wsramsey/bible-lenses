# The Rise of Islam (622–750 AD)
*Christianity's greatest external challenger — and the events that defined the divide*

## The Timeline
- **610 AD**: Muhammad receives first revelation
- **622 AD**: The Hijra — migration to Medina; Year 1 of the Islamic calendar
- **632 AD**: Muhammad dies; Islam controls Arabia
- **636 AD**: Arab armies defeat Byzantium at Battle of Yarmouk; Syria and Palestine fall
- **642 AD**: Egypt falls; Alexandria (heart of Christian theology) becomes Muslim
- **711 AD**: Spain invaded; Muslim armies reach the Pyrenees
- **732 AD**: Battle of Tours/Poitiers — Charles Martel stops the advance into France

## The Theological Challenge
Islam presented a direct challenge to Christian Christology:
- Jesus is a prophet (*Isa*), not the Son of God
- The Trinity is polytheism (*shirk*) — the greatest sin
- The crucifixion either didn't happen or didn't accomplish what Christians claim
- The Quran corrects the corrupted Bible

## What Christianity Lost
By 750 AD, Islam controlled the original heartlands of Christianity:
- Jerusalem, Antioch, Alexandria, Carthage — all Muslim
- North Africa (Augustine's home) — gone
- Much of Spain — 700 years of Muslim rule

## The Long-Term Impact
- **The Crusades** — Christian attempt to reclaim the Holy Land (1095–1291)
- **Theology** — [[John of Damascus]] first major Christian apologist against Islam
- **Transmission of knowledge** — Islamic scholars preserved Greek philosophy; it returned to Europe through Spain
- **Ongoing** — Islam and Christianity together account for half the world's population; their relationship remains unresolved
