# The Fall of Rome (410–476 AD)
*The empire falls — the church survives*

## What Happened
- **410 AD**: Alaric's Visigoths sacked Rome — the first time in 800 years; the psychological shock was enormous
- **476 AD**: Odoacer deposed the last Western Emperor Romulus Augustulus; the Western Roman Empire ceased to exist

## Augustine's Response
Augustine wrote *The City of God* (413–426) partly in response to pagan charges that Christianity had weakened Rome. His argument: there are two cities — the City of God (ordered by love of God) and the City of Man (ordered by love of self). Rome was always the City of Man; its fall is no theological crisis. Christians are pilgrims, not citizens of any earthly city.

## Why It Matters for Church History
- The church became the primary institution of civilization in the West
- Bishops took on civic functions (feeding the poor, maintaining roads, negotiating with barbarians)
- [[Gregory the Great]] (590–604) showed the church could govern when the empire couldn't
- The monasteries became the preservers of learning
- The papacy filled the political vacuum left by the emperor
