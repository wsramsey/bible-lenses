# The Donatist Controversy (311–411 AD)
*Who is the true church? The pure or the mixed?*

## The Origin
After Diocletian's persecution, the question: could bishops who had handed over (*tradere*) the Scriptures to avoid martyrdom continue to exercise valid ministry? The Donatists said no — the church must be pure; sacraments performed by unworthy clergy are invalid.

## Augustine's Response
The church is a *corpus permixtum* — a mixed body of wheat and tares (Matt 13). Sacraments are valid *ex opere operato* — by the act itself, regardless of the minister's holiness. The church's holiness is not the holiness of its members but of Christ.

## Augustine's Tragic Error
Under pressure, Augustine invoked Luke 14:23 ("compel them to come in") to justify using state force against the Donatists. This became the primary theological justification for coercion of heretics for 1,000 years — the Inquisition's ancestry.

## The Permanent Questions
- **Is the church a pure community** (Donatist/Anabaptist vision) **or a mixed body** (Augustine/Catholic/Magisterial Reformation)?
- **Does the holiness of a minister affect the validity of sacraments?**
- **Can the state ever coerce religious conformity?**
