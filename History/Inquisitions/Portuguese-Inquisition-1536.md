# The Portuguese Inquisition (1536–1821)
*Portugal's version — and its colonial extension to Goa*

## Origin
King João III of Portugal requested a papal inquisition from Pope Paul III. After years of negotiation (partly because Portuguese conversos lobbied Rome against it), it was established in 1536.

## Structure
Four tribunals: Lisbon, Évora, Coimbra (later suppressed), and Goa (from 1560).

## Primary Target: The New Christians
Like Spain, Portugal had forcibly baptized its Jewish population in 1497 (João II ordered mass baptism — the most brutal forced conversion in Iberian history). The resulting *cristãos novos* (New Christians) were the primary target.

**Key difference from Spain**: Portuguese conversos retained more Jewish communal identity than Spanish conversos; the suspicion of crypto-Judaism persisted for generations.

## The Goa Inquisition (1560–1812)
The most brutal extension of Portuguese inquisitorial power. Established in Goa (India) primarily to police the New Christian population and convert Indian Catholics.

**Targets**: 
- New Christians in Goa practicing crypto-Judaism or crypto-Hinduism
- Native Indians who relapsed into Hindu practices after baptism
- Anyone practicing traditional Indian customs considered incompatible with Christianity

**Methods**: same as Portugal but in a colonial context with even less oversight

**The scale**: thousands of trials; the exact number of executions is disputed (estimates range from hundreds to thousands)

**Voltaire** in *Candide* satirized the Goa Inquisition — it became a symbol of colonial religious violence in Enlightenment critique

## The Numbers
Approximately 1,175 confirmed executions in Portugal proper over 285 years; the Goa numbers are less certain.

## Abolition
Suppressed by the Marquis of Pombal in 1765 (largely), formally abolished 1821.
