# The Roman Inquisition / Holy Office (1542–1965)
*The Vatican's own tribunal — from Galileo to modernism*

## Origin
Pope Paul III established the **Congregation of the Holy Office** (*Sacra Congregatio Romanae et Universalis Inquisitionis*) on July 21, 1542 — primarily to combat Protestantism in Italy.

The driving force was Cardinal Gian Pietro Carafa, later Pope Paul IV (1555–1559) — the most zealous anti-Protestant reformer of the Counter-Reformation.

## How It Differed from the Spanish Inquisition
- Under direct papal control (not royal)
- Operated primarily in the Papal States and Italian territories
- More focused on theological correctness than ethnic/racial conversion
- More collegial process (committee of cardinals) than individual inquisitors
- Maintained the **Index of Forbidden Books** (*Index Librorum Prohibitorum*)

## The Index of Forbidden Books (1559–1966)
Cardinal Carafa established the first Index in 1559. It listed books Catholics were forbidden to read without special dispensation.

Notable entries:
- All of Erasmus's works
- All Protestant authors
- Copernicus's *De Revolutionibus* (1616–1758)
- Galileo's *Dialogue* (1633–1822)
- Descartes's works
- Locke, Hume, Voltaire, Rousseau
- Kant, Mill, Comte
- Darwin (never formally listed — but de facto)
- The Index was abolished in 1966 (Paul VI)

## The Giordano Bruno Case (1600)
Bruno — Dominican friar, philosopher, mathematician — was burned at the stake in Rome on February 17, 1600.

**His "heresies"**: denial of the Trinity, the Incarnation, Mary's virginity, and transubstantiation; belief in multiple worlds; possible pantheism.

Bruno is often portrayed as a martyr for science (because he believed in heliocentrism and an infinite universe). The reality: his execution was primarily for theological heresies, not science. But his case became the symbol of the church's hostility to free inquiry.

**Modern assessment**: Pope John Paul II expressed "profound sorrow" over Bruno's death. No formal rehabilitation.

## The Galileo Affair (1616 and 1633)
The most famous case — and the most misrepresented.

**1616**: The Copernican theory declared "contrary to Holy Scripture." Galileo warned not to hold or defend it.

**1632–1633**: Galileo published *Dialogue Concerning the Two Chief World Systems* — clearly favoring Copernicanism and making the pope's own arguments sound foolish. Tried by the Inquisition.

**The sentence**: Galileo forced to recant heliocentric view; placed under house arrest for the rest of his life (died 1642 at Arcetri).

**What the case was really about**:
1. Galileo had violated the 1616 injunction
2. He had made the pope (Urban VIII) look foolish
3. The science was contested (Galileo had no proof of heliocentrism he could demonstrate)
4. The church's interpretation of Scripture (Psalm 93:1, Joshua 10:13) was being challenged

**What it wasn't**: The church vs. science as such. The church supported astronomical research; most astronomers were clerics.

**The formal rehabilitation**: John Paul II in 1992 acknowledged the church's error. The commission's report was criticized for being too partial to the church.

## The Modernist Controversy (1899–1910)
The Roman Inquisition (now called the Holy Office) condemned **Catholic Modernism** under Pius X:
- *Lamentabili* (1907) — condemned 65 Modernist propositions
- *Pascendi* (1907) — described Modernism as "the synthesis of all heresies"
- The **Anti-Modernist Oath** (1910) — required of all clergy, professors, and seminary officials until 1967

**Targets**: Alfred Loisy (French biblical scholar), George Tyrrell (Jesuit theologian), Friedrich von Hügel

## The 20th Century Holy Office
The Holy Office continued to investigate and sometimes condemn theologians throughout the 20th century:
- **Hans Küng** (1979) — stripped of teaching authority for challenging infallibility
- **Charles Curran** (1986) — removed from Catholic University for dissent on sexual ethics
- **Leonardo Boff** (1985) — silenced for liberation theology
- **Tissa Balasuriya** (1997) — excommunicated (later lifted) for Mariology
- **Multiple liberation theologians** investigated under Ratzinger's leadership

## Renamed and Reformed
- 1908: renamed **Congregation of the Holy Office**
- 1965: renamed **Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith** (CDF) by Paul VI
- 2022: renamed **Dicastery for the Doctrine of the Faith** by Francis

Joseph Ratzinger headed the CDF 1981–2005 — earning the nickname "God's Rottweiler" — before becoming Pope Benedict XVI.
