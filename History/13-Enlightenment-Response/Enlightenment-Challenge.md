# The Enlightenment and Christianity (1650–1800)
*Reason dethroned revelation — or tried to*

## The Challenge
The Enlightenment challenged Christianity on multiple fronts:
- **Reason as authority** — Descartes, Locke, Kant: reason, not revelation, is the foundation of knowledge
- **Historical criticism** — Spinoza, Simon: the Bible is a human document, subject to historical analysis
- **Deism** — many Enlightenment thinkers (Newton, Jefferson, Voltaire, Franklin) kept a God who created the universe but denied miracles, revelation, and incarnation
- **Moral critique** — Voltaire: the church has caused more harm than good; *Écrasez l'infâme* (crush the infamous thing)
- **Hume on miracles** — the evidence for miracles never outweighs the evidence that reports of miracles are mistaken

## The Christian Responses
1. **Apologetics** — Paley's *Natural Theology* (1802); argument from design
2. **Pietism** — [[Philip Spener]], [[Count Zinzendorf]]: the heart matters more than the head; personal experience of God against Enlightenment rationalism
3. **Kant's religion within reason** — religion is fundamentally about morality; Jesus as moral teacher; stripped of metaphysics
4. **[[Friedrich Schleiermacher]]** — religion is not cognition but *feeling* (absolute dependence); relocates religion away from reason's attack

## The Deist Founding Fathers
Jefferson, Franklin, and many American founders were deists or unitarian-adjacent. The Constitution is explicitly secular. But the First Amendment protecting religious freedom was argued by Baptists and Quakers — not deists. The American religious settlement is complex.
