# Charlemagne Crowned Holy Roman Emperor (800 AD)
*Christmas Day, Rome — the political marriage of Christianity and Western civilization*

## What Happened
Pope Leo III crowned Charlemagne Emperor of the Romans on Christmas Day, 800 AD, in St. Peter's Basilica. Einhard (Charlemagne's biographer) says Charlemagne was surprised; historians debate this.

## The Carolingian Renaissance
Charlemagne brought scholars to his court — most importantly **Alcuin of York** — and launched a program of educational reform:
- Standardized Latin script (Carolingian minuscule — the ancestor of our lowercase letters)
- Cathedral schools in every diocese
- Standardized liturgy across the empire
- Systematic copying of manuscripts
- Correction of the Bible text

## The Dark Side: Forced Conversion
Charlemagne forced the Saxons to convert under threat of death. The **Massacre of Verden** (782): 4,500 Saxons executed for refusing baptism. Alcuin protested: "Faith is a free act of the will."

## The Precedent
The imperial coronation created the template for the medieval Holy Roman Empire — and the century-long struggle over whether the pope or the emperor held ultimate authority (Investiture Controversy).
