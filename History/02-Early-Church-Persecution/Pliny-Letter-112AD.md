# Pliny's Letter to Trajan (c. 112 AD)
*The most important pagan description of early Christian worship*

## What It Is
Pliny the Younger, governor of Bithynia, wrote to Emperor Trajan asking how to handle Christians. His letter is the most detailed non-Christian description of early Christian practice.

## What He Says
Christians "meet on a fixed day before dawn and sing a hymn to Christ as to a god, and bind themselves by an oath (*sacramentum*) not to do any crime... they eat food of an ordinary kind." He tortured two deaconesses to investigate; found "nothing but depraved, excessive superstition."

## Why It Matters
- Independent confirmation that Christians worshipped Jesus as God ("as to a god") by c. 112 AD — within living memory of the apostles
- The *sacramentum* (oath) possibly refers to baptism or the Eucharist
- Shows Christianity was significant enough to require imperial attention
- Trajan's reply: don't seek Christians out, but prosecute those who won't recant
