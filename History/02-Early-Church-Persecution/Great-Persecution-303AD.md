# The Great Persecution (303–311 AD)
*The last and worst Roman persecution — and the one that failed*

## What Happened
Emperor Diocletian (pressured by co-emperor Galerius) issued four edicts:
1. Destroy churches and Scriptures
2. Imprison clergy
3. Free clergy who sacrifice to Roman gods
4. All citizens must sacrifice

## The Traditores Problem
Some clergy handed over (*tradere*) the Scriptures to avoid death — *traditores* ("those who hand over"; root of "traitor"). When persecution ended, what to do with these clergy? Could their sacraments be valid? This created the **Donatist Controversy** that consumed North Africa for a century.

## The Aftermath
- Constantine reversed all persecution within two years
- The church that survived emerged stronger than before
- The martyrs (especially in North Africa and Egypt) became objects of veneration
- The Donatist schism revealed a permanent tension: rigorist vs. accommodationist Christianity
