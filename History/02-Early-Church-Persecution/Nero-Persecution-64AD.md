# Nero's Persecution (64 AD)
*The first imperial persecution — Peter and Paul die in Rome*

## What Happened
After the Great Fire of Rome (July 64 AD), Nero blamed Christians. Tacitus (*Annals* 15.44): Christians were "covered with the skins of beasts, torn by dogs, or doomed to the flames." The apostles Peter and Paul died in Rome during this period (tradition: Peter crucified upside down, Paul beheaded).

## Why It Matters
- First state-sponsored persecution of Christians
- Establishes the martyr tradition as central to Christian identity
- Peter and Paul's deaths in Rome anchor the Roman church's authority claim
- Revelation's "Beast" imagery likely references Nero (666 = Nero Caesar in Hebrew gematria)

## The Theology of Martyrdom
Martyrdom (*martyria* = witness) became the paradigmatic Christian act — bearing witness to the resurrection by willingness to die. Ignatius of Antioch (martyred c. 107) wrote: "I am the wheat of God; I must be ground by the teeth of wild beasts to become the pure bread of Christ."
