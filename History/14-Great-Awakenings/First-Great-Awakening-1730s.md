# The First Great Awakening (1730s–1740s)
*The revival that created American evangelicalism*

## What Happened
A wave of religious revival swept the American colonies and Britain simultaneously:
- **Jonathan Edwards** in Northampton, Massachusetts: "Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God" (1741); also the philosopher of revival
- **George Whitefield**: itinerant preacher who drew crowds of 30,000+; preached outdoors (unprecedented); crossed denominational lines
- **John and Charles Wesley**: methodical ("Methodist") approach to the Christian life; field preaching; societies for accountability

## Why It Matters
1. **Created American evangelical culture** — direct emotional conversion experience, personal faith, cross-denominational
2. **Undermined deference to established churches** — ordinary people could hear God directly; didn't need bishops
3. **Set conditions for revolution** — "The First Awakening was the First American Revolution" (some historians); created a common American religious culture across colonies
4. **Founded Methodism** — which became the largest Protestant denomination in America by 1800

## Edwards's Theology of Revival
Edwards was no emotional populist — his *Religious Affections* (1746) is the most rigorous analysis of Christian experience ever written. He distinguished between true and false religious affections, insisting that genuine revival produced lasting moral transformation, not just emotional intensity.
