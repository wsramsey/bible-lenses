# The Great Schism (1054 AD)
*Rome and Constantinople split — still separated*

## The Immediate Cause
Cardinal Humbert, representing Pope Leo IX, placed a bull of excommunication on the altar of the Hagia Sophia against Patriarch Michael Cerularius. Cerularius excommunicated Humbert in return. (The mutual excommunications were formally lifted by Paul VI and Athenagoras I in 1964 — a gesture, not a reunion.)

## The Real Causes (Building for Centuries)
1. **The Filioque** — The Western church added "and from the Son" (*filioque*) to the Nicene Creed without an ecumenical council; the East considered this heretical and procedurally illegal
2. **Papal primacy** — Rome claimed jurisdictional supremacy over all churches; Constantinople claimed it was first *among equals*, not supreme
3. **Cultural/linguistic divide** — West was Latin, East was Greek; after the 6th century they could barely communicate
4. **Political** — The Byzantine Empire and the Papacy were in constant tension over Italy, the Balkans, and missionary territories

## The Filioque in Depth
The original Creed (381): the Spirit "proceeds from the Father"
Western addition: "proceeds from the Father **and the Son**"

Eastern objection: this changes the eternal relations within the Trinity without conciliar authority; it also seems to subordinate the Spirit to the Son.

## The Living Schism
The Catholic-Orthodox split is the oldest surviving schism in Christianity (970+ years). The two largest Christian bodies in the world — Roman Catholic (1.3 billion) and Eastern Orthodox (~300 million) — cannot share communion.
