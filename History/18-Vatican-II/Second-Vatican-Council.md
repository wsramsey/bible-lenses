# The Second Vatican Council (1962–1965)
*The most significant event in 20th century Christianity*

## John XXIII's Vision
Pope John XXIII convened the council with the word **aggiornamento** — "bringing up to date." He wanted to "open the windows" of the church to the modern world. His critics feared he'd let the smoke of Satan in; his supporters saw the Holy Spirit.

## The Major Documents
- ***Lumen Gentium*** — On the Church: the church as People of God, not just hierarchy; collegiality of bishops; the universal call to holiness
- ***Dei Verbum*** — On Divine Revelation: Scripture and Tradition; historical-critical method legitimized
- ***Sacrosanctum Concilium*** — On the Liturgy: Mass in vernacular languages; facing the congregation; participation
- ***Gaudium et Spes*** — On the Church in the Modern World: engagement with contemporary culture, not rejection
- ***Nostra Aetate*** — On Non-Christian Religions: Jews are not collectively guilty of Christ's death; respect for Islam, Hinduism, Buddhism
- ***Dignitatis Humanae*** — On Religious Liberty: the right to religious freedom; a reversal of the 19th-century condemnation of liberalism

## The Ongoing Battle
Vatican II's interpretation remains contested:
- **"Spirit of Vatican II"** (progressives): the council opened a reform process that must continue — women's ordination, married priests, relaxed sexual ethics
- **Hermeneutic of continuity** ([[Joseph Ratzinger]]/Benedict XVI): Vatican II must be read in continuity with Tradition; it was not a rupture
