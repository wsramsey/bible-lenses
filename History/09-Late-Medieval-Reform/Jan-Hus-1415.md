# Jan Hus Burned at Constance (1415 AD)
*"You may roast the goose — but a swan will come"*

## Who Was Jan Hus
Jan Hus (1369–1415) was a Czech priest, theologian, and university rector in Prague. Deeply influenced by John Wycliffe. Argued:
- Scripture is the supreme authority, above pope or council
- The church is the body of the elect, not the institution
- Simony (selling church offices) and clerical immorality must end
- The laity should receive the Eucharist in both kinds (bread and wine)

## The Execution
The Council of Constance summoned Hus under an imperial safe-conduct. Once there, the council revoked the safe-conduct ("heretics need not be kept"). Hus was tried, refused to recant, and burned at the stake on July 6, 1415. His ashes were thrown into the Rhine.

## "A Goose Will Come"
Hus means "goose" in Czech. Tradition records him saying at the stake: "You are now going to burn a goose, but in a century you will have a swan whom you can neither roast nor boil." One hundred and two years later, Martin Luther posted the 95 Theses.

## The Hussite Wars
Hus's execution provoked the Hussite Wars (1419–1434) — Czech armies defeated five crusades launched against them. The Hussites survived, creating a proto-Protestant tradition that lasted until the Reformation.
