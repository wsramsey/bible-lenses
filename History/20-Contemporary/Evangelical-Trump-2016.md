# White Evangelicalism and Trumpism (2016–present)
*The most divisive event in American Christianity since the Civil War*

## The 2016 Election
81% of white evangelicals voted for Donald Trump. For many observers inside and outside the church, this was incomprehensible given Trump's documented lifestyle and character. For others, it was pragmatic: Supreme Court justices, religious liberty.

## The Rupture
The Trump alignment fractured evangelical leadership:
- **Supporters**: Jerry Falwell Jr. (Liberty University), Eric Metaxas, Robert Jeffress, Franklin Graham
- **Critics**: Russell Moore, David French, Beth Moore, Ed Stetzer, the Evangelical Covenant Church
- **Russell Moore's resignation letter** (2021) revealed the Southern Baptist Executive Committee had covered up sex abuse and treated Trump critics as enemies

## Christian Nationalism
A more extreme variant: the claim that America was founded as a Christian nation and must be governed by Christian principles — sometimes accompanied by January 6 imagery (Jesus flags at the Capitol).

## The Deconstruction Acceleration
For millions of younger evangelicals, the Trump alignment was the final straw: if this is what evangelical Christianity produces politically, something is theologically wrong. The deconstruction wave accelerated.

## The Questions
- **Has American evangelicalism become a political tribe rather than a theological tradition?**
- **Is "evangelical" still a meaningful theological category?**
- **What is the church's proper relationship to political power?**
