# The Sexual Revolution and the Church (1960s–present)
*The sharpest point of contact between Christianity and contemporary culture*

## Humanae Vitae (1968)
Pope Paul VI's encyclical reaffirmed the Catholic prohibition on artificial contraception — against the recommendation of his own commission. It was the most controversial papal document of the 20th century.
- Most Catholics in the West ignored it (*cafeteria Catholicism*)
- It defined a generation's relationship (or estrangement) with the Church
- Its core argument: the sexual act must not artificially separate its unitive and procreative dimensions

## The LGBTQ+ Debate
The most live current controversy in Western Christianity:
- **Traditional position** (Catholic, Orthodox, most evangelical): homosexual acts intrinsically disordered; marriage is male-female
- **Revisionist position** (mainline Protestant, some evangelical): the biblical texts don't address committed same-sex relationships; inclusion is the faithful response
- **The splits**: Episcopal (2003 — Gene Robinson); Anglican Communion threatened with schism; United Methodist Church split (2024); Southern Baptist and evangelical churches holding traditional position

## Purity Culture
The evangelical "purity culture" of the 1990s–2000s (Joshua Harris, *I Kissed Dating Goodbye*) — shame-based sexual ethics focused on female virginity — produced documented psychological harm. Harris himself later deconstructed his book and left the faith.

## The Deconstruction Wave
Millions of young Americans have "deconstructed" their evangelical faith, often citing:
1. The church's handling of sex abuse
2. Sexual ethics (LGBTQ+ exclusion)
3. The political identification of evangelicalism with Trump
4. Intellectual doubts (evolution, biblical inerrancy)
