# The Catholic Sex Abuse Crisis (exposed 2002)
*The most catastrophic institutional failure in modern Christianity*

## The Exposure
The *Boston Globe*'s Spotlight team published in January 2002 that the Archdiocese of Boston had systematically moved abusive priests from parish to parish rather than reporting them to authorities. Cardinal Bernard Law resigned. The story triggered global investigations.

## The Scale
- **United States**: John Jay Report (2004): 4,392 priests accused; 10,667 minor victims (1950–2002)
- **Ireland**: Ryan Report (2009): systematic abuse in Catholic-run institutions over decades; state-church conspiracy
- **Australia**: Royal Commission (2017): 4,444 Catholic institutions; 7% of priests accused
- **France**: CIASE Report (2021): 330,000 victims since 1950; the largest single study

## The Pattern
1. Priest abuses; victim reports to bishop
2. Bishop quietly moves priest to new parish
3. New parish unaware; abuse continues
4. Institutional survival prioritized over victims
5. Theological language used to silence: "forgive," "scandal," "pastoral charity"

## The Theological Questions
- **Can an institution that does this claim moral authority?**
- **Celibacy** — contested connection; most child abusers are married men; but the celibacy culture enabled concealment
- **Clericalism** — the root diagnosis of Pope Francis: treating priests as above accountability
- **The laity** — systematic exclusion of lay oversight enabled the cover-up
