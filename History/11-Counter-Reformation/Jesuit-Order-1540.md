# The Society of Jesus Founded (1540)
*The shock troops of the Counter-Reformation — and the world's most effective educators*

## The Founding
Ignatius of Loyola and six companions took vows at Montmartre (1534). Pope Paul III formally approved the Society of Jesus in 1540. Its distinctive features:
- **No choir obligation** — mobile missionaries, not enclosed monks
- **Special vow of obedience to the pope** — deployable anywhere
- **Spiritual Exercises** — the formation system Ignatius developed; four weeks of structured prayer and contemplation; still widely used
- **Education as mission** — Jesuits founded schools everywhere they went

## Francis Xavier
Xavier went with the first mission to Asia. In 10 years (1542–1552) he traveled to India, Sri Lanka, Malacca, Japan, and died trying to reach China. Baptized an estimated 30,000+ people. The greatest missionary since Paul.

## Jesuit Achievements
- Founded 700+ schools and universities worldwide (Georgetown, Fordham, Loyola, 200+ more)
- Matteo Ricci in China — the inculturation model of mission
- Reductions in Paraguay — protected indigenous peoples from slavers
- Produced [[Karl Rahner]], [[Henri de Lubac]], [[Jean Daniélou]], [[Pierre Teilhard de Chardin]]
- Suppressed by Pope Clement XIV in 1773 (political pressure from Catholic monarchies); restored 1814
