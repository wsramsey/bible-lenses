# The Rise of Global South Christianity
*The most dramatic demographic shift in religious history*

## The Numbers
- **1900**: 80% of Christians lived in Europe and North America
- **2020**: 67% of Christians live in Africa, Asia, and Latin America
- **2050** (projected): ~75% in the Global South

## Africa
Sub-Saharan Africa has gone from 10 million Christians in 1900 to 700+ million today. Much of this growth was led by Africans, not Western missionaries. African Independent Churches (AICs) and Pentecostal churches are the driving force.

## Latin America
Historically Catholic, but dramatic Pentecostal growth since the 1960s. Brazil now has more Pentecostals than any country except the US. The Catholic Church is losing millions to Pentecostalism annually.

## Asia
South Korea went from 1% Christian (1900) to 30%+ Christian (2020). China has 80-130 million Christians (estimates vary widely). The church in Iran is reportedly growing rapidly despite persecution.

## The Theological Implications
- **Ancestor veneration, healing, spiritual warfare** are live issues for most Global South Christians in ways that European theology didn't address
- **Prosperity gospel** — widely spread, widely criticized; responds to real poverty
- **The center of Christianity has moved south** — what does this mean for doctrine, practice, and leadership?
