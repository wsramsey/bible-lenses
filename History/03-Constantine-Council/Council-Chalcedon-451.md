# Council of Chalcedon (451 AD)
*The definitive statement on who Jesus is*

## The Question
After Ephesus, the opposite error emerged: **Eutyches** taught that after the Incarnation, Christ had only one nature (divine absorbed the human) — *Monophysitism*.

## The Definition
The Council of Chalcedon produced the **Chalcedonian Definition** — the most precise Christological statement ever formulated:

> "...our Lord Jesus Christ, the same perfect in Godhead and also perfect in manhood; truly God and truly man... in two natures, **without confusion, without change, without division, without separation**; the distinction of natures being by no means taken away by the union, but rather the property of each nature being preserved, and concurring in one person..."

The four negatives (*without confusion, without change, without division, without separation*) define the boundaries — ruling out both Nestorianism (division) and Monophysitism (confusion).

## The Aftermath — Permanent Schism
The **Oriental Orthodox churches** (Egyptian Coptic, Ethiopian, Armenian, Syriac) rejected Chalcedon as Nestorian. They are *Miaphysite* — affirming one united nature in Christ. This schism has never been healed. The Coptic Church of Egypt (12 million members) traces this break.

## Why It Still Matters
Chalcedon is the ecumenical baseline:
- **Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant** all affirm Chalcedon
- **Oriental Orthodox** do not
- **Jehovah's Witnesses, LDS, and others** reject it
- Every Christian confession implicitly takes a position here
