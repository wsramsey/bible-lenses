# Council of Ephesus (431 AD)
*Is Mary the Mother of God? — and what's really at stake*

## The Controversy
**Nestorius**, Archbishop of Constantinople, objected to calling Mary *Theotokos* (God-bearer, Mother of God). He preferred *Christotokos* (Christ-bearer). His concern: Mary is the mother of the human Jesus, not of the eternal Son of God.

**Cyril of Alexandria** responded: If you divide Christ into two persons (divine and human), you destroy the unity of the Savior. The person born of Mary is the same person as the eternal Son — therefore Mary is rightly called *Theotokos*.

## The Council's Decision
Nestorius was deposed and exiled. *Theotokos* was affirmed.

## Why It Matters
- **Christologically**: The question is whether Jesus is one person (Cyril) or effectively two persons loosely joined (Nestorius)
- **Mariologically**: *Theotokos* became foundational to Catholic and Orthodox Mariology
- **The Nestorian Church**: Nestorius's followers formed the Church of the East (still existing in Iraq and India) — one of Christianity's oldest surviving branches
