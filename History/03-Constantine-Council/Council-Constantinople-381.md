# First Council of Constantinople (381 AD)
*The Holy Spirit gets his place in the Creed*

## What Happened
Theodosius I called the council to settle the Pneumatomachian controversy: the "Spirit-fighters" denied the full divinity of the Holy Spirit. The Cappadocian Fathers ([[Basil of Caesarea]], [[Gregory of Nyssa]], Gregory of Nazianzus) had developed the theology of the Spirit's full divinity.

The council:
1. Confirmed Nicaea
2. Added the clauses on the Holy Spirit to the Creed: "the Lord, the Giver of Life, who proceeds from the Father, who together with the Father and Son is worshipped and glorified"
3. Condemned multiple heresies (Arianism, Apollinarianism, Macedonianism)

## The Filioque Controversy (seeds planted here)
The original Creed says the Spirit "proceeds from the Father." The Western church later added "*filioque*" — "and from the Son." This addition was never approved by an ecumenical council and became the primary theological cause of the Great Schism (1054).
