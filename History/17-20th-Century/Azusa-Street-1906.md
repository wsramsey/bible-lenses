# Azusa Street Revival (1906)
*The most globally consequential revival in Christian history*

## What Happened
William J. Seymour, a Black preacher and son of slaves, led a revival at 312 Azusa Street in Los Angeles beginning April 9, 1906. For three years, the revival ran continuously — multiple services daily, attracting thousands. Speaking in tongues (*glossolalia*), healing, prophecy, interracial worship (remarkable in Jim Crow America).

## Why It's Significant
The Azusa Street Revival launched **Pentecostalism** — now the fastest-growing Christian movement in history:
- ~700 million Pentecostal and charismatic Christians worldwide (the largest renewal movement in church history)
- The majority of Christianity's growth in the Global South (Africa, Latin America, Asia) is Pentecostal or charismatic
- Four of the five largest churches in the world are Pentecostal

## The Racial Dimension
Seymour was Black; Azusa Street was explicitly interracial at a time when this was illegal in parts of America. Frank Bartleman wrote: "The color line was washed away in the blood." It didn't last — Pentecostalism eventually segregated along American racial lines — but the founding vision was remarkable.
