# Karl Barth's *Epistle to the Romans* (1919)
*"A bomb on the playground of theologians"*

## The Context
August 1914: 93 German intellectuals — including Barth's own liberal theology professors — signed a statement supporting Kaiser Wilhelm II's war. Barth was devastated. Liberal theology had failed its most basic test: it had become the spiritual legitimation of German nationalism.

## The *Romans*
Barth wrote a commentary on Romans that was unlike any theology written in 200 years. Not historical-critical analysis. Not application of Enlightenment categories. But — direct, explosive engagement with Paul's text as the living Word of God addressed to the present:
- God is "wholly Other" — not a projection of human values, not the summit of human religious experience
- The Gospel is **crisis** (*Krisis* = judgment) — it judges all human religion, including Christianity
- The "infinite qualitative distinction between God and humanity" (Kierkegaard) — God cannot be domesticated

Adolf von Harnack (the greatest liberal theologian): it was as if Barth had "fallen off a cliff."

## Why It Matters
Barth's *Romans* launched **neo-orthodoxy** — a return to the Reformers and the Church Fathers, taking Scripture and the creeds seriously, refusing to dissolve Christianity into modern categories. It remains the most influential Protestant theological work of the 20th century.
