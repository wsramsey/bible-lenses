# The Confessing Church and Barmen Declaration (1934)
*The church says No to Hitler*

## The Deutsche Christen (German Christians)
When Hitler came to power (1933), the "German Christians" movement sought to align the church with National Socialism — removing the Old Testament (too Jewish), replacing the cross with the swastika, Aryan paragraph excluding Jewish Christians from ministry.

## The Barmen Declaration
Barth drafted, the synod affirmed (1934): six theses rejecting National Socialism's claims over the church:

> "Jesus Christ, as he is attested for us in Holy Scripture, is the one Word of God which we have to hear and which we have to trust and obey in life and in death." 

> "We reject the false doctrine, as though there were areas of our life in which we would not belong to Jesus Christ, but to other lords..."

## Bonhoeffer's Witness
[[Dietrich Bonhoeffer]] went further: not just passive resistance but active conspiracy against Hitler. He was executed on April 9, 1945 — three weeks before the German surrender.

His *Letters and Papers from Prison* and *The Cost of Discipleship* became the most read Christian books of the 20th century.

## The Failure
Most German Christians went along. Most of the Confessing Church was passive resistance only. The churches did not prevent the Holocaust. This failure haunts German (and global) theology to this day.
