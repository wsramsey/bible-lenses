# Council of Jerusalem (c. 49 AD)
*The first church council — and the most consequential*

> "It seemed good to the Holy Spirit and to us not to burden you with anything beyond the following requirements..." (Acts 15:28)

## What Happened
The question: must Gentile converts be circumcised and keep the Mosaic Law? Paul and Barnabas (arguing no) vs. Pharisaic Christians (arguing yes) traveled to Jerusalem. After debate, James delivered the verdict: Gentiles need not be circumcised. Minimum requirements only (no idolatry, no sexual immorality, no strangled meat or blood).

## Why It Matters
This is the most important decision in Christian history after the resurrection itself. If the Jerusalem Council had gone the other way — if circumcision had been required — Christianity would have remained a Jewish sect. The decision to admit Gentiles without requiring full Torah observance made Christianity a universal religion.

## The Theological Stakes
- **Paul's gospel** — justification by faith, not works of the Law; the Law cannot save
- **James's wisdom** — maintaining Jewish-Gentile fellowship; practical minimum requirements
- **The Spirit's role** — "it seemed good to the Holy Spirit and to us" — the first use of this formula, which becomes standard in council decisions
