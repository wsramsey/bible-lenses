# Paul's Missionary Journeys (46–57 AD)
*The journeys that carried Christianity across the Mediterranean*

## The Three Journeys
1. **First (46–48 AD)** — Cyprus, Pisidian Antioch, Iconium, Lystra, Derbe; first Gentile churches planted
2. **Second (49–52 AD)** — Philippi, Thessalonica, Berea, Athens (Areopagus speech), Corinth (18 months)
3. **Third (53–57 AD)** — Ephesus (3 years), Macedonia, Corinth, collection for Jerusalem

## The Letters
Paul wrote while traveling: Galatians, 1–2 Thessalonians, 1–2 Corinthians, Romans, Philippians, Philemon, Colossians, Ephesians — roughly half the New Testament.

## Why It Matters
In 10 years Paul traveled ~10,000 miles and planted churches in the major cities of the Roman Empire. His strategy — urban centers, synagogues first, then Gentiles, leave a leader, write letters — created the institutional backbone of Western Christianity.

## The Areopagus Speech (Acts 17)
At Athens Paul engaged Greek philosophy directly: "In him we live and move and have our being" (quoting Epimenides). The model for contextual theology — finding the point of contact between the Gospel and the culture.
