# Pentecost (c. 30 AD)
*Jerusalem · The founding event of the church*

> "When the day of Pentecost came, they were all together in one place... All of them were filled with the Holy Spirit and began to speak in other tongues." (Acts 2:1–4)

## What Happened
Fifty days after the resurrection, the disciples gathered in Jerusalem. The Holy Spirit descended as wind and fire. Peter preached; 3,000 were baptized in one day. The church was born.

## Why It Matters
Pentecost is the church's birthday — the moment the resurrection became a movement. It establishes several permanent features of Christian identity:
- **The Spirit as the church's power**, not human organization
- **Baptism** as the entry rite
- **The breaking of bread** (Eucharist) as the central practice
- **The apostles' teaching** as authoritative

## The Jewish Context
Pentecost was Shavuot — the Jewish festival celebrating the giving of the Torah at Sinai. Luke's choice of this date is theological: the Spirit is the new Torah written on hearts (Jer 31:33; Ezek 36:26), not on stone.

## Legacy
- **Charismatic/Pentecostal movements** see this as the normative pattern for Christian experience
- **Traditional churches** see it as a unique founding event, not necessarily repeatable
- **All churches** trace their origin here
