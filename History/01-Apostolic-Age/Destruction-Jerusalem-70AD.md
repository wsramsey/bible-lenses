# Destruction of Jerusalem and the Temple (70 AD)
*The event that separated Christianity from Judaism*

## What Happened
The Jewish revolt against Rome (66–73 AD) ended with Titus's siege of Jerusalem. The Temple was burned to the ground (August 70 AD). Josephus estimates 1.1 million Jews died; 97,000 were enslaved. The Arch of Titus in Rome depicts the menorah being carried away.

## Why It Matters for Christianity
1. **The Temple cult ended** — no more sacrifices, priesthood, Levitical system; the theological void had to be filled
2. **Christian-Jewish separation** — Jewish Christians fled to Pella before the siege; the break between church and synagogue accelerated
3. **Vindication of Jesus's prophecy** — "Not one stone will be left on another" (Mark 13:2); the Gospels were written in this context
4. **Hebrews makes sense** — the letter arguing Christ replaced the Temple system was written to Jews facing/experiencing this event

## The Rabbinic Response
Judaism survived by relocating its center from Temple to Torah. The Pharisees reconvened at Yavneh (Jamnia) under Johanan ben Zakkai. The Talmud is the monument to this survival.

## The Christian Interpretation
Varied:
- **Preterists** — Matthew 24, Mark 13 were fulfilled in 70 AD, not future
- **Traditional** — 70 AD was a type of the final judgment
- **Historical** — the Temple's destruction ended the old covenant age; Christianity is the new Temple
