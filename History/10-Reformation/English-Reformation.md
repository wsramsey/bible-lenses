# The English Reformation (1534–1662)
*The most theologically confused Reformation — and the most globally influential*

## The Stages
1. **Henry VIII (1534)** — Act of Supremacy; king as head of Church of England; dissolution of monasteries; doctrinally Catholic
2. **Edward VI (1547–1553)** — Protestant theology introduced; Cranmer's **Book of Common Prayer** (1549, 1552)
3. **Mary I (1553–1558)** — Return to Rome; 283 Protestants burned (including Cranmer); "Bloody Mary"
4. **Elizabeth I (1558–1603)** — The **Elizabethan Settlement**: Protestant doctrine, Catholic structure; the *via media*
5. **Civil War and Commonwealth (1640s)** — Puritans overthrow monarchy; Cromwell's republic
6. **Restoration (1660) and Act of Uniformity (1662)** — 2,000 Puritan ministers ejected from the Church of England; **Nonconformism** born

## Thomas Cranmer's Prayer Book
The **Book of Common Prayer** (1549/1552/1662) is one of the most beautiful liturgical documents in any language. Cranmer's genius: deeply Protestant theology in language that could be read as Catholic. The intentional ambiguity of the Elizabethan settlement in liturgical form.

Still used by 85 million Anglicans worldwide.

## The Long Shadow
- **The King James Bible (1611)** — authorized by James I; the most influential work of English prose
- **The Westminster Standards (1646)** — the Presbyterian answer to Anglicanism; still used by Reformed churches worldwide
- **Nonconformism** — Baptists, Congregationalists, Quakers, Methodists; all emerged from the English Reformation's aftermath
- **The American colonies** — Puritan New England; the First Amendment traces to Baptist and Quaker arguments
