# Calvin's Geneva (1541–1564)
*The most influential city in Reformation history*

## What Calvin Built
After his initial expulsion and return, Calvin spent 23 years transforming Geneva into a model Reformed city:
- **The Consistory** — disciplinary body of pastors and elders; enforced moral conduct
- **The Geneva Academy** — trained Reformed pastors; sent them to France, the Netherlands, Scotland, England
- **The Institutes** — the systematic theology of the Reformed tradition, revised four times
- **Congregational singing** — the Genevan Psalter; Calvin insisted ordinary people sing Scripture

## The Execution of Servetus (1553)
Michael Servetus, a Spanish theologian who denied the Trinity, was burned at the stake in Geneva with Calvin's approval. Calvin sought a less painful death (beheading); the city council insisted on burning. Servetus refused to recant.

This remains Calvin's most controversial act — and a paradigm of the Reformation's failure to consistently apply the principle of Scripture over tradition to questions of religious coercion.

## Geneva's Global Reach
Geneva became the "Protestant Rome." Reformed churches spread to:
- France (Huguenots) — 10% of France's population by 1562
- Scotland (John Knox; Presbyterian)
- Netherlands (Dutch Reformed; eventually the most Calvinist society in Europe)
- England (Puritan movement)
- New England (the Massachusetts Bay Colony)
