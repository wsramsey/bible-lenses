# The Thirty Years' War (1618–1648)
*The war that ended the wars of religion — and produced the secular state*

## What It Was
The most destructive war in European history before the 20th century. It began as a Protestant-Catholic conflict in Bohemia, expanded into a European war, and eventually involved every major European power. Germany lost 25–40% of its population.

## The Phases
1. **Bohemian (1618–1625)** — Defenestration of Prague; Catholic vs. Protestant
2. **Danish (1625–1629)** — Denmark intervenes for Protestants; loses
3. **Swedish (1630–1635)** — Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden turns the tide for Protestants
4. **French (1635–1648)** — Catholic France allies with Protestant powers against Catholic Habsburgs; pure geopolitics

## The Peace of Westphalia (1648)
The peace treaty that ended the war:
- **Cuius regio, eius religio** — "whose realm, his religion"; but now including Calvinism alongside Lutheranism and Catholicism
- **Religious minorities protected** — states could not force conversion
- **State sovereignty** established as the organizing principle of international relations

## Why It Matters
Westphalia is the birth of the modern secular state. When religion produces such catastrophic wars, states must be organized on non-religious principles. The Enlightenment's argument for separating church and state was powered by the Thirty Years' War.
