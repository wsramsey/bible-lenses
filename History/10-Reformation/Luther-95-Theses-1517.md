# Luther Posts the 95 Theses (October 31, 1517)
*The document that split Western Christianity*

## The Context
Johann Tetzel was selling indulgences near Wittenberg — certificates of reduced purgatory time, funded by the Archbishop of Mainz and the Pope for the rebuilding of St. Peter's Basilica. His sales pitch: "As soon as the coin in the coffer rings, the soul from purgatory springs."

## The 95 Theses
Luther posted 95 propositions for academic debate on the door of the Castle Church in Wittenberg (the academic notice board). They were in Latin. He also sent them to the Archbishop of Mainz.

Within weeks — via the printing press — they spread across Germany. Within months, across Europe.

## The Key Arguments
- Thesis 1: "When our Lord and Master Jesus Christ said 'Repent,' he willed the entire life of believers to be one of repentance"
- Thesis 27–29: Tetzel's jingle is blasphemous; the pope cannot reduce penalties for sin by decree
- Thesis 62: "The true treasure of the church is the holy gospel of the glory and grace of God"

## The Trial at Worms (1521)
Summoned before the Diet of Worms, Luther was asked to recant. His reply:

> "Unless I am convinced by the testimony of the Scriptures or by clear reason... I am bound by the Scriptures I have quoted and my conscience is captive to the Word of God. I cannot and will not recant anything, since it is neither safe nor right to go against conscience. **Here I stand; I can do no other. God help me. Amen.**"

He was declared an outlaw. Hidden at the Wartburg by Frederick the Wise, he translated the New Testament into German in 11 weeks.

## Why It Matters
The Reformation permanently divided Western Christianity. Its effects:
- The printing press + Protestant Bible → mass literacy
- The individual conscience as the final authority → modernity's individualism
- 30,000+ Protestant denominations today trace their origin here
