# The World Augustine Was Born Into
*354–430 AD · North Africa and the Dying Roman Empire*

## The Political World
Augustine was born in 354 in Thagaste, a small town in Roman North Africa (modern Algeria). He died in 430 as the Vandals were literally besieging the city of Hippo, where he was bishop. His entire adult life unfolded during the slow-motion collapse of the Western Roman Empire.

**Key events during Augustine's lifetime:**
- **376** — Visigoths cross the Danube fleeing the Huns; the barbarian migrations begin
- **380** — Theodosius makes Christianity the official Roman religion (Augustine is 26)
- **382** — Augustine goes to Rome to teach rhetoric; deeply ambitious, spiritually restless
- **386** — Augustine's conversion in a Milan garden; he is 32
- **410** — Alaric's Visigoths sack Rome — the psychological earthquake of the age; Augustine is 56 and begins *The City of God* partly in response
- **429** — Vandals invade North Africa; Augustine's world is ending literally
- **430** — Augustine dies during the Vandal siege of Hippo

## The Intellectual World
Augustine grew up breathing:
- **Cicero** — his *Hortensius* ignited Augustine's love of wisdom
- **Manichaeism** — the dualist religion that explained evil as cosmic conflict; Augustine was a Manichee for 9 years
- **Neoplatonism** — he read Plotinus before converting; it showed him immaterial reality was real
- **Donatism** — the rigorist schism that dominated North African Christianity; Augustine spent 20 years fighting it
- **Pelagianism** — the British monk who said humans can choose God; Augustine spent his last decades refuting it

## Why This Matters for Reading Augustine
When Augustine writes about the "restless heart" — he is a man who was restless for 32 years before converting, who watched the greatest civilization in history collapse around him, who spent his life in theological controversy, who wrote *City of God* because pagans blamed Christians for Rome's fall.

His theology of original sin isn't abstract pessimism — it's the theology of a man who watched humans do terrible things in the name of religion and empire, who knew his own heart's capacity for self-deception, who watched a thousand-year civilization crumble.

**The question Augustine's world forced:** If the Christian empire can fall, what is secure? His answer: only the City of God, which is not of this world.
