# The World Martin Luther Was Born Into
*1483–1546 AD · The Late Medieval Crisis and the Printing Press*

## The World at Luther's Birth (1483)
- The Ottoman Empire has just taken Constantinople (1453) — the Eastern Roman Empire is gone
- Columbus is 9 years old; America is unknown to Europeans
- The printing press is 28 years old — Gutenberg's Bible was printed in 1455
- The Italian Renaissance is in full flower — Botticelli is painting, Leonardo is beginning his career
- The papacy is at its most corrupt: Innocent VIII is pope; his successor Alexander VI (Borgia) will have children by multiple mistresses while serving as pope
- The Black Death has killed 30-60% of Europe in living memory; it still returns periodically
- The Hundred Years' War just ended (1453)

## The Church Luther Grew Up In
- **Indulgences** — certificates reducing time in purgatory; sold aggressively; funding St. Peter's Basilica
- **The papacy in crisis** — the Great Western Schism (1378–1417) had three simultaneous popes; still living memory
- **Jan Hus** — burned at Constance (1415) for saying similar things to what Luther would say; Luther initially didn't know this
- **Popular piety** — pilgrimage, relic veneration, saint's days; religion was everywhere and deeply superstitious by modern standards
- **Humanist reform** — Erasmus was trying to reform the church from within; his *Praise of Folly* (1509) mocked clerical corruption

## The Technology That Changed Everything
**The printing press** is the most important context for the Reformation. Without it, Luther is Jan Hus — burned and forgotten.

- Luther posted the 95 Theses in October 1517
- By December 1517, printed copies had spread across Germany
- By early 1518, they were across Europe
- For the first time in history, ideas could spread faster than institutions could respond

Luther later said: "Printing is God's highest act of grace." He wasn't wrong.

## Luther's Psychological World
- **The terror of God** — medieval piety was saturated with fear of divine judgment; hell was vivid and imminent
- **The monastery** — Luther entered the Augustinian monastery in 1505 after a lightning strike; he was terrified of death and judgment
- **The anfechtungen** — Luther's word for spiritual attacks; crushing anxiety about his salvation; the system of confession and penance gave no peace
- **The breakthrough** — reading Romans 1:17 in the tower: "the righteousness of God" is not God's standard that condemns us but the righteousness God *gives* us through faith

## Why This Matters for Reading Luther
Luther's theology of grace is not academic. It's the testimony of a man who nearly broke himself trying to earn God's favor through the medieval penitential system — and then discovered he didn't have to.

When he writes about the freedom of the Christian, he's writing from the inside of a liberation he personally experienced. When he attacks the papacy's sale of indulgences, he's attacking the system that had terrorized him.
