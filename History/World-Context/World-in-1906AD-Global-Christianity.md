# The World That Shaped the 20th Century Christian Thinkers
*1900–1945 · The Century's First Great Crisis*

## The Context That Produced These Thinkers
The Christian thinkers of the first half of the 20th century — Barth, Bonhoeffer, Lewis, Chesterton, Day, Weil, Merton — were all shaped by the same catastrophic world:

## World War I (1914–1918)
- **17 million dead**
- **Poison gas, machine guns, trench warfare** — industrial technology applied to mass killing
- **The death of liberal optimism** — progress, reason, civilization — all revealed as thin veneers
- **Christian responses:** Barth's neo-orthodoxy; pacifism; the Social Gospel's collapse

## The Russian Revolution (1917) and Communism
- **Bolshevik revolution** — explicitly anti-Christian; churches closed, clergy killed
- **Communism as secular religion** — complete with eschatology, martyrs, sacred texts
- **The appeal:** It promised justice for the poor; many Christian intellectuals (including the young Dorothy Day) were drawn to it
- **The horror:** Gulags, forced collectivization, 20 million Soviet deaths under Stalin

## The Rise of Fascism (1920s–1930s)
- **Mussolini (Italy), Hitler (Germany), Franco (Spain)**
- **The Concordat (1933)** — Catholic Church signed agreement with Hitler; deeply compromising
- **The Deutsche Christen** — German Christians aligning the church with National Socialism
- **The Confessing Church** — Barth, Bonhoeffer, Niemöller; the resistance
- **The Spanish Civil War (1936–1939)** — Catholics largely supported Franco; Protestants and leftists supported the Republic; Simone Weil fought for the Republic

## The Great Depression (1929–1939)
- **25% unemployment in the US** — bread lines, Hoovervilles, mass despair
- **Dorothy Day's Catholic Worker movement** — founded 1933, directly responding to the Depression
- **The Social Gospel's crisis** — could Christianity address economic catastrophe?

## World War II (1939–1945)
- **60–85 million dead**
- **The Holocaust** — 6 million Jews; the endpoint of Christian antisemitism
- **Hiroshima and Nagasaki** — civilization can destroy itself
- **C.S. Lewis broadcasting** on BBC during the Blitz — *Mere Christianity* born from wartime radio talks to a nation being bombed

## The Specific Contexts of Individual Thinkers

**C.S. Lewis:** An atheist until 1929, converted partly through conversations with Tolkien. Wrote *The Screwtape Letters* (1942) and broadcast *Mere Christianity* during the Blitz — Christianity as the answer to a civilization destroying itself.

**Dietrich Bonhoeffer:** The Confessing Church; the conspiracy against Hitler; executed April 9, 1945. His *Letters and Papers from Prison* written from a Nazi cell.

**Dorothy Day:** Converted 1927; founded the Catholic Worker 1933; served the poor through the Depression and WWII; consistently anti-war when American Catholics mostly supported every war.

**Simone Weil:** Brilliant French philosopher, Jewish background; drawn to Christianity but never baptized; worked in factory, fought in Spain, died of tuberculosis in London 1943 — refusing to eat more than occupied France's rations.
