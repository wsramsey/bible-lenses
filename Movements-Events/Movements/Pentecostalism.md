# Pentecostalism
*The fastest-growing Christian movement in history*

## The Numbers
- **~700 million** Pentecostal and charismatic Christians worldwide (2023)
- The largest Protestant "family" — larger than all traditional Protestantism combined
- Dominant form of Christianity in Latin America, sub-Saharan Africa, South Korea
- Four of the five largest churches in the world are Pentecostal

## The Origin: Topeka (1901)
Charles Fox Parham's Bible school in Topeka, Kansas. On January 1, 1901, Agnes Ozman spoke in tongues — the first recorded instance of glossolalia in the modern Pentecostal movement.

Parham taught the "initial evidence" doctrine: the baptism of the Holy Spirit is evidenced by speaking in tongues. This became the defining Pentecostal distinctive.

## The Azusa Street Revival (1906)
→ See [[Azusa Street Revival (1906)]] for full account

William Seymour; Los Angeles; interracial; continuous services for three years; missionaries spread across the world.

## The Assemblies of God (1914)
The largest Pentecostal denomination in the US. 69 million members worldwide. Founded partly to provide doctrinal stability to the rapidly spreading movement.

## The Oneness Pentecostals
A significant minority: deny the Trinity; baptize "in Jesus' name only." The largest Oneness body: United Pentecostal Church International.

## The Charismatic Renewal (1960s)
Pentecostal experience spread into mainline denominations:
- **Dennis Bennett** (Episcopal priest, Van Nuys, CA, 1960): spoke in tongues; announced it from the pulpit; national news
- **Catholic Charismatic Renewal** (Duquesne University, 1967): spread rapidly through Catholic universities and parishes
- By the 1970s, the charismatic experience was present in virtually every denomination

## The Third Wave / Vineyard (1980s)
**John Wimber** and the Vineyard movement: "power evangelism"; healing; prophecy; without the classical Pentecostal insistence on tongues as initial evidence.

**The Kansas City Prophets / Toronto Blessing**: Controversial manifestations — "holy laughter," "slain in the Spirit," prophecy movements; contested within the charismatic world.

## The Global South Explosion
The Pentecostal movement's most significant development: explosive growth in Africa, Latin America, and Asia:
- **Brazil**: the most Pentecostal nation on earth; 42% of the population; Universal Church of the Kingdom of God; Assembly of God Brazil
- **Nigeria**: Living Faith Church (Winners Chapel); Redeemed Christian Church of God; massive influence
- **South Korea**: Yoido Full Gospel Church (800,000 members — the world's largest single congregation)
- **China**: the underground house church movement; widely Pentecostal/charismatic in character

## The Prosperity Gospel Problem
A mutation of Pentecostalism:
- God guarantees financial blessing to those who give and have faith
- Sickness is a lack of faith
- Kenneth Hagin, Kenneth Copeland, Joel Osteen, Creflo Dollar
- Condemned by virtually every serious theologian across traditions
- Exploits the poor; contradicts the NT directly
- Massively influential in the Global South where poverty is acute
