# The Charismatic Renewal
*Pentecostal experience invades the historic churches*

## The Beginning: Van Nuys (1960)
On April 3, 1960, **Dennis Bennett** — rector of St. Mark's Episcopal Church, Van Nuys, California — announced from the pulpit that he had spoken in tongues. Two assistant pastors immediately resigned. The story made *Time* and *Newsweek*.

This was the beginning of the charismatic movement in mainline Protestant denominations. Within a decade, it had spread to Lutherans, Presbyterians, Methodists, Baptists — and Catholics.

## The Catholic Charismatic Renewal (1967)
The most surprising development: Pentecostal experience inside the Catholic Church.

**The Duquesne Weekend** (February 1967): A group of Catholic professors and students at Duquesne University prayed together; several spoke in tongues and experienced what they described as baptism in the Holy Spirit.

**Notre Dame** became the center; annual conferences drew tens of thousands.

**Cardinal Léon-Joseph Suenens** of Belgium became the movement's episcopal patron; gave it legitimacy within the hierarchy.

By 1975: 10 million Catholic charismatics worldwide. Paul VI received a delegation warmly.

## The Vineyard Movement (1970s–1980s)
**John Wimber** (1934–1997): Former rock musician; became a Quaker; then associated with Calvary Chapel; founded the Vineyard Association of Churches.

**"Power evangelism"**: Healing, prophecy, and deliverance as tools of evangelism; not separate from proclamation but integral to it.

**MC510 at Fuller Seminary**: Wimber's course on "Signs, Wonders and Church Growth" at Fuller became famous and controversial; eventually discontinued.

The Vineyard became the most influential charismatic movement in the mainline evangelical world; its worship music (Vineyard Music) shaped evangelical worship globally.

## The Toronto Blessing (1994)
A revival that began at the Airport Vineyard church in Toronto:
- "Holy laughter" — uncontrolled laughing
- "Slain in the Spirit" — falling to the floor
- Roaring, shaking, prophetic experiences
- Hundreds of thousands visited; spread globally

Extremely controversial: John Wimber eventually disassociated the Toronto church from the Vineyard.

## The New Apostolic Reformation (NAR)
C. Peter Wagner coined the term: a new "apostolic age" in which God is restoring apostles and prophets to the church.

Features:
- Belief in present-day apostles with authority over regions
- "Spiritual mapping" — identifying territorial demonic strongholds
- "Spiritual warfare" — prayer and spiritual activity to "take" geographic areas
- **The Seven Mountains Mandate**: Christians should take control of the 7 spheres of society (government, media, education, arts, religion, family, business)

The NAR has become significantly politicized in the US context; associated with QAnon-adjacent movements and the January 6 events.
