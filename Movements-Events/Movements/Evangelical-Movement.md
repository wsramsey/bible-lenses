# The Evangelical Movement
*The most influential Protestant movement in the English-speaking world*

## David Bebbington's Quadrilateral (1989)
The standard definition of evangelical Christianity — four characteristics:

1. **Conversionism**: The need for a personal conversion experience ("born again")
2. **Activism**: The expression of the Gospel in effort, especially mission and social reform
3. **Biblicism**: A high regard for Scripture; it governs faith and practice
4. **Crucicentrism**: The centrality of Christ's atoning sacrifice

## Origins: The 18th Century Awakenings
Evangelicalism emerged from the **First Great Awakening** (1730s–1740s):
- **George Whitefield**: Transatlantic itinerant preacher; crossed denominational lines; preached to 30,000+
- **Jonathan Edwards**: The theologian of revival; *Religious Affections* distinguished true from false conversion
- **John Wesley**: Methodism; small groups; social holiness; the founder of organized evangelicalism

## The 19th Century
- **Charles Finney**: "New measures" revivalism; the anxious bench (precursor to the altar call); abolitionism
- **D.L. Moody**: Urban mass evangelism; Chicago; YMCA; non-theological; pragmatic
- **The Holiness Movement**: Wesley's "entire sanctification"; Keswick Convention (Britain); the Higher Life movement
- **The Social Gospel** overlap: Evangelicals led abolition, prison reform, women's suffrage, temperance

## The Fundamentalist-Modernist Split (1920s)
→ See [[Fundamentalist-Modernist Controversy]]

The modernist takeover of mainline denominations; evangelical departure into separate institutions.

## Neo-Evangelicalism (1940s–1950s)
**Carl F.H. Henry** and **Billy Graham** led a new generation that wanted to:
- Maintain evangelical doctrine (inerrancy, substitutionary atonement, personal conversion)
- BUT engage culture seriously (not fundamentalist withdrawal)
- Founded *Christianity Today* (1956)
- The Billy Graham crusades (1949–2005) defined evangelical culture for 50 years

## The Political Turn (1970s–present)
- **The Moral Majority (1979)**: Jerry Falwell Sr.; the Religious Right; abortion and homosexuality as political issues
- **The Christian Coalition**: Pat Robertson
- **The evangelical-Republican alignment** — by 1980 virtually complete
- **The Trump era (2016–present)**: 81% of white evangelicals voted for Trump; the most divisive moment in evangelical history

## The Current Fragmentation
Post-2016, "evangelical" is increasingly:
- A political identity (white evangelical Republican)
- More than a theological identity
- The "post-evangelical" and "exvangelical" movements are partly a reaction

**The global evangelical movement** is far more diverse than the American version:
- Global South evangelicalism is growing explosively
- Latin American evangelicalism is less political
- African evangelicalism is more supernatural/Pentecostal
- Asian evangelicalism (especially Korea) is intensely missionary

## Key Institutions
- **Christianity Today** (1956) — magazine
- **InterVarsity Christian Fellowship** (1941) — campus ministry
- **Campus Crusade for Christ / Cru** (1951)
- **The Lausanne Movement** (1974) — global evangelical missions
- **World Vision** (1950) — largest Christian humanitarian organization
