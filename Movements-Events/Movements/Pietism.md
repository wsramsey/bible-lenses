# Pietism
*The heart over the head — the movement that shaped modern evangelicalism*

## The Crisis Pietism Responded To
Post-Reformation German Lutheranism had become orthodox but cold:
- **Confessional orthodoxy**: battles over doctrine; catechisms; the right formulas
- **Little emphasis on personal transformation or devotional life
- **Clergy-controlled**: laypeople passive recipients of sacraments and sermons
- **The Thirty Years' War** (1618–1648) had devastated Germany; nominal Christianity everywhere

## Philip Jakob Spener (1635–1705)
His *Pia Desideria* (Pious Desires, 1675) is the founding document of Pietism. His proposals:
1. More use of Scripture among laypeople
2. **Collegia pietatis** — small groups (*conventicles*) for Bible study and spiritual growth
3. More emphasis on the priesthood of all believers
4. Preaching for transformation, not just doctrinal instruction
5. Training of clergy in piety, not just theology
6. Christian social reform

The *collegia pietatis* — small group Bible studies — are the direct ancestor of every small group movement in evangelicalism.

## August Hermann Francke (1663–1727)
Took Pietism in a more activist direction. Founded at Halle:
- An orphanage
- Schools for the poor
- A teacher training college
- A pharmacy
- A publishing house
- The first Protestant missionary training center (sent missionaries to India — the Tranquebar Mission, 1706)

**Halle became the center of a global Protestant network**: its graduates went to Russia (court chaplains), England (influenced Wesley), India, and America.

## Count Zinzendorf and the Moravians (1700–1760)
**Nikolaus Ludwig von Zinzendorf** (1700–1760): Godson of Spener; inherited an estate at Herrnhut (Saxony); welcomed Moravian Brethren refugees (descendants of Jan Hus's movement).

**The Herrnhut Revival (August 13, 1727)**: A Pentecost-like experience at a communion service; the community experienced a profound unity and joy; the beginning of the **Moravian Brethren** as a missionary movement.

**The Moravian Prayer Meeting**: Started on August 27, 1727 and ran continuously for **100 years** — members took two-hour shifts 24 hours a day.

**Moravian missions**: The first modern Protestant missions; before Carey; to the West Indies, Greenland, Labrador, South Africa, among slaves in the Caribbean. Zinzendorf himself traveled to the Caribbean, spending time among enslaved people.

**John Wesley and the Moravians**: Wesley met Moravian missionaries on a ship to Georgia (1735); was profoundly influenced; attended a Moravian meeting at Aldersgate Street (May 24, 1738) where his heart was "strangely warmed" — his conversion experience. Methodism is Pietism in Anglican dress.

## Pietism's Legacy
Virtually every evangelical tradition traces to Pietism:
- **Small group movement** → cell churches, home groups, accountability groups
- **Personal conversion experience** → the evangelical altar call
- **Scripture in the vernacular for laypeople** → Bible distribution movements
- **Social action** → Francke's institutions → Wilberforce → the evangelical social conscience
- **Modern missions** → Moravians → Carey → the entire missionary enterprise
- **Methodism** → the largest Protestant tradition in America by 1800
