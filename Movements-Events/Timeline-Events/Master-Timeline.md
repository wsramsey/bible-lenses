# The Complete Timeline of Christian History
*From Pentecost to the present — every major event*

## 1st Century (1–100 AD)
- **c. 30 AD** — The Crucifixion and Resurrection of Jesus
- **c. 30 AD** — Pentecost; founding of the church (Acts 2)
- **c. 44 AD** — James the Apostle martyred (first apostolic martyr, Acts 12)
- **c. 46–57 AD** — Paul's missionary journeys
- **c. 49 AD** — Council of Jerusalem (Acts 15)
- **c. 57–60 AD** — Paul's letters written (Romans, Corinthians, Galatians, etc.)
- **64 AD** — Great Fire of Rome; Nero's persecution; Peter and Paul martyred
- **66–73 AD** — Jewish War against Rome
- **70 AD** — Destruction of Jerusalem and the Temple
- **c. 70–100 AD** — The Gospels written (Mark c.65–70; Matthew, Luke c.80–90; John c.90–100)
- **c. 95 AD** — Domitian's persecution; John exiled to Patmos; Revelation written
- **c. 96 AD** — 1 Clement written (earliest post-NT Christian document)

## 2nd Century (100–200 AD)
- **c. 107 AD** — Ignatius of Antioch martyred; his seven letters
- **c. 112 AD** — Pliny's letter to Trajan (non-Christian confirmation of Christian worship)
- **c. 125–150 AD** — Gnostic challenge (Valentinus, Marcion); early canon formation
- **c. 140 AD** — Marcion proposes his own canon (triggers orthodox canon formation)
- **c. 155 AD** — Polycarp of Smyrna martyred
- **c. 160–180 AD** — Justin Martyr; first Christian apologists to Greeks
- **c. 177 AD** — Martyrs of Lyon; Irenaeus
- **c. 185 AD** — Irenaeus's *Against Heresies* (against Gnosticism); canon defined
- **c. 196 AD** — Victor I threatens to excommunicate Eastern churches over Easter date

## 3rd Century (200–300 AD)
- **c. 200 AD** — Tertullian (first major Latin Christian theologian); coins "Trinity"
- **c. 215 AD** — Clement of Alexandria; Origen begins Catechetical School
- **c. 240 AD** — Origen's major works; allegorical interpretation
- **249–251 AD** — Decian Persecution; first empire-wide persecution
- **251 AD** — Novatian Schism (controversy over lapsed)
- **253–260 AD** — Valerian Persecution; Cyprian of Carthage martyred
- **c. 270 AD** — Anthony the Great goes into the Egyptian desert; monasticism begins
- **303–311 AD** — The Great Persecution under Diocletian; worst in history

## 4th Century (300–400 AD) — The Great Turning Point
- **312 AD** — Constantine's vision at the Milvian Bridge; conversion (disputed)
- **313 AD** — **EDICT OF MILAN** — Christianity made legal throughout the empire *(the event you mentioned)*
- **318 AD** — Arius begins teaching; the Arian controversy
- **325 AD** — **FIRST COUNCIL OF NICAEA** — Arianism condemned; Nicene Creed; *homoousios*
- **328 AD** — Athanasius becomes Bishop of Alexandria; *Athanasius contra mundum*
- **337 AD** — Constantine baptized on deathbed; dies
- **c. 340–420 AD** — Jerome; translates the Bible into Latin (the Vulgate)
- **356 AD** — Athanasius exiled for the fourth time by Arian emperors
- **361–363 AD** — Julian the Apostate; attempted pagan revival; fails
- **c. 354–430 AD** — Augustine of Hippo
- **370 AD** — Basil of Caesarea; the Basiliad (first hospital)
- **378 AD** — Battle of Adrianople; Goths defeat Rome; the empire's military crisis
- **380 AD** — **THEODOSIUS MAKES CHRISTIANITY THE OFFICIAL STATE RELIGION** (Edict of Thessalonica)
- **381 AD** — **FIRST COUNCIL OF CONSTANTINOPLE** — Holy Spirit's divinity affirmed; Nicene Creed completed
- **382 AD** — Jerome commissioned to translate the Bible into Latin
- **386 AD** — Augustine's conversion in a Milan garden
- **391 AD** — Theodosius prohibits pagan worship; closes temples
- **393 AD** — Synod of Hippo; recognizes the canon of Scripture
- **397 AD** — Synod of Carthage confirms the 27-book NT canon

## 5th Century (400–500 AD)
- **410 AD** — Alaric's Visigoths sack Rome; Augustine begins *City of God*
- **410–431 AD** — Pelagian Controversy (Augustine vs. Pelagius on grace and free will)
- **413–426 AD** — Augustine writes *City of God*
- **431 AD** — **COUNCIL OF EPHESUS** — Nestorius condemned; *Theotokos* affirmed
- **432 AD** — Patrick arrives in Ireland as bishop/missionary
- **450 AD** — Pope Leo I's *Tome*; Council preparation
- **451 AD** — **COUNCIL OF CHALCEDON** — Two natures of Christ defined; Oriental Orthodox schism
- **476 AD** — Fall of the Western Roman Empire; Odoacer deposes Romulus Augustulus
- **496 AD** — Clovis I of the Franks baptized; Frankish Christianity begins

## 6th Century (500–600 AD)
- **c. 480–550 AD** — Benedict of Nursia; the Rule; Monte Cassino
- **529 AD** — Justinian closes Plato's Academy in Athens; Council of Orange condemns Semi-Pelagianism
- **533 AD** — Justinian's *Corpus Juris Civilis* (Roman law with Christian framing)
- **537 AD** — Hagia Sophia completed; "Solomon, I have surpassed you"
- **563 AD** — Columba founds Iona monastery; re-evangelization of Scotland begins
- **590–604 AD** — **GREGORY THE GREAT** — Pastoral Rule; Gregorian Chant; sends Augustine to England; the last great Latin Father

## 7th Century (600–700 AD)
- **597 AD** — Augustine of Canterbury arrives in England; Æthelberht of Kent converted
- **610 AD** — Muhammad receives his first revelation (Quran)
- **622 AD** — The Hijra; Islam begins; the Muslim calendar begins
- **632 AD** — Muhammad dies; Islamic expansion begins
- **636 AD** — Arab armies take Syria; Palestine falls to Islam
- **638 AD** — Jerusalem surrenders to Caliph Umar
- **641–642 AD** — Egypt falls; Alexandria under Muslim control
- **664 AD** — Synod of Whitby; Roman vs. Celtic Christianity; Rome wins
- **680–681 AD** — **THIRD COUNCIL OF CONSTANTINOPLE** — Monothelitism condemned; Honorius I posthumously condemned

## 8th Century (700–800 AD)
- **711 AD** — Arab armies invade Spain; Visigothic kingdom falls
- **716–717 AD** — Arab siege of Constantinople; fails; Greek fire
- **722 AD** — Battle of Covadonga; beginning of the Reconquista
- **726 AD** — Leo III orders destruction of icons; **Iconoclasm** begins
- **732 AD** — **Battle of Tours/Poitiers** — Charles Martel stops Arab advance into France
- **754 AD** — Boniface martyred; Germany Christianized
- **787 AD** — **SECOND COUNCIL OF NICAEA** — Icons restored; Iconoclasm condemned
- **800 AD** — **Charlemagne crowned Holy Roman Emperor** by Pope Leo III (Christmas Day)

## 9th Century (800–900 AD)
- **827 AD** — Arab conquest of Sicily begins
- **858–867 AD** — Photius, Patriarch of Constantinople; Photian Schism begins
- **863 AD** — Cyril and Methodius begin mission to Slavs; Cyrillic alphabet created
- **869–870 AD** — **FOURTH COUNCIL OF CONSTANTINOPLE** — Photius condemned (East would reject this)

## 10th Century (900–1000 AD)
- **910 AD** — Cluny Abbey founded; Cluniac reform movement
- **960s AD** — Christianization of Bohemia, Poland (966), Hungary beginning
- **988 AD** — **Vladimir I of Kievan Rus baptized** — Russia becomes Christian

## 11th Century (1000–1100 AD)
- **1054 AD** — **THE GREAT SCHISM** — Rome and Constantinople excommunicate each other; Christianity permanently divided
- **1073–1085 AD** — Gregory VII; Investiture Controversy; Canossa (1077)
- **1095 AD** — **POPE URBAN II CALLS THE FIRST CRUSADE** at Clermont
- **1096 AD** — Rhineland massacres of Jews; People's Crusade; First Crusade begins
- **1098 AD** — Anselm's *Cur Deus Homo*; Cistercian order founded
- **1099 AD** — Crusaders capture Jerusalem; massacre of inhabitants

## 12th Century (1100–1200 AD)
- **1115 AD** — Bernard of Clairvaux founds Clairvaux; Cistercian expansion
- **1140s AD** — Gratian's *Decretum*; canon law systematized
- **1150s AD** — University of Paris founded; medieval university system begins
- **1170 AD** — Thomas Becket murdered in Canterbury Cathedral
- **c. 1173 AD** — Peter Waldo's poverty movement; Waldensians
- **1184 AD** — **Episcopal Inquisition established** (*Ad abolendam*)
- **1187 AD** — Saladin recaptures Jerusalem
- **1189–1192 AD** — Third Crusade; Richard I and Saladin
- **1198–1216 AD** — Innocent III; peak of medieval papal power

## 13th Century (1200–1300 AD)
- **1204 AD** — **Fourth Crusade sacks Constantinople** — the Catholic-Orthodox wound that never healed
- **1206 AD** — Francis of Assisi's conversion; Franciscan movement begins
- **1209 AD** — **Albigensian Crusade begins** — massacre of Béziers
- **1215 AD** — **Fourth Lateran Council** — transubstantiation defined; annual confession required
- **1216 AD** — Dominicans founded
- **1217–1221 AD** — Fifth Crusade (Egypt); fails
- **1225–1274 AD** — Thomas Aquinas; *Summa Theologiae*
- **1228–1229 AD** — Frederick II negotiates Jerusalem back by diplomacy
- **1231 AD** — **Papal Inquisition established** (Gregory IX)
- **1248–1254 AD** — Seventh Crusade (Louis IX); fails
- **1270 AD** — Eighth Crusade; Louis IX dies of plague
- **1291 AD** — Fall of Acre; end of Crusader states

## 14th Century (1300–1400 AD)
- **1302 AD** — *Unam Sanctam* (Boniface VIII) — extreme papal supremacy claim
- **1305–1377 AD** — Avignon Papacy ("Babylonian Captivity")
- **1307–1312 AD** — Knights Templar suppressed; burned
- **c. 1320–1327 AD** — Meister Eckhart's mystical theology; later condemned
- **1321 AD** — Last known Cathar burned; Catharism eliminated
- **1347–1351 AD** — **THE BLACK DEATH** — 1/3 of Europe dies; mysticism intensifies
- **1373 AD** — Julian of Norwich's revelations ("All shall be well")
- **1378–1417 AD** — **Great Western Schism** — two then three simultaneous popes
- **1380s AD** — John Wycliffe translates Bible into English; condemned posthumously
- **1382 AD** — Wycliffe's English Bible; first complete English translation

## 15th Century (1400–1500 AD)
- **1415 AD** — **JAN HUS BURNED at Constance** — proto-Reformation martyr
- **1419–1434 AD** — **Hussite Wars** — first Protestant military victory
- **1431 AD** — Joan of Arc burned
- **1439 AD** — Council of Florence; attempted Catholic-Orthodox reunion; fails
- **1453 AD** — **FALL OF CONSTANTINOPLE** — end of the Byzantine Empire; Hagia Sophia becomes a mosque
- **1455 AD** — Gutenberg Bible printed — the information revolution begins
- **1478 AD** — **SPANISH INQUISITION** established by Ferdinand and Isabella
- **1492 AD** — Fall of Granada; end of Reconquista; expulsion of Jews from Spain; Columbus sails
- **1495–1527 AD** — The Renaissance popes (Alexander VI, Julius II, Leo X)

## 16th Century (1500–1600 AD) — THE REFORMATION
- **1509 AD** — Erasmus publishes *In Praise of Folly*
- **1513–1521 AD** — Leo X; commissioned Michelangelo's Sistine Chapel; excommunicated Luther
- **1516 AD** — Erasmus's Greek New Testament (humanist recovery of sources)
- **1517 AD** — **LUTHER POSTS THE 95 THESES** (October 31) — the Reformation begins
- **1519 AD** — Zwingli begins reform in Zürich; Luther debates Eck at Leipzig
- **1521 AD** — Diet of Worms; Luther declared an outlaw; hidden at Wartburg; translates NT
- **1524–1525 AD** — Peasants' War; 100,000 dead
- **1525 AD** — **Anabaptists founded** — Conrad Grebel and friends re-baptize each other
- **1527 AD** — Sack of Rome; Schleitheim Confession (Anabaptist)
- **1529 AD** — Diet of Speyer (Protestant first used); Marburg Colloquy (Luther vs. Zwingli fail)
- **1530 AD** — Augsburg Confession (Lutheran)
- **1534 AD** — **Henry VIII's Act of Supremacy** — Church of England created; Luther's German Bible
- **1536 AD** — Calvin's *Institutes* (first edition); Tyndale strangled and burned
- **1540 AD** — **Jesuits founded** by Ignatius of Loyola
- **1541 AD** — Calvin establishes Geneva as Reformed city
- **1542 AD** — **Roman Inquisition established**; Francis Xavier in India
- **1545–1563 AD** — **COUNCIL OF TRENT** — Catholic Counter-Reformation; defined Catholic doctrine against Protestantism
- **1549 AD** — Book of Common Prayer (Cranmer); first edition
- **1553–1558 AD** — Mary I; Protestant martyrs (Cranmer, Ridley, Latimer)
- **1559 AD** — Calvin's *Institutes* (final edition); Index of Forbidden Books
- **1562–1598 AD** — French Wars of Religion; 3 million dead
- **1563 AD** — Heidelberg Catechism; 39 Articles (Church of England)
- **1568–1648 AD** — Dutch Revolt / Eighty Years' War
- **1571 AD** — **Battle of Lepanto** — Christian fleet defeats Ottoman navy
- **1572 AD** — **St. Bartholomew's Day Massacre** — 5,000–30,000 Huguenots killed
- **1582 AD** — Gregorian calendar reform
- **1598 AD** — **Edict of Nantes** — religious toleration in France

## 17th Century (1600–1700 AD)
- **1600 AD** — Giordano Bruno burned
- **1605 AD** — Gunpowder Plot (Catholic assassination attempt on James I)
- **1611 AD** — **KING JAMES BIBLE** published
- **1618–1648 AD** — **THIRTY YEARS' WAR** — 8 million dead; modern state system born
- **1620 AD** — Pilgrims land at Plymouth; Puritan colonization of America begins
- **1628 AD** — Comenius (Moravian educator) flees Bohemia
- **1633 AD** — **Galileo forced to recant** by Roman Inquisition
- **1636 AD** — Harvard College founded (Puritan)
- **1641–1652 AD** — Irish Rebellion; Cromwell's campaign in Ireland
- **1642–1651 AD** — English Civil War
- **1646 AD** — Westminster Confession of Faith (Presbyterian)
- **1648 AD** — **Peace of Westphalia** — modern international relations; religious toleration
- **1649 AD** — **Execution of Charles I** — first regicide by Parliament
- **1656 AD** — Cromwell readmits Jews to England
- **1662 AD** — Act of Uniformity; 2,000 Puritan ministers ejected; Nonconformism born
- **1670s AD** — Pietism begins (Spener's *Pia Desideria*, 1675)
- **1685 AD** — Revocation of Edict of Nantes; Huguenots expelled from France; Bach born
- **1689 AD** — Glorious Revolution; William and Mary; Toleration Act
- **1690 AD** — **Battle of the Boyne** — Protestant victory in Ireland

## 18th Century (1700–1800 AD)
- **1703 AD** — John Wesley born; Jonathan Edwards born
- **1720s–1740s AD** — **FIRST GREAT AWAKENING** (Edwards, Whitefield, Wesley)
- **1727 AD** — **Moravian revival at Herrnhut** — 100-year prayer meeting begins
- **1729 AD** — John Wesley founds the Holy Club at Oxford (future Methodism)
- **1738 AD** — Wesley's Aldersgate experience ("strangely warmed"); Methodist movement
- **1740s AD** — George Whitefield's transatlantic revivals
- **1741 AD** — Edwards preaches "Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God"
- **1760s AD** — Beginning of Enlightenment challenge to Christianity (Voltaire, Hume)
- **1770s AD** — **American Revolution** — shaped by Protestant Dissent and Enlightenment
- **1780 AD** — Robert Raikes founds Sunday Schools; mass literacy movement
- **1789 AD** — French Revolution; de-Christianization of France
- **1791 AD** — First Amendment (US); religious freedom; separation of church and state
- **1792 AD** — William Carey sails to India; modern missionary movement begins
- **1795 AD** — London Missionary Society founded

## 19th Century (1800–1900 AD)
- **1804 AD** — British and Foreign Bible Society founded
- **1807 AD** — Wilberforce; **British slave trade abolished**
- **1808 AD** — American Colonization Society (anti-slavery evangelicals)
- **1812 AD** — Princeton Theological Seminary separates from College of New Jersey
- **1820s–1840s AD** — **SECOND GREAT AWAKENING** (Finney, camp meetings)
- **1830 AD** — Book of Mormon published; LDS Church founded
- **1833 AD** — **Slavery abolished throughout British Empire** (Wilberforce dies three days later)
- **1833 AD** — Oxford Movement begins (Keble's Assize Sermon)
- **1834 AD** — Spanish Inquisition formally abolished
- **1844 AD** — YMCA founded
- **1845 AD** — John Henry Newman converts to Catholicism; Southern Baptist Convention founded (to defend slaveholders)
- **1848 AD** — Karl Marx's *Communist Manifesto*; Revolutions across Europe
- **1854 AD** — Immaculate Conception declared dogma (Pius IX)
- **1855 AD** — David Livingstone sees Victoria Falls
- **1858 AD** — Apparitions at Lourdes; Marian apparition tradition
- **1859 AD** — Darwin's *On the Origin of Species*; the science-faith crisis begins
- **1865 AD** — William Booth founds what becomes the Salvation Army
- **1866–1895 AD** — Hudson Taylor; China Inland Mission
- **1869–1870 AD** — **FIRST VATICAN COUNCIL** — papal infallibility defined; Pius IX
- **1875 AD** — Keswick Convention begins; Higher Life movement
- **1878–1903 AD** — Leo XIII; *Rerum Novarum* (1891) — Catholic Social Teaching
- **1886 AD** — Moody Bible Institute founded
- **1891 AD** — *Rerum Novarum* — first major Catholic Social Teaching encyclical
- **1893 AD** — Parliament of World's Religions, Chicago; interfaith dialogue begins

## 20th Century (1900–2000 AD)
- **1906 AD** — **AZUSA STREET REVIVAL** — Pentecostalism born
- **1909–1915 AD** — *The Fundamentals* published; Fundamentalism as a movement
- **1910 AD** — Edinburgh World Missionary Conference; ecumenical movement begins
- **1914–1918 AD** — World War I; liberal theology's credibility destroyed
- **1917 AD** — Russian Revolution; Communist persecution of the church begins
- **1919 AD** — Karl Barth's *Epistle to the Romans* — "a bomb on the playground of theologians"
- **1920s AD** — Fundamentalist-Modernist Controversy; Scopes Trial (1925)
- **1929 AD** — Lateran Treaty; Vatican City established as a state; Great Depression begins
- **1930 AD** — Lambeth Conference permits contraception (Anglican); first major Protestant break
- **1931 AD** — Georges Lemaître (Catholic priest) proposes Big Bang theory
- **1933 AD** — Hitler becomes chancellor; Deutsche Christen movement; Dorothy Day founds Catholic Worker
- **1934 AD** — **Barmen Declaration** — Confessing Church defies Hitler
- **1937 AD** — *Mit Brennender Sorge* — encyclical against Nazism (Pius XI)
- **1938 AD** — *Kristallnacht*; Christian-Jewish relations crisis
- **1939–1945 AD** — World War II; Holocaust; 6 million Jews killed
- **1945 AD** — Bonhoeffer executed (April 9); Nag Hammadi documents discovered
- **1947 AD** — **Dead Sea Scrolls discovered**; C.S. Lewis publishes *Miracles*
- **1948 AD** — State of Israel established; **World Council of Churches** founded; UN Declaration of Human Rights
- **1949 AD** — Billy Graham's Los Angeles Crusade; national fame
- **1950 AD** — Assumption of Mary declared dogma (Pius XII); World Vision founded
- **1952 AD** — Revised Standard Version Bible
- **1954 AD** — Brown v. Board of Education; Civil Rights movement begins
- **1956 AD** — *Christianity Today* founded (Carl Henry, Billy Graham)
- **1960 AD** — Dennis Bennett speaks in tongues; charismatic renewal in mainline churches
- **1962–1965 AD** — **SECOND VATICAN COUNCIL** — the most significant Catholic event of the century
- **1963 AD** — Martin Luther King Jr. "Letter from Birmingham Jail"; "I Have a Dream"
- **1964 AD** — Civil Rights Act; Vatican lifts 1054 mutual excommunications (gesture only)
- **1965 AD** — *Humanae Vitae* preparation begins; *Nostra Aetate* (on Jews); *Dignitatis Humanae* (religious liberty)
- **1967 AD** — Catholic Charismatic Renewal (Duquesne); Six-Day War; Israel captures Jerusalem
- **1968 AD** — *Humanae Vitae* — contraception ban upheld; Medellín Conference (liberation theology)
- **1969 AD** — James Cone's *Black Theology and Black Power*
- **1974 AD** — Lausanne Congress on World Evangelization; Lausanne Covenant
- **1975 AD** — First women ordained as priests in the Episcopal Church (USA)
- **1978 AD** — Three popes (Paul VI died, John Paul I died after 33 days, John Paul II elected)
- **1979 AD** — Moral Majority founded (Falwell); evangelical political mobilization
- **1979 AD** — Gustavo Gutiérrez's liberation theology; Oscar Romero's prophetic witness
- **1980 AD** — Oscar Romero assassinated
- **1981 AD** — Ratzinger appointed prefect of CDF; John Paul II survives assassination attempt
- **1986 AD** — Assisi Prayer Meeting for Peace; John Paul II with world religious leaders
- **1989 AD** — Fall of the Berlin Wall; collapse of Communist Eastern Europe
- **1992 AD** — John Paul II apologizes for Galileo affair; Catechism of the Catholic Church
- **1994 AD** — Toronto Blessing; *Evangelicals and Catholics Together* statement
- **1995 AD** — Southern Baptist Convention formally apologizes for pro-slavery founding
- **1997 AD** — Promise Keepers; men's movement; peak of evangelical cultural power
- **1998 AD** — Good Friday Agreement (Northern Ireland); Oscar Romero beatification begins
- **2000 AD** — **Day of Pardon** — John Paul II asks forgiveness for church's sins (March 12)

## 21st Century (2000–present)
- **2001 AD** — 9/11; Islam and Christianity relations crisis; "War on Terror"
- **2002 AD** — Boston Globe breaks Catholic sex abuse scandal; the institutional reckoning
- **2003 AD** — Gene Robinson ordained as openly gay Episcopal bishop; Anglican crisis
- **2004 AD** — Mel Gibson's *The Passion of the Christ* — cultural moment
- **2005 AD** — **John Paul II dies; Benedict XVI elected** (Joseph Ratzinger)
- **2006 AD** — Regensburg Lecture (Benedict on Islam and reason); controversy
- **2008 AD** — Peter Enns fired from Westminster Seminary; inerrancy debate
- **2009 AD** — Anglican Personal Ordinariates established for Anglo-Catholics to enter Rome
- **2010 AD** — John Henry Newman beatified
- **2011 AD** — Rob Bell's *Love Wins*; evangelical universalism debate
- **2013 AD** — **BENEDICT XVI RESIGNS** (first in 600 years); **FRANCIS ELECTED** — first Jesuit pope; first from the Americas
- **2015 AD** — *Laudato Si* (Francis on environment); Obergefell (same-sex marriage legalized in US); United Methodist Church begins fracture
- **2016 AD** — 81% of white evangelicals vote for Trump; evangelical identity crisis
- **2017 AD** — 500th anniversary of the Reformation; Lutheran-Catholic joint commemoration
- **2018 AD** — Oscar Romero canonized; McCarrick scandal (US cardinal defrocked)
- **2019 AD** — Notre-Dame de Paris fire; Rachel Held Evans dies; Amazon Synod
- **2021 AD** — Capitol insurrection; Christian nationalism crisis; Canadian residential school graves discovered
- **2022 AD** — Pope Francis apologizes in Canada for residential schools; Russia's war in Ukraine divides Orthodox Christianity; United Methodist split formal
- **2023 AD** — Synod on Synodality (Francis); Vatican officially repudiates Doctrine of Discovery; ongoing LGBTQ debate
- **2024 AD** — Global Methodist Church fully separates from United Methodist Church
