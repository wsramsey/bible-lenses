# Missionary Routes — Timeline and Geography

## Wave 1: The Apostolic Mediterranean (30–100 AD)
**Jerusalem → Mediterranean cities → Rome**

Key routes:
- **Paul**: Jerusalem → Syria → Asia Minor → Greece → Rome
- **Philip**: Jerusalem → Samaria → Gaza → Caesarea
- **Thomas** (tradition): Jerusalem → India (Kerala)
- **Mark** (tradition): Jerusalem → Alexandria → Egypt

The pattern: follow Roman roads and sea routes; target cities; synagogues as entry points

## Wave 2: The Roman Empire (100–313 AD)
**Center → Periphery within Empire**

Christianity spread through informal networks — merchants, soldiers, slaves:
- North Africa (Carthage by 180s)
- Gaul (Lyon by 177; Irenaeus)
- Britain (by 200s)
- Persia (Church of the East established 3rd century)

## Wave 3: Beyond the Empire (300–700 AD)
**Empire → Barbarian territories**

- **Ulfilas** → Goths (Danube frontier)
- **Patrick** → Ireland
- **Columba** → Scotland
- **Augustine of Canterbury** → England
- **Frumentius** → Ethiopia
- **Church of the East** → Persia, Central Asia, China (Nestorian Stele, Xi'an, 635 AD)

## Wave 4: Medieval Northern and Eastern Europe (700–1200)
**West → North and East**

- **Boniface** → Germany (Bavaria, Hesse, Thuringia)
- **Cyril and Methodius** → Moravia → Bulgaria → Serbia → Russia
- **Ansgar** → Scandinavia
- **Christianization of Poland** (966) → Bohemia (9th c.) → Hungary (1000) → Lithuania (1387 — last pagan nation in Europe)

## Wave 5: The Iberian Global Mission (1492–1600)
**Spain and Portugal → Americas, Africa, Asia**

**Spanish**:
- Caribbean (1492) → Central America → Mexico → Peru → Philippines
- The Franciscan, Dominican, Augustinian orders as the primary missionaries

**Portuguese**:
- Brazil → West Africa → East Africa → Goa (India) → Malacca → Japan

## Wave 6: The Protestant Great Century (1792–1914)
**Britain and America → Global**

Major mission fields:
- **India**: Serampore (Carey) → all India
- **China**: coastal cities (19th c.) → interior (Hudson Taylor)
- **Africa**: West Africa (Sierra Leone, freed slaves) → South Africa → East Africa (Livingstone) → Central Africa
- **Pacific Islands**: Tahiti (LMS, 1797) → spreading across Polynesia → Melanesia → Papua New Guinea
- **Korea**: first Protestant missionary 1884; explosive growth after 1900

## Wave 7: The 20th Century Shift (1900–present)
**Two-directional flow**

The old pattern (West to South) continues, but:
- **Korea → the world**: 27,000+ Korean missionaries; more per capita than any country except the US
- **Brazil → Africa and Europe**
- **Nigeria → Europe**: Redeemed Christian Church of God, UK
- **Philippines → diaspora church planting globally**
- **China → Central Asia and the Middle East**: underground Chinese missionary movement

## The Unreached People Groups

The **Joshua Project** (evangelical missions tracking): identifies approximately 7,400 "unreached people groups" — ethnic groups with less than 2% Christian population and no indigenous church capable of evangelizing without outside help.

Major concentrations:
- The **10/40 Window** (coined by Luis Bush, 1990): the rectangle between 10 and 40 degrees North latitude; covers North Africa, the Middle East, South Asia, and East Asia; contains 69% of the world's population and most of the unreached peoples
- Central Asia (Uzbekistan, Kazakhstan, Tajikistan) — post-Soviet; Muslim majority; limited access
- The Arab world — restricted access; significant growth among Muslim-background believers

## The Role of Bible Translation

**Wycliffe Bible Translators / SIL International**: founded 1934; working in 2,200+ languages; has done more for linguistic preservation than any other organization; created the first written forms for hundreds of languages

**The number**: The Bible (in full or part) has been translated into 3,500+ languages. More than any other text in history by a large margin.
