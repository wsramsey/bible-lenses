# S01E01 — "The Upper Room"
*30–70 AD · The 40 days that changed everything*

## The Story
The episode opens not at the crucifixion but at what comes after. The disciples are locked in a room. Peter has denied Christ three times. Thomas has declared he won't believe without physical evidence. The women came back from the tomb with a story no one is sure how to process.

Then the appearances begin.

## The Historical Evidence
**Primary sources within 20 years of the events:**
- **1 Corinthians 15:3–8** (Paul, c. 54 AD): "He appeared to Cephas, then to the twelve. Then he appeared to more than five hundred brothers at one time, most of whom are still alive... Then he appeared to James, then to all the apostles."
- **Paul was writing to a community that could check this claim** — the 500+ witnesses were alive
- **The criterion of embarrassment**: women as first witnesses (legally discounted in 1st-century Jewish context); no one fabricating this story would choose women as primary witnesses

**The transformation of Peter:**
- Luke 22:54–62: Peter denies Christ three times by a fire in the high priest's courtyard
- John 21:15–17: Jesus restores Peter three times by a fire on the beach — "Do you love me? Feed my sheep"
- Acts 2:14–41: Peter stands before the crowd and preaches; 3,000 baptized
- **The same man.** The transformation requires explanation.

## Pentecost — What the Sources Say
**Acts 2** (Luke, c. 85 AD): Rushing wind, tongues of fire, speaking in languages. 3,000 baptized in one day.

**Pliny the Younger** (c. 112 AD, independent): Christians meet "before dawn" and sing "hymns to Christ as to a god" — the practice attested within 80 years.

**The Didache** (c. 50–120 AD): The earliest manual of church practice; instructions for baptism, fasting, Eucharist — the liturgy of Pentecost formalized in a document that predates most of the NT.

## The Spread — Without Power
**By 100 AD, Christian communities existed in:**
- Jerusalem, Antioch, Damascus, Alexandria (Egypt), Corinth, Athens, Rome, Ephesus, Philippi, Thessalonica, Cyprus

**The mechanism**: Not missionaries primarily. Merchants, slaves, soldiers, women moving through the Roman Empire's trade networks. The faith moved along Roman roads and sea lanes faster than any institution could direct it.

**Rodney Stark** (*The Rise of Christianity*, 1996): Mathematical modeling of a 40% growth rate per decade from 1,000 members in 40 AD produces 31,722,489 members by 350 AD — consistent with the historical record. This growth rate is comparable to the Mormon Church's historical growth rate. It requires no miracles to explain — just the ordinary social dynamics of an effective movement.

## The Narrative Arc
The episode ends on Paul — tent-maker, former persecutor, writing a letter to Rome from a rented house, "preaching the kingdom of God and teaching about the Lord Jesus Christ with all boldness and without hindrance." Acts ends there, in mid-sentence, as if the story is still going. It is.
