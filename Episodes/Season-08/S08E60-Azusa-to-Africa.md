# S08E60 — "Azusa to Africa"
*1906–2023 AD · The faith finds new people to carry it*

## The Story
A broken-down building in Los Angeles. A Black preacher, son of slaves, blind in one eye. Interracial worship in Jim Crow America. Three years of continuous services. Missionaries leaving within months to carry it across the globe. Fast-forward 117 years: 700 million Pentecostal and charismatic Christians. The center of Christianity has moved from Geneva and Rome to Lagos and Seoul.

## The Historical Data

**The Numbers:**
| Year | Christians in Sub-Saharan Africa | % of continent |
|------|----------------------------------|----------------|
| 1900 | ~10 million | ~9% |
| 1950 | ~30 million | ~20% |
| 1980 | ~200 million | ~45% |
| 2010 | ~516 million | ~63% |
| 2023 | ~700 million | ~65% |

**Global Christian population distribution:**
- 1900: 80% in Europe and North America
- 2023: 67% in Africa, Asia, and Latin America

**Pentecostal growth:**
- 1906 (Azusa Street): small movement
- 1980: 100 million Pentecostals globally
- 2023: ~700 million Pentecostal and charismatic Christians worldwide

**The largest churches in the world** (by weekly attendance):
1. Yoido Full Gospel Church, Seoul — 480,000 members
2. Living Faith Church, Lagos — 100,000+ weekly
3. Redeemed Christian Church of God, Nigeria — 5 million+ members globally
4. Universal Church of the Kingdom of God, Brazil — 12 million members

## Why It Grew

**The Pentecostal offer:**
- Direct encounter with God (not mediated through institution)
- Healing prayer that responds to immediate physical need
- Community and belonging in urbanizing contexts
- Prosperity gospel's appeal to the aspirationally poor (theologically problematic but sociologically powerful)
- Spirit-filled worship that is emotionally satisfying

**[[Philip Jenkins]]** (*The Next Christendom*, 2002):
The Global South Christianity that is growing is:
- Charismatic
- Supernaturalist (takes spiritual warfare, healing, and prophecy seriously)
- Biblically conservative on sexuality and gender
- Socially conservative in many ways
- **Not** the progressive Western Christianity that is declining

Jenkins: "The Christianity that is spreading fastest in the Global South looks more like medieval Christianity than modern liberal Protestantism."

## The Reverse Mission
By 2023, the global mission flow has reversed:
- South Korea sends 27,000+ missionaries (more per capita than any country)
- Nigeria's RCCG has 750+ churches in the UK alone
- Brazil's Universal Church has operations in 180 countries
- Chinese house church missionaries are operating in Central Asia and the Middle East

The former "mission fields" are now mission senders. The faith that moved from Jerusalem to Rome to London to New York has found new carriers.

## The Final Question
The episode — and the series — ends not with an answer but a face. An unknown woman in Lagos, or Nairobi, or São Paulo. Praying. In her own language. With her own music. In a church her grandmother couldn't have imagined.

She has never heard of Karl Barth or John Calvin or the Council of Nicaea. But she says the same words Peter said in the upper room 2,000 years ago: *Jesus is Lord.*

The story is not over.
