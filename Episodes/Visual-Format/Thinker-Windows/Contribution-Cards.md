# Thinker Contribution Cards
*How each thinker appears in the visual experience*

## The Format
Each thinker gets a 60–90 second "window" — a visual card that appears when they're first introduced.

**Structure:**
1. Portrait (5 seconds, silent)
2. Their name + dates appear
3. A single quote from their work (10–15 seconds, read aloud)
4. Their contribution in one line (5 seconds, text)
5. The question their life asks us today (15 seconds, narration)

---

## The Cards

### Augustine of Hippo (354–430)
**Portrait**: Philippe de Champaigne, LACMA M.78.15
**Quote**: *"Thou madest us for Thyself, and our heart is restless, until it repose in Thee."*
**Contribution**: He gave Western Christianity its understanding of sin, grace, and the searching heart
**The question**: *What are you looking for that keeps moving just out of reach?*

### Thomas Aquinas (1225–1274)
**Portrait**: Fra Angelico, San Marco Cell 7 (or Zurbarán's Apotheosis, Seville)
**Quote**: *"Grace does not destroy nature but perfects it."*
**Contribution**: He made faith and reason friends — the synthesis that still holds
**The question**: *Do you think knowing more about how something works makes it less beautiful? Or more?*

### Julian of Norwich (1342–c.1416)
**Portrait**: Modern statue outside Norwich Cathedral
**Quote**: *"All shall be well, and all shall be well, and all manner of thing shall be well."*
**Contribution**: She wrote this after a near-death illness, during a plague, and meant every word
**The question**: *Have you ever said "it will be okay" and not known whether it was true — and said it anyway?*

### Martin Luther (1483–1546)
**Portrait**: Lucas Cranach the Elder, Uffizi
**Quote**: *"Here I stand. I can do no other. God help me."*
**Contribution**: He broke the institutional monopoly on religious authority and put the Bible in everyone's hands
**The question**: *What would you refuse to unsay even if it cost you everything?*

### Teresa of Ávila (1515–1582)
**Portrait**: Rubens-style, KHM Vienna
**Quote**: *"Christ has no body now on earth but yours, no hands but yours, no feet but yours."*
**Contribution**: She mapped the interior life of prayer more precisely than anyone before or since
**The question**: *When did you last feel completely still inside? What was happening?*

### Blaise Pascal (1623–1662)
**Portrait**: Palace of Versailles portrait
**Quote**: *"The heart has its reasons of which reason knows nothing."*
**Contribution**: He lived at the edge of faith and doubt and refused to pretend the edge wasn't there
**The question**: *Have you ever believed something you couldn't prove? Was that weakness or wisdom?*

### Søren Kierkegaard (1813–1855)
**Portrait**: Drawing by his brother
**Quote**: *"The function of prayer is not to influence God, but rather to change the nature of the one who prays."*
**Contribution**: He said Christianity required a leap — and that the leap was the point, not a problem to be solved
**The question**: *What have you committed to that you couldn't guarantee in advance?*

### Dietrich Bonhoeffer (1906–1945)
**Portrait**: Prison photograph, 1944
**Quote**: *"Cheap grace is the deadly enemy of our church. Cheap grace means grace sold on the market like cheapjacks' wares."*
**Contribution**: He showed that following Christ might cost everything — and did it himself
**The question**: *Is there anything you believe so deeply that it would actually cost you something to act on it?*

### Simone Weil (1909–1943)
**Portrait**: Photograph, 1941
**Quote**: *"Attention is the rarest and purest form of generosity."*
**Contribution**: She stood at the threshold of the church and said the view from outside was also necessary
**The question**: *Who do you actually pay attention to — full, unhurried attention — in a given week?*

### C.S. Lewis (1898–1963)
**Portrait**: NPG photograph
**Quote**: *"You don't have a soul. You are a soul. You have a body."*
**Contribution**: He translated the ancient faith into a language that 20th-century skeptics could hear
**The question**: *When did something beautiful — a piece of music, a landscape, a story — make you feel like there was more going on than you could explain?*

### Martin Luther King Jr. (1929–1968)
**Portrait**: March on Washington photograph, 1963
**Quote**: *"The arc of the moral universe is long, but it bends toward justice."*
**Contribution**: He showed that the Gospel has political consequences — and paid with his life
**The question**: *What injustice do you walk past every day because fixing it would be inconvenient?*

### Henri Nouwen (1932–1996)
**Portrait**: Nouwen Society archive photograph
**Quote**: *"Joy does not simply happen to us. We have to choose joy and keep choosing it every day."*
**Contribution**: He translated the mystical tradition into the language of the wounded
**The question**: *Who in your life is suffering alone right now, waiting for someone to just be present?*

### Dorothy Day (1897–1980)
**Portrait**: Catholic Worker archive photograph
**Quote**: *"We have all known the long loneliness and we have learned that the only solution is love and that love comes with community."*
**Contribution**: She proved that the Gospel's preferential option for the poor was not a metaphor
**The question**: *When did you last do something for someone who could not possibly repay you?*

### N.T. Wright (1948–)
**Portrait**: Contemporary photograph
**Quote**: *"You are what you love, and you love whatever you spend your time and money and energy on."*
**Contribution**: He showed that the resurrection wasn't just a belief but a claim about the actual structure of reality
**The question**: *If what you love tells us who you are — what do your last 30 days say about you?*

### René Girard (1923–2015)
**Portrait**: Stanford photograph
**Quote**: *"Man is the creature who does not know what to desire, and he turns to others in order to make up his mind."*
**Contribution**: He showed that Christianity is the only story that consistently sides with the victim — and that this changes everything
**The question**: *Who is being blamed in your community right now? Is it possible they're innocent?*

### Flannery O'Connor (1925–1964)
**Portrait**: Photograph with peacock, Georgia College
**Quote**: *"To the hard of hearing you shout, and for the almost-blind you draw large and startling figures."*
**Contribution**: She showed that grace looks like violence to the person it's happening to
**The question**: *What is the most inconvenient thing that has ever turned out to be good for you?*
