# Key Paintings as Portals — The Complete List
*Each painting opens onto connected nodes. This is the map.*

---

## The Good Shepherd (Catacomb of Priscilla, c. 250 AD)
**Museum**: In situ, Rome
**What it opens onto**:
→ [[Early Church]] · [[Roman Persecutions]] · [[Early Christian Symbols]]
→ [[Luke 15 — The Lost Sheep]] · [[John 10 — The Good Shepherd]]
→ *Question*: Why did they paint him this way — anonymous, young, no halo? What were they afraid of?

---

## The Rublev Trinity Icon (c. 1410)
**Museum**: Tretyakov Gallery, Moscow
→ [[The Trinity]] · [[Genesis 18 — Three Visitors]] · [[Perichoresis — Divine Dance]]
→ [[Andrei Rublev]] · [[Gregory Palamas]] · [[Henri Nouwen]] (wrote a book on it)
→ *Question*: There's an empty space at the table facing you. What would it mean to sit down?

---

## The Ghent Altarpiece (Van Eyck, 1432)
**Museum**: Saint Bavo's Cathedral, Ghent
→ [[Revelation 5 — The Lamb]] · [[The Eucharist]] · [[Communion of Saints]]
→ [[Flemish Netherlandish Art]] · [[Medieval Theology]] · [[The Just Judges — stolen 1934]]
→ *Question*: The painting survived Napoleon, the Nazis, and a theft that was never solved. What's worth that much protection?

---

## The Isenheim Altarpiece (Grünewald, c. 1512–1516)
**Museum**: Musée Unterlinden, Colmar
→ [[The Crucifixion]] · [[Theodicy and Suffering]] · [[Isaiah 53]]
→ [[Karl Barth]] (kept it above his desk) · [[Jürgen Moltmann]] (*The Crucified God*)
→ *Question*: This was painted for patients dying of skin disease. They looked at it every day. Does suffering need a reason to be bearable, or just a companion?

---

## Rogier van der Weyden — Descent from the Cross (c. 1435)
**Museum**: Museo del Prado, Madrid — P002825
→ [[Mary of Nazareth]] · [[The Crucifixion]] · [[Mater Dolorosa]]
→ [[Grief]] · [[Theodicy and Suffering]] · [[The Incarnation]]
→ *Question*: Mary's body mirrors her son's body exactly. She's dying in sympathy. What does it mean to share someone's suffering so completely you take it into your own body?

---

## Michelangelo — The Creation of Adam (1511)
**Museum**: Sistine Chapel, Vatican
→ [[Adam]] · [[Imago Dei]] · [[Creation Theology]]
→ [[Michelangelo]] · [[Pope Julius II]] · [[Renaissance Humanism]]
→ *Question*: God reaches toward Adam. Adam reaches back — but lazily, his finger lower, less urgent. Is that accurate?

---

## Caravaggio — The Calling of Saint Matthew (1599–1600)
**Museum**: San Luigi dei Francesi, Rome
→ [[Matthew the Apostle]] · [[Matthew 9:9]] · [[Call Narratives]]
→ [[Grace]] · [[The Unexpected]] · [[Counter-Reformation]]
→ *Question*: Matthew is pointing at himself: "Me?" The light follows Christ's finger. When did something find you that you weren't looking for?

---

## Rembrandt — The Return of the Prodigal Son (c. 1668)
**Museum**: State Hermitage Museum, St. Petersburg
→ [[Luke 15 — Prodigal Son]] · [[Grace]] · [[Forgiveness]]
→ [[Henri Nouwen]] (*The Return of the Prodigal Son*) · [[Rembrandt Biblical Works]]
→ *Question*: The father's hands — one masculine, one feminine. Rembrandt collapsed gender in the image of divine tenderness. What does that say about who God is?

---

## El Greco — Burial of the Count of Orgaz (1586–1588)
**Museum**: Church of Santo Tomé, Toledo
→ [[Heaven and Earth]] · [[Intercession of Saints]] · [[Counter-Reformation]]
→ [[Teresa of Ávila]] · [[John of the Cross]] · [[Spanish Mysticism]]
→ *Question*: Heaven and earth meet in the middle of the painting. There's a precise dividing line. Where is that line in your life?

---

## Zurbarán — Agnus Dei (c. 1635–1640)
**Museum**: Museo del Prado, Madrid — P007293
→ [[The Eucharist]] · [[Revelation 5]] · [[John 1:29]]
→ [[Zurbarán]] · [[Lamb of God Theology]] · [[Counter-Reformation]]
→ *Question*: A lamb. Bound. Alone in the frame. Nothing else. Sometimes the most profound statement is the most minimal one. What have you stripped away to get to the essential thing?

---

## Grünewald — Isenheim Altarpiece (Resurrection panel)
**Museum**: Musée Unterlinden, Colmar
→ [[The Resurrection]] · [[The Glorified Body]] · [[Hope]]
→ [[Karl Barth]] · [[Jürgen Moltmann]] · [[Death and Afterlife]]
→ *Question*: The same altarpiece has the most devastating crucifixion and the most explosive resurrection. They were meant to be seen together. Is it possible to fully experience the second without sitting with the first?

---

## Raphael — The School of Athens (1509–1511)
**Museum**: Vatican, Stanza della Segnatura
→ [[Plato]] · [[Aristotle]] · [[Faith and Reason]]
→ [[Thomas Aquinas]] · [[Renaissance Humanism]] · [[The University]]
→ *Question*: Plato points up. Aristotle points down. They've been arguing for 2,500 years. Which direction do you point?

---

## Michelangelo — Pietà (1498–1499)
**Museum**: St. Peter's Basilica, Vatican
→ [[Mary of Nazareth]] · [[The Incarnation]] · [[The Crucifixion]]
→ [[Kenosis]] · [[Grief]] · [[The Body Matters]]
→ *Question*: The Creator of the universe is lying across a woman's lap. She's holding him. What do you hold that is beyond your capacity to protect?

---

## Marc Chagall — Reims Cathedral Windows (1974)
**Museum**: In situ, Reims Cathedral
→ [[Holocaust]] · [[Jewish-Christian Relations]] · [[Beauty After Catastrophe]]
→ [[Nostra Aetate (Vatican II)]] · [[Marc Chagall]] · [[The Resurrection]]
→ *Question*: A Jewish artist creating Christian stained glass for a cathedral rebuilt after two world wars. Is beauty an act of resistance?

---

## Barnett Newman — Stations of the Cross (1958–1966)
**Museum**: National Gallery of Art, Washington DC
→ [[The Passion]] · [[The Cry of Dereliction]] · [[Abstract Sacred Art]]
→ [[Modern Sacred Art]] · [[Theodicy]] · [[Where Is God?]]
→ *Question*: Fourteen black and white abstract paintings titled *Lema Sabachthani* — "Why hast thou forsaken me?" No images. Just the question. Is the question itself an act of faith?
