# S04E25 — "Here I Stand"
*October 31, 1517 – April 17, 1521 · The 3.5 years that split Western Christianity*

## The Story
A monk posts a list of academic arguments. Within months they're across Europe. Within years, he's facing the Emperor. The printing press turned a theological dispute into a revolution.

## The Primary Sources

**The 95 Theses** (October 31, 1517):
Luther sent them to Archbishop Albert of Mainz with a letter. They were posted on the Castle Church door (the academic notice board). Key theses:
- Thesis 1: "When our Lord and Master Jesus Christ said, 'Repent,' he willed the entire life of believers to be one of repentance."
- Thesis 27: "There is no divine authority for preaching that the soul flies out of purgatory immediately the money clinks in the bottom of the chest."
- Thesis 62: "The true treasure of the church is the Most Holy Gospel of the glory and the grace of God."

**The spread via printing press:**
- Printed copies spread across Germany within weeks
- By early 1518, translated from Latin into German and distributed across Europe
- Luther's works: 300,000 copies sold between 1517 and 1520 — an astonishing number for the era

**The Diet of Worms** (April 17–18, 1521):
Johann Eck asked Luther to recant. Luther's reply:
> "Unless I am convinced by the testimony of the Scriptures or by clear reason... I am bound by the Scriptures I have quoted and my conscience is captive to the Word of God. I cannot and will not retract anything, since it is neither safe nor right to go against conscience. Here I stand. I can do no other. God help me. Amen."

Luther was declared an outlaw. Anyone could kill him legally.

**Frederick the Wise's "kidnapping"** (April–March 1522):
Luther was "ambushed" on the road — by Frederick's agents, protecting him. He spent 10 months at Wartburg Castle, disguised as "Junker Georg" (Knight George), and translated the New Testament into German in 11 weeks.

**The German New Testament** (September 1522):
5,000 copies sold immediately. Luther's principle: he listened to "the mother in the home, the children on the streets, the common man in the marketplace." He used the vernacular of ordinary people, not academic German. He created modern literary German.
