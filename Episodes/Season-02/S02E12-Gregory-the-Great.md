# S02E12 — "Gregory the Great"
*590–604 AD · The bishop who held civilization together*

## The Story
Rome is dying. The Tiber floods annually. The Lombards raid the countryside. The Eastern emperor in Constantinople is too far away to help. Gregory — a monk who wanted to stay in his monastery — becomes pope and does what emperors couldn't: he feeds the city, negotiates with the barbarians, and sends a monk named Augustine to England.

## The Primary Sources

**The *Pastoral Rule*** (591 AD):
Written in the first year of his papacy; immediately translated into Greek by the Emperor; Charlemagne later required every bishop in his empire to have a copy. Its thesis: the bishop must know his people individually and apply the right medicine to the right soul. It catalogues different types of people and how to address each — the young vs. the old; the proud vs. the humble; the married vs. the celibate; the sick vs. the healthy. The first systematic pastoral theology.

**Gregory's letters** (survived: 854 of them):
A window into medieval administration and pastoral theology. He wrote:
- To the Emperor in Constantinople about theological disputes
- To bishops across the Western church about discipline
- To Augustine in England about how to treat pagan customs ("don't destroy their temples; purify and repurpose them; don't destroy their festivals; give them Christian content")
- To merchants who were enslaved the Angles: "They are Angles — but they should be Angels"

**The Gregorian Chant connection:**
Gregory didn't invent the chant style named for him but he standardized and organized the liturgical music of the Roman church. The resulting body of music — Gregorian chant — is the oldest surviving composed music in Western civilization.

## The Historical Context
When Gregory became pope (590 AD):
- The Western Roman Empire had ceased to exist (476 AD) — 114 years earlier
- The church was the only functioning institution across most of Western Europe
- Bishops were the de facto civic administrators of their cities
- The monasteries were the only centers of education and manuscript preservation

Gregory understood this. He didn't wait for the empire to come back. He governed.
