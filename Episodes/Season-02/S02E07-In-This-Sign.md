# S02E07 — "In This Sign"
*312–380 AD · What power does to a faith built on a cross*

## The Story
October 28, 312. Constantine sees a vision before the Battle of Milvian Bridge — or says he does. Within a year, Christianity is legal. Within 70 years, it is mandatory. The movement that grew by dying now grows by winning. What does this change?

## The Historical Evidence

**The Vision — Three Accounts:**
1. **Lactantius** (*De Mortibus Persecutorum*, c. 315): Constantine received a dream telling him to mark his soldiers' shields with "the heavenly sign of God" — the Chi-Rho
2. **Eusebius** (*Life of Constantine*, c. 337): Constantine saw a cross of light in the sky with the words "In this sign conquer" (*In hoc signo vinces*); then a dream of Christ confirming it
3. **Eusebius** earlier (*Ecclesiastical History*, c. 313): No vision mentioned at all

The discrepancy is itself evidence: the story grew in the telling. The political reality is undeniable: Constantine won. And Christianity benefited.

**The Edict of Milan** (313 AD):
Not a single document but a rescript — letters sent to provincial governors. Key phrase:
> "We decided to grant to Christians and everybody else liberty to follow whatever religion each one wished, so that whatever divinity there is in the seat of heaven may be appeased and made propitious towards us and towards all who are placed under our authority."

Note: religious toleration for *everyone*, not Christianity specifically. Constantine was politically careful.

**Nicaea (325 AD) — What Actually Happened:**
300 bishops gathered. Many bore physical marks of torture from Diocletian's persecution — blind in one eye, missing hands. Constantine arrived in purple silk, not yet baptized. He listened but could not vote. The bishops argued about *homoousios* vs. *homoiousios* — a single iota.

The vote: overwhelming for Nicaea. Arius and two bishops refused to sign. They were exiled.

**The Aftermath:**
Constantine was baptized on his deathbed — by an Arian bishop. His sons continued the Arian controversy. Within 50 years: Athanasius exiled five times. Jerome: "The whole world groaned to find itself Arian." The council's victory required another 50 years of struggle.

**Theodosius I (380 AD) — The Real Turn:**
The Edict of Thessalonica made *Nicene Christianity* the official state religion. Paganism and heresy became illegal. This — not Constantine — is the actual Constantinian shift.

## The Counter-Narrative: John Howard Yoder
[[John Howard Yoder]] (*The Politics of Jesus*, 1972): "The Constantinian heresy" — when the church identified with political power, it betrayed its founding. The persecuted church was more authentic than the imperial church. Every subsequent atrocity in Christianity's name flows from this moment.

The counter: [[Oliver O'Donovan]] — Christendom was a genuine (if imperfect) response to the Gospel's political implications. The question is not whether Christianity should engage politics but how.
