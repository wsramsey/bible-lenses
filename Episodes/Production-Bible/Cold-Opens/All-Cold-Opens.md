# Cold Opens — All 63 Episodes
*Each episode opens with a primary source read cold — no context, no introduction*
*The listener figures out what they're hearing as the episode unfolds*

---

## Season 1

**E01 — The Upper Room**
*Read without introduction:*
> "He appeared to Cephas, then to the twelve. Then he appeared to more than five hundred brothers at one time, most of whom are still alive, though some have fallen asleep. Then he appeared to James, then to all the apostles. Last of all, as to one untimely born, he appeared also to me."
— Paul of Tarsus, First Letter to the Corinthians, Chapter 15. Written approximately 54 AD. Within 20 years of the events described.

**E02 — The Road to Rome**
> "When I came to you, brothers and sisters, I did not come with eloquence or human wisdom as I proclaimed to you the testimony about God. For I resolved to know nothing while I was with you except Jesus Christ and him crucified. I came to you in weakness with great fear and trembling."
— Paul, First Corinthians 2:1-3. Written from Ephesus, approximately 54 AD.

**E03 — Fire and the Word**
> "They were arrested; Nero fastened the guilt on a class hated for their abominations, called Christians by the populace. Covered with the skins of beasts, they were torn by dogs and perished, or were nailed to crosses, or were doomed to the flames and burnt, to serve as a nightly illumination, when daylight had expired."
— Tacitus, Annals, Book 15. Written approximately 116 AD. A Roman senator describing what happened in Rome in 64 AD.

**E04 — Marketplace of Gods**
> "On an appointed day, before dawn, they sing a hymn to Christ as to a god. They bind themselves by oath — not to any crime, but not to commit fraud, theft, or adultery, not to betray a trust, not to refuse to return a deposit when called upon to do so. After this, it is their custom to separate, and to come together again to share food — of an ordinary and innocent kind."
— Pliny the Younger, Governor of Bithynia, Letter to Emperor Trajan. Written approximately 112 AD.

**E05 — The War Over the Book**
> "I do not want any other gospel than that which is contained in the law and the prophets and the gospels; and I would not believe even the Lord himself if he were to teach anything contrary to them."
— Polycarp of Smyrna, quoting his position to Marcion. Recorded by Irenaeus of Lyon, c. 185 AD. [PAUSE] Marcion's response to Polycarp has not survived.

**E06 — Perpetua's Diary**
> "While I was still with the police authorities, my father came to me out of love for me and tried to dissuade me, saying, 'My daughter, have pity on my grey head. Have pity on your father, if I deserve to be called your father. If I have raised you to the prime of your life, if I have preferred you to all your brothers, do not hand me over to disgrace among men.'"
— Vibia Perpetua, from her prison diary. Written in Carthage, 203 AD. She was 22 years old. She was executed in the arena three days after writing this.

## Season 2

**E07 — In This Sign**
> "I, Constantine Augustus, and I, Licinius Augustus, met happily in Milan and discussed everything that concerned the public advantage and safety. Among other things we resolved that legal provisions should be made to secure respect for divinity, namely, to grant both to the Christians and to all others free choice to follow whatever form of worship they wished."
— The Edict of Milan, 313 AD. Two emperors, neither of them Christian, writing to provincial governors. This sentence ended 249 years of persecution.

**E08 — Athanasius Contra Mundum**
> "There was a time when he was not."
— Arius of Alexandria, c. 318 AD. Five words. The most consequential theological error in Christian history. The entire Council of Nicaea assembled to decide whether this sentence was true.

**E09 — Augustine's Garden**
> "Thou madest us for Thyself, and our heart is restless, until it repose in Thee."
— Augustine of Hippo, Confessions, Book 1. Written approximately 397 AD. He had been a Christian for 11 years when he wrote this. He had spent 32 years being restless before that.

**E10 — The Desert**
> "A brother came to see Abba Moses and asked for a word. The old man said to him: 'Go and sit in your cell, and your cell will teach you everything.'"
— The Sayings of the Desert Fathers. Written down approximately 4th-5th century AD. The advice was given sometime in the 3rd-4th century in the Egyptian desert. It has not been improved on.

**E11 — Patrick**
> "I was then about sixteen years of age. I did not know the true God. I was taken into captivity in Ireland with many thousands of people — and deservedly so, because we had turned away from God and did not keep his commandments, and disobeyed our bishops who used to remind us of our salvation."
— Patrick of Ireland, Confession. Written approximately 450 AD. He was describing his kidnapping 30 years earlier. He was kidnapped from Britain, enslaved in Ireland for 6 years, escaped, trained as a bishop, and went back.

**E12 — Gregory the Great**
> "The art of arts is the government of souls."
— Gregory I, Pastoral Rule. Written 591 AD. He had been pope for one year. He would govern the church, the city of Rome, and much of Italy for the next 13 years while being sick, barely able to walk, and convinced the world was about to end. The Pastoral Rule is still read by Christian leaders today.

## Season 3

**E13 — The Desert and the University**
> "What has Athens to do with Jerusalem? What has the Academy to do with the Church?"
— Tertullian of Carthage, c. 200 AD. [PAUSE] The answer, as it turned out, was: quite a lot. The medieval university was built on the premise that Athens and Jerusalem had everything to do with each other. It produced Thomas Aquinas. And Anselm. And the modern scientific method. Tertullian was wrong. But it's still a great question.

**E14 — Canossa**
> "I, King Henry, beg and implore your apostolic grace to grant me absolution and forgiveness for my sins. I am willing to give full satisfaction for my disobedience."
— Henry IV, Holy Roman Emperor, to Pope Gregory VII. Written January 1077 AD. The Emperor had been standing barefoot in the snow outside the castle at Canossa for three days. He was the most powerful ruler in Europe. He had been excommunicated by the pope. This letter got him readmitted to the church.

**E15 — God Wills It**
> "Let those who in the past have been accustomed perversely to wage private warfare against the faithful now go against the infidels. Let those who for a long time have been robbers now become knights. Let those who have been fighting against their brothers and relatives now fight in a proper way against the barbarians."
— Pope Urban II, Council of Clermont, November 27, 1095 AD. The crowd's response was: *Deus vult.* God wills it. The First Crusade began.

## Season 4

**E22 — The Black Death**
> "All shall be well, and all shall be well, and all manner of thing shall be well."
— Julian of Norwich, Revelations of Divine Love, 1373 AD. She received her visions during a near-fatal illness. She was writing into a world where the Black Death had killed one-third of Europe's population 25 years earlier and was still returning in periodic waves. All was not well. She chose to believe it would be.

**E23 — Avignon**
> "Come, come, Holy Father. I, your poor miserable daughter, have no longer any patience — I have been waiting so long, and I can wait no longer. Come, come, and resist no more the will of God who is calling you."
— Catherine of Siena, letter to Pope Gregory XI, 1376 AD. She was a Dominican tertiary, 29 years old, with no official status in the church. He was the most powerful bishop on earth, resident in Avignon, under the thumb of the King of France. She called him "Babbo mio" — my daddy. He moved back to Rome.

**E24 — Jan Hus**
> "You may roast this goose — but in a hundred years will come a swan whom you cannot roast."
— Jan Hus, reportedly spoken at the stake. Constance, July 6, 1415. [PAUSE] One hundred and two years later, Martin Luther posted the 95 Theses.

**E25 — Here I Stand**
> "Unless I am convinced by the testimony of the Scriptures or by clear reason — for I do not trust either in the pope or in councils alone, since it is well known that they have often erred and contradicted themselves — I am bound by the Scriptures I have quoted and my conscience is captive to the Word of God. I cannot and will not retract anything, since it is neither safe nor right to go against conscience."
— Martin Luther, Diet of Worms, April 17, 1521 AD. He was 37 years old. He was declared an outlaw four days later. He lived for 25 more years.

## Season 5

**E31 — Cortés and Las Casas**
> "We ask and require that you acknowledge the Church as the Ruler and Superior of the whole world and the high priest called Pope, and in his name the King and Queen of Spain as lords of this land; and that you consent and give place that these religious fathers should declare and preach to you the aforesaid. If you do so, you will do well. And that which you are obliged to do to Their Highnesses, and we in their name shall receive you in all love and charity. If you do not do this, and maliciously make delay in it, we certify to you that with the help of God, we shall powerfully enter into your country, and shall make war against you in all ways and manners that we can, and shall subject you to the yoke and obedience of the Church and of Their Highnesses; and we shall take your persons and your wives and your children, and shall make slaves of them. And the deaths and losses which shall accrue from this are your fault."
— The Requerimiento, 1513 AD. Written by Juan López de Palacios Rubios. Read aloud, in Spanish, to indigenous peoples who spoke no Spanish, before military attacks. Bartolomé de las Casas, who witnessed this, wrote: "I don't know whether to laugh or cry."

**E32 — The Slave and the Gospel**
> "Between the Christianity of this land and the Christianity of Christ, I recognize the widest possible difference. I love the pure, peaceable, and impartial Christianity of Christ; and I therefore hate the corrupt, slaveholding, women-whipping, cradle-plundering, partial and hypocritical Christianity of this land. Indeed, I can see no reason, but the most deceitful one, for calling the religion of this land Christianity."
— Frederick Douglass, Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass, 1845 AD. He had escaped slavery two years earlier. He was 27 years old.

## Season 6

**E40 — Darwin and the Dove**
> "I can indeed hardly see how anyone ought to wish Christianity to be true; for if so the plain language of the text seems to show that the men who do not believe, and this would include my Father, Brother, and almost all my best friends, will be everlastingly punished. And this is a damnable doctrine."
— Charles Darwin, Autobiography, written 1876 AD, not published until after his death. Darwin was buried in Westminster Abbey by the Church of England. The rector called him "our greatest scientist." Darwin had spent 40 years arguing with himself about what to believe.

**E41 — The Great Awakening**
> "The God that holds you over the pit of hell, much as one holds a spider, or some loathsome insect over the fire, abhors you, and is dreadfully provoked: his wrath towards you burns like fire; he looks upon you as worthy of nothing else, but to be cast into the fire."
— Jonathan Edwards, "Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God," July 8, 1741 AD. He read this in a flat monotone. People screamed and grabbed the pews as if the floor was tilting. He was describing not the wrath of God but the *precariousness* of existence — the thread by which life hangs. He spent the rest of his career asking: what is a real conversion, and what is manufactured emotion?

## Season 7

**E50 — The Bomb in the Playground**
> "We the undersigned representatives of German Science and Art, serve notice hereby to the civilized world as a pledge for our faith in the entirely defensive character of this war and for the exclusive responsibility of England, which is at the root of the struggle for existence that has been forced upon us."
— "Manifesto of the Ninety-Three," October 4, 1914 AD. Signed by 93 leading German intellectuals including: Adolf von Harnack, the greatest liberal theologian of the era. Karl Barth read this. His theological world collapsed. He began writing.

**E51 — Bonhoeffer**
> "I am certain that neither death, nor life, nor angels, nor rulers, nor things present, nor things to come, nor powers, nor height, nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord."
— Dietrich Bonhoeffer, letter from prison, Tegel Prison, Berlin, 1943 AD. He was executed April 9, 1945. Three weeks before Germany surrendered. The prison doctor who witnessed his death wrote: "I have hardly ever seen a man die so entirely submissive to the will of God."

**E52 — "Daddy, Why?"**
> "I have a dream that my four little children will one day live in a nation where they will not be judged by the color of their skin but by the content of their character."
— Martin Luther King Jr., Lincoln Memorial, August 28, 1963 AD. King was 34 years old. He had been in jail, bombed, stabbed, and threatened with death. He was a Baptist pastor from Atlanta. The speech was largely improvised — he set aside his notes midway through. It was a sermon.

## Season 8

**E58 — Azusa to Africa**
> "The color line was washed away in the blood."
— Frank Bartleman, eyewitness account of the Azusa Street Revival, 1906 AD. Written when interracial worship in Los Angeles was not just unusual — in many American cities, it was illegal.

**E62 — Deconstruction**
> "I want to be clear: I no longer identify as a Christian. This is a painful admission for me because my faith was the most important thing in my life for most of my life. But the more I have studied the history of Christianity, the more I have come to see that the institution I was committed to and the Christ I thought I was following were not the same thing. I still don't know what to do with that."
— Anonymous, from an online forum post, 2019 AD. Posted by a person who had served in ministry for 20 years. Name withheld. Posted by 43,000 people. [PAUSE] This is not a new story. It happened to Erasmus. It happened to Thomas Cranmer. It happened to Charles Darwin. What is new is that 43,000 people responded in 48 hours.

**E63 — The Sunrise (Series Finale)**
> "I am the Alpha and the Omega, the First and the Last, the Beginning and the End."
— Revelation 22:13. Written on the island of Patmos, approximately 95 AD. The last book of the Christian Bible. The last words of the series. [LONG PAUSE — 5 seconds] The story is not over.
