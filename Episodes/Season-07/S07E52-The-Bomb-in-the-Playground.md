# S07E52 — "The Bomb in the Playground"
*1914–1945 AD · The century that tested everything*

## The Story
August 1914. Karl Barth's teachers — the greatest liberal theologians in Germany — sign a manifesto supporting the Kaiser's war. Barth's world collapses. He begins writing. Five years later, his commentary on Romans is described as "a bomb on the playground of theologians."

1933. Hitler becomes chancellor. The Deutsche Christen (German Christians) align the church with National Socialism. Barth drafts the Barmen Declaration. Bonhoeffer joins the conspiracy against Hitler. April 9, 1945 — Bonhoeffer is hanged. Three weeks before Germany surrenders.

## The Primary Sources

**Barth's *Epistle to the Romans*** (1919, revised 1922):
> "If I have a system, it is limited to a recognition of what Kierkegaard called the 'infinite qualitative distinction' between time and eternity, and to my regarding this as possessing negative significance for all human powers and possibilities. The relation between God and man, between this world and his Kingdom, is a non-human, non-historical happening."

In plain English: God is not the highest human value. God is the judge of all human values, including religion. No human achievement — not culture, not civilization, not the church — stands before God uncriticized.

**The Barmen Declaration** (1934):
Written primarily by Barth:
> "Jesus Christ, as he is attested for us in Holy Scripture, is the one Word of God which we have to hear and which we have to trust and obey in life and in death."
> "We reject the false doctrine, as though there were areas of our life in which we would not belong to Jesus Christ, but to other lords."

**Bonhoeffer's letters from prison** (1943–1945):
> "I am certain that neither death, nor life, nor anything else in all creation will be able to separate me from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord."

The prison doctor's account of Bonhoeffer's death at Flossenbürg (April 9, 1945):
> "I saw Pastor Bonhoeffer... kneeling on the floor praying fervently to his God. I was most deeply moved by the way this lovable man prayed, so devout and so certain that God heard his prayer. At the place of execution, he again said a short prayer and then climbed the steps to the gallows, brave and composed. His death ensued after a few seconds. In the almost fifty years that I worked as a doctor, I have hardly ever seen a man die so entirely submissive to the will of God."

## The Holocaust — What the Church Did and Didn't Do

**What was done:**
- The Deutsche Christen: aligned with Nazism; rewrote hymnals; introduced the Aryan paragraph excluding Jewish Christians
- Most of the German Protestant church was passive — not actively Nazi, not actively resistant
- The Catholic Church signed the Concordat (1933) with Hitler
- Pope Pius XII's silence remains deeply disputed

**What the Confessing Church did:**
- The Barmen Declaration (1934): theological resistance
- Niemöller, Bonhoeffer: arrested, imprisoned, executed
- Raoul Wallenberg: Swedish Lutheran diplomat who saved 100,000+ Hungarian Jews before being arrested by the Soviets
- Le Chambon-sur-Lignon: French Huguenot village that sheltered 3,000–5,000 Jews; led by Pastor André Trocmé

**The theological reckoning:**
The Holocaust was not a Christian act. But it was made possible by 1,900 years of Christian antisemitism. *Nostra Aetate* (Vatican II, 1965) formally repudiated the deicide charge. It took the church 1,900 years to say clearly: the Jews did not kill Jesus, and even if they had, no collective punishment is justified.
