# S03E13 — "The Desert and the University"
*800–1200 AD · How monks saved civilization and invented the university*

## The Historical Evidence

**What monasteries preserved:**
Every classical text we have from Greece and Rome was preserved in monastery scriptoria. The alternative was loss. We have:
- All of Virgil, Horace, Ovid, Cicero — from monastic copies
- The works of Aristotle (in fragments, via monasteries; the fuller text came back via Arabic)
- Pliny the Elder, Suetonius, Tacitus — all known from a handful of monastery manuscripts
- The *Venerable Bede* (673–735): the most important historian of early medieval Britain; produced at Jarrow monastery; his *Ecclesiastical History* is the primary source for English Christianity to 735 AD

**The Carolingian Renaissance** (c. 800–850):
Charlemagne assembled scholars at his court — most importantly **Alcuin of York** — and mandated that every cathedral maintain a school. The standardization of Latin script (Carolingian minuscule — the basis of our modern lowercase letters) allowed manuscripts to be copied reliably across Europe.

**Cluny** (910 AD): 1,450 monasteries in one network. The largest organized institution in medieval Europe outside the papacy. The Cluniac Reform: liturgy, education, hospitality. Every monastery a school, a hospital, a library.

**The First University** (Bologna, 1088):
Students organized a guild (*universitas*) to negotiate with the city. They hired professors. The university as self-governing community of scholars was born. By 1250: 15+ universities across Europe. The Papal Charter gave them independence from local bishops — the institutional autonomy that made free inquiry possible.

**Anselm of Canterbury** (1033–1109):
*Cur Deus Homo* (1098) — "Why did God become human?" A strictly rational argument: God cannot simply forgive; satisfaction must be made; only a God-man can make infinite satisfaction for infinite offense. The first great medieval theology done in the new mode: *fides quaerens intellectum* — faith seeking understanding.
