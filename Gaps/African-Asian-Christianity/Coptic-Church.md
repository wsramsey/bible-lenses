# The Coptic Orthodox Church
*The church of Mark the Evangelist — one of Christianity's oldest living traditions*

## Founded by Mark (c. 42 AD)
The Coptic Church traces its founding to the Apostle Mark, who is said to have arrived in Alexandria around 42 AD and founded the church. The **Catechetical School of Alexandria** — the first Christian theological school — emerged from this community.

Alexandria became the intellectual engine of early Christianity:
- **[[Origen of Alexandria]]** (184–253): the greatest biblical scholar of antiquity; first systematic Christian theologian; his *Hexapla* (six-column parallel OT text) is the first work of biblical criticism
- **[[Athanasius of Alexandria]]** (296–373): defender of Nicene orthodoxy against Arianism; exiled five times; "Athanasius contra mundum"
- **[[Cyril of Alexandria]]** (376–444): champion of *Theotokos* at Ephesus; brilliant but ruthless

## The Chalcedonian Break (451)
→ See [[Council of Chalcedon (451)]]

The Coptic Church rejected Chalcedon's "two natures" formula as Nestorian. They are **Miaphysite** — one united nature in Christ (*mia physis*) — not Monophysite (which implies only a divine nature). The distinction matters: the Copts do not deny Christ's humanity.

**The result**: The Coptic Church separated from both Rome and Constantinople in 451 and has been independent ever since — 1,570+ years.

## Survival Under Islam (642 AD–present)
Arab armies conquered Egypt in 641–642. The Copts became a *dhimmi* minority under Islamic rule:
- The Arabic word for "Egypt" (*Misr*) gives the Copts their name (*Qibti* = Coptic = Egyptian)
- The Copts preserved the Coptic language (descended from ancient Egyptian) — the only surviving daughter language of pharaonic Egypt
- Regular persecution; church construction restricted; periodic pogroms
- The **Coptic language** survives only in the liturgy — one of history's most remarkable linguistic survivals

## The Coptic Theological Contribution

**Monasticism**: The Egyptian desert gave Christianity its monastic tradition. [[Anthony the Great]], [[Pachomius]] (founder of communal monasticism), [[Macarius of Egypt]], [[Evagrius Ponticus]] — all Copts. The Desert Fathers are Coptic Christianity's greatest gift to the world.

**Biblical scholarship**: The Catechetical School produced [[Clement of Alexandria]], [[Origen]], and [[Didymus the Blind]] — the most sophisticated biblical interpreters of antiquity.

**The Sahidic Coptic NT**: The oldest complete NT manuscripts include Coptic versions — crucial for textual criticism.

## The Modern Coptic Church
- ~12 million Copts in Egypt (~10% of the population)
- ~4 million in the diaspora (US, Australia, Europe)
- **Pope Tawadros II** (elected 2012) — 118th Pope of Alexandria
- Regularly experiencing martyrdom: the 21 Coptic martyrs killed by ISIS in Libya (February 15, 2015) — canonized immediately; their blood on the beach became an icon
- **The Coptic Cross**: distinctive cross with equal arms; used in tattoos that identify Copts publicly as Christians in Muslim-majority Egypt
