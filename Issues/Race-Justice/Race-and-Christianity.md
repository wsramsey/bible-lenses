# Race, Racism, and the Church
*The church that invented human dignity also defended slavery for 1,800 years*

## The Historical Record
**Christianity and slavery:**
- Slavery defended biblically in the antebellum American South; the "Curse of Ham" (Genesis 9); Paul's "slaves, obey your masters"
- Southern Baptist Convention *founded in 1845* to defend slaveholders' right to be missionaries
- The Confederate States of America fought under a explicitly Christian banner; its constitution invoked "the favor and guidance of Almighty God"

**Christianity and abolition:**
- Quakers first organized opposition to slavery (1688)
- Evangelical Anglicans (Wilberforce, Clapham Sect) ended the British slave trade
- Frederick Douglass used Christianity *against* slavery: distinguished "the Christianity of Christ" from "the Christianity of this land"
- Harriet Tubman: deeply Christian; understood herself as Moses

**Segregation:**
- Southern white Christianity overwhelmingly supported segregation
- Bob Jones University: didn't admit Black students until 1971; banned interracial dating until 2000
- King's "Letter from Birmingham Jail" addressed to *white moderate clergy* urging him to wait

**Civil Rights:**
- The movement was a Christian movement, led by Black Baptist pastors, organized through churches
- King's theology drew on Augustine, Aquinas, Buber, Tillich, Niebuhr

## What the Scholars Say
**[[James Cone]]** — *Black Theology and Black Power* (1969): God is on the side of the oppressed; Jesus is Black (in the sense that he identifies with the oppressed); white theology has been complicit in white supremacy; the cross is about liberation

**[[Martin Luther King Jr.]]** — "the arc of the moral universe is long, but it bends toward justice"; the beloved community; nonviolent resistance as the force of love; agape as the engine of social change

**[[Howard Thurman]]** — *Jesus and the Disinherited* (1949; MLK carried it on the bus): Jesus speaks to those "with their backs against the wall"; the Gospel is addressed to the poor and dispossessed

**[[Esau McCaulley]]** — *Reading While Black*: the Black Church tradition has developed hermeneutical wisdom (reading Scripture under oppression) that the whole church needs; Scripture *does* address racial injustice

**[[Jemar Tisby]]** — *The Color of Compromise*: white American Christianity's complicity in racism is not a side effect but a central feature; the church must reckon with this history

**[[N.T. Wright]]** — Galatians 3:28 is the Magna Carta of human equality; in Christ, ethnic and social divisions are eschatologically relativized; racial reconciliation is a Gospel issue

## The Contemporary Debate
**Critical Race Theory** entered evangelical discourse around 2020:
- SBC resolution debate; some evangelical leaders concerned about CRT's ideological assumptions
- [[Russell Moore]], [[Ed Stetzer]], many others: racial justice is a Gospel issue; CRT is a sociological tool that can be used alongside (not replacing) Christian categories
- Opponents: CRT is Marxist critical theory incompatible with Christianity; it divides by race rather than uniting in Christ

## The Ongoing Reality
- Black and white Christians in America still attend predominantly segregated churches
- "The most segregated hour in America is 11am on Sunday morning" — often attributed to MLK
- The reckoning with American Christianity's racial history is ongoing
