# Evolution and Creation
*Christianity's most sustained engagement with modern science*

## The Positions (from most conservative to most liberal)

### Young-Earth Creationism (YEC)
Genesis 1–2 is a historical account of a six-day creation approximately 6,000–10,000 years ago. The scientific evidence for an old earth and evolution has been misinterpreted.
- **Key organizations**: Answers in Genesis (Ken Ham), Institute for Creation Research
- **Key arguments**: the "kinds" of Genesis are real biological categories; the Flood accounts for geological evidence; radiometric dating is unreliable
- **Scholarly note**: no mainstream scientists accept YEC; it requires rejection of physics, geology, biology, and astronomy simultaneously. Most biblical scholars also reject it as a misreading of Genesis's genre.

### Old-Earth Creationism (OEC)
The universe is billions of years old (accepting mainstream science) but species were specially created; evolution is false or severely limited.
- **Key scholars**: Hugh Ross (Reasons to Believe), William Dembski
- **Variations**: Gap theory (gap between Genesis 1:1 and 1:2); Day-Age theory (each "day" is an era); Progressive Creation

### Intelligent Design (ID)
Evolution cannot explain the "irreducible complexity" of biological systems; design inference is scientifically valid; a designer must be posited.
- **Key scholars**: Michael Behe (*Darwin's Black Box*), Stephen Meyer (*Darwin's Doubt*)
- **Legal status**: ruled not scientific by US courts (*Kitzmiller v. Dover*, 2005)
- **Scientific note**: mainstream biologists reject ID's arguments; the "irreducible complexity" examples have been answered

### Theistic Evolution / Evolutionary Creation
God created through the process of evolution; there is no conflict between evolutionary science and Christian faith; Genesis 1–2 is theological poetry, not scientific description.
- **Key organizations**: BioLogos (Francis Collins), Faraday Institute
- **Key scholars**: [[Francis Collins]], [[N.T. Wright]], [[Alister McGrath]], [[Denis Alexander]]
- **The hermeneutical move**: Genesis 1 is an ancient temple inauguration text (John Walton); it answers "who" and "why," not "how" and "when"

### Non-overlapping Magisteria (NOMA)
Science and religion answer different questions and cannot conflict.
- **Proposed by**: Stephen Jay Gould
- **Critique**: too simple; the questions do overlap (historical claims in Scripture; theological claims about human nature)

## What the Scholars Say
**[[Augustine of Hippo]]** — warned against interpreting Genesis literally in ways that conflict with natural knowledge; "it is a disgraceful and dangerous thing for an infidel to hear a Christian talking nonsense about science." Written in *The Literal Meaning of Genesis*.

**[[Thomas Aquinas]]** — held that Scripture has multiple senses; the literal sense need not always be the primary sense; he would likely have accommodated evolution

**[[Karl Barth]]** — deliberately agnostic about the science-faith question; theology and science operate at different levels; but *creation* is a theological claim, not a scientific one

**[[N.T. Wright]]** — theistic evolution; Genesis 1 is not concerned with the mechanism of creation; human beings are specially created *through* the evolutionary process; the image of God is vocation, not biological attribute

**[[Alvin Plantinga]]** — *Where the Conflict Really Lies* (2011): the real conflict is between naturalism and science (they undermine each other), not between Christianity and science; theistic evolution is philosophically coherent

**[[Francis Collins]]** — *The Language of God*: geneticist and Christian; the human genome is "the language in which God created life"; BioLogos; evolution is how God created

## Key Theological Questions
1. What is Genesis 1–2? History? Poetry? Myth? Temple inauguration text?
2. Is there a historical Adam and Eve? Does the theology require them?
3. Does evolution undermine the doctrine of original sin?
4. What is the "image of God" if humans evolved from lower primates?
5. Does evolution's violence and death before humanity undermine belief in a good Creator?
