# The Age of the Universe and Earth

## The Scientific Consensus
- **Universe**: approximately 13.8 billion years old (Big Bang cosmology)
- **Earth**: approximately 4.5 billion years old (radiometric dating, geology)
- **Life**: approximately 3.5 billion years old (fossil record)
- **Homo sapiens**: approximately 300,000 years old

## The Young-Earth Position
The universe is 6,000–10,000 years old based on biblical genealogies (Archbishop Ussher calculated 4004 BC for creation). The scientific dating methods are fundamentally flawed.

**The "flood geology" argument**: most geological evidence (strata, fossils) was deposited during Noah's flood; this explains what appears to be billions of years of history in thousands.

## The Old-Earth Christian Response
- **Augustine** and the early church didn't read Genesis as giving a date
- The "days" of Genesis 1 may be non-literal (Day-Age theory)
- The gap theory, framework hypothesis, and other readings accommodate old-earth science
- YEC requires rejecting not just biology but physics (radiometric dating involves nuclear physics), astronomy (we see light from 13 billion light-years away), and geology

## The Big Bang and God
Interestingly, the Big Bang (proposed by a Catholic priest, Georges Lemaître, 1927) supports the idea of a beginning — "Let there be light" has new resonance. Some atheist scientists initially resisted the Big Bang for this reason.

**[[Robert Barron]]** and other Catholic thinkers: the Big Bang doesn't prove God but is consistent with creation *ex nihilo*.
