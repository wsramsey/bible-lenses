# Baptism
*Water, faith, and the most divisive sacramental question in Christianity*

## The Fundamental Division: Who Gets Baptized?

### Infant Baptism (Paedobaptism)
Infants of believing parents are baptized as the covenant sign of their inclusion in the covenant community — the NT equivalent of circumcision.
- **Traditions**: Catholic, Orthodox, Lutheran, Anglican, Reformed/Presbyterian, Methodist
- **Theological basis**: Covenant theology (circumcision → baptism); household baptisms in Acts; "Repent and be baptized, you and your household" (Acts 2:38–39)
- **Two Catholic sub-views**: (1) Baptism regenerates — washing away original sin (ex opere operato); (2) Baptism incorporates into the covenant community; faith must follow

### Believer's Baptism (Credobaptism)
Only those who consciously confess faith should be baptized — baptism is the public declaration of an existing faith.
- **Traditions**: Baptist, Anabaptist, Pentecostal, most evangelical non-denominational
- **Theological basis**: The NT pattern is hear → believe → be baptized; infants cannot believe; baptism is the sign of faith already present
- **Historical origin**: The Anabaptists (1525) — re-baptized adults who had been baptized as infants; hence "Anabaptist" (re-baptizer)

## The Mode Question: Immersion, Pouring, or Sprinkling?
- **Immersion**: Baptists, most evangelical; *baptizo* means "to immerse"; symbolizes burial and resurrection (Romans 6)
- **Pouring** (*affusion*): most Reformed, Anglican, Catholic
- **Sprinkling** (*aspersion*): some Reformed
- Most scholars acknowledge the NT probably practiced immersion; most also acknowledge the early church quickly moved to pouring for practical reasons

## What the Scholars Say
**[[Augustine of Hippo]]** — infant baptism necessary; original sin present from birth; unbaptized infants face a "mildest damnation" (*mitissima poena*) — a view later softened

**[[Martin Luther]]** — infant baptism valid; faith can be present even in infants in a mysterious way; but Lutheran baptism is also regenerative (baptismal regeneration)

**[[John Calvin]]** — infant baptism as covenant sign; the sign doesn't automatically convey grace; it seals the covenant promise; faith must follow

**[[Karl Barth]]** — *Church Dogmatics* IV/4: late in life reversed himself; infant baptism is not baptism in the NT sense; "Christian baptism is not a sacrament" — it is the first act of Christian obedience following an experience of grace; shocked the Reformed world

**[[N.T. Wright]]** — supports infant baptism as covenant incorporation; the children of believers are within the covenant; but the sacrament requires ongoing nurture

## The Rebaptism Question
Can someone baptized as an infant be rebaptized as a believing adult? Baptists say yes (the first was not real baptism). Catholics, Lutherans, Reformed say no — to rebaptize is to deny the first baptism's validity and show distrust in God's action.
