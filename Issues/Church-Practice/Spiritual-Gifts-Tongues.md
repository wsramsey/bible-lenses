# Spiritual Gifts and Speaking in Tongues
*Are the miraculous gifts for today or did they cease with the apostles?*

## What the NT Says
Paul's most extended treatment: 1 Corinthians 12–14
- **Tongues** (*glossolalia*): speaking in a language not naturally known; "speaking to God" (14:2); requires interpretation in the assembly
- **Prophecy**: speaking words from God to the congregation; greater than tongues for building up the church
- **Healing, miracles, discernment, knowledge, wisdom, administration**: all listed as gifts of the Spirit
- Paul regulates but does not prohibit tongues; "do not forbid speaking in tongues" (14:39)
- Acts 2 (Pentecost), Acts 10 (Cornelius), Acts 19 (Ephesus): three key episodes where tongues appear

## Cessationism
The "sign gifts" (tongues, prophecy, healing, miracles) were given to authenticate the apostles and establish the church; they ceased when the apostolic age ended and the NT canon was complete.
- **B.B. Warfield** (*Counterfeit Miracles*, 1918): the foundational cessationist argument
- **John MacArthur** (*Charismatic Chaos*, 1992; *Strange Fire*, 2013): the gifts have ceased; charismatic practice is at best self-deception, at worst demonic
- **The 1 Corinthians 13:10 argument**: "when the perfect comes, the imperfect disappears" — cessationists argue "the perfect" = the completed canon; most scholars disagree

## Continuationism
The gifts continue throughout church history; God's Spirit continues to work through all the gifts listed in the NT.
- **Wayne Grudem** (*Systematic Theology*): "open but cautious" continuationism; gifts continue but must be tested
- **Gordon Fee**: the NT nowhere says the gifts will cease; cessationism is a theological inference, not exegesis
- **Pentecostal/Charismatic tradition**: the gifts are normative Christian experience; the second blessing; Spirit baptism

## The Charismatic Renewal
1960s–1970s: the charismatic renewal moved tongues and gifts into mainline Protestant and Catholic churches (the "Catholic charismatic renewal" began at Duquesne University, 1967). The "third wave" (1980s–): John Wimber, Vineyard, "signs and wonders."

## The Prosperity Gospel Problem
The charismatic tradition's most serious failure: the prosperity gospel conflates spiritual gifting with material blessing; healing is "guaranteed" by sufficient faith; failure to be healed = failure of faith. This is devastating pastorally and theologically rejected by virtually all serious theologians.

## Key Questions
1. What is the hermeneutical basis for cessationism? Is it in the text?
2. Are "tongues" in Acts the same phenomenon as in 1 Corinthians?
3. How do we test prophetic words today?
4. What do we do with the global Pentecostal explosion (700 million) if the gifts have ceased?
