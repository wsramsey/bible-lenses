# Church and State
*"Render to Caesar" — the question that never goes away*

## The Biblical Tension
**Romans 13:1–7**: "Let every person be subject to the governing authorities, for there is no authority except from God." Written under Nero.

**Revelation 13**: The beast rising from the sea = Rome; the number of the beast = Nero. The state as demonic power demanding worship.

**Acts 5:29**: "We must obey God rather than human beings."

These texts are in irreducible tension — and they were written within decades of each other.

## The Historical Models

### Caesaropapism
The emperor/state controls the church. Byzantine model; Eastern Orthodox tradition historically.

### Papal Theocracy
The pope controls the state; the spiritual power is above the temporal. Medieval Catholic ideal; Gregory VII, Innocent III.

### Two Kingdoms / Two Cities
Augustine: the City of God and the City of Man operate by different logics; Christians are citizens of both.
Luther: the kingdom of God (church/gospel) and the kingdom of the world (law/sword) are distinct; Christians serve in both.

### Theocracy / Christian Nationalism
God's law should govern the state. Calvin's Geneva; Puritan New England; Christian Reconstruction (Rushdoony).

### Separation of Church and State
The state has no authority over religion; religion has no authority over the state. American Baptist/Quaker origins; First Amendment.

### The Benedict Option (Dreher)
The church should withdraw from culture-war politics and build thick alternative communities; the political battle is lost; focus on formation.

## What the Scholars Say
**[[Augustine of Hippo]]** — the two cities; the Christian can serve the earthly city while belonging to the heavenly; the church uses the earthly city's peace for its own purposes; but he also supported coercion of heretics (Donatists) using Luke 14:23 — his tragic error

**[[Martin Luther]]** — the two kingdoms; the sword is necessary in a fallen world; the Christian in the kingdom of God (left hand) serves the Gospel; in the kingdom of the world (right hand) serves as magistrate, soldier, executioner; the error: the kingdoms became too separate and enabled German Lutheranism's capitulation to Nazism

**[[John Calvin]]** — civil government serves God's purposes; the magistrate's role is sacred; Geneva as a Reformed city; theocratic tendency; Michael Servetus burned

**[[Karl Barth]]** — the Barmen Declaration: the church owes its ultimate allegiance to Jesus Christ, not to any state; "the German Christians" who aligned with Hitler had made the state an idol

**[[Reinhold Niebuhr]]** — Christian realism; nations are sinful; power must be checked by power; the church should engage politics but without illusions; liberal idealism (Social Gospel) is naive

**[[John Howard Yoder]]** — *The Politics of Jesus*: the church's political witness is its own ordered life; the cross is the paradigm of political action; nonviolence is not withdrawal but a specific political stance; the Constantinian compromise was the fall of the church

**[[Oliver O'Donovan]]** — *The Desire of the Nations*: Christendom was a genuine response to the Gospel's political claims; political authority serves Christ's lordship; the church must engage the political order

**[[Rod Dreher]]** — *The Benedict Option*: the culture war is over; Christians lost; focus on building communities of virtue and formation
