# Divorce and Remarriage
*The issue Jesus addressed most directly — and that the church has struggled with ever since*

## What Jesus Said
Jesus's teaching on divorce is stricter than both Roman and Jewish law of his day:
- **Matthew 5:32**: "Anyone who divorces his wife, except for sexual immorality (*porneia*), causes her to become an adulteress"
- **Matthew 19:9**: the exception clause again — "except for *porneia*"
- **Mark 10:11–12**: no exception clause — "Anyone who divorces his wife and marries another commits adultery"
- The tension between Matthew and Mark has been debated for 2,000 years

## The Traditions

### Catholic
Marriage is a sacrament — indissoluble. Divorce is not recognized; divorced Catholics who remarry cannot receive communion.
**Annulment**: not "Catholic divorce" — a declaration that a valid sacramental marriage never existed (due to lack of consent, maturity, freedom, etc.). Critics: effectively divorce with theological paperwork.

### Eastern Orthodox
Marriage is a sacrament — but can be "dissolved" by the church for grave reasons; allows remarriage (up to three marriages total); the second and third are penitential ceremonies

### Protestant (Reformation position)
Luther and Calvin: marriage is a civil covenant, not a sacrament; divorce permitted for adultery and desertion; remarriage permitted

### Contemporary Evangelical
Wide range: from no-divorce (except Matthew exception) to more permissive (abuse, abandonment)

## What the Scholars Say
**[[Augustine of Hippo]]** — marriage is indissoluble; the bond (*vinculum*) persists even after separation; no remarriage while the other spouse lives

**[[Thomas Aquinas]]** — marriage as sacrament; the bond mirrors Christ's indissoluble union with the church; annulment possible but rare

**[[John Calvin]]** — divorce for adultery and malicious desertion; remarriage permitted; the Matthean exception is real

**[[John Stott]]** — *Issues Facing Christians Today*: the Matthew exception permits divorce for *porneia*; remarriage is permitted after genuine repentance and if the former marriage is truly dead

**[[David Instone-Brewer]]** — *Divorce and Remarriage in the Bible*: the most thorough recent exegetical study; Jewish background of Deuteronomy 24 and Exodus 21:10–11 shows material and emotional neglect also grounds for divorce in Jewish law; Paul reflects this in 1 Cor 7

## The Pastoral Reality
- Divorce rate among Christians approximately equals the general population
- Many divorced and remarried people are in churches; how they are treated varies enormously
- Abuse survivors in no-divorce churches face impossible choices
