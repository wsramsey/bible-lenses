# Sex Before Marriage
*The widest gap between official teaching and actual behavior*

## The Historical Teaching
All major Christian traditions have historically taught that sexual intercourse belongs exclusively within marriage. The Greek word *porneia* (translated "sexual immorality") in the NT includes any sex outside marriage.

**The theological basis:**
- The body is the temple of the Holy Spirit (1 Cor 6:19–20)
- The "one flesh" union (*Genesis 2:24*) is permanent covenant, not temporary arrangement
- Sexual union creates a covenantal bond; casual or uncommitted sex fragments the person
- Marriage as the covenant context for the total gift of self

## The Modern Crisis
- **Statistics**: in the US, ~80% of unmarried evangelical Christians report having had premarital sex
- **The gap** between official teaching and actual behavior is the largest of any sexual ethics issue
- **Purity culture (1990s–2000s)**: the evangelical response; purity pledges, purity balls, *I Kissed Dating Goodbye*; produced documented psychological harm (shame, sexual dysfunction, unrealistic expectations) — and failed anyway

## What the Scholars Say
**[[Augustine of Hippo]]** — sexual desire (*concupiscence*) is disordered after the Fall; even within marriage it carries the taint of the Fall; virginity is the higher calling

**[[Thomas Aquinas]]** — fornication is gravely disordered; it treats the sexual act as if it were casual when it creates a covenantal bond; natural law argument

**[[John Paul II / Karol Wojtyła]]** — *Theology of the Body*: the most sophisticated contemporary Catholic sexual ethics; the body expresses the spousal meaning of self-donation; premarital sex is a lie told with the body — promising total self-gift without the covenantal commitment

**[[Lewis Smedes]]** — *Sex for Christians* (1976): the most thoughtful evangelical treatment; fornication is wrong because it separates what belongs together; but pastoral sensitivity about situations that don't fit neat categories

**[[Rowan Williams]]** — *The Body's Grace* (1989): what makes sex holy is whether it enables genuine vulnerability and mutual recognition; applies to both married and unmarried contexts; the most influential Anglican revisionist argument

## The Purity Culture Reckoning
Joshua Harris (*I Kissed Dating Goodbye*, 1997) became the emblem of evangelical purity culture. He publicly apologized, deconstructed his book, and left the faith. The harm caused:
- Body shame (especially for women)
- Unrealistic romantic expectations
- Inability to form healthy relationships
- Double standard (burden fell disproportionately on women)
- Shame spiral when the inevitable happened

## Key Questions
1. Is the *porneia* prohibition culturally bound or transcultural?
2. Does the "one flesh" union require legal marriage or covenantal commitment?
3. How do we distinguish harmful purity culture from genuine Christian sexual ethics?
4. What does pastoral care look like for the 80%?
