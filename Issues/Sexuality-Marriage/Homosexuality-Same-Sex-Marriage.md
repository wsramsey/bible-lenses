# Homosexuality and Same-Sex Marriage
*The most divisive issue in contemporary Western Christianity*

## The Historical Context
The church never had a unified, systematic teaching on homosexuality until the modern period forced the question. What existed:
- **Ancient world**: pederasty and same-sex acts were common in Greek and Roman culture; the NT texts respond to this context
- **Medieval canon law**: sodomy classified as a sin against nature (following Aquinas's natural law framework)
- **The Reformation**: maintained the traditional prohibition; Luther and Calvin added no new arguments
- **Pre-20th century**: the topic was rarely discussed systematically; the acts were condemned, the orientation concept didn't exist

**The modern context that changed everything:**
- 1948: Alfred Kinsey's *Sexual Behavior in the Human Male* — sexuality as a spectrum
- 1973: American Psychiatric Association removes homosexuality from DSM (no longer classified as mental illness)
- 1960s–1970s: gay liberation movement; coming-out culture; the question becomes personal and unavoidable
- 2003: Gene Robinson ordained as openly gay Episcopal bishop — first major institutional rupture
- 2015: *Obergefell v. Hodges* — US Supreme Court legalizes same-sex marriage

## The Traditional Position
**What it claims:** Homosexual acts are intrinsically disordered; marriage is the union of a man and woman; celibacy is the calling for those with same-sex attraction.

**The biblical argument:**
- Genesis 1–2: the complementarity of male and female is creational; the "one flesh" union is male-female
- Leviticus 18:22; 20:13: prohibitions on male same-sex acts
- Romans 1:26–27: same-sex acts described as "contrary to nature" (*para phusin*)
- 1 Corinthians 6:9; 1 Timothy 1:10: *arsenokoitai* and *malakoi* — debated translation

**Who holds it:** Catholic, Eastern Orthodox, Southern Baptist, most evangelical denominations, Anglican GAFCON, Global South Christianity

## The Affirming Position
**What it claims:** The biblical texts address cultic prostitution, pederasty, and exploitative relationships — not committed same-sex partnerships; sexual orientation is a modern concept the ancient writers didn't have; loving same-sex relationships can be holy.

**The revisionist arguments:**
- The NT context: Roman pederasty and sexual slavery, not monogamous partnerships
- *Arsenokoitai* translation disputed — "man-beds" = abusive practice, not orientation
- The trajectory of Scripture moves toward inclusion (Gentiles, women, Samaritans)
- Natural law: if orientation is given, can it be "unnatural"?

**Who holds it:** Episcopal Church (USA), ELCA, UCC, PC(USA), some Methodists, some Anglican provinces

## What the Major Scholars Have Said
**[[Thomas Aquinas]]** — sodomy is a sin against nature (*contra naturam*); the sexual act's purpose is procreation; same-sex acts frustrate this; among the gravest sins

**[[Karl Barth]]** — homosexuality is a "physical, psychological and social sickness"; the Word of God calls the homosexual to conversion; he would likely revise this under pressure from his own Christological method

**[[N.T. Wright]]** — traditional position; the male-female complementarity is built into creation; Paul's *para phusin* argument is about creation order, not cultural convention

**[[William Loader]]** — the most thorough NT scholar on sexuality; the NT texts do prohibit same-sex acts; but the question is whether we follow the NT's specific instructions in all cases (we don't on slavery)

**[[Eugene Rogers]]** — *Sexuality and the Christian Body*: same-sex relationships can participate in the covenant love of God; Barth's own theology of covenant demands inclusion

**[[James V. Brownson]]** — *Bible, Gender, Sexuality*: the most thorough revisionist biblical argument; the *one-flesh* union is about kinship and covenant, not gender differentiation

## The Major Church Ruptures
- **Anglican Communion**: 2003 Gene Robinson; GAFCON formed; Global South churches breaking communion with The Episcopal Church (USA)
- **United Methodist Church**: split into United Methodist Church (affirming) and Global Methodist Church (traditional), finalized 2024
- **Presbyterian**: multiple splits; PCUSA (affirming) vs. PCA and ECO (traditional)
- **Lutheran**: ELCA (affirming) vs. LCMS and WELS (traditional)

## The Pastoral Reality
Every congregation has LGBTQ+ members, parents of LGBTQ+ children, and people who have left the church over this issue. The debate is not abstract.

## Key Questions That Shape the Debate
1. Is sexual orientation a given (natural) or a disordered desire?
2. Are the biblical texts addressing orientation or specific acts in specific cultural contexts?
3. Does the trajectory of Scripture (inclusion of Gentiles, abolition of slavery) authorize further inclusion?
4. What is marriage for — procreation, covenant love, or both?
5. Is celibacy a genuine calling or an unjust burden?
