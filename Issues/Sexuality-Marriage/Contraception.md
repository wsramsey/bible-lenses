# Contraception
*The issue that split Catholic and Protestant sexual ethics in the 20th century*

## The Historical Picture
Until 1930, virtually all Christian traditions condemned artificial contraception. The change:
- **1930 Lambeth Conference**: Anglican bishops permitted contraception in limited circumstances — the first major Protestant break
- **1968 Humanae Vitae**: Pope Paul VI reaffirmed the Catholic prohibition against the recommendation of his own commission; the most rejected papal document in modern history

## The Catholic Position (Humanae Vitae)
Every act of sexual intercourse must remain open to procreation. Artificial contraception separates the *unitive* (bonding) and *procreative* meanings of sexuality, which belong inseparably together.
- **Natural Family Planning (NFP)**: permitted — works with the body's natural cycles
- The logic: contraception treats fertility as a disease to be suppressed; it changes the meaning of the sexual act

**Theologians who developed it:** [[Thomas Aquinas]] (natural law), [[Karol Wojtyła]] (Theology of the Body)

## The Protestant Position
Most Protestant traditions permit artificial contraception within marriage. The reasons:
- The primary purpose of marriage is not only procreation but companionship (Gen 2:18)
- 1 Corinthians 7 implies the couple has authority over their own sexual life
- The natural law argument doesn't compel the conclusion

**Theologians:** [[Karl Barth]], [[Dietrich Bonhoeffer]], virtually all mainline and evangelical ethicists

## The Onan Question
Genesis 38:8–10: Onan "spills his seed on the ground" to avoid raising children for his dead brother — God kills him. Historical Catholic argument: this condemns contraception. Most modern scholars: Onan's sin was violating the levirate duty, not contraception per se.

## Key Debates
- Does "natural law" compel the Catholic conclusion, or is it a specific reading?
- Does the Theology of the Body (JPII) provide a more compelling argument than simple natural law?
- Why do 85%+ of practicing Catholic women use artificial contraception despite the teaching?
