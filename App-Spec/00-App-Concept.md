# Bible Lenses App — Core Concept

## The One-Sentence Pitch
**A navigable knowledge graph of 2,000 years of Christian history and thought — every idea connected to every other idea, explored through paintings, portraits, artifacts, and primary sources.**

## The Experience
You're standing in a dark space. In front of you: a glowing node. Augustine of Hippo. His portrait. His dates. One sentence about who he was.

You press the right arrow. His connections radiate outward. Each one glowing. Each one labeled.

- → The Council of Nicaea (he was 11 when it happened; it shaped everything he believed)
- → The Fall of Rome (he was writing *City of God* when the Visigoths sacked it)
- → Pelagius (his great theological enemy; the argument they had is still being had today)
- → The Confessions (his book; his voice; his words)
- → Monica (his mother; the reason he converted)
- → The restless heart (the idea; the theology)
- → Philippe de Champaigne (the painter who painted him 1,200 years later)

You press Enter on any of them. You're there.

## What Makes It Different from Wikipedia

| Wikipedia | Bible Lenses |
|-----------|--------------|
| Text-first | Visual-first |
| Linear articles | Spatial graph |
| Mouse-only navigation | Keyboard-navigable |
| Flat links | Typed connections (influence/event/typology/conflict/artwork) |
| No sound | Sound design per era |
| No visual arc | The painting IS the entry point |
| Dead text | Primary sources read aloud |
| No question | Every node ends with a question |

## The Keyboard Navigation (Core UX)
```
Arrow keys     — move through the graph
Enter          — enter a node
Backspace/ESC  — go back
Space          — expand connections
Tab            — cycle through connection types
Numbers 1-9    — jump to numbered connections
/              — search
P              — pull up the painting/artwork for this node
Q              — hear the primary source quote
T              — see the timeline position
G              — go to the full graph view
```

This is a keyboard-first experience. Mouse works too. But keyboard makes it feel like piloting something.
