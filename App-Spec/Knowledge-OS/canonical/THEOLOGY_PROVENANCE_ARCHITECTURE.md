# Theology Provenance Architecture

## Purpose
Map theology accurately by treating beliefs as historically developed, framework-dependent, and practically embodied rather than as isolated static propositions.

## Core claim
Theologically meaningful accuracy requires tracking at least five layers:
1. **Definition** — what the belief claims
2. **Genealogy** — where it came from and what shaped it
3. **Framework** — what larger assumptions/system it lives inside
4. **Lived implications** — how it affects ordinary life and practice
5. **Historical mutation** — how it changes across eras and traditions

## Required note families
- Core belief notes
- Development history notes
- Worldview/framework notes
- Lived implications notes
- Hermeneutic variance notes
- Character notes
- Tradition notes
- Controversy notes

## Accuracy rule
A strong answer should ideally be grounded at the intersection of:
- a figure or text
- a belief
n- a framework/worldview
- a hermeneutic/tradition
- a development history
- practical implications

## Relationship types to preserve
- depends on
- reacts against
- develops from
- mutates into
- is embodied by
- is interpreted through
- produces
- stabilizes
- challenges
- assumes

## Why this matters
Without these layers, the vault will flatten living theological systems into disconnected definitions. With them, it can represent theology as a historical, interpretive, and practical ecology.
