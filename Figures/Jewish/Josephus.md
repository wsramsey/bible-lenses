# Flavius Josephus
*37–100 AD · Jewish Historian · The Primary Outside Witness*

> **Why he's here:** He is the most important non-Christian source for understanding the world of the New Testament, and one of only two non-Christian ancient authors who mentions Jesus by name. His *Jewish Antiquities* and *Jewish War* are indispensable for any serious engagement with the historical Jesus and the apostolic era.

## What He Wrote
- **The Jewish War** (*Bellum Judaicum*, c. 75) — history of the Jewish revolt (66–73 AD) including the destruction of the Temple; he was a general who switched sides
- **Jewish Antiquities** (*Antiquitates Judaicae*, c. 93) — history of the Jewish people from creation to his own time
- **The Testimonium Flavianum** (Ant. 18.3.3) — the most debated passage; mentions Jesus as "a wise man... the Christ"; probably partially authentic but partially interpolated by Christian scribes

## Significance for New Testament Studies
- Describes John the Baptist, Herod, Caiaphas, Pontius Pilate, and James "the brother of Jesus" (Ant. 20.9.1) — this last reference is widely considered authentic
- His account of the destruction of Jerusalem (70 AD) illuminates Jesus's predictions in the Olivet Discourse
- His descriptions of the Pharisees, Sadducees, and Essenes are the fullest outside the NT

## His Personal Story
Led Jewish forces in Galilee in 66 AD; surrendered to Vespasian and prophesied he would become emperor (he did); spent the rest of his life in Rome under imperial patronage. His fellow Jews considered him a traitor.
