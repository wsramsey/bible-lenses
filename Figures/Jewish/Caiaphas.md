# Caiaphas
*c. 14 BC – c. 46 AD · High Priest · The Trial of Jesus*

> **Why he's here:** He is the figure who made the judicial decision that led to the crucifixion. His statement (John 11:50) — "it is better that one man should die for the people than that the whole nation should perish" — is simultaneously cynical political calculation and (John notes) unwitting prophecy. His ossuary was discovered in Jerusalem in 1990, making him one of the few New Testament figures with direct archaeological attestation.

## What He Did
- **High priest 18–36 AD** — the longest tenure of any high priest in the period; a political survivor under both Roman prefects Gratus and Pilate
- **Orchestrated the arrest of Jesus** — the Sanhedrin meeting (John 11:47–53) where the decision was made
- **Presided at the trial** — the night trial condemned Jesus for blasphemy; the charge brought to Pilate was sedition
- **Sadducean politics** — the Sadducees collaborated with Rome to preserve Temple worship; Jesus's Temple disruption was an existential threat to this arrangement

## Archaeological Note
His limestone ossuary, inscribed "Joseph son of Caiaphas," was found in a Jerusalem burial cave in 1990 — one of the most significant archaeological confirmations of a New Testament figure.
