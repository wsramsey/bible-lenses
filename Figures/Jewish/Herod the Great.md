# Herod the Great
*73–4 BC · King of Judea · The World Jesus Was Born Into*

> **Why he's here:** To understand the Gospels you have to understand Herod. His rebuilt Temple was the center of Jewish life that Jesus would teach in, disrupt, and predict the destruction of. His massacre of the innocents (Matt 2) — historically plausible given his character — frames the Incarnation as immediately a story of power and threat. Jesus was born into his world.

## What He Did
- **Rebuilt the Temple** — the Second Temple as Jesus knew it was Herod's temple; a massive construction project that expanded the Temple Mount
- **Massacre of the innocents (Matt 2)** — ordered the killing of infants in Bethlehem; consistent with his documented character (he executed his own sons)
- **Built Caesarea Maritima** — a Roman-style city with an artificial harbor; Hellenistic Israel
- **Client king** — ruled at Roman pleasure; the model of collaboration with empire that defined the Jewish political options in Jesus's day

## Significance for the Gospels
The Temple Jesus teaches in is Herod's Temple. The priestly aristocracy (Caiaphas's family) owed their position to Herodian patronage. The political tensions Jesus navigates — Roman power, Herodian complicity, Pharisaic resistance, Zealot violence — are all products of the world Herod made.
