# Justinian I
*482–565 · Byzantine Emperor · Christian Civilization*

> **Why he's here:** The emperor who crystallized the Byzantine synthesis of Christian faith and Roman law into the civilization that shaped both Eastern Europe and Western medieval law. His *Corpus Juris Civilis* is the foundation of most European legal systems. His Hagia Sophia was the greatest building in the world for a thousand years and a theological statement in stone.

## What He Did
- **Hagia Sophia (537)** — "Solomon, I have surpassed thee"; the church as cosmic statement
- **Corpus Juris Civilis (529–534)** — codified Roman law with Christian moral framing; basis of civil law in most of the world
- **Reconquered North Africa and Italy** — temporary restoration of Roman territorial unity
- **Theological controversies** — personally drafted theological documents; convened the Second Council of Constantinople (553); condemned the Three Chapters; tried (unsuccessfully) to reconcile Chalcedonians and Monophysites
- **Persecuted pagans, Samaritans, and heretics**

## Empress Theodora
His wife Theodora — former actress, possibly monophysite — was co-ruler in fact; her political instincts saved the throne during the Nika riots.

## Influenced
- Eastern Orthodox political theology (*symphonia* — harmony of church and state)
- Western medieval canon law (via *Corpus Juris Civilis*)
- Byzantine art and architecture
