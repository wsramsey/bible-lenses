# Theodosius I
*347–395 · Roman Emperor · Christianity Becomes the State*

> **Why he's here:** If Constantine made Christianity legal, Theodosius made it mandatory. His Edict of Thessalonica (380) declared Nicene Christianity the official religion of the Roman Empire and made paganism and heresy illegal. The "Constantinian shift" people debate is really the *Theodosian* shift — this is where church and empire fully merged.

## What He Did
- **Edict of Thessalonica (380)** — declared all Romans must hold the Nicene faith; made Christianity the state religion
- **Outlawed paganism (391)** — closed temples, banned pagan sacrifice
- **Called the Council of Constantinople (381)** — completed the Nicene Creed (added the clause on the Holy Spirit)
- **Submitted to Ambrose of Milan's penance (390)** — after the Thessalonica massacre, Bishop Ambrose barred him from communion until he did public penance; the first major assertion of church authority over an emperor

## Significance
The Ambrose incident is the seedbed of medieval church-state conflict. The idea that the emperor is subject to the church's moral authority — which eventually became Pope Gregory VII vs. Emperor Henry IV — begins here.

## Influenced
- The entire medieval Christendom synthesis
- [[Augustine of Hippo]] (Theodosius's reign was Augustine's context)
- All subsequent Christian political theology
