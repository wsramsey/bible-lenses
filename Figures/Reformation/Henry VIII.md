# Henry VIII
*1491–1547 · King of England · The English Reformation*

> **Why he's here:** He created the Church of England — not for theological reasons but for a divorce. The irony is rich: the man who broke with Rome had been awarded the title *Fidei Defensor* (Defender of the Faith) by Pope Leo X in 1521 for writing against Luther. His break with Rome was political and personal; the theological content of the English Reformation was supplied by others (Cranmer, Tyndale).

## What He Did
- **Act of Supremacy (1534)** — made the king the Supreme Head of the Church of England
- **Dissolved the monasteries (1536–1541)** — seized monastic lands; massive wealth transfer; ended 1,000 years of English monastic life
- **Six wives** — the proximate cause of the Reformation; Catherine of Aragon, Anne Boleyn (executed), Jane Seymour, Anne of Cleves, Catherine Howard (executed), Catherine Parr
- **Executed Thomas More and John Fisher** — for refusing to accept his supremacy
- **Kept Catholic doctrine** — the Six Articles (1539) retained transubstantiation, clerical celibacy, and other Catholic positions; he was never Protestant theologically

## Was He Christian?
He believed in God, attended Mass daily, and had Thomas More executed for refusing to acknowledge his authority. His Christianity was real but entirely self-serving.

## Influenced
- The entire Anglican tradition
- The English Bible (Tyndale's work was enabled by the break with Rome)
- [[Richard Hooker]]'s *Laws of Ecclesiastical Polity* (the theological settlement)
