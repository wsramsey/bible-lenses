# Napoleon Bonaparte
*1769–1821 · Emperor of France · Secularism's Carrier*

> **Why he's here:** Napoleon didn't destroy Christianity — but he permanently altered its social position across Europe. His armies spread the French Revolutionary settlement (civil marriage, divorce, religious toleration, abolition of church courts) wherever they conquered. The map of European secularism largely follows Napoleon's conquests.

## What He Did
- **Concordat of 1801** — negotiated settlement with Pius VII; the church got official recognition, the state got control of episcopal appointments; neither side won
- **Spread the Code Napoléon** — civil law replacing canon law across Europe; marriage as civil contract, not sacrament
- **Imprisoned Pius VII** — 1809–1814; the pope refused to sanction his divorce; Napoleon had him arrested
- **Abolished feudalism and church courts** wherever his armies went
- **Famously said:** "Religion is what keeps the poor from murdering the rich"

## His Personal Religion
Probably a deist who saw religion as a social utility. At St. Helena: "I believe in neither God nor Devil." Also reportedly said: "How can you have order in a state without religion?" Both statements are probably genuine.

## Influenced
- European secularization
- Church-state separation as the modern norm
- Catholic defensive posture in the 19th century
