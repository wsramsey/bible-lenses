# Queen Elizabeth I
*1533–1603 · Queen of England · The Anglican Settlement*

> **Why she's here:** She engineered the religious settlement that made Anglicanism what it is — a deliberately ambiguous middle way between Rome and Geneva. The Elizabethan Settlement (1559) restored the Protestant Prayer Book but kept episcopal government and enough Catholic ceremonial to be tolerable to the middle. Her famous response on the Eucharist: "His was the word that spake it / He took the bread and brake it / And what that word doth make it / That I believe and take it."

## What She Did
- **Act of Supremacy (1559)** — restored the royal supremacy over the church (reversed under Mary I)
- **Act of Uniformity (1559)** — restored the 1552 Prayer Book with modifications; required weekly attendance
- **The Elizabethan Settlement** — kept bishops, kept liturgy, allowed both Catholic and Protestant interpretation of the Eucharist; the studied ambiguity is the Anglican genius
- **Executed Catholics and Puritans** — neither extreme was tolerated; recusant priests executed as traitors

## Influenced
- The entire Anglican theological tradition
- [[Richard Hooker]] (the Settlement's theologian)
- [[John Donne]], [[George Herbert]] (the via media in literature)
