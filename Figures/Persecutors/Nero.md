# Nero
*37–68 AD · Roman Emperor · The First Persecution*

> **Why he's here:** He is the Beast. Whether Revelation 13's 666 is a gematria for Nero Caesar (it works in Hebrew transliteration) or not, he is the context for understanding the first Roman persecution of Christians. Peter and Paul both died in Rome under his reign (c. 64–68 AD). His persecution was the first state-sponsored attack on Christianity.

## What He Did
- **Great Fire of Rome (64 AD)** — possibly arson, possibly accident; Nero blamed the Christians
- **First persecution** — Tacitus (*Annals* 15.44): "Nero fastened the guilt and inflicted the most exquisite tortures on a class hated for their abominations, called Christians by the populace... Mockery of every sort was added to their deaths. Covered with the skins of beasts, they were torn by dogs... or doomed to the flames and burnt, to serve as a nightly illumination"
- **Peter and Paul** — early tradition places both deaths in Rome under Nero
- **His death** — fled Rome, reportedly said "What an artist dies in me," killed himself; the year of four emperors followed

## Significance for New Testament
Paul's letter to the Romans (written c. 57 AD) calls for prayer for governing authorities; he had no idea. The Book of Revelation, written under Domitian but referencing Nero, uses him as the type of imperial antichrist.
