# Charlemagne
*742–814 · Holy Roman Emperor · Christian Europe*

> **Why he's here:** He created "Europe" as a political-cultural concept. Crowned Holy Roman Emperor by Pope Leo III on Christmas Day 800, Charlemagne unified most of Western Europe under a Christian political framework, standardized the liturgy (Carolingian reform), promoted literacy through cathedral schools, and forced the conversion of the Saxons at sword point. The tension between his genuine piety and his violence defines the Carolingian project.

## What He Did
- **Crowned Emperor 800** — "renovation of the Roman Empire"; the precedent for all Holy Roman Emperors
- **Carolingian Renaissance** — promoted education, standardized Latin, collected manuscripts, reformed monasteries
- **Saxon Wars (772–804)** — conquest and forced baptism of the Saxons; the Massacre of Verden (782, 4,500 killed)
- **Standardized the liturgy** — imposed Roman liturgical practice across the Frankish church
- **Kept [[Alcuin of York]] at court** — the leading scholar of the age as his theological adviser

## The Forced Conversion Problem
[[Alcuin of York]] wrote to Charlemagne arguing that baptism without faith is meaningless — one of the first systematic objections to forced conversion. Charlemagne didn't fully listen.

## Influenced
- The entire Holy Roman Empire (962–1806)
- Medieval church-state synthesis
- The "Christian West" as political concept
