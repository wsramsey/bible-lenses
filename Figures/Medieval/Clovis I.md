# Clovis I
*466–511 · King of the Franks · The Frankish Conversion*

> **Why he's here:** His baptism was the pivotal moment in Western European Christianization. When Clovis converted to Nicene (not Arian) Christianity around 496/508, he aligned the most powerful Germanic kingdom with Rome against the Arian Visigoths and Burgundians. Gregory of Tours called him a "new Constantine." The partnership between Frankish kingship and the Roman church became the template for medieval Europe.

## What He Did
- **Baptism (496 or 508)** — reportedly converted after praying to Christ during the Battle of Tolbiac; his queen Clotilde (later a saint) had been pushing him for years
- **Conquered Gaul** — defeated the Visigoths at Vouillé (507), establishing Frankish dominance
- **Allied with the papacy** — the Franks became the papacy's military protectors

## Was He Genuinely Christian?
His post-baptism career included murdering virtually all rival Frankish chieftains. Gregory of Tours presents him favorably. The honest answer: his conversion was both politically strategic and (probably) genuinely sincere, as was often true of royal conversions.

## Influenced
- The Carolingians (Charlemagne's dynasty descended from his kingdom)
- The pattern of royal conversion as the mechanism of national Christianization
