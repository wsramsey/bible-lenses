# Pope Innocent III
*1160–1216 · Pope · Peak Medieval Papacy*

> **Why he's here:** The most powerful pope in history. During his reign (1198–1216) the papacy controlled more political territory and exercised more authority over kings than at any other moment. He launched the Fourth Crusade (which sacked Constantinople), called the Fourth Lateran Council (which defined transubstantiation and required annual confession), and proclaimed himself "Vicar of Christ" — a title his predecessors had reserved for bishops.

## What He Did
- **Fourth Crusade (1202–1204)** — intended for Jerusalem; ended by sacking Constantinople; permanent wound in Catholic-Orthodox relations
- **Albigensian Crusade (1209)** — military campaign against the Cathar heresy in southern France
- **Fourth Lateran Council (1215)** — defined transubstantiation; required annual confession; restricted Jews; the most consequential medieval council before Trent
- **Approved Franciscans and Dominicans** — recognized both [[Francis of Assisi]] and Dominic's movements
- **Forced King John to submit** — Magna Carta (1215) was partly a reaction to his interference in English affairs

## Influenced
- The entire trajectory of medieval Catholicism
- Catholic-Orthodox division (Constantinople sack)
- The Inquisition (formalized under his successors)
