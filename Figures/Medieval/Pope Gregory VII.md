# Pope Gregory VII
*1015–1085 · Pope · The Gregorian Reform*

> **Why he's here:** The pope who made the most radical claim in medieval church history: the pope has authority to depose emperors. His *Dictatus Papae* (1075) listed 27 propositions including "the Roman pontiff alone can depose or reinstate bishops" and "he may depose emperors." The showdown with Emperor Henry IV at Canossa (1077) — Henry standing barefoot in the snow for three days to beg absolution — was the symbolic peak of medieval papal power.

## What He Did
- **Dictatus Papae (1075)** — the most extreme statement of papal supremacy ever issued
- **Investiture Controversy** — banned lay investiture (kings appointing bishops); Henry IV rejected this; Gregory excommunicated him
- **Canossa (1077)** — Henry IV stood in the snow for three days; Gregory lifted the excommunication; Henry reconquered Italy shortly after
- **Died in exile** — "I have loved justice and hated iniquity; therefore I die in exile"

## Significance
The Investiture Controversy forced the separation of spiritual and temporal authority — which paradoxically eventually led to the secular state. [[Harold Berman]]'s *Law and Revolution* argues modern Western law begins here.

## Influenced
- All medieval church-state theory
- [[Thomas Aquinas]]'s political theology
- The eventual Protestant rejection of papal political power
