# Plato
*428–348 BC · Greek Philosopher · The Permanent Background*

> **Why he's here:** [[Alfred North Whitehead]] said all Western philosophy is "footnotes to Plato." For Christian theology specifically: Augustine read Plato before converting and said the Platonists had grasped almost everything except the Incarnation. The soul's immortality, the distinction between the material and the ideal, the divided line, the cave allegory — all became permanent furniture in Christian theology whether acknowledged or not.

## What He Contributed to Christian Thought
- **The immortal soul** — distinct from the body; the body is the soul's prison or vehicle; Christian anthropology borrowed and modified this
- **The Forms** — eternal, immaterial realities of which material things are copies; easily mapped onto divine ideas in the Christian God's mind
- **Eros as ascent** — the *Symposium*'s ladder of love ascending from physical beauty to Beauty itself; became the template for mystical ascent theology
- **The cave** — humans mistake shadows for reality; illumination is the goal; obvious Christian resonances
- **The Demiurge** — a craftsman-god who orders pre-existing matter; Christian theology rejected the pre-existing matter but kept much of the framing

## Shaped
- [[Origen of Alexandria]] (direct Platonist)
- [[Augustine of Hippo]] (read Plato via the Platonists before converting)
- [[Pseudo-Dionysius the Areopagite]] (Neoplatonism)
- [[Thomas Aquinas]] (via Aristotle, who was Plato's student)
- [[Meister Eckhart]], [[Gregory of Nyssa]], virtually all mystical theology
