# Sigmund Freud
*1856–1939 · Psychoanalyst · Religion as Neurosis*

> **Why he's here:** Freud's framework — conscious/unconscious, repression, projection, wish-fulfillment — became the unavoidable language of 20th century psychology, including Christian pastoral care. His specific claim (*The Future of an Illusion*, 1927) that God is a projection of the father-figure is the psychological version of atheism that theology has had to answer. Every Christian approach to counseling and spiritual direction is in implicit conversation with Freud.

## Key Concepts
- **God as wish-fulfillment** — religion projects an idealized father-figure onto the cosmos; it is the "universal obsessional neurosis of humanity"
- **The unconscious** — vast regions of the psyche below conscious awareness drive behavior; humans are not as rational as Enlightenment assumed
- **Repression** — painful truths are forced out of consciousness; they return as symptoms
- **Oedipus complex** — his theory of religion's origin in patricide (*Totem and Taboo*)

## Theological Responses
- **[[Paul Tillich]]** — used psychoanalytic categories while rejecting reductionism
- **[[Henri Nouwen]]** — deeply integrated psychology and spirituality
- **Christian counseling movement** — partly defined by its relationship to Freud (integrate? reject? modify?)
- **[[C.S. Lewis]]** — "the Freudian argument... is a double-edged sword"
