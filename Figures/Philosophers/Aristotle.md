# Aristotle
*384–322 BC · Greek Philosopher · The Philosopher*

> **Why he's here:** [[Thomas Aquinas]] called him simply "the Philosopher" — no other name needed. Aristotle's metaphysics (act/potency, substance/accident, four causes), his ethics (virtue, *eudaimonia*, the mean), his logic, and his natural philosophy became the intellectual framework of all medieval Catholic theology. The cosmological argument for God's existence is essentially Aristotelian.

## What He Contributed to Christian Thought
- **The Unmoved Mover** — the cosmological argument; something must be purely actual, moving all without being moved; Aquinas's Five Ways are this argument
- **Act and potency** — God is Pure Act (*actus purus*); creation is a mixture of actuality and potentiality; the foundation of Thomistic metaphysics
- **Substance and accident** — the philosophical framework for transubstantiation
- **Four causes** — material, formal, efficient, final; final causality (teleology) grounds natural law ethics
- **Virtue ethics** — character formed by habit; the *Nicomachean Ethics* is the basis of [[Thomas Aquinas]]'s moral theology
- **Hylomorphism** — soul as form of the body; against Platonic dualism; the body matters

## Shaped
- [[Thomas Aquinas]] (primary; called him "the Philosopher")
- All Scholastic theology
- Natural law tradition
- [[Alasdair MacIntyre]] (virtue ethics revival)
