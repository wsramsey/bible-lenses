# Charles Darwin
*1809–1882 · Naturalist · Evolution*

> **Why he's here:** No scientific discovery has forced more theological rethinking than evolution by natural selection. Every Christian tradition has had to decide what to do with Darwin: young-earth rejection, old-earth accommodation, theistic evolution, evolutionary creationism. The conversation is unresolved and ongoing.

## What He Proposed
- **Natural selection** — random variation + differential survival = the appearance of design without a designer
- **Common descent** — all life shares common ancestors, including humans and apes
- **Deep time** — the Earth is ancient; Genesis 1 cannot be a literal 7-day chronology

## Theological Responses
- **Young-earth creationism** — reject Darwin; Genesis is literal; the evidence has been misread
- **Old-earth creationism** — the Earth is old but species were specially created; evolution is false or limited
- **Theistic evolution** — God works through evolution; no conflict with faith; [[Francis Collins]], [[N.T. Wright]]
- **Evolutionary creationism (BioLogos)** — evolution is how God created; theological anthropology must be rethought
- **Intelligent Design** — evolution cannot explain irreducible complexity; design inference is scientifically valid

## His Own Faith
Raised Anglican, studied for ministry, lost faith gradually. The death of his daughter Annie (1851) was decisive. Described himself as agnostic — a word his friend T.H. Huxley coined.
