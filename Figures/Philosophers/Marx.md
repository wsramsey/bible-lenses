# Karl Marx
*1818–1883 · Philosopher · Religion as Ideology*

> **Why he's here:** [[Gustavo Gutiérrez]]'s liberation theology, [[James Cone]]'s Black theology, and every Christian engagement with economic justice since 1848 is in conversation with Marx — whether they acknowledge it or not. His analysis of ideology (ideas serve the interests of the powerful), his critique of alienated labor, and his vision of history as class struggle became the framework within which 20th century progressive Christianity operated.

## Key Concepts
- **Religion is the opium of the people** — full quote: "Religion is the sigh of the oppressed creature, the heart of a heartless world... It is the opium of the people." Not just dismissal — religion is a symptom of real suffering, and it numbs people to their oppression
- **Ideology** — ideas don't float free; they serve class interests; "the ruling ideas of every epoch are the ideas of the ruling class"
- **Historical materialism** — history is driven by material/economic forces, not ideas; the economic base determines the ideological superstructure
- **Alienation** — workers are separated from the product of their labor, from each other, and from their human potential
