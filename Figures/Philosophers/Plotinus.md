# Plotinus
*204–270 · Neoplatonist · Augustine's Bridge*

> **Why he's here:** Augustine read Plotinus's *Enneads* (in Latin translation) just before his conversion and said they showed him the existence of immaterial reality — the crucial step his Manichaean materialism had blocked. Plotinus didn't convert him to Christianity, but he made conversion philosophically possible. The entire Christian mystical tradition (Pseudo-Dionysius, Meister Eckhart, the Cloud author) is swimming in Neoplatonic water.

## Key Concepts
- **The One** — the absolute source of all reality; utterly simple, beyond being and beyond thought; the soul's ultimate destination
- **Emanation** — reality flows from the One (Nous/Intellect → Soul → Matter) like light from the sun; not creation from nothing
- **Return** — the soul's deepest desire is to return to the One; mystical union is the goal
- **The inner eye** — the soul has a faculty capable of seeing the One; not physical sight but intellectual/spiritual vision

## Shaped
- [[Augustine of Hippo]] (read him before conversion; decisive)
- [[Pseudo-Dionysius the Areopagite]] (his Christian Neoplatonism is barely modified Plotinus)
- [[Meister Eckhart]] (the "ground" language is Neoplatonic)
- All apophatic theology
