# Friedrich Nietzsche
*1844–1900 · Philosopher · "God Is Dead"*

> **Why he's here:** Every major Christian theologian of the 20th century had to respond to Nietzsche. His diagnosis of Christianity as slave morality, his proclamation of God's death, and his call for a revaluation of all values set the terms for modern theological apologetics. He is the most honest and most dangerous critic of Christianity — honest because he understood what was at stake if God dies.

## Key Concepts
- **God is dead** (*Die Gott ist tot*) — not an atheist argument but a cultural diagnosis; Western civilization built on Christian values but has killed the God those values require; the consequences haven't hit yet
- **Slave morality** — Christianity inverted Greek noble morality; weakness, humility, and suffering became virtues because the weak resented the strong; *ressentiment* as the root of Christian ethics
- **Will to power** — the fundamental drive of all life; Christianity suppresses it under guilt and self-denial
- **Eternal recurrence** — the ultimate test: if this life repeats infinitely, would you live it the same way?
- **Übermensch** — the one who creates values beyond Christian morality; the human possibility beyond nihilism
