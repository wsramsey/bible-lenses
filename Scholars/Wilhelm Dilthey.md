# Wilhelm Dilthey
*1833–1911 · Philosopher · The Human Sciences*

> **Why he's here:** He extended [[Friedrich Schleiermacher]]'s hermeneutics from textual interpretation to all the human sciences (history, sociology, psychology). His key distinction: the natural sciences *explain* (Erklären) by finding causal laws; the human sciences *understand* (Verstehen) by entering into the lived experience of historical actors. Every biblical scholar who says "we must understand this text in its historical context" is operating in Dilthey's world.

## Key Concepts
- **Verstehen** (understanding) — the distinctive method of the human sciences; not causal explanation but empathetic re-experiencing of historical life
- **Lived experience** (*Erlebnis*) — the irreducible unit of human life; what interpretation must recover
- **The hermeneutical circle** — developed from Schleiermacher; understanding moves between part and whole, between text and context
- **Historicism** — all human expressions are historically conditioned; there is no view from nowhere; this cuts both ways for theology

## Shaped By
- [[Friedrich Schleiermacher]] (direct; wrote his biography)
- Kant (epistemological framework)
- Hegel (historical consciousness)

## Influenced
- **[[Hans-Georg Gadamer]]** (built on and critiqued Dilthey)
- **[[Rudolf Bultmann]]** (historical method)
- All 20th century philosophy of social science
- Historical-critical biblical scholarship's self-understanding

## Primary Works
- *Introduction to the Human Sciences* (1883)
- *The Formation of the Historical World in the Human Sciences* (1910)
- *The Rise of Hermeneutics* (1900)
