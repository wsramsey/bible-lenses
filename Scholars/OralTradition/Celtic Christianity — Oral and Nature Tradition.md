# Celtic Christianity — Oral and Nature Tradition
*5th–12th century · Celtic / Western · Transmission: oral / poetic / natural*

> Christianity in Ireland, Scotland, Wales, and Brittany developed with distinctive characteristics: extreme asceticism (peregrinatio — wandering for God), nature as a second book of revelation, highly poetic theology, thin places (where heaven and earth nearly touch), strong women's monasticism (Brigid of Kildare). The Synod of Whitby (664) largely ended its independence by aligning England with Rome. Its theology was oral and poetic before Columba and others began writing it down.
