# Apophthegmata Patrum (Sayings of the Desert Fathers)
*4th–7th century AD (collected) · Eastern Orthodox / Desert Monasticism · Transmission: oral*

> Short, pithy stories and sayings from the Egyptian desert monks (Abbas and Ammas — fathers and mothers). Not systematic theology — wisdom transmitted through story and aphorism. 'Abba, give me a word.' The two main collections: the Alphabetical (by monk's name) and the Systematic (by topic). These sayings shaped Eastern monasticism, were translated into Latin (influencing Benedict's Rule), and continue to circulate in contemplative communities worldwide.
