# Slave Religion and the Negro Spiritual
*18th–19th century (systematized) · African-American · Transmission: oral / musical*

> Enslaved African Americans developed a distinctive theology transmitted entirely through song — the spirituals. Exodus as the master narrative (Go Down Moses). Heaven as both real destination and coded freedom. The suffering Christ as co-sufferer, not distant judge. Double meaning: 'Steal Away' (to Jesus / to freedom). W.E.B. Du Bois called the spirituals the greatest American musical contribution. Howard Thurman and James Cone both argue this tradition contains a more radical gospel than anything in white American Christianity.
