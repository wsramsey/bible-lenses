# Orthodox Divine Liturgy (Liturgy of St. John Chrysostom)
*4th century (core) — continuously prayed to present · Eastern Orthodox · Transmission: liturgical*

> The primary Eucharistic liturgy of the Orthodox church, prayed every Sunday for 1,600 years. Theology transmitted through repeated ritual participation, not primarily through text. The liturgy contains the Nicene Creed, multiple Scripture readings, epiclesis (invocation of Holy Spirit), and the Great Entrance. Alexander Schmemann's entire theology is a meditation on this liturgy. The Orthodox say: 'The church doesn't have a liturgy — the church IS a liturgy.'
