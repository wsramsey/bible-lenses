# Martin Dibelius
*1883–1947 · Protestant · Gospel Form Criticism*

> **Why he's here:** The co-founder (with [[Rudolf Bultmann]]) of Gospel Form Criticism. While Gunkel applied form criticism to the OT, Dibelius and Bultmann applied it to the Gospels. Their question: what oral forms circulated in the early church before the Gospels were written? Dibelius's answer: paradigms (pronouncement stories), tales (miracle stories), legends, exhortations — each with its own community setting.

## Key Concepts
- **Paradigm** — a brief story climaxing in a pronouncement of Jesus; designed for preaching; the most historically reliable form
- **Tale** (*Novelle*) — miracle story with detailed narrative; more legendary development; less reliable historically
- **Legend** — story about a holy person; hagiographic; least historically reliable
- **The kerygma drives the tradition** — the early church shaped the Gospel traditions to serve preaching needs; the community is the author as much as any individual
- **Against Wellhausen on the Gospels** — source criticism (Mark, Q) tells you what documents existed; form criticism tells you what existed before the documents

## Shaped By
- **[[Hermann Gunkel]]** (applied his OT method to NT)
- Liberal Protestant NT tradition

## Influenced
- **[[Rudolf Bultmann]]** (parallel development; together they define the field)
- All 20th century Gospel scholarship
- The quest for the historical Jesus (by raising the question of tradition history)
