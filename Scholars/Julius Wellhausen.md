# Julius Wellhausen
*1844–1918 · Protestant · Documentary Hypothesis*

> **Why he's here:** He gave the Documentary Hypothesis (JEDP) its classic formulation. His *Prolegomena to the History of Israel* (1878) argued that the Pentateuch was compiled from four distinct sources (Jahwist, Elohist, Deuteronomist, Priestly) and that the Priestly source was the latest — which meant Israel's elaborate sacrificial system was late, not Mosaic. This turned the Old Testament's self-understanding on its head. Every OT scholar since has had to position themselves relative to Wellhausen.

## Historical Context
19th-century German Protestant scholarship. Wellhausen was applying to the Old Testament the same historical-critical methods that had been applied to classical texts. He resigned his theology professorship because he felt his conclusions were incompatible with training pastors.

## Key Concepts
- **JEDP** — four sources: Jahwist (J), Elohist (E), Deuteronomist (D), Priestly (P); compiled in stages
- **P is late** — the Priestly source (Leviticus, Numbers, much of Exodus) is post-exilic, not Mosaic; Israel's religion evolved from simple to complex, not complex from the beginning
- **Evolutionary model** — Israel's religion developed like an organism; prophecy preceded law; the prophets were the high point
- **History of traditions** — the texts preserve traditions but also reflect the concerns of later editors

## Shaped By
- K.H. Graf (source division precursor)
- Wilhelm De Wette (Deuteronomy as Josiah's reform)
- Hegel (evolutionary model of religious development)

## Influenced
