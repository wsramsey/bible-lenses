# Billy Graham
*1918–2018 · Evangelical · Mass Evangelism*

> **Why he's here:** The defining evangelical figure of the 20th century. Graham preached in person to more people than anyone in history — an estimated 215 million in 185 countries. His integrity, his friendship with every US president from Truman to Obama, and his refusal to exploit scandal made him the most trusted religious figure in America for 50 years.

## Historical Context
Mid-20th century America. Graham emerged from Youth for Christ in the late 1940s and became a national figure after his 1949 Los Angeles crusade. His decision to integrate his crusades in the American South in the 1950s was politically costly and morally significant.

## Key Concepts
- **The kerygma** — the simple proclamation of the Gospel; death, burial, resurrection, call to repent and believe; never complicated
- **Decision theology** — salvation requires a personal decision for Christ; the "altar call" as its form
- **Biblical authority** — "The Bible says..." as rhetorical and theological anchor
- **Integrity** — the Modesto Manifesto (1948): financial transparency, no exaggeration of numbers, no criticism of local churches; these commitments defined his ministry
