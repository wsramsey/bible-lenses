# Francis de Sales
*1567–1622 · Catholic · Devout Humanism*

> **Why he's here:** The patron saint of writers and journalists, and the theologian who democratized holiness. His *Introduction to the Devout Life* was the first systematic spiritual guide written for laypeople living in the world — not monks or nuns. His core thesis: holiness is possible, even obligatory, for merchants, married people, and courtiers.

## Historical Context
Post-Tridentine France and Savoy. The Catholic Reformation was rebuilding after the Protestant rupture. Geneva — Calvin's city — was literally next door. Francis spent years as a missionary in Calvinist territory.

## Key Concepts
- **Universal call to holiness** — devotion adapts to each state of life; the soldier, the widow, the merchant each have their own devout life
- **Gentle spiritual direction** — famous for patience; "A spoonful of honey catches more flies than a barrel of vinegar"
- **Love as the heart of religion** — *Treatise on the Love of God* is his masterwork; all spiritual life flows from love
- **Salesian optimism** — against Jansenist rigorism; human nature is wounded but not destroyed
