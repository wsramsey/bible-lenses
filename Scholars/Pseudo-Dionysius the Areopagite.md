# Pseudo-Dionysius the Areopagite
*c. 500 · Christian · Patristic / Mystical / Neoplatonic*

> **Theological lean:** Apophatic/cataphatic theology, Celestial hierarchy, Mystical union

## Hermeneutical Lenses

- [[Lens - Mystical / Apophatic]] — [Mystical / Apophatic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apophatic_theology)
  *God beyond language; silence, negation, union; Gregory of Nyssa, Eckhart*
- [[Lens - Allegorical / Spiritual]] — [Allegorical / Spiritual](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allegorical_interpretation_of_the_Bible)
  *Text has deeper spiritual meaning beyond literal; Origen, Eckhart*
- [[Lens - Phenomenological]] — [Phenomenological](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenomenology_of_religion)
  *Experience of the sacred as primary; Ricoeur, Otto, Marion*

## Shaped By

- [[Plato]] — *philosophical*
  > Proclus and Neoplatonism deeply shape the Areopagite's hierarchy and apophasis

## Influenced

- [[Thomas Aquinas]] — *mystical*
  > Aquinas commented on Pseudo-Dionysius; apophatic elements in Summa
- [[Meister Eckhart]] — *mystical*
  > Eckhart's apophatic theology directly inherits the Dionysian tradition
- [[John of the Cross]] — *mystical*
  > John's dark night draws on Dionysian apophasis mediated through medieval tradition
- [[Jean-Luc Marion]] — *foundational*
  > Marion's phenomenology of givenness retrieves Dionysian apophatic theology

## Primary Works

- *The Mystical Theology*
- *The Divine Names*
- *The Celestial Hierarchy*
