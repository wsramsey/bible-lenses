# David Bentley Hart
*1965– · Eastern Orthodox · Philosophical Theology*

> **Why he's here:** The most erudite and combative Christian intellectual writing in English today. Hart ranges across patristics, philosophy, literature, music, and culture with formidable learning and sharp polemical wit. His *The Beauty of the Infinite* (2003) is the most comprehensive theological aesthetics since [[Hans Urs von Balthasar]]. His *That All Shall Be Saved* (2019) argues for universal salvation as the only coherent position given the nature of God and the human will.

## Key Concepts
- **Classical theism** — God is not a being among beings but Being Itself (*esse ipsum*); not the biggest thing in the universe but the transcendent ground of all existence; the New Atheists attack a strawman God, not the God of classical theism
- **The Beauty of the Infinite** — against [[John Milbank]]: Christian ontology is not simply peace but beauty; the Trinity is an infinite perichoretic *poesis*; creation participates in divine beauty
- **Universal salvation** (*apokatastasis*) — his most controversial position; if God is infinite good, and if freedom is defined as orientation toward the good, then hell as eternal separation from God is incoherent; all rational creatures must ultimately be reconciled; drew fierce response from Milbank, Barron, and others
- **Against the New Atheists** — his *Atheist Delusions* (2009) is the most sophisticated Christian response to Dawkins, Hitchens, et al.; argues their atheism is historically illiterate
