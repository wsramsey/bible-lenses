# G.E.M. (Elizabeth) Anscombe
*1919–2001 · Catholic · Analytic Moral Philosophy*

> **Why she's here:** Her 1958 paper "Modern Moral Philosophy" is the most important short piece in 20th century ethics — and its argument is that modern moral philosophy (both consequentialism and Kantian deontology) is incoherent because it uses moral concepts (*ought*, *duty*, *obligation*) that only make sense within a divine law framework that modern philosophy has abandoned. Her prescription: return to Aristotelian virtue ethics. [[Alasdair MacIntyre]]'s *After Virtue* is an extended development of this paper. She was also [[Ludwig Wittgenstein]]'s student, closest friend, and his literary executor.

## Key Concepts
- **"Modern Moral Philosophy"** — *ought* is a concept from divine law ethics; without God as lawgiver, it is an empty word; modern philosophy uses it as if it still meant something; this is the source of moral incoherence
- **Consequentialism refuted** — there are acts that are intrinsically wrong regardless of consequences; her example: judicial murder of an innocent person to prevent greater harm; "But the consequences will be terrible!" "Then terrible they must be"
- **Intention** — her *Intention* (1957) is the foundational text of action theory; what makes an action the action it is is the intention under which it is done
- **Absolute moral prohibitions** — some things are simply forbidden regardless of outcomes; this is her most contested claim and her most important
