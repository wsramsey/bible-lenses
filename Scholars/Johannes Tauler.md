# Johannes Tauler
*1300–1361 · Catholic · Rhineland Mysticism*

> **Why he's here:** The most practically influential of the Rhineland mystics. Where Eckhart was speculative, Tauler was pastoral. His sermons on the interior life shaped Luther, the Friends of God movement, and 500 years of Protestant devotional literature.

## Historical Context
Rhineland Germany, 14th century — the Black Death, the Great Schism, the condemnation of Meister Eckhart. Tauler preached to Dominican nuns and lay *Beguine* communities during decades of crisis, keeping Eckhart's mystical vision alive in pastoral form.

## Biography
Born in Strasbourg. Dominican friar, disciple of Meister Eckhart. Studied in Cologne, possibly under Eckhart directly. After Eckhart's condemnation, Tauler was careful to express similar ideas in more orthodox language. His sermons circulated widely in manuscript; Luther read and annotated them deeply.

## Key Concepts
- **Grunt der Seele** (ground of the soul) — the deepest center where God and soul meet; inherited from Eckhart but given more orthodox framing
- **Gelassenheit** — yielding, letting go, abandonment to God; precursor to de Caussade and quietism
- **Passive purification** — God strips the soul of all supports to create space for union
- **Practical mysticism** — union with God expressed in ordinary work and suffering, not just contemplation
