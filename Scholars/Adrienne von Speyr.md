# Adrienne von Speyr
*1902–1967 · Catholic · Mystical Theology*

> **Why she's here:** The mystical visionary whose work is inseparable from [[Hans Urs von Balthasar]]'s theology — he always said his and her work formed "two halves of a single whole." A Swiss physician who converted under Balthasar's direction in 1940, she subsequently received mystical visions and Balthasar transcribed over 60 volumes of her dictations. Her theology of Holy Saturday — Christ's descent into hell as identification with the God-forsaken — is among the most profound and controversial ideas in 20th-century theology.

## Key Concepts
- **Holy Saturday** — between cross and resurrection, Christ enters the total darkness of hell, identifying with absolute God-forsakenness; this is the depths of divine love
- **Trinitarian missions** — the Son's descent into hell is the revelation of the eternal Trinitarian relations; mission reflects procession
- **Obedience** — the fundamental Christian virtue; Christ's obedience to the Father is the pattern of all creaturely existence
- **Marian receptivity** — Mary's fiat is the paradigm of creaturely openness to God; the feminine principle in theology
