# William Alston
*1921–2009 · Episcopal · Philosophy of Religion*

> **Why he's here:** His *Perceiving God* (1991) is the most rigorous philosophical defense of religious experience as a genuine source of knowledge about God. Alston argued that mystical perception of God is epistemically on a par with sense perception — both are forms of direct experience that can ground genuine knowledge. This gave analytic philosophy of religion a sophisticated epistemological foundation for taking religious experience seriously.

## Key Concepts
- **Doxastic practices** — practices of forming beliefs from experience (sensory, memorial, introspective, mystical) that are prima facie reliable; "Christian mystical practice" is one such practice
- **Perceiving God** — the direct, non-inferential awareness of God that mystics report; epistemically similar to sensory experience; the burden of proof is on those who deny it
- **Epistemic circularity** — all doxastic practices are epistemically circular (you can only validate sense perception using sense perception); this doesn't undermine them; mystical practice is in the same position
- **Speech act theory applied to Scripture** — his later work; the illocutionary acts Scripture performs
