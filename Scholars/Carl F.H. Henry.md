# Carl F.H. Henry
*1913–2003 · Evangelical · Neo-Evangelical Theology*

> **Why he's here:** The theological architect of post-WWII neo-evangelicalism and founding editor of *Christianity Today* (1956). His *The Uneasy Conscience of Modern Fundamentalism* (1947) called fundamentalism to social engagement; his six-volume *God, Revelation and Authority* is the most comprehensive evangelical systematic theology of the 20th century.

## Historical Context
Mid-20th century America. Henry was part of the founding cohort (with [[Billy Graham]], Harold Ockenga) who sought to create a theologically serious, socially engaged evangelicalism distinct from both liberalism and fundamentalist withdrawal.

## Key Concepts
- **Propositional revelation** — God's revelation is verbal, propositional, rational; Scripture communicates truths that can be stated in sentences
- **Uneasy conscience** — fundamentalism's withdrawal from social engagement is a theological failure; the Gospel has social implications
- **Evangelical rationalism** — faith and reason are compatible; apologetics has a legitimate role
- **God, Revelation and Authority** — God speaks; revelation is cognitive; Scripture is authoritative; this is the Henrician thesis in brief
