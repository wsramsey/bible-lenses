# John Wesley
*1703–1791 · Christian · Wesleyan / Arminian*

> **Theological lean:** Prevenient grace, Entire sanctification, Practical holiness, Quadrilateral

## Hermeneutical Lenses

- [[Lens - Literal / Grammatical]] — [Literal / Grammatical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biblical_literalism)
  *Plain meaning of words in grammar and syntax; Reformation method*
- [[Lens - Practical / Pastoral]] — [Practical / Pastoral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pastoral_theology)
  *Scripture applied to soul care and ministry practice; Baxter, Peterson, Nouwen*

## Shaped By

- [[Thomas à Kempis]] — *direct*
  > Wesley read Imitation of Christ as a young man; decisive formative text
- [[Martin Luther]] — *formative*
  > Aldersgate: Luther's preface to Romans read aloud → Wesley's heart 'strangely warmed'
- [[Richard Baxter]] — *formative*
  > Wesley read and was shaped by Baxter's pastoral writings

## Influenced

- [[Andrew Murray]] — *foundational*
  > Murray's Keswick Higher Life theology is Wesleyan holiness adapted to Reformed context
- [[Oswald Chambers]] — *foundational*
  > Chambers's entire sanctification theology is Wesleyan
