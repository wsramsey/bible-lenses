# Søren Kierkegaard
*1813–1855 · Christian · Existentialist / Anti-Hegelian*

> **Theological lean:** Indirect communication. Leap of faith. The individual before God (the single one). Three stages: aesthetic, ethical, religious. Abraham's binding of Isaac as the teleological suspension of the ethical. Christendom as the enemy of Christianity.

## Historical Context
Post-Napoleonic Denmark — comfortable, established Lutheran church. Kierkegaard's attack on the church establishment (the Corsair affair, the attack on Bishop Martensen) was scandalous. Reacting against Hegel's systematic philosophy that swallowed everything into the Absolute — Kierkegaard insisted on the irreducible individual.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Son of a melancholy father who believed he had cursed God in his youth and would be punished through his children. Seven siblings — five died before their father, then the father died. Kierkegaard thought he might be next and wrote furiously. Broke off his engagement to Regine Olsen — a decision that haunted his entire output. Died at 42, possibly from childhood spinal condition.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Indirect communication — truth that can only be received, not transferred. The pseudonymous authorship (Either/Or under one name, Works of Love under another) as a hermeneutical strategy to prevent direct assimilation.
