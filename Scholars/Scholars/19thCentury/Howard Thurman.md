# Howard Thurman
*1900–1981 AD · Christian · African-American / Mystical*

> **Theological lean:** Jesus as a poor Jew under Roman occupation — speaking directly to the disinherited. Inner authority and the mystical as resources for resistance. Love as the cornerstone of non-violent resistance. The fellowship of the committed across racial lines.

## Historical Context
Jim Crow America. Thurman grew up in Daytona under segregation. A meeting with Gandhi in 1935 (Gandhi asked why more Black Americans hadn't adopted non-violence) shaped his thought. Founded the first intentionally interracial, interdenominational church in the US (Church for the Fellowship of All Peoples, San Francisco, 1944). Martin Luther King Jr. carried a copy of Thurman's Jesus and the Disinherited with him on the Montgomery bus boycott.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Grandson of a slave. Received graduate education when few schools accepted Black students. Became Dean of Marsh Chapel at Boston University — the first Black dean of a major predominantly white university chapel. Mentored MLK Jr. directly.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Reading Jesus's words through the experience of the oppressed and disinherited. The Sermon on the Mount as a survival manual for people with their backs against the wall.
