# Charles Hodge
*1797–1878 AD · Christian · Old Princeton / Calvinist / Inerrancy*

> **Theological lean:** Systematic theology as science. Biblical inerrancy as the foundation of all theology. Covenant theology. The Westminster Confession as authoritative. Common Sense Realism applied to Scripture — the Bible's facts are as certain as scientific facts.

## Historical Context
19th century America — the rise of German higher criticism threatened traditional biblical authority. Hodge built Princeton Seminary into the fortress of confessional Reformed orthodoxy against liberal theology. He reportedly boasted that no new idea had ever originated at Princeton Seminary — meaning Old Princeton was faithful to the Reformed tradition, not innovative.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Taught at Princeton for 56 years (1820-1878). Estimated he had 3,000 students — more than any other American theologian. Debated Darwin with a critique of evolution that was sophisticated for its day. His Systematic Theology (1872-73) was the standard Reformed systematic for generations.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Inductive — Scripture is a mine of facts; theology systematizes the facts as natural science systematizes physical facts. The Common Sense Realist method means the plain meaning of Scripture is accessible to all.
