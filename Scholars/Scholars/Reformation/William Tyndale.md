# William Tyndale
*1494–1536 AD · Christian · Biblical Humanism / Martyred Translator*

> **Theological lean:** Scripture in English for every plowboy. Justification by faith. The literal sense of Scripture as primary. Covenant theology (before Calvin formalized it). Fierce anti-clericalism.

## Historical Context
England under Henry VIII — possessing an English Bible was illegal. Tyndale translated in secret and smuggled copies into England. He was pursued across Europe by Henry's agents for years before being betrayed, strangled, and burned near Brussels in 1536. His dying prayer: 'Lord, open the King of England's eyes.' Within 3 years, Henry VIII authorized an English Bible based largely on Tyndale's translation.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Priest, linguist, exile. Mastered 7 languages. Went to Luther's Wittenberg, then translated from Erasmus's Greek NT and the Hebrew OT. Lived in hiding, working with sympathetic merchants, smuggling printed Testaments into England in bales of cloth. Betrayed by Henry Phillips, arrested 1535, executed October 6, 1536.

## Hermeneutical Approach
The grammatical-literal sense of Hebrew and Greek as the primary meaning. His English prose shaped the King James Bible — scholars estimate 83% of the KJV NT is Tyndale's words.
