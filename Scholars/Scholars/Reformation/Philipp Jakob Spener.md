# Philipp Jakob Spener
*1635–1705 AD · Christian · Pietism / Collegium Pietatis*

> **Theological lean:** The heart over the head — genuine conversion and holy living over doctrinal orthodoxy. Small group Bible study (collegia pietatis) as the instrument of renewal. The priesthood of all believers taken seriously. Hope for better times in the church.

## Historical Context
Post-Thirty Years' War Germany (1618-48) — the war had devastated Germany physically and spiritually. Lutheran Orthodoxy had become dry, doctrinal, defensive. Spener's *Pia Desideria* (1675) was a bombshell — calling the church to genuine spiritual renewal through small groups, personal Bible study, and practical holiness.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Lutheran pastor in Frankfurt where he started the first collegia pietatis — weekly small group Bible study and prayer meetings in his home. Eventually court preacher in Dresden and Berlin. His movement was controversial — accused of separatism and fanaticism. His spiritual godson was August Hermann Francke, who built the Pietist social institutions.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Scripture read for transformation, not just information. The devotional reading tradition alongside academic exegesis.
