# Thomas Cranmer
*1489–1556 AD · Christian · Reformed Anglican / Liturgical*

> **Theological lean:** Justification by faith (Calvinist). Eucharistic presence as spiritual, not corporal (against both Catholic transubstantiation and Lutheran consubstantiation). Scripture as supreme authority. Vernacular liturgy for all.

## Historical Context
The English Reformation under Henry VIII, Edward VI, and Mary I. Cranmer navigated the volatile Tudor court — serving Henry VIII despite theological convictions, advancing Reformation under the boy-king Edward VI, then arrested when Catholic Mary I came to the throne. Under interrogation he recanted his Protestant faith, then publicly recanted his recantation and thrust his right hand (that had signed the recantation) into the fire first.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Archbishop of Canterbury. Architect of the English Reformation's theological and liturgical character. His story ends tragically and heroically — burned at the stake by Mary I in 1556. His gesture at the stake — burning the hand that had signed his recantation first — became a defining image of Protestant martyrdom.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Scripture interpreted through the Fathers and creeds. The liturgy as a teaching instrument — what you pray is what you believe (lex orandi lex credendi). The Book of Common Prayer as theology for ordinary people.
