# Jacob Arminius
*1560–1609 AD · Christian · Arminian / Conditional Election*

> **Theological lean:** God's election is conditional on foreseen faith, not unconditional. Christ died for all, not the elect alone. Grace is resistible. Humans have genuine free will to accept or reject salvation. Apostasy is possible.

## Historical Context
The Dutch Reformed church in the aftermath of the Spanish war of independence. Arminius was a Reformed pastor and professor asked to refute the anti-Calvinist Dirck Coornhert — and found Coornhert's arguments more persuasive than he'd expected. His modifications to Calvinism sparked a controversy resolved (against him) at the Synod of Dordt (1618-19) three years after his death.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born during the Spanish massacre of Oudewater (his family killed, he survived). Studied in Geneva under Calvin's successor Beza. Became a pastor in Amsterdam, then professor at Leiden. The controversy over his views on predestination consumed his last years. Died before Dordt — which condemned his views posthumously.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Scripture read with attention to the whole counsel of God — texts about God's universal salvific will must balance texts about election. Neither can be made to disappear.
