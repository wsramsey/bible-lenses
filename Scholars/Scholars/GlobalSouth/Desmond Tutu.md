# Desmond Tutu
*1931–2021 AD · Christian · Ubuntu Theology / Liberation*

> **Theological lean:** Ubuntu — I am because we are. The Trinity as the model of community. Apartheid as a theological heresy, not merely a political evil. Restorative justice (Truth and Reconciliation Commission). The goodness of creation and human dignity as the foundation of human rights.

## Historical Context
South African apartheid (1948-1994). Tutu became the most prominent voice opposing apartheid on theological grounds from within the country. Nobel Peace Prize 1984. Chaired the Truth and Reconciliation Commission (1995-1998) — the model for transitional justice worldwide. His theology was inseparable from his politics.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Son of a schoolteacher. Trained as a teacher, became a priest when apartheid made teaching intolerable. Became Archbishop of Cape Town in 1986. His strategy of international sanctions against South Africa was controversial — some thought it would harm the people it aimed to help. Post-apartheid, wrestled honestly with the limits of reconciliation without justice.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Reading Scripture from the underside — the God of the Exodus takes sides with the oppressed. Ubuntu as a hermeneutical lens: the community's flourishing, not individual salvation, is the goal.
