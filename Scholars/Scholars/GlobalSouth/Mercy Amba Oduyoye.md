# Mercy Amba Oduyoye
*1934– AD · Christian · African Womanist Theology*

> **Theological lean:** African women's experience as a theological source. The circle of concerned African women. Beads and strands — African women's theology as jewelry: each bead distinct but connected. Critique of patriarchy in both African culture and imported Christianity.

## Historical Context
Post-independence Africa and the Women's Movement. Oduyoye was the first African woman to gain international prominence as a systematic theologian. Founded the Circle of Concerned African Women Theologians (1989) — a network that has produced hundreds of African women theologians.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born Ghana. Father was a Methodist minister. Worked at WCC in Geneva, taught at Harvard and Princeton. Married the Nigerian theologian Modupe Oduyoye. Her infertility (in a culture where a woman's value was tied to childbearing) became a theological resource — she wrote about it with remarkable honesty.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Women's experience as hermeneutical lens. The margins of the margins — African women who are marginalized by colonialism, patriarchy, and poverty.
