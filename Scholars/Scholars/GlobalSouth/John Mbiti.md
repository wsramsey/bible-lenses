# John Mbiti
*1931–2019 AD · Christian · African Theology / Comparative Religion*

> **Theological lean:** African religions contain genuine knowledge of God (the High God of African traditions is the same as the God of Scripture). Time in African thought as primarily past-oriented ('Zamani' — the past) vs Western future orientation. African theology as legitimate Christian theology, not merely contextual adaptation.

## Historical Context
Post-independence Africa — the question of whether Christianity was a colonizing Western religion or could be genuinely African was urgent. Mbiti argued African traditional religion was a preparatio evangelica (preparation for the Gospel), paralleling Justin Martyr's claim about Greek philosophy.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Grew up in Kenya. PhD from Cambridge (on New Testament eschatology in African thought). Led the WCC's Ecumenical Institute at Bossey, Switzerland. Returned to Kenya, became an Anglican priest late in life. The founding figure of academic African theology.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Cross-cultural hermeneutics — reading Scripture in dialogue with African traditional concepts of God, time, community, and death.
