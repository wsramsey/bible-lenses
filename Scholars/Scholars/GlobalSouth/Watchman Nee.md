# Watchman Nee
*1903–1972 AD · Christian · Chinese House Church / Mystical*

> **Theological lean:** The spiritual man (trichotomy: spirit, soul, body). The church as local expression of the Body of Christ — no denominations, only the church in a locality. The normal Christian life as radical union with Christ. Suffering as the path of the cross.

## Historical Context
China in revolution — nationalist China, then Communist China. Watchman Nee (Ni Tuoseng) built a network of house churches across China. When the Communists took power in 1949, foreign missionaries were expelled but the indigenous Chinese church Nee had built continued. He was arrested in 1952, imprisoned for 20 years, died in prison. His books, smuggled out, became enormously influential in Western evangelical circles.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born Fuzhou. Converted at 17. Began holding meetings in his home — the beginning of the house church movement he would build across China. Arrested 1952, charged with 'immorality and other crimes' (probably fabricated). Died in labor camp 1972. His legacy: the house church movement in China that survived Maoism.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Typological — OT characters and events as types of spiritual realities. The spiritual sense accessed through the human spirit, not the intellectual soul.
