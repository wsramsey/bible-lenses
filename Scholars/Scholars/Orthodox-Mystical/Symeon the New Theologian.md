# Symeon the New Theologian
*949–1022 AD · Christian · Byzantine Mysticism / Charismatic*

> **Theological lean:** Direct personal experience of God — not as rare mystical exception but as the normal Christian life. The vision of God as light. Weeping (penthos) as the gift of the Spirit. The person who has not experienced the Holy Spirit cannot be a priest or bishop — a radical claim.

## Historical Context
Byzantine Constantinople — a highly formalized, institutionalized Christianity. Symeon insisted that direct experiential knowledge of God was not reserved for saints of the past but available to anyone who truly sought it. This was controversial — he was exiled from Constantinople for venerating his own spiritual father as a saint without church authorization.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Abandoned a career at the imperial court to become a monk. Experienced a vision of divine light as a young man that defined his whole life. Eventually became abbot of St. Mamas monastery in Constantinople. Exiled in 1009 for the unauthorized veneration controversy. Died in exile, venerated as a saint almost immediately.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Experiential — Scripture interpreted through the lens of the soul's direct encounter with God. The theologian is not the one who knows about God but the one who prays and encounters God.
