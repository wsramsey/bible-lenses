# Gregory Palamas
*1296–1359 AD · Christian · Hesychast / Palamite Theology*

> **Theological lean:** Essence/energies distinction — God's essence is absolutely unknowable; God's energies are the ways God genuinely acts in the world and can be experienced. The uncreated light (the light of Tabor) is not a created symbol but God's genuine self-communication. Deification (theosis) through participation in the divine energies.

## Historical Context
The Hesychast controversy (1337-1351) — Barlaam of Calabria attacked the hesychast monks' claim to see the uncreated light of God. Palamas defended them. The controversy was settled at Constantinople in 1351 in Palamas's favor. This is the Orthodox Reformation — a defining moment that shaped Orthodox theology as decisively as Nicaea.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Monk on Mount Athos (the monastic republic on a Greek peninsula). Became Archbishop of Thessaloniki. Canonized 1368, just 9 years after his death. The second Sunday of Great Lent is dedicated to him in the Orthodox church — placing him alongside Orthodoxy itself as a gift.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Apophatic — God's essence beyond all language and thought. But cataphatic — God's energies genuinely communicate God. Scripture and Tradition as the vehicle of the energies.
