# Evagrius Ponticus
*345–399 AD · Christian · Desert Father / Contemplative Theology*

> **Theological lean:** Eight logismoi (thoughts/spirits) that disturb the soul (precursor to seven deadly sins — John Cassian transmitted this to the West). Apatheia as the goal (not passionlessness but mastery of passions). Theologia as direct contemplation of the Trinity beyond all images. Praktike (active life) → Physike (contemplation of nature) → Theologia.

## Historical Context
The Egyptian desert of the 4th century — a vast movement of thousands fleeing the world to find God. Evagrius was one of the most brilliant men there, having been a distinguished theologian in Constantinople before fleeing a romantic entanglement. He systematized the Desert Fathers' practical wisdom into a psychology of the soul that still shapes how Christians think about temptation and prayer.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Student of Basil and Gregory of Nazianzus. Became archdeacon in Constantinople under Gregory. Fled to Jerusalem, then Egypt after an affair. The desert transformed him. Condemned posthumously at the Fifth Ecumenical Council (553) for Origenist positions — his works survived disguised under other names.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Allegorical reading in service of contemplative ascent. Scripture's literal meaning gives way to spiritual meaning as the soul ascends from praktike to theologia.
