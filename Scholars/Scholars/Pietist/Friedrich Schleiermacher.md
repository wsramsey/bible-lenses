# Friedrich Schleiermacher
*1768–1834 AD · Christian · Liberal Protestant / Father of Modern Theology*

> **Theological lean:** Religion is the feeling of absolute dependence (schlechthinnige Abhängigkeit). Theology must begin with religious experience, not Scripture or doctrine. Christology based on Jesus's perfect God-consciousness. Hermeneutics as a universal science of interpretation.

## Historical Context
Post-Enlightenment Germany. The Romantic movement was reclaiming feeling and imagination against Enlightenment rationalism. Schleiermacher was a Romantic theologian — but also a rigorous academic. His audience was the educated despisers of religion (the title of his first book) — people who had given up Christianity as intellectually untenable. He tried to show that the heart of religion was not doctrine or morality but experience.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Son of a Calvinist minister, educated in Moravian schools (deep formation, later complicated). Became Reformed preacher in Berlin, professor at Halle, then founding professor at the new University of Berlin (1810). Friend of the Romantic circle (Schlegel, Novalis). His Reden über die Religion (Speeches on Religion, 1799) made him famous.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Founded modern hermeneutics as a discipline — the general theory of interpretation. The hermeneutical circle: whole and part must be read in light of each other. Interpretation requires understanding the author's psychological and historical situation. Ricoeur, Gadamer, and all subsequent hermeneutics begin here.
