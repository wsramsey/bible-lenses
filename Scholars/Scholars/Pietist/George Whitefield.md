# George Whitefield
*1714–1770 AD · Christian · Calvinist Methodist / Great Awakening*

> **Theological lean:** Calvinist on grace (broke with Wesley over this). New birth as essential conversion experience. Open-air preaching to masses. Itinerant evangelism crossing denominational lines. God's sovereignty in salvation.

## Historical Context
The Great Awakening — the first mass revival movement in the English-speaking world. Whitefield preached to crowds of 30,000 in the open air before microphones existed (Franklin measured his voice could reach 30,000). He crossed the Atlantic 13 times. His preaching style — theatrical, emotional, dramatic — invented modern revivalism.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born Gloucester, England in a tavern (his mother ran the Bell Inn). Oxford (Holy Club with the Wesleys). Ordained at 21. His first sermon caused several to 'go mad.' Broke with Wesley over Calvinist/Arminian divide. Became patron of Bethesda orphanage in Georgia. Benjamin Franklin (a skeptic) was his close friend and published his journals.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Extemporaneous, dramatic preaching from the text. The New Birth as the interpretive center — all Scripture points to the necessity of genuine conversion.
