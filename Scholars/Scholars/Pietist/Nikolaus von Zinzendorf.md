# Nikolaus von Zinzendorf
*1700–1760 AD · Christian · Pietist / Ecumenical / Missions*

> **Theological lean:** Christ-centered piety. The wounds of Christ as the focus of devotion (sometimes excessive — the Sichtungszeit excesses). Ecumenism across Protestant denominations. Global missions before the modern missions movement. The community (Gemeine) as the form of Christian life.

## Historical Context
The Herrnhut community — refugees from Bohemia (Hussite descendants) settled on Zinzendorf's estate. The Pentecost of Herrnhut (August 13, 1727): a remarkable outpouring that launched the first Protestant missionary movement. Moravians sent missionaries to the Caribbean, Africa, and the Americas before anyone else — including to the enslaved people of the Caribbean in 1754 (direct connection to abolitionism).

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Count, nobleman, Pietist. Tutored by Spener's associate Francke. Had a transformative encounter with a Dürer painting of Christ's suffering at age 19. Welcomed Hussite refugees onto his estate. The Herrnhut community's 100-year prayer meeting (24/7 intercessory prayer, sustained for 100 years) became legend in evangelical circles.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Christocentric devotional reading — every text illuminates some aspect of Christ's person and saving work.
