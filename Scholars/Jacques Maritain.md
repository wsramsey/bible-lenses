# Jacques Maritain
*1882–1973 · Catholic · Neo-Thomism*

> **Why he's here:** The 20th century's greatest Thomistic philosopher and the intellectual architect of Catholic engagement with modern democracy. His *Integral Humanism* argued for a "new Christendom" that respected human dignity and pluralism without surrendering transcendence. Influenced the UN Declaration of Human Rights and the Second Vatican Council.

## Historical Context
20th-century France. Maritain converted from liberal Protestantism to Catholicism under the influence of Léon Bloy. Became a professor, fled to the US during WWII, served as French ambassador to the Holy See.

## Key Concepts
- **Integral humanism** — neither secular humanism (man without God) nor theocratic humanism (God without human freedom) but both together
- **Personalism** — the human person (not the individual) as the fundamental political and moral unit
- **Natural law** — moral norms accessible to reason, the basis for universal human rights
- **Distinction of planes** — philosophy and theology have distinct but compatible methods
