# Bart Ehrman
*1955– · Post-Christian · Textual Criticism*

> **Why he's here:** The vault is intellectually honest only if it includes the voices that challenge the tradition, not just those that defend it. Ehrman is the most widely read NT critic of the 21st century. Every scholar in this vault who defends the resurrection, inerrancy, or orthodox Christology is in direct conversation with this tradition.

## Historical Context
Late 20th–21st century America. The internet made academic biblical criticism accessible to mass audiences for the first time. Ehrman's popular books sold millions and introduced skeptical scholarship to people who had never encountered it — including many who subsequently left Christianity. He represents the collision between rigorous academic method and popular religious faith.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born Kansas. Evangelical convert in high school — attended Moody Bible Institute (fundamentalist) and Wheaton College (evangelical). Went to Princeton Theological Seminary to study with Bruce Metzger, the greatest NT textual critic of the 20th century. Hundreds of thousands of manuscript variants led him to question inerrancy. The problem of suffering completed his move to agnosticism. His trajectory (evangelical → agnostic through rigorous scholarship) mirrors Augustine's in reverse.

## Written Where & Why
*Misquoting Jesus* (2005) — his breakthrough popular book on textual variants, written to bring academic textual criticism to general readers. All subsequent books follow the same pattern: academic scholarship translated for a popular audience that deserves to know what scholars actually think.

## Contemporaries
- [[N.T. Wright]] — directly debates Ehrman on resurrection; the two most widely read NT scholars of the era arguing opposite conclusions
- [[Richard Bauckham]] — *Jesus and the Eyewitnesses* written partly in response to this skeptical tradition
- [[E.P. Sanders]] — Ehrman inherits Sanders's apocalyptic Jesus framework
- [[Bruce Metzger]] — Princeton mentor; master and student reached opposite conclusions from the same textual data

## Writing Style
Accessible, narrative-driven, self-implicating. Always tells the story of his own intellectual journey alongside the scholarship. Enormously effective popular communicator — probably the main reason millions of ordinary Americans know what textual criticism is.
