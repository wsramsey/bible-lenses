# John Milbank
*1952– · Anglican · Radical Orthodoxy*

> **Why he's here:** The founder and lead thinker of Radical Orthodoxy — the movement that claims theology has surrendered too much to secular disciplines (sociology, philosophy, economics) and must reclaim its position as the queen of sciences. His *Theology and Social Theory* (1990) argued that secular social theory is not neutral but embodies a particular (Gnostic, nihilist) ontology that theology must expose and replace with a Christian ontology of peace.

## Key Concepts
- **Theology and Social Theory** — secular sociology (Durkheim, Weber, Marx) is not neutral science but a rival theology; it must be unmasked as such
- **Ontology of peace** — creation is gift; the fundamental structure of reality is peaceful donation, not violent conflict; [[Augustine of Hippo]]'s *City of God* vs. the earthly city
- **Ontology of violence** — secular thought assumes an original violence (Hobbes, Nietzsche, Derrida); Christianity offers the alternative
- **Theology as the queen of sciences** — theology doesn't borrow frameworks from secular disciplines; it provides the framework within which all other disciplines operate
- **Participation** — all finite reality participates in the infinite; the Neoplatonic-Thomistic alternative to modern immanence
