# Jacques Derrida
*1930–2004 · Philosopher · Deconstruction*

> **Why he's here:** Deconstruction is the philosophical position that every biblical hermeneutics must either engage or consciously reject. Derrida's claim that texts always defer meaning (*différance*), that the "transcendental signified" (a fixed meaning outside language) is impossible, and that every text contains the seeds of its own undoing — these are direct challenges to the claim that Scripture has a determinate, authoritative meaning. Every post-1970 evangelical hermeneutics is in part a response to Derrida.

## Historical Context
Post-war France. Derrida was an Algerian Jew educated in France. His 1966 lecture "Structure, Sign and Play" at Johns Hopkins introduced deconstruction to America. Spent his career at the École Normale Supérieure and UC Irvine.

## Key Concepts
- **Différance** — meaning is always deferred (never fully present) and differentiated (defined by contrast with other terms); there is no final meaning
- **The transcendental signified** — the idea that there is a fixed, external meaning that language points to; Derrida argues this is impossible; "there is nothing outside the text"
- **Deconstruction** — not destruction; the demonstration that texts undermine their own claims to stable meaning; every text contains marginal voices that destabilize the center
- **Logocentrism** — Western thought privileges speech over writing, presence over absence, the same over the different; all these hierarchies can be deconstructed
