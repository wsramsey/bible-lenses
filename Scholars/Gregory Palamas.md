# Gregory Palamas
*1296–1359 · Eastern Orthodox · Hesychasm*

> **Why he's here:** The theologian who gave hesychasm its philosophical defense and became the normative interpreter of Orthodox mystical theology. His distinction between God's essence (unknowable, unreachable) and God's energies (the divine life in which humans genuinely participate) resolves the central paradox of mysticism: how can finite creatures share in infinite God?

## Historical Context
14th-century Byzantium — the Hesychast Controversy. The monks of Mount Athos claimed they could see the uncreated divine light of Tabor through hesychast prayer. The humanist scholar Barlaam of Calabria attacked this as absurd — if God is unknowable, you can't see him with bodily eyes. Palamas defended the monks and won at three councils (1341, 1347, 1351). The victory defined Orthodox theology permanently.

## Biography
Born to a noble Constantinople family. Educated at the imperial court, then withdrew to Mount Athos at age 20. Spent years as hesychast monk. Drawn into the controversy reluctantly. Eventually became Archbishop of Thessalonica. Canonized 1368, just 9 years after death.

## Key Concepts
- **Essence-Energies distinction** — God's essence is absolutely unknowable and unreachable; God's energies are the divine life as God turns toward creation; participation in the energies is real participation in God
- **Uncreated light** — the light of Tabor is not created light but divine energy; the hesychasts genuinely see God
- **Theosis** — deification through participation in divine energies; the goal of all Christian life
- **Hesychia** — stillness; the interior silence in which the heart opens to the divine light
