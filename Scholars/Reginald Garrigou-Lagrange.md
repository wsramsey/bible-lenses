# Reginald Garrigou-Lagrange
*1877–1964 · Catholic · Strict Thomism*

> **Why he's here:** The "Sacred Monster of Thomism" — the last great neo-Scholastic before Vatican II. His two-volume *The Three Ages of the Interior Life* is the most comprehensive Catholic mystical theology of the 20th century, synthesizing Aquinas, John of the Cross, and Teresa into a rigorous systematic account of contemplative development. He was [[Karol Wojtyła]]'s doctoral director.

## Key Concepts
- **Three ages of the interior life** — purgative, illuminative, unitive; the classical schema systematically developed
- **Infused contemplation** — mystical prayer is not extraordinary but the normal development of sanctifying grace; available to all, achieved by few
- **Dark night as normal** — John of the Cross's dark nights are the standard purification, not exceptional mystical phenomena
- **Strict Thomism** — against any attempt to "update" or soften Aquinas; predestination, grace, causality all held rigorously
