# Dorothy Day
*1897–1980 · Catholic · Catholic Worker Movement*

> **Why she's here:** The most important American Catholic of the 20th century outside the hierarchy. Day co-founded the Catholic Worker movement in 1933 — newspapers, houses of hospitality, farms — and lived among the poor for 50 years while writing and speaking against war, capitalism, and the comfortable church. Her cause for canonization is open.

## Historical Context
Depression-era New York. Day had lived through bohemian Greenwich Village radicalism, an abortion, a common-law marriage, and a profound conversion before founding the Catholic Worker with Peter Maurin in 1933. She was under FBI surveillance for decades.

## Key Concepts
- **The poor as Christ** — Matthew 25 taken absolutely literally; every hungry, homeless, imprisoned person is Christ
- **Works of mercy** — the primary Christian response to the social order; not programs but personal encounter
- **Voluntary poverty** — chosen solidarity with the poor, not romantic but concrete: sleep in the same beds, eat the same food
- **Pacifism** — absolute; she opposed WWII, the Cold War, nuclear weapons; often alone in the Catholic world
