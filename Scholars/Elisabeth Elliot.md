# Elisabeth Elliot
*1926–2015 · Evangelical · Suffering & Obedience*

> **Why she's here:** The most influential evangelical woman writer of the 20th century. Her husband Jim Elliot was killed by the Aucas (Huaorani) of Ecuador in 1956; she then returned to live among them as a missionary. Her books on suffering, obedience, and femininity shaped two generations of evangelical women, and her radio program *Gateway to Joy* ran for 13 years.

## Key Concepts
- **Obedience** — "Do the next thing"; the fundamental Christian practice is not feeling but obedience to the known will of God
- **Suffering as gift** — not sentimentalized; suffering is the primary means of conformity to Christ
- **Femininity** — complementarian; her *Let Me Be a Woman* shaped evangelical gender theology for a generation
- **Jim Elliot's journals** — "He is no fool who gives what he cannot keep to gain what he cannot lose"; she edited and published his journals, making them one of the most influential mission documents of the era
