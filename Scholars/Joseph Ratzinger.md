# Joseph Ratzinger (Benedict XVI)
*1927–2022 · Catholic · Augustinian Theology*

> **Why he's here:** The rare theologian-pope. Ratzinger was a *peritus* at Vatican II, a progressive at 30, a conservative by 50, and the most theologically literate head of the Catholic Church since perhaps Leo the Great. His *Jesus of Nazareth* trilogy is serious Christological scholarship; his *Introduction to Christianity* remains the finest accessible summary of Catholic faith in the 20th century.

## Historical Context
Postwar Bavaria. Formed by the youth church resistance to Nazism. Became professor, then Archbishop of Munich, then Prefect of the Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith under John Paul II (1981–2005), then Pope (2005–2013). First pope to resign in 600 years.

## Key Concepts
- **Logos** — creation has a rational structure that corresponds to the divine Word; reason and faith are not opposed
- **Hermeneutic of continuity** — Vatican II must be read in continuity with Tradition, not as a rupture; against "the spirit of Vatican II"
- **Liturgy as cosmic event** — the Mass is not performance but participation in the eternal liturgy of heaven; his *The Spirit of the Liturgy* (2000) echoes Guardini
- **Relativism** — the "dictatorship of relativism" is his diagnosis of the modern crisis; truth is not negotiable
