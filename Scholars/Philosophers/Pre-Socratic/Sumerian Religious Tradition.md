# Sumerian Religious Tradition
*c. 4000–2000 BC · Ancient Near Eastern Religion · Temple / Cosmogonic*

> **Theological lean:** Divine assembly (An, Enlil, Enki), creation from chaos, human beings created to serve gods, flood narrative, descent to the underworld, temple as cosmic axis

## Historical Context
The world's first literate civilization. Developed writing, law codes (Ur-Nammu, later Hammurabi), and sophisticated religious literature. Israel emerged from a cultural world thoroughly shaped by Mesopotamian thought — the Genesis narratives are in direct dialogue with Sumerian and Babylonian myths.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Not an individual but a civilization. The Sumerians produced the world's oldest written literature including the Epic of Gilgamesh (with its flood narrative), hymns to Inanna, and creation myths that parallel Genesis.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Mythological cosmogony — origins explain present reality. Temple ritual re-enacts cosmic creation and maintains world order.
