# Zeno of Citium
*334–262 BC · Greek Philosophy · Early Stoicism / Logos theology*

> **Theological lean:** Logos as rational principle pervading all reality. Natural law. Virtue as the only good. Providence. The sage who follows reason participates in the divine Logos.

## Historical Context
Post-Alexander's conquests had created a cosmopolitan world where Greek philosophy needed to speak to everyone, not just Athenian citizens. Stoicism was the philosophy of the Hellenistic and Roman world — the intellectual air Paul breathed in Tarsus, the framework his audience at Athens (Acts 17) would recognize.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born in Citium, Cyprus (possibly Phoenician descent). Shipwrecked on way to Athens, discovered Socrates through a bookseller, stayed. Founded his school in the Stoa Poikile (Painted Porch — hence 'Stoic'). His works are lost — known through later Stoics and critics.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Allegorical interpretation of Homer and Hesiod — the myths conceal philosophical truths about the Logos. This Stoic allegorical method was adopted by Philo for the Torah and by Clement/Origen for Scripture.
