# Egyptian Religious Tradition (Pharaonic)
*c. 3100–30 BC · Ancient Egyptian Religion · Cosmic / Royal theology*

> **Theological lean:** Ma'at (cosmic order/justice), afterlife judgment, resurrection (Osiris), solar theology (Ra/Aten), divine kingship, creation through speech (Logos parallel)

## Historical Context
Egypt was Israel's defining formative context — slavery, exodus, wilderness. The Wisdom literature of Egypt (Instruction of Amenemope) has direct parallels with Proverbs 22-24. The Aten revolution under Akhenaten (c. 1353 BC) is the world's first recorded monotheism, predating Moses. Origen, Clement of Alexandria, Philo — all Alexandrian — were shaped by Egyptian intellectual culture.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
A 3,000-year civilization that shaped everything around it. The Book of the Dead, Pyramid Texts, and Coffin Texts represent sophisticated theological traditions about judgment, afterlife, and resurrection that predate Christianity by millennia.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Ritual re-enactment of cosmic myths maintains world order. Creation through divine speech (the Memphite Theology — Ptah creates through his word) is a direct parallel to John 1:1.
