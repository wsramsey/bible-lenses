# Socrates
*470–399 BC · Greek Philosophy · Dialectical / Ethical*

> **Theological lean:** The unexamined life is not worth living. Knowledge as virtue. The daimonion (divine inner voice). Death as the soul's release. Died for truth — the pagan martyr who haunts Christian apologetics.

## Historical Context
Athenian democracy at its height, then collapse after the Peloponnesian War. Executed 399 BC for corrupting youth and impiety — died rather than renounce his philosophical mission. Justin Martyr calls him a 'Christian before Christ.' His death haunts Western thought as the paradigmatic case of dying for truth.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Wrote nothing. Known entirely through Plato's dialogues. Claimed to know only that he knew nothing (ironic wisdom). Son of a sculptor, married to Xanthippe, served in military. His trial and death (hemlock, surrounded by disciples) became the template for all subsequent martyrdom narratives including Jesus.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Dialectical — truth emerges through rigorous questioning, not assertion. The midwife metaphor: the philosopher helps others give birth to truth already within them. No fixed doctrine — only the method.
