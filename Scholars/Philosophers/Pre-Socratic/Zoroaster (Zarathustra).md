# Zoroaster (Zarathustra)
*c. 1500–600 BC (disputed) · Zoroastrianism · Proto-dualist / Prophetic*

> **Theological lean:** Cosmic dualism (Ahura Mazda vs Angra Mainyu), eschatology, resurrection, final judgment, heaven/hell, angels and demons

## Historical Context
Zoroastrianism was the dominant religion of the Persian Empire, which conquered Babylon in 539 BC and freed the Jewish exiles. The returning Jews had spent decades immersed in Zoroastrian culture. Scholars debate how much Jewish apocalypticism, angelology, resurrection doctrine, and cosmic dualism were shaped by this encounter.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Legendary prophet and religious reformer. Dates are contested — some place him around 1500 BC, others around 600 BC. Received visions of Ahura Mazda (the Wise Lord). His Gathas (hymns) are among the oldest religious texts still in liturgical use.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Prophetic revelation from Ahura Mazda. Cosmic moral struggle between truth (asha) and lie (druj) as the interpretive framework for all existence.
