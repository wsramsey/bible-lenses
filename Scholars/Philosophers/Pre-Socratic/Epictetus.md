# Epictetus
*50–135 AD · Greek Philosophy · Late Stoicism / Practical Ethics*

> **Theological lean:** Radical distinction between what is 'up to us' (prohairesis — will, judgment, desire) and what is not. God as father of all. Inner freedom regardless of external circumstances. Virtue through radical acceptance.

## Historical Context
Born a slave in Nero's Rome. Freed, became the most influential philosopher of his era. Taught that a slave could be freer than an emperor — radical in Rome's slave society. His student Arrian wrote down his Discourses. The emperor Marcus Aurelius was shaped by him. Paul's 'I have learned, in whatsoever state I am, therewith to be content' (Phil 4:11) is almost verbatim Epictetan.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born enslaved in Hierapolis. His master reportedly broke his leg to test his equanimity — Epictetus reportedly said calmly 'I told you that would break.' Freed, settled in Rome, expelled with other philosophers by Domitian (89 AD), settled in Nicopolis. Lived simply. Never wrote — his student Arrian recorded his lectures.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Practical ethics through clear distinction between what we control and what we don't. Philosophy as therapy for the soul, not academic exercise.
