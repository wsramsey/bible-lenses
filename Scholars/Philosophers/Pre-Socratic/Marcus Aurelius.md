# Marcus Aurelius
*121–180 AD · Greek Philosophy · Roman Stoicism / Meditations*

> **Theological lean:** Logos as rational world-soul. Duty, service, impermanence. The emperor who meditated on his own insignificance. God as reason pervading all. Death as natural dissolution.

## Historical Context
Roman Emperor 161-180 AD — the most powerful man in the world, writing private meditations on humility and mortality. Persecuted Christians (the Lyons martyrs 177 AD occurred under him, though his direct involvement is debated). The Meditations were never intended for publication — a private spiritual journal.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Adopted by Emperor Antoninus Pius. Spent much of his reign at war on the Danube frontier. Student of Epictetus's student Junius Rusticus. The Meditations written in Greek during military campaigns — a man with ultimate worldly power practicing radical inner detachment.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Daily philosophical practice — returning to principles against the resistance of circumstance. The Meditations as spiritual exercises (Pierre Hadot's reading influenced Dallas Willard's concept of spiritual disciplines).
