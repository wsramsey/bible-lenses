# Saadia Gaon
*882–942 AD · Jewish · Jewish Rationalist Philosophy*

> **Theological lean:** First systematic Jewish philosopher. Reason and revelation are compatible. The Torah's commandments are rational. Rational defense of creation ex nihilo. Rejected Karaism. The soul's immortality.

## Historical Context
The Geonic period — Jewish life centered in the academies (yeshivot) of Babylonia under Islamic rule. The Karaite challenge (Scripture alone, rejecting rabbinic tradition) required a systematic rational defense of rabbinic Judaism. Saadia's work is the Jewish Augustinian synthesis — philosophy in service of traditional faith.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born Egypt, became Gaon (head) of the Sura Academy in Babylonia — the most prestigious Jewish academic position. Fiercely controversial — feuded bitterly with the Exilarch. Translated the entire Hebrew Bible into Arabic for Arabic-speaking Jews. Died in Babylonia.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Reason and tradition in harmony — when Scripture seems to contradict reason, re-read Scripture allegorically. Four sources of knowledge: sense perception, reason, logical inference, reliable tradition.
