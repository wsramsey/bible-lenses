# Baruch Spinoza
*1632–1677 AD · Jewish / Philosophical · Rationalist / Pantheist*

> **Theological lean:** God = Nature (Deus sive Natura). Scripture is a human document written in human historical contexts, not divine dictation. The historical-critical method of Bible reading. Human freedom through understanding necessity. Democracy and religious tolerance.

## Historical Context
17th-century Amsterdam — a relatively tolerant city but still with limits. Spinoza was excommunicated by the Amsterdam Jewish community in 1656 with one of the harshest cherem (excommunication) decrees in history. The reasons are unclear — possibly his critique of Scripture's divine authorship, possibly his views on the soul. He became a lens grinder to support himself while writing.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Son of Portuguese Marrano Jews who had returned to Judaism in Amsterdam. Excommunicated at 23. Worked as lens grinder (ironically — a man who ground lenses for seeing who re-ground the lenses of biblical interpretation). Refused a professorship at Heidelberg to keep his intellectual freedom. Died at 44 of lung disease, probably from lens-grinding dust.

## Hermeneutical Approach
The founding figure of modern biblical criticism. Scripture must be read like any historical document — who wrote it, when, why, for what audience. Moses did not write the entire Pentateuch. The Bible is not one unified book but a library of human responses to God.
