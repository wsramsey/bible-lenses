# Ibn Arabi
*1165–1240 AD · Islamic Philosophy / Mysticism · Wahdat al-Wujud (Unity of Being)*

> **Theological lean:** Wahdat al-Wujud — unity of all being in God. Every creature is a manifestation (tajalli) of a divine name. The Perfect Human (al-Insan al-Kamil) as the mirror of all divine attributes. Mystical union. The Quran as infinitely layered symbol.

## Historical Context
Medieval Islamic Spain (al-Andalus) — the most sophisticated multicultural civilization in Europe. Muslims, Jews, and Christians living in relative tolerance, their intellectuals in dialogue. Ibn Arabi's mystical pantheism was controversial in Islam (some called him a heretic) but profoundly influential. His concepts of divine self-disclosure have remarkable parallels with Christian mystical theology — both drawing from Neoplatonism.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born Murcia, Spain. Claimed spiritual experiences from childhood including visions of prophets. Traveled widely — Mecca, Egypt, Anatolia, finally Damascus where he died. Prolific — wrote over 300 works. Called 'the Greatest Shaykh' (al-Shaykh al-Akbar).

## Hermeneutical Approach
Esoteric Quranic interpretation — every verse has 7 levels of meaning. The created world as a book revealing God's names. Mystical experience grants hermeneutical access unavailable to rational commentary.
