# Al-Kindi
*801–873 AD · Islamic Philosophy · Arab Neoplatonism*

> **Theological lean:** First Arab philosopher. Reconciled Greek philosophy with Islamic theology. God as the First Being, pure intellect. Reason and revelation harmonious. The soul's intellectual ascent.

## Historical Context
The Abbasid Caliphate's House of Wisdom in Baghdad was translating the entire Greek philosophical corpus into Arabic. Al-Kindi was at the center of this — his work made Plato, Aristotle, and the Neoplatonists available to the Islamic world, which then transmitted them back to the Latin West through Averroes and Avicenna. Without Al-Kindi, Aquinas has no Aristotle.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Called 'the Philosopher of the Arabs.' Supervised translation projects at the House of Wisdom. Wrote over 260 works. Later fell out of favor with the Abbasid caliph and was beaten and his library confiscated. Most of his works are lost.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Reason and revelation both lead to truth — philosophy is not the enemy of Islam. Mathematical and musical proportions reflect divine order.
