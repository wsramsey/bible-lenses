# John Owen
*1616–1683 · Christian · Puritan / Calvinist*

> **Theological lean:** Particular redemption, Mortification, Trinitarian communion

## Hermeneutical Lenses

- [[Lens - Literal / Grammatical]] — [Literal / Grammatical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biblical_literalism)
  *Plain meaning of words in grammar and syntax; Reformation method*
- [[Lens - Redemptive-Historical]] — [Redemptive-Historical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redemptive-historical_hermeneutics)
  *Reads OT as foreshadowing fulfilled in Christ; Vos, Ridderbos*
- [[Lens - Covenantal]] — [Covenantal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covenant_theology)
  *Reads Scripture through covenant of works/grace; Westminster tradition*

## Shaped By

- [[John Calvin]] — *foundational*
  > Owen is the greatest English Calvinist; deeply shaped by Calvin's theology

## Influenced

- [[Richard Baxter]] — *contemporary*
  > Baxter and Owen were contemporaries and rivals; both shaped by same tradition
- [[John Piper]] — *direct*
  > Piper has written on Owen's mortification and is deeply Owenian

## Primary Works

- *Mortification of Sin*
- *Communion with God*
- *Hebrews Commentary*
