# Francis Watson
*1956– · Anglican · Theological Hermeneutics*

> **Why he's here:** The leading voice for theological interpretation of Scripture against purely historical-critical approaches. His *Text and Truth* (1997) argued that the historical-critical method, by bracketing theological questions, produces not neutrality but a distorted reading. His *Gospel Writing* (2013) offers a comprehensive account of how the four Gospels were written in relation to each other — and why that matters theologically.

## Key Concepts
- **Against the historical-critical enclosure** — the assumption that "real" interpretation is historical-critical and theological interpretation is merely devotional is itself a theological position (liberal Protestant) masquerading as neutrality
- **The canonical gospel** — the four Gospels as a canonical whole; their differences are features not bugs; each presents the one gospel from a distinct angle
- **Text and truth** — theological truth is accessible through the text; the text is not just a historical artifact but a medium of revelation
- **Paul and hermeneutics** — Paul's own reading of Scripture (especially in Galatians and Romans) is itself a hermeneutical practice worth learning from
