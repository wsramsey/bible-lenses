# Apostolic Succession — The Chain
*Trunk / Lineage*

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apostolic_succession)

The claim that authority in the church flows through an unbroken chain of bishops from the Apostles. Catholic, Orthodox, and some Anglican traditions consider this essential. Protestants generally reject it in favor of doctrinal succession.

## The Petrine Claim (Roman Catholic)
The papacy claims Peter as first bishop of Rome → each pope inherits his authority.
Key early bishops: Peter → Linus → Anacletus → Clement I → Evaristus → Alexander I → Sixtus I → Telesphorus → Hyginus → Pius I → Anicetus → Soter → Eleutherius → Victor I...

## The Five Patriarchates (Pentarchy)
The ancient church was governed by five great sees — a structure formalized at Chalcedon (451):
- **Rome** (claims primacy) → [[Leo the Great]] → eventual Pope
- **Constantinople** (New Rome after 330) → second in honor → eventual Ecumenical Patriarch
- **Alexandria** → [[Athanasius of Alexandria]] → eventually Coptic Pope + Greek Patriarch
- **Antioch** → [[Ignatius of Antioch]] → Syrian Orthodox + Greek Orthodox + Maronite
- **Jerusalem** → James the Just (brother of Jesus) → Latin + Greek + Armenian Patriarchate
