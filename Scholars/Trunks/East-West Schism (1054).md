# East-West Schism (1054)
*Trunk Split*

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East%E2%80%93West_Schism)

**The Great Schism.** The formal split between Roman Catholicism and Eastern Orthodoxy.

## Causes (centuries in the making)
- **Filioque controversy** — the Western church added 'and the Son' (filioque) to the Nicene Creed's statement that the Spirit proceeds from the Father. The East considered this a unilateral doctrinal change without an Ecumenical Council.
- **Papal authority** — Rome claimed jurisdiction over the whole church; Constantinople claimed equality of honor, not submission.
- **Cultural drift** — Latin West and Greek East had been diverging for centuries in language, liturgy, and theological method.
- **Political** — Charlemagne's coronation (800) as Western Emperor created a rival power structure.

## The Moment
Cardinal Humbert placed a bull of excommunication on the altar of Hagia Sophia (July 16, 1054). Patriarch Michael Cerularius excommunicated Humbert in return. (The excommunications were mutually lifted in 1964 by Pope Paul VI and Patriarch Athenagoras.)
