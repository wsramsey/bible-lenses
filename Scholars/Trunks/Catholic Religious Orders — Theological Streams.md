# Catholic Religious Orders — Theological Streams
*Catholic Sub-Trunk*

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_order)

The Catholic church's theological diversity was largely organized through competing religious orders, each with a distinct spirituality and intellectual tradition.

## Benedictines (529 AD — Benedict of Nursia)
*Ora et Labora* — prayer and work. The Rule of Benedict shaped Western monasticism. Preserved learning through the Dark Ages. [[Gregory the Great]] was Benedictine. [[Anselm of Canterbury]] was Benedictine Abbot.

## Dominicans (1216 — Dominic)
Founded to combat heresy through preaching and scholarship. The Order of Preachers. Produced the greatest medieval theologians: [[Thomas Aquinas]], [[Meister Eckhart]], [[Albertus Magnus]], [[Gustavo Gutiérrez]].

## Franciscans (1209 — Francis of Assisi)
Poverty, simplicity, creation spirituality. Intellectual tradition through [[Bonaventure]], Duns Scotus, Roger Bacon. Emotional, affective piety — Francis's stigmata. [[Brennan Manning]] was Franciscan.

## Jesuits (1540 — [[Ignatius of Loyola]])
The shock troops of the Counter-Reformation. Education (Jesuit universities worldwide), missions, intellectual rigor, obedience to the Pope. Spiritual Exercises as systematic formation. Produced [[Jean-Pierre de Caussade]], Teilhard de Chardin, [[Hans Urs von Balthasar]] (associated), Karl Rahner.

## Carmelites (12th c. — reformed by Teresa of Ávila and John of the Cross, 16th c.)
The mystical tradition. Dark Night of the Soul, Interior Castle. [[Teresa of Ávila]], [[John of the Cross]], [[Thomas Merton]] (Trappist — Cistercian, related), [[Brother Lawrence]] (Carmelite lay brother).

## Cistercians / Trappists
Reform of Benedictines. [[Bernard of Clairvaux]] (12th c.) — bridal mysticism, Crusades. [[Thomas Merton]] at Gethsemani.
