# Eastern Orthodox — Autocephalous Churches
*Orthodox Trunk Map*

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church)

After the East-West Schism, Eastern Orthodoxy developed as a communion of self-governing (autocephalous) churches, each with its own Patriarch, all in communion with Constantinople.

## Chalcedonian Orthodox (in order of honor)
1. **Ecumenical Patriarchate of Constantinople** — first among equals, based in Istanbul
2. **Greek Orthodox Church** — largest in the Mediterranean world
3. **Russian Orthodox Church** — largest Orthodox church globally (~100M); became autocephalous 1589; enormous cultural and theological influence (Dostoevsky, Tolstoy)
4. **Serbian Orthodox Church** — became autocephalous 1219
5. **Romanian Orthodox Church** — largest Orthodox church in Western Europe
6. **Bulgarian Orthodox Church**
7. **Georgian Orthodox Church** — claims apostolic founding by Andrew
8. **Orthodox Church in America** — [[Alexander Schmemann]]'s church

## Oriental Orthodox (split at Chalcedon 451)
Rejected the two-natures formula:
- **Coptic Orthodox Church** (Egypt) — traces to Mark the Evangelist; survived under Islam
- **Ethiopian Orthodox Church** — includes 1 Enoch and Jubilees in its Bible canon
- **Armenian Apostolic Church** — first nation to adopt Christianity (301 AD)
- **Syriac Orthodox Church** (Antioch)
- **Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church** (India — traces to Thomas the Apostle)

## Why It Matters
Each national church absorbed its culture's intellectual tradition. Russian Orthodoxy produced a distinctive philosophical theology (sobornost, theosis in political theology). Greek Orthodoxy preserved the Cappadocian and Dionysian heritage. The Oriental Orthodox preserved texts (Enoch, Jubilees, etc.) lost to Western Christianity.
