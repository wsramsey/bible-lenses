# Pietism, Evangelicalism & The Methodist Movement (1675–1800)
*Protestant Sub-Trunk*

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pietism)

A reaction against Protestant scholasticism — the faith of the heart against the faith of the head.

## German Pietism (1675)
Philipp Jakob Spener's *Pia Desideria* (1675) called for small group Bible study (collegia pietatis), personal conversion, and practical holiness against Lutheran orthodoxy's dry confessionalism. August Hermann Francke built orphanages and schools. Influenced Zinzendorf and the Moravians.

## Moravians (Zinzendorf, 1720s)
Nikolaus von Zinzendorf's community at Herrnhut. Sent missionaries worldwide. Their intense personal piety and missions energy directly awakened [[John Wesley]] — his Aldersgate experience followed contact with Moravians.

## Methodist Revival (1738)
[[John Wesley]] and George Whitefield's open-air preaching in England. Class meetings for accountability. Social action (abolition, prison reform). Arminian grace — everyone can be saved. Split from Anglicanism eventually; became the Methodist Church.

## American Evangelical Movement (First & Second Great Awakenings)
[[Jonathan Edwards]] (First Awakening, 1740) → Charles Finney (Second Awakening, 1820s) → D.L. Moody → [[Billy Graham]] (20th century mass evangelism). The Holiness movement spun off Methodism → Pentecostalism (Azusa Street, 1906) → Charismatic Renewal (1960s) → modern megachurch evangelicalism.

## The Keswick / Deeper Life Movement
Hannah Whitall Smith, [[Andrew Murray]], [[Oswald Chambers]] — 'higher life' or entire sanctification. The interior life, surrender, abiding in Christ.
