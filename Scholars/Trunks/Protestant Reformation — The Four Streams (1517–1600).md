# Protestant Reformation — The Four Streams (1517–1600)
*Trunk Split*

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protestant_Reformation)

The Reformation was not one movement but four distinct streams that diverged almost immediately.

## 1. Lutheran Stream (1517)
[[Martin Luther]] in Germany. Grace alone, faith alone, Scripture alone. Two sacraments (Baptism and Eucharist — real presence). Kept much of Catholic liturgy. State-church model. → [[Dietrich Bonhoeffer]], [[Paul Tillich]], [[Gerhard Forde]], [[Rudolf Bultmann]]

## 2. Reformed / Calvinist Stream (1519)
[[Ulrich Zwingli]] in Zurich, then [[John Calvin]] in Geneva. More radical break with Catholic forms. Symbolic Eucharist (Zwingli) or spiritual presence (Calvin). Sovereignty of God, predestination. Presbyterian/congregational polity. → Westminster Confession → [[Jonathan Edwards]], [[R.C. Sproul]], [[Tim Keller]], [[John Piper]]

## 3. Anglican Stream (1534)
Henry VIII breaks with Rome over divorce — but English Reformation's theology was Calvinist (Cranmer). Via media between Catholic forms and Protestant doctrine. Maintained bishops and apostolic succession (disputed). → [[C.S. Lewis]], [[N.T. Wright]], [[John Stott]], [[J.I. Packer]]

## 4. Radical / Anabaptist Stream (1525)
Conrad Grebel, Felix Manz in Zurich — rebaptism of adults, refusal of state church, pacifism, literal discipleship. Persecuted by both Catholics and Lutherans. → [[Menno Simons]] → [[John Howard Yoder]], [[Stanley Hauerwas]] (influenced)

## The Eucharist Controversy
Luther and Zwingli met at Marburg (1529) and agreed on 14 of 15 articles. The one disagreement — whether Christ is bodily present in the bread and wine — split the Protestant movement permanently and has never been healed.
