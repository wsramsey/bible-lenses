# Avignon Papacy & Great Western Schism (1305–1417)
*Internal Catholic Crisis*

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avignon_papacy)

**The papacy moves to France.** For 67 years (1309-1377) the Pope ruled from Avignon under French influence. Catherine of Siena famously urged Pope Gregory XI to return to Rome.

## Great Western Schism (1378–1417)
After the return to Rome, competing claimants produced two — then three — simultaneous popes, each excommunicating the others. This crisis devastated papal authority and directly prepared the ground for the Reformation.

- **Conciliarism arose** — the idea that a General Council is superior to the Pope. This was a direct intellectual precursor to Luther's appeal to councils against popes.
- **Jan Hus** burned at the Council of Constance (1415) despite a safe-conduct promise — his martyrdom radicalized a generation and directly influenced Luther.
- **John Wycliffe** in England attacked papal authority, translated Scripture into English. His followers (Lollards) were precursors of the English Reformation.

## Why It Matters for the Tree
The Avignon period and the Schism are the soil in which the Reformation grew. By 1517, no serious person could claim the papacy was incorrupt. Luther's challenge landed in ground that had been loosened for 100 years.
