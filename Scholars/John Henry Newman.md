# John Henry Newman
*1801–1890 · Catholic · Development of Doctrine*

> **Why he's here:** The most important English-speaking theologian of the 19th century. Newman's conversion from Anglican to Catholic in 1845 was the theological earthquake of the Victorian era. His *Essay on the Development of Christian Doctrine* solved a problem that had stopped him from converting: how do you account for the difference between early Christianity and medieval Catholicism? His answer shaped all subsequent Catholic theology.

## Historical Context
Victorian England — the Oxford Movement (1833–1845) sought to recover patristic and sacramental roots in the Anglican church. Newman led it, then followed it to its logical conclusion by converting to Rome. Became a cardinal in 1879. Beatified 2010.

## Key Concepts
- **Development of doctrine** — doctrine doesn't change but develops; like a seed to tree, not a caterpillar to butterfly; seven tests of authentic development
- **Conscience** — "the aboriginal Vicar of Christ"; primary locus of divine communication; his famous toast "to Conscience first, and to the Pope afterwards"
- **Illative sense** — the faculty that accumulates convergent probabilities into certitude; how we actually reason in real life
- **Grammar of Assent** — distinguishes notional assent (intellectual) from real assent (existential); faith requires real assent
