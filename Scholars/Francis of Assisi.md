# Francis of Assisi
*1181–1226 · Catholic · Creation Mysticism*

> **Why he's here:** The most beloved figure in Christian history outside the New Testament. Francis didn't write systematic theology — he *lived* a theology of radical kenosis, poverty, and kinship with all creation. His *Canticle of the Creatures* is the first major vernacular poem in Italian and a theology of creation as siblings.

## Historical Context
12th–13th century Italy. Rapid urbanization, mercantile wealth, and clerical corruption created hunger for authentic Christian witness. Francis and Clare offered a living alternative — owning nothing, serving the poorest, treating nature as family.

## Biography
Born to a wealthy cloth merchant in Assisi. Military adventures, illness, and a series of spiritual encounters (leper encounter, crucifix speaking at San Damiano) transformed him. Stripped off his clothes in the town square, renounced his inheritance, began rebuilding churches literally and the church figuratively. Received the stigmata in 1224. Died singing.

## Key Concepts
- **Radical poverty (Lady Poverty)** — not as ascetic discipline but as mystical identification with the kenotic Christ
- **Fraternity** — Brother Sun, Sister Moon, Brother Wind, Sister Death; creation as family
- **The poor Christ** — Christ is encountered in the leper, the sick, the outcast
- **Joy (laetitia)** — spiritual joy as fruit of poverty; the *Fioretti* stories of perfect joy

## Writing Style
Francis barely wrote. *Canticle of the Creatures*, a few letters, *Rule*, *Testament*. His life was the text.

## Shaped By
