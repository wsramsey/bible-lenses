# Franz Rosenzweig
*1886–1929 · Jewish · The Star of Redemption*

> **Why he's here:** On the eve of his planned conversion to Christianity (1913), he attended a Yom Kippur service and returned to Judaism instead. He then wrote *The Star of Redemption* — one of the great philosophical-theological works of the 20th century — arguing that Judaism and Christianity are the two permanent truths of God's relation to humanity: Judaism is the eternal fire (already with God), Christianity is the eternal rays (still going out to the world). His influence on both [[Karl Barth]] and [[Hans Urs von Balthasar]] is documented; his *new thinking* (dialogical, speech-based) shaped [[Martin Buber]].

## Key Concepts
- **The Star of Redemption** — three elements (God, World, Human) in three relationships (Creation, Revelation, Redemption); the six-pointed Star of David as philosophical diagram
- **New thinking** — against idealism's monism; reality is genuinely three: God, World, Humanity; they relate but are not reducible to each other
- **Speech-thinking** — the model of thought is speech, not solitary contemplation; thinking requires a partner; time matters (you cannot know in advance what you will say or how the other will respond)
- **Judaism and Christianity** — two genuine modes of God's self-disclosure; Judaism is the eternal people already with God; Christianity is the mission to bring the world to God; neither is complete without the other
- **Revelation as love** — the Song of Songs is Rosenzweig's central text; revelation is God saying "I love you"; the human response is the birth of the soul
