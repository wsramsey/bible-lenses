# Paul of Tarsus

> Justification, grace, Spirit, body of Christ — the most contested and generative voice in Christian theology

## Influenced

- [[Origen of Alexandria]] — *direct* · Origen's allegorical method applied especially to Pauline texts
- [[Augustine of Hippo]] — *direct* · Romans 13 triggers Augustine's conversion; Paul on grace shapes entire theology
- [[John Chrysostom]] — *direct* · Chrysostom's greatest commentaries are on Pauline epistles
- [[Irenaeus of Lyon]] — *direct* · Irenaean recapitulation reads Paul's Adam-Christ typology
- [[Martin Luther]] — *direct* · Romans and Galatians: Luther's tower experience reading 'righteousness of God'
- [[John Calvin]] — *direct* · Calvin's commentaries on Paul are the heart of his exegetical project
- [[Karl Barth]] — *direct* · Romans commentary re-reads Paul against liberal Protestant domestication
- [[Gustavo Gutiérrez]] — *direct* · Gutiérrez reads Paul's solidarity with the poor as central to the gospel
- [[James Cone]] — *direct* · Cone re-reads Paul's freedom language through Black American experience
