# The Cloud of Unknowing (Anonymous)
*c. 1375 · Catholic · Apophatic Contemplation*

> **Why it's here:** The most practical manual of apophatic prayer in the English language. Written by an unknown English contemplative for a young disciple, it teaches that God cannot be known by thought but only touched by love — and that the way forward is to place a "cloud of forgetting" beneath (over all created things) and a "cloud of unknowing" above (between the soul and God), and to strike that cloud with "a sharp dart of longing love."

## Historical Context
14th-century England — the same era as Julian of Norwich and Margery Kempe. The Black Death, Peasants' Revolt, Lollardy. A surge of vernacular spirituality written in Middle English for ordinary Christians.

## About the Author
Unknown. Probably a Carthusian monk or priest in the East Midlands. Also wrote *The Book of Privy Counselling*, *Epistle of Prayer*, and translated Pseudo-Dionysius's *Mystical Theology* into Middle English — which tells us exactly where his sources lay.

## Key Concepts
- **Cloud of Unknowing** — the darkness between soul and God that cannot be penetrated by intellect, only by love
- **Cloud of Forgetting** — placed beneath, covering all memories, ideas, and created things
- **Naked intent** — stripped desire directed toward God alone, with no specific thought or image
- **Short prayer** — a single word (he recommends "God" or "Love") repeated as contemplative anchor
- **Apophatic method** — God is beyond all concepts; thinking about God is not the same as touching God

## Writing Style
Informal, direct, sometimes impatient with his disciple. Very practical. Uses vernacular English (Middle English) with theological precision.
