# Oliver O'Donovan
*1945– · Anglican · Ethics of the Resurrection*

> **Why he's here:** The most important Protestant political theologian of the late 20th century. His *Resurrection and Moral Order* (1986) grounds all Christian ethics in the resurrection — not as one doctrine among others but as the vindication of created order, the defeat of death, and the basis for moral knowledge. His *The Desire of the Nations* (1996) offers the most rigorous evangelical political theology available.

## Key Concepts
- **Resurrection as foundation** — the resurrection vindicates creation; moral order is not destroyed by sin but restored by the risen Christ; ethics begins here
- **Natural order** — creation has a given moral structure that human reason can (imperfectly) discern; the resurrection confirms rather than replaces it
- **Political authority** — authority is derived, not absolute; political authority serves human flourishing and is judged by its response to Christ's lordship
- **Christendom** — not a mistake but a genuine (imperfect) response to the Gospel's political implications; neither uncritical celebration nor wholesale rejection
- **Desire of the nations** — political life is the arena in which humanity's longing for justice and peace is expressed; Christ is the answer to what the nations desire
