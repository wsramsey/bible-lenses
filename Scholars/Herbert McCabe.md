# Herbert McCabe OP
*1926–2001 · Catholic · Wittgensteinian Thomism*

> **Why he's here:** The most underread important Catholic philosopher of the 20th century. McCabe combined deep Thomistic theology with Wittgenstein's philosophy of language to produce a theology that is simultaneously intellectually rigorous and pastorally accessible. His *God Matters* and *God Still Matters* are collections of essays that illuminate the Trinity, the Eucharist, ethics, and prayer with unusual clarity. His sermon openings were legendary: he once began "If you don't love you're dead, and if you do, they'll kill you."

## Key Concepts
- **God is not a thing** — following Aquinas; God does not exist *in* the world as one being among others; "God" is not a name for a very large or very powerful creature; this is the beginning of all theology
- **Language about God** — Wittgenstein + Aquinas: theological language doesn't describe God directly; it redirects our language; analogical predication
- **Ethics as friendship** — ethics is not primarily about rules but about what kind of person you want to be; the virtues are the forms of friendship; following Aristotle-Aquinas
- **The Eucharist** — his sermons on the Mass are among the finest short theological writing of the century; presence, sacrifice, community
