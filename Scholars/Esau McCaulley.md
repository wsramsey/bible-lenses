# Esau McCaulley
*1983– · Anglican Evangelical · Black Church Hermeneutics*

> **Why he's here:** NT scholar, New York Times contributing opinion writer, and the most visible voice for Black Church hermeneutics within evangelical and Anglican contexts. His *Reading While Black* (2020) argues that the Black Church tradition has developed interpretive practices for reading Scripture under oppression that the broader church needs.

## Key Concepts
- **Reading while Black** — the Black Church's hermeneutic is not a deviation but a gift; centuries of reading Scripture under oppression produced distinctive and necessary insights
- **Scripture as liberation** — the Bible's story is fundamentally about God's rescue of the oppressed; this is not eisegesis but the plain sense
- **Policing, anger, and lament** — his chapters on these topics in *Reading While Black* are the most widely discussed
