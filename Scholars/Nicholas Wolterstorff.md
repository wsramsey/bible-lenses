# Nicholas Wolterstorff
*1932– · Reformed · Analytic Philosophy*

> **Why he's here:** With [[Alvin Plantinga]], the co-architect of Reformed Epistemology — the project of showing that Christian belief can be rationally warranted without argument. But Wolterstorff's range is broader: his work covers aesthetics (*Works and Worlds of Art*), justice (*Justice: Rights and Wrongs*), divine speech (*Divine Discourse*), and — most personally — grief (*Lament for a Son*). His *Lament* is one of the most honest Christian accounts of bereavement ever written.

## Key Concepts
- **Reformed epistemology** — belief in God can be "properly basic" — rationally held without inference from other beliefs; the evidentialist challenge to theism fails
- **Divine discourse** — Scripture is God's speech to humanity; not just human words about God but divine discourse; this requires a sophisticated philosophy of speech and delegation
- **Justice as rights** — against the view that justice is merely about right order or the greatest good; persons have rights grounded in their worth before God; this is specifically a Christian contribution to political philosophy
- **Shalom** — the Hebrew concept of comprehensive flourishing; peace, justice, and delight in right relationship with God, neighbor, and creation; the goal of Christian social engagement
- **Lament** — his son Eric died in a mountain climbing accident; *Lament for a Son* is the theodicy of a bereaved father; arguably his most read work
