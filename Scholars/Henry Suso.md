# Henry Suso
*1295–1366 · Catholic · Rhineland Mysticism*

> **Why he's here:** The poet of the Rhineland mystics. Where Eckhart was the philosopher and Tauler the preacher, Suso was the troubadour — drawing on courtly love tradition to express union with Eternal Wisdom. His *Little Book of Eternal Wisdom* became one of the most widely read spiritual texts of the late Middle Ages.

## Historical Context
Same Rhineland context as Tauler. Suso was a direct disciple of Eckhart and suffered for it — accused of heresy and removed from his teaching post after Eckhart's condemnation. He spent years itinerant before being rehabilitated.

## Biography
Born near Constance. Dominican, studied under Meister Eckhart in Cologne. His early life was marked by extreme asceticism (self-mortification) he later abandoned. His *Little Book of Truth* brought him under suspicion; his later works are more careful and more beautiful.

## Key Concepts
- **Eternal Wisdom** — Christ as personified divine wisdom, with whom the soul is in a spousal relationship
- **Bridal mysticism** — the soul as bride drawn into union with the divine Beloved (Song of Songs tradition)
- **Suffering as path** — purification through endured suffering, not just interior detachment
- **Abandonment** — surrendering all self-will to God's wisdom

## Writing Style
Lyrical, personal, emotionally vivid. *Little Book of Eternal Wisdom* reads almost like mystical poetry in prose.
