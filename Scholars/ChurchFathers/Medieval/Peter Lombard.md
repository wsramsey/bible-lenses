# Peter Lombard
*1096–1160 AD · Christian · Scholastic / Compilatory*

> **Theological lean:** The Four Books of Sentences — systematic collection of patristic opinions on theological questions. Not an original thinker but the most influential textbook in medieval theology. Every major medieval theologian wrote a Commentary on the Sentences.

## Historical Context
Medieval theology was taught through commenting on authoritative texts. Lombard created the standard textbook that every theology student for 400 years had to master. Aquinas, Bonaventure, Ockham, Luther — all wrote Sentences commentaries. To understand medieval theology you must understand what Lombard compiled.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born in Novara, northern Italy. Studied in Bologna, Reims, and Paris. Became Bishop of Paris in 1159, died the following year. His Sentences were almost condemned twice but survived to become the theological curriculum of Europe.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Compilatory — gathering and organizing patristic authorities. The arrangement itself is a theological statement: the order in which questions are addressed reflects a theological vision of God, creation, incarnation, and sacraments.
