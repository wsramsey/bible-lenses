# Hildegard of Bingen
*1098–1179 AD · Christian · Prophetic / Mystical / Natural Philosophy*

> **Theological lean:** Viriditas (greening power) — God's life-giving force in creation. Prophetic visions of the living light. Music as cosmic theology. Feminine wisdom (Sapientia). The human person as microcosm of the cosmos. Natural medicine.

## Historical Context
12th century Germany — a woman exercising theological authority through prophetic visions when no other avenue was open to her. Corresponded with popes, emperors, and abbots. Conducted preaching tours (extraordinary for a woman of her era). Canonized in 2012 and declared a Doctor of the Church — only the fourth woman ever.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Tenth child, offered to the church at age 8 as a tithe. Grew up enclosed with the anchoress Jutta, eventually became abbess. Had visions from childhood but only began writing at age 42 when commanded by God and authorized by Bernard of Clairvaux. Founded her own monastery at Rupertsberg over the objections of her abbot.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Visionary — receives direct illumination ('the living light') and writes what she sees. Music and image as primary theological languages alongside text. The body and senses as legitimate theological sources.
