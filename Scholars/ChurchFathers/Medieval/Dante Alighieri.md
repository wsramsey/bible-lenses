# Dante Alighieri
*1265–1321 AD · Christian · Literary Theology / Lay Theology*

> **Theological lean:** The Divine Comedy as theology in narrative form. Aquinas's Scholastic theology translated into vision and story. Love as the motor of the universe ('the love that moves the sun and other stars'). Purgatory as genuine transformation. Hell as self-chosen isolation from love.

## Historical Context
Florence in the age of Guelphs and Ghibellines — brutal political conflict between papal and imperial factions. Dante backed the wrong side, was exiled from Florence in 1302 under pain of death if he returned. He never did. The Comedy written in exile — the greatest work of Western literature written by a man who had lost everything.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born Florence. Fell in love with Beatrice Portinari at age 9 — she died at 24 and became the theological symbol of divine love guiding the soul to God. Politically active, exiled at 37. Wandered northern Italy for 20 years writing. Died in Ravenna shortly after completing the Paradiso.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Four senses of Scripture applied to the whole poem: literal (the journey), allegorical (the soul's journey to God), moral (the correction of vice), anagogical (the beatific vision). Literature as a legitimate vehicle for theology — exactly C.S. Lewis's and Dorothy Sayers's claim.
