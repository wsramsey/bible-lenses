# William of Ockham
*1287–1347 AD · Christian · Nominalism / Voluntarism*

> **Theological lean:** Nominalism — universals are just names (nomina), not real things. Only individuals exist. God's will is absolutely free and could have made different moral laws. Razor: do not multiply entities beyond necessity. Scripture alone as theological authority (anticipating sola scriptura). Papal authority limited.

## Historical Context
The late medieval crisis of scholasticism. In conflict with Pope John XXII over Franciscan poverty, fled to Munich under the protection of the Holy Roman Emperor. His nominalism shattered the Thomistic synthesis by severing faith and reason — God's will is free to command anything, so reason cannot tell us what God commands; only Scripture can. This is the direct philosophical root of the Reformation's sola scriptura.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Franciscan friar from Ockham, Surrey. Studied and taught at Oxford. Summoned to Avignon by the Pope to answer charges of heresy — fled before the verdict with the Franciscan Minister General and other friars to Munich. Spent the rest of his life in exile writing political theology against papal authority. Probably died in the Black Death (1347).

## Hermeneutical Approach
Literal reading of Scripture as the primary authority — reason cannot constrain what God might reveal. This anti-allegorical instinct flows through Luther.
