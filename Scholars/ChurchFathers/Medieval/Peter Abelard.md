# Peter Abelard
*1079–1142 AD · Christian · Scholastic / Dialectical*

> **Theological lean:** Sic et Non (Yes and No) — dialectical method applied to theological authorities. Moral influence theory of atonement (Christ's death as supreme moral example moving us to love, not satisfaction of divine justice). Faith seeking understanding through reason.

## Historical Context
12th-century Paris — the birth of the university. Abelard was the most brilliant and controversial teacher of his age. His love affair with Heloise (his student) ended in his castration by her uncle's hired men, his becoming a monk, her becoming a nun — one of medieval history's most extraordinary stories. Bernard of Clairvaux opposed him and had him condemned at the Council of Sens (1141).

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
The rock star of medieval philosophy. Students traveled from across Europe to hear him lecture. His romance with Heloise and its violent ending changed both their lives. Their letters are among the most intimate in medieval literature. Castrated by Heloise's uncle Fulbert, became a monk, continued writing despite condemnations.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Sic et Non — presenting contradictory statements from authoritative sources to force resolution through reason. The dialectical method that Aquinas perfected (question-objection-reply) is essentially Abelard's method systematized.
