# Benedict of Nursia
*480–547 AD · Christian · Benedictine / Western Monasticism*

> **Theological lean:** Ora et labora (prayer and work). The monastery as school of the Lord's service. Moderation, stability, obedience. The abbot as father. Balance of communal life with contemplation.

## Historical Context
The Western Roman Empire had collapsed (476 AD). Italy was in chaos from barbarian invasions. Benedict created stable communities that preserved learning, agriculture, and Christian civilization through the Dark Ages. Pope Gregory the Great called him 'the light of the West.' The Benedictine monasteries became the universities, hospitals, and cultural centers of medieval Europe.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born to wealthy family in Nursia (modern Norcia, Italy). Went to Rome for education, fled the corruption, lived as a hermit in Subiaco, eventually founded Monte Cassino monastery. His sister Scholastica founded an adjacent women's community. The Rule was written for his community but became universal.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Scripture read through the Office (eight daily prayer times). The liturgical reading of the Psalms shapes the community's imagination. Practical wisdom over speculative theology.
