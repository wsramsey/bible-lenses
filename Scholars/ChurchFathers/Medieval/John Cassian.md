# John Cassian
*360–435 AD · Christian · Desert / Semi-Pelagian*

> **Theological lean:** Transmitted Egyptian desert spirituality to the West. Practical monastic theology. Eight principal vices (precursor to seven deadly sins). Contemplation as the goal of the Christian life. Semi-Pelagian on grace (Augustine's opponent).

## Historical Context
The bridge between Eastern monasticism and the Western church. Cassian lived among the Egyptian desert fathers, then came West and founded monasteries near Marseilles. His Institutes and Conferences became the primary textbook of Western monasticism — Benedict required monks to read him. Every subsequent Western spiritual formation tradition traces through Cassian.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born probably in modern Romania. Traveled to Bethlehem, then spent years in Egypt among the desert fathers. Came to Marseilles around 415, founded two monasteries. Got entangled in the Pelagian controversy — Augustine considered his view on grace semi-Pelagian and wrote against it.

## Hermeneutical Approach
Spiritual reading of Scripture in service of contemplative progress. The four senses of Scripture (literal, allegorical, tropological/moral, anagogical) were formalized partly through his influence.
