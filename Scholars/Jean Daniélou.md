# Jean Daniélou
*1905–1974 · Catholic · Nouvelle Théologie*

> **Why he's here:** A key figure in the *nouvelle théologie* movement alongside [[Henri de Lubac]]. Daniélou was the patristics specialist — his studies of the theology of Jewish Christianity and the Greek Fathers (especially Gregory of Nyssa) recovered dimensions of early Christianity lost in Scholastic systematization. His *The Bible and the Liturgy* is a foundational text of the liturgical renewal.

## Key Concepts
- **Biblical typology** — the Old Testament events are types fulfilled in Christ; this method (not allegory strictly) is the primary patristic hermeneutic
- **History of salvation** — God acts in history; salvation history is real history with real patterns
- **Jewish Christianity** — the earliest Christianity was Jewish; recovering this reshapes how we understand the New Testament
- **Liturgy and Scripture** — the liturgy is where Scripture is most fully interpreted; they belong together

## Shaped By
- [[Gregory of Nyssa]] (translated and studied him deeply)
- [[Origen of Alexandria]] (patristic tradition)
- [[Henri de Lubac]] (colleague and friend)

## Influenced
- Second Vatican Council (liturgical constitution)
- Catholic biblical-liturgical renewal
