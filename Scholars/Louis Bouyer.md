# Louis Bouyer
*1913–2004 · Catholic · Liturgical Theology*

> **Why he's here:** A Lutheran pastor who converted to Catholicism and became one of the great liturgical theologians of the 20th century. His *Liturgical Piety* and *The Meaning of Sacred Scripture* shaped the liturgical movement before Vatican II. Famously and acidly critical of the post-Vatican II liturgical reforms despite being part of the reform committee — his *Memoirs* are remarkably candid.

## Key Concepts
- **Word and Eucharist** — Scripture and Eucharist belong together as the two tables of the Lord; the Mass is the proclamation of the Word completed in the Eucharistic sacrifice
- **Cosmic liturgy** — the church's worship participates in the cosmic praise of the Trinity; echoes Maximus the Confessor
- **Mysticism and theology** — his *Introduction to Spirituality* argues mysticism is not a specialty but the heart of theology
- **Critical orthodoxy** — orthodox on all doctrines but fierce in criticizing institutional mediocrity and liturgical banality
