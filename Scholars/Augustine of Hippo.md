# Augustine of Hippo

> 🌍 Augustine was **African** — born in Thagaste, North Africa (modern Algeria), bishop of Hippo Regius (modern Annaba, Algeria). The early church's greatest Latin theologian was not European. His North African context — multilingual, cosmopolitan, with strong Donatist and Manichaean competing traditions — shaped everything he wrote.

*354–430 · Christian · Patristic / North African*

> **Theological lean:** Grace, Original Sin, Just War, Neo-Platonic synthesis

## Historical Context
The Roman Empire was collapsing. Alaric sacked Rome in 410 AD — an event so traumatic it shook the entire Mediterranean world's confidence in civilization. Augustine wrote *City of God* directly in response, while barbarian armies circled. North Africa was the intellectual center of Latin Christianity. Hippo was a port city — cosmopolitan, multilingual, full of trade and theological controversy.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born to a pagan father (Patricius) and a devout Christian mother (Monica) who prayed for him for decades. Spent his 20s as a Manichaean, chasing intellectual and sexual satisfaction. Kept a concubine for 15 years and had a son (Adeodatus). His restlessness is the engine of the *Confessions* — 'our heart is restless until it rests in Thee.' Ambrose's preaching in Milan cracked him intellectually; Romans 13 broke him spiritually in a garden in 386 AD.

## Written Where & Why
*Confessions* (397-400) — written as Bishop of Hippo, looking back on his conversion. Part prayer, part autobiography, part theology. *City of God* (413-426) — written over 13 years in response to pagans blaming Christians for Rome's fall. *On the Trinity* — 15 years of labor, his most technical work. *Anti-Pelagian writings* — forced by controversy with Pelagius and Julian of Eclanum over grace and free will.

## Contemporaries & Intellectual World
Jerome (rival, correspondent — their letters are sharp), Pelagius (theological opponent on grace), Ambrose (mentor), John Chrysostom (Eastern contemporary, never met), Donatists (schismatic opponents in North Africa).

## Writing Style & Influence on Others
Rhetorical master — trained as a professional orator before conversion. *Confessions* invented the genre of spiritual autobiography in Western literature. Dictated most works to secretaries. Style ranges from soaring lyrical prayer to brutal technical argument. Every subsequent Western theologian writes in his shadow.

## Pressures & Controversies
Donatist schism tore North Africa (Augustine controversially approved state coercion against them). Pelagian controversy consumed his last decades. Vandals besieged Hippo as he lay dying. He burned his personal library before death to prevent it falling into barbarian hands.
