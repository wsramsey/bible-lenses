# Martin Heidegger
*1889–1976 · Philosopher · Being and Time*

> **Why he's here:** Trained as a Jesuit seminarian, steeped in medieval Catholic philosophy, and then the most important philosopher of the 20th century — whose work is saturated with secularized theology whether he acknowledged it or not. [[Rudolf Bultmann]] was his colleague and read *Being and Time* in manuscript; the existential hermeneutics of the NT (authentic existence, fallenness, anxiety, decision) is Heideggerian. [[Karl Rahner]] applied his transcendental method. [[Hans-Georg Gadamer]] was his student.

## Key Concepts
- **Being (*Sein*) vs. beings (*Seiende*)** — the fundamental question of philosophy is the meaning of Being itself, not the study of particular beings; metaphysics has forgotten this (*Seinsvergessenheit*)
- **Dasein** — human existence; the being for whom its own being is an issue; thrown into a world, always already in a situation
- **Thrownness (*Geworfenheit*)** — we find ourselves already in a world, a language, a history we didn't choose; always already situated
- **Authenticity** — owning one's existence; running ahead to death; against the anonymous "they-self" (*das Man*)
- **Hermeneutic circle** — understanding is always already interpretation; the circle is not vicious but the structure of all understanding
- **The fourfold and dwelling** — later Heidegger; earth, sky, mortals, divinities; dwelling poetically on the earth

## The Nazi Problem
Heidegger joined the Nazi party in 1933 and never fully recanted. His *Black Notebooks* reveal deeper antisemitism than previously known. Using his work requires moral accounting.
