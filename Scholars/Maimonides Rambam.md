# Maimonides (Rambam)
*1138–1204 · Jewish · Rationalist / Aristotelian*

> **Theological lean:** Negative theology, 13 Principles, Halakhic codification, Aristotle synthesis

## Hermeneutical Lenses

- [[Lens - Philosophical Theology]] — [Philosophical Theology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophical_theology)
  *Applies philosophy to theological questions; Aquinas, Tillich, Plantinga*
- [[Lens - Natural Theology]] — [Natural Theology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_theology)
  *God known through reason and nature; Aquinas, Paley, McGrath*
- [[Lens - Peshat]] — [Peshat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peshat)
  *Plain/contextual meaning; primary Jewish exegetical method; Rashi, Ibn Ezra*

## Shaped By

- [[Rashi Rabbi Shlomo Yitzchaki]] — *intellectual*
  > Both in Rabbinic tradition though Maimonides is rationalist where Rashi is peshat
- [[Plato]] — *philosophical*
  > Maimonides mediated Aristotelianism into Jewish thought as Aquinas did for Christianity
- [[Aristotle]] — *philosophical*
  > Guide for the Perplexed is an Aristotelian-Jewish synthesis
- [[Ibn Sina Avicenna]] — *transmission*
  > Avicenna's Neoplatonic-Aristotelian synthesis reached Maimonides via Islamic philosophy

## Primary Works

- *Guide for the Perplexed*
- *Mishneh Torah*
