# E.D. Hirsch Jr.
*1928– · Literary Theorist · Authorial Intent*

> **Why he's here:** The most important defender of authorial intent as the determinate meaning of texts — and therefore the theoretical anchor for evangelical hermeneutics against postmodern reader-response theory. His *Validity in Interpretation* (1967) was a direct response to [[Hans-Georg Gadamer]] and argued that without authorial intent as the norm, interpretation becomes arbitrary. Most evangelical hermeneutics textbooks are implicitly Hirschian.

## Key Concepts
- **Meaning vs. significance** — *meaning* is what the author meant (determinate, fixed); *significance* is the text's relevance to the interpreter's situation (variable); conflating them produces interpretive anarchy
- **Validity in interpretation** — interpretations can be better or worse; authorial intent is the standard; not all readings are equally valid
- **Genre** — the author's choice of genre sets the rules for interpretation; recognizing genre is the first step to understanding meaning
- **Against Gadamer** — fusion of horizons collapses the distinction between what the text means and what it means *to me*; this makes biblical authority impossible
