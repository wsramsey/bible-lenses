# Clare of Assisi
*1194–1253 · Catholic · Poverty / Contemplation*

> **Why she's here:** The co-founder and theological deepener of the Franciscan charism. Clare resisted repeated papal pressure to accept property for her community — holding the "privilege of poverty" for 27 years until it was finally granted. Her four letters to Agnes of Prague contain a compact mystical theology of gazing on Christ as mirror.

## Historical Context
13th-century Assisi, same world as Francis. Clare ran the women's side of the movement from San Damiano for 40 years without ever leaving — while Francis traveled and preached, Clare's prayer was the still center.

## Biography
Born noble in Assisi. Inspired by Francis's preaching at age 18, she fled her family at night, received the habit from Francis, and eventually settled at San Damiano. The Poor Ladies (later Poor Clares) grew to communities across Europe. Clare spent her last years bedridden but reportedly sustained her community through prayer and reportedly repelled a Saracen attack by holding up the Blessed Sacrament.

## Key Concepts
- **Mirror mysticism** — Christ as mirror; to gaze on him is to see and become your true self
- **Privilege of poverty** — owning nothing as participation in the kenotic Christ
- **Contemplation as activity** — enclosure is not passivity; Clare's prayer is active spiritual warfare and intercession
- **Sorores minores** — "lesser sisters"; the feminine expression of Francis's fratres minores

## Writing Style
Intimate, direct, theologically precise. Her letters to Agnes are among the finest short mystical texts of the era.
