# Anthony Thiselton
*1937– · Anglican · Philosophical Biblical Hermeneutics*

> **Why he's here:** The most important bridge between continental philosophical hermeneutics and evangelical biblical studies. His *The Two Horizons* (1980) introduced [[Hans-Georg Gadamer]] and [[Ludwig Wittgenstein]] to a generation of evangelical scholars who had never engaged them. His later *New Horizons in Hermeneutics* (1992) is the most comprehensive survey of hermeneutical theory in relation to biblical interpretation ever written. Thiselton made it impossible for serious evangelical scholars to ignore hermeneutical philosophy.

## Key Concepts
- **Two horizons** — the horizon of the text (its original context, language, world) and the horizon of the interpreter (present situation, questions, assumptions); interpretation is the meeting of both
- **Gadamer for evangelicals** — effective history, fusion of horizons, and the role of tradition in understanding — all applicable to Scripture without abandoning its authority
- **Wittgenstein and language games** — meaning is use; words function differently in different contexts; biblical language must be understood in its own "game"
- **Speech-act theory** — following Austin and Searle; Scripture doesn't just describe but performs — promises, commands, warns; anticipates [[Kevin Vanhoozer]]
- **The transformative text** — Scripture's meaning is not just cognitive but existential; it creates new possibilities for the reader

## Shaped By
- **[[Hans-Georg Gadamer]]** (primary philosophical influence)
- **Ludwig Wittgenstein** (philosophy of language)
- J.L. Austin / John Searle (speech-act theory)
- **[[Rudolf Bultmann]]** (engaged throughout, critically)

## Influenced
- **[[Kevin Vanhoozer]]** (Speech Act applied to Scripture; explicitly built on Thiselton)
- Evangelical hermeneutics as a discipline
- British evangelical scholarship
