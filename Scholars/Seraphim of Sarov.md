# Seraphim of Sarov
*1754–1833 · Eastern Orthodox · Hesychasm*

> **Why he's here:** The towering figure of Russian Orthodox mysticism and the most accessible expression of the hesychast tradition for modern readers. His conversation with Nicholas Motovilov — where Motovilov reportedly saw Seraphim transfigured in divine light — is the most famous account of theosis in modern Christianity.

## Historical Context
18th–19th century Russia. The hesychast tradition was largely dormant in Russian Orthodoxy; Seraphim revived it through lived example at Sarov monastery and the Diveyevo convent. He lived as hermit, recluse, and staretz (spiritual father) in sequence.

## Biography
Born Prokhor Moshnin in Kursk. Entered Sarov monastery 1778. Spent years as hermit in the forest, then three years standing on a rock in constant prayer, then 1,000 days living as recluse (enclosure). After years of silence, emerged in 1815 to receive thousands of pilgrims daily, greeting all with "My joy!" and "Christ is risen!" regardless of the liturgical season.

## Key Concepts
- **Acquisition of the Holy Spirit** — the goal of Christian life is not rule-keeping or even virtue but the acquisition of the Holy Spirit as transforming presence
- **Prayer of the heart** — hesychast inner prayer (Jesus Prayer) as the method
- **Divine light** — the Tabor light experienced visibly in theosis; the Motovilov conversation is the key text
- **Joy** — Seraphim's defining characteristic; holiness expressed as radiant, uncontainable joy

## Writing Style
Mostly oral. His conversations were recorded by others; *On the Acquisition of the Holy Spirit* (the Motovilov conversation) is the key text. Very accessible.

## Shaped By
- [[Maximus the Confessor]] (theosis theology, mediated through Russian tradition)
- [[Symeon the New Theologian]] (divine light)
- Paisius Velichkovsky (revival of hesychasm in Russia)
- *Philokalia* (compilation of hesychast texts)

## Influenced
- All modern Russian Orthodox spirituality
- [[Kallistos Ware]] (his writings introduce Seraphim to Western readers)
- Charismatic theology (unexpected cross-pollination via Holy Spirit emphasis)

## Primary Works
- *On the Acquisition of the Holy Spirit* (the Motovilov conversation, recorded 1831)
- Various recorded conversations and instructions
