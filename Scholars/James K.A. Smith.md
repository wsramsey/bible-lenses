# James K.A. Smith
*1970– · Evangelical/Reformed · Liturgical Anthropology*

> **Why he's here:** The most intellectually sophisticated evangelical philosopher of his generation. His *Desiring the Kingdom* trilogy rethought Christian education and formation by arguing that humans are fundamentally *lovers* (not thinkers) shaped by *liturgies* (not just ideas). This Augustinian-phenomenological anthropology has reshaped how many evangelicals think about worship, education, and culture.

## Key Concepts
- **Humans as liturgical animals** — we are what we worship; desires and habits (not beliefs) are the core of the self
- **Cultural liturgies** — shopping malls, stadiums, universities are secular liturgies that form desires; Christian counter-formation requires thick liturgical practice
- **Thick vs thin community** — against thin, low-liturgy worship as insufficient formation; recovery of embodied, sacramental practice
- **The imaginary** — following Charles Taylor; the background assumptions that make our world seem obvious; Christian formation must address these
