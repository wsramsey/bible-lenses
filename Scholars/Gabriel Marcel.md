# Gabriel Marcel
*1889–1973 · Catholic · Christian Existentialism*

> **Why he's here:** The Catholic counterweight to Sartre's atheist existentialism. Marcel converted to Catholicism in 1929 and developed a phenomenology of hope, fidelity, and being that offers a personal, relational alternative to both Heideggerian ontology and Sartrean nothingness. His distinction between *problem* and *mystery* is one of the most useful in 20th century philosophy.

## Key Concepts
- **Being vs. having** — *having* treats the world as a collection of objects to possess; *being* is participation in a reality that exceeds possession; the spiritual life moves from having to being
- **Problem vs. mystery** — a *problem* is something in front of me that I can solve by standing apart from it; a *mystery* is something I am involved in; God, freedom, evil, love — these are mysteries not problems
- **Hope** — not optimism (a calculation about outcomes) but a fundamental orientation of being toward the future; his most important contribution
- **Fidelity** — creative fidelity to persons and commitments as the form of love over time; against existentialist rootlessness
- **Intersubjectivity** — the "I" is constituted in relation to "thou"; not isolated consciousness but relational being
