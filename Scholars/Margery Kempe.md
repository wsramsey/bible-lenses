# Margery Kempe
*c. 1373–1438 · Catholic · Lay/Affective Mysticism*

> **Why she's here:** The author of the first autobiography in English. A married laywoman from Norfolk with 14 children, she experienced visions, compulsive weeping during religious experiences, and undertook pilgrimages to Jerusalem, Rome, and Compostela. Her account is raw, unpolished, and theologically real — the spiritual life of someone who isn't a monk or nun.

## Historical Context
Same era as the Cloud author and Julian. Margery knew Julian personally and sought her spiritual counsel — one of the few documented meetings between mystical figures of the era. Her weeping was controversial; contemporaries often found her insufferable and she was interrogated multiple times for heresy.

## Biography
Born to a prominent burgess family in King's Lynn, Norfolk. Married John Kempe, had 14 children. After a postpartum breakdown and vision of Christ, she underwent spiritual transformation. Negotiated a vow of celibacy with her husband. Dictated (she was probably illiterate) her book to two scribes in her old age.

## Key Concepts
- **Compunction and tears** — involuntary weeping as spiritual gift; she couldn't stop, and it was embarrassing
- **Bridal mysticism** — conversations and intimacies with Christ as spiritual spouse
- **Pilgrimage** — literal travel as spiritual discipline; Jerusalem especially transformed her
- **Lay mysticism** — holiness available outside monastery; marriage and family as spiritual context

## Writing Style
First person, conversational, repetitive, occasionally chaotic. Refers to herself in third person ("this creature"). Fascinating precisely because it's not polished.

## Shaped By
- [[Julian of Norwich]] (met personally; Julian encouraged her)
- [[Bridget of Sweden]] (pilgrim role model)
