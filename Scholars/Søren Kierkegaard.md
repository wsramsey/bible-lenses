# Søren Kierkegaard
*1813–1855 · Christian · Existentialist / Anti-Hegelian*

> **Theological lean:** Indirect communication, Leap of faith, Individual before God, Three stages

## Hermeneutical Lenses

- [[Lens - Existentialist]] — [Existentialist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_existentialism)
  *Meaning through personal authentic encounter; Bultmann, Kierkegaard, Tillich*
- [[Lens - Philosophical Theology]] — [Philosophical Theology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophical_theology)
  *Applies philosophy to theological questions; Aquinas, Tillich, Plantinga*
- [[Lens - Phenomenological]] — [Phenomenological](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenomenology_of_religion)
  *Experience of the sacred as primary; Ricoeur, Otto, Marion*

## Influenced

- [[Karl Barth]] — *foundational*
  > Barth's Romans commentary is saturated with Kierkegaard's 'infinite qualitative difference'
- [[Jürgen Moltmann]] — *formative*
  > Moltmann engages Kierkegaard's concept of despair in Theology of Hope
- [[Paul Tillich]] — *philosophical*
  > Tillich's existentialist theology is unthinkable without Kierkegaard's categories
- [[rudolph-bultmann]] — *philosophical*
  > Bultmann's demythologization is existentialist in Kierkegaard's sense
- [[Paul Ricoeur]] — *foundational*
  > Ricoeur's hermeneutics of the subject engages Kierkegaard's indirect communication

## Primary Works

- *Either/Or*
- *Fear and Trembling*
- *The Sickness Unto Death*
- *Concluding Unscientific Postscript*
