# Kristin Kobes Du Mez
*1975– · Reformed Historian · Evangelical Studies*

> **Why she's here:** Her *Jesus and John Wayne* (2020) is the most widely read and discussed academic book about American evangelicalism in recent memory. A Calvin University historian, Du Mez argues that white evangelical support for authoritarian leadership and complementarian gender roles is not a betrayal of evangelicalism but its cultural logic — built over decades through celebrity pastors, Christian publishing, and militarized masculinity.

## Key Concepts
- **Militant masculinity** — white evangelical culture constructed a specifically martial vision of Christian manhood; this is the cultural background of its politics
- **Evangelical industrial complex** — the publishing, speaking, and media culture that shaped evangelical popular consciousness
- **Complementarianism as culture** — gender complementarianism is not just theological but functions as cultural identity marker
