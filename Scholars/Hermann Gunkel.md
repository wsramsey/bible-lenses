# Hermann Gunkel
*1862–1932 · Protestant · Form Criticism*

> **Why he's here:** The founder of Form Criticism (*Formgeschichte*) and the scholar who most transformed how we read the Psalms and Genesis. Gunkel moved behind [[Wellhausen]]'s source documents to ask: what oral forms and genres existed before the text? Every psalm belongs to a *Gattung* (genre/type) with a *Sitz im Leben* (life setting — the social occasion that generated it). This approach opened the pre-literary world behind the biblical text.

## Key Concepts
- **Gattung** (genre/type) — literary forms have typical structures, vocabulary, and settings; identifying the genre is the first hermeneutical step
- **Sitz im Leben** (life setting) — every genre arises from a recurring social situation; hymns from worship, laments from crisis, sagas from tribal storytelling
- **Form Criticism** — identify the genre → reconstruct the life setting → understand the text's original function → interpret its meaning
- **Religionsgeschichte** (History of Religions) — Israel's religion must be understood in the context of ancient Near Eastern religions; not unique but in dialogue with them

## Shaped By
- [[Julius Wellhausen]] (source criticism he moved beyond)
- Comparative religion scholarship (Babylonian parallels)

## Influenced
- **[[Rudolf Bultmann]]** (applied Gunkel's method to the Gospels)
- **Martin Dibelius** (Gospel form criticism)
- All subsequent Psalms scholarship
- Modern commentary series structure (form, genre, setting)

- *Commentary on Genesis* (1901)
- *The Psalms* (1926)
