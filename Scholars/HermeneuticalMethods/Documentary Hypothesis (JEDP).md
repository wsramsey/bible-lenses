# Documentary Hypothesis (JEDP)

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Documentary_hypothesis)

> The dominant theory of Pentateuchal composition in critical scholarship. The Torah was compiled from four originally independent source documents: J (Yahwist — uses YHWH), E (Elohist — uses Elohim), D (Deuteronomist — Deuteronomy and the historical books), P (Priestly — genealogies, laws, priestly material). Proposed by Wellhausen (1878), building on earlier work. Revolutionized how scholars read the OT.

## Philosophical Roots
- Spinoza (Theologico-Political Treatise — Moses didn't write the whole Torah)
- Jean Astruc (1753 — noticed two divine names)
- Julius Wellhausen (Prolegomena to the History of Israel, 1878)

## Key Practitioners
- [[Julius Wellhausen]]
- [[Martin Noth]]
- [[Frank Moore Cross]]
- [[Richard Elliott Friedman]]

## How It Works
1. Notice repetitions, contradictions, and style differences in the text
2. Two creation accounts (Gen 1 = P, Gen 2-3 = J) with different divine names and theologies
3. Two flood accounts interleaved (J: 7 pairs of clean animals; P: 2 of each)
4. Trace each source's distinctive vocabulary, theology, and concerns
5. Reconstruct the history of Israel's religion from the sources' development

## Primary Sources It Illuminates
- Genesis (two creation accounts, two flood accounts)
- Exodus
- Leviticus
- Numbers
- Deuteronomy

## Strengths
Explains the doublets and contradictions in the text. Grounded in close reading of the Hebrew. Revolutionized OT scholarship.

## Critiques
Increasingly questioned — Scandinavian school prefers oral tradition model. 'Final form' canonical critics (Brevard Childs) say the redacted whole is the theological text, not the hypothetical sources. Conservative scholars defend Mosaic authorship.
