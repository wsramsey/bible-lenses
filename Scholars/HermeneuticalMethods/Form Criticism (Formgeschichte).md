# Form Criticism (Formgeschichte)

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Form_criticism)

> Identifies the oral forms (genres) that existed before the written text, and their Sitz im Leben (life setting in the community). Applied to the Gospels by Bultmann and Dibelius: the Gospel traditions circulated as independent units (pronouncement stories, miracle stories, legends, sayings) before being collected. Applied to the OT by Hermann Gunkel: the Psalms had specific liturgical settings (individual lament, communal praise, royal psalm, etc.).

## Philosophical Roots
- Romantic interest in oral tradition and folk literature
- Hermann Gunkel (OT form criticism)
- Martin Dibelius
- Rudolf Bultmann

## How It Works
1. Identify the literary form/genre of each pericope (unit)
2. Determine the Sitz im Leben — what community need created/preserved this form?
3. Work backward from the written form to the oral tradition behind it
4. Assess authenticity — some forms preserve early tradition, others reflect community creation
5. Bultmann: most Gospel material reflects the early church's needs, not Jesus's actual words

## Primary Sources It Illuminates
- Psalms (genre analysis)
- Gospel pericopes
- Sayings of Jesus
- Miracle stories

## Strengths
Explains the pre-literary history of the text. Identifies the oral roots of written tradition. Recognizes community context in transmission.

## Critiques
Assumes oral tradition is unreliable by default. Ignores eyewitness testimony (Bauckham's counter-argument). The 'community creation' model assumes more than it can prove. N.T. Wright and Richard Bauckham both push back strongly.
