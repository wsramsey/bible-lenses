# New Perspective on Paul

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Perspective_on_Paul)

> A paradigm shift in Pauline studies beginning with E.P. Sanders (1977) arguing that 1st-century Judaism was NOT a religion of works-righteousness earning salvation, but of 'covenantal nomism' — you get IN by grace (election), you stay IN by Torah observance. This means Paul's opponents in Galatians were not legalists trying to earn salvation but were enforcing Jewish identity boundary markers. 'Works of the Law' = circumcision, food laws, Sabbath — not moral earning.

## Philosophical Roots
- Reaction against Lutheran reading of Paul
- Dead Sea Scrolls discovery (1947)
- Historical-Critical method

## Key Practitioners
- [[E.P. Sanders]]
- [[James D.G. Dunn]]
- [[N.T. Wright]]
- [[Michael Bird (Post-NPP)]]

## How It Works
1. Read Paul's opponents as real 1st-century Jews, not as Luther's Catholic opponents
2. 'Justification' = declaration of covenant membership, not forensic acquittal of guilt
3. 'Works of the Law' = identity boundary markers (circumcision, food laws) not moral achievement
4. 4QMMT uses the exact phrase 'works of the Torah' for halakhic boundary disputes — confirms NPP reading
5. Paul's concern is Gentile inclusion in the covenant people, not individual guilt/merit

## Primary Sources It Illuminates
- Galatians
- Romans
- Philippians 3
- 4QMMT Dead Sea Scroll
- Psalms of Solomon
- 1 Maccabees

## Strengths
Takes Jewish sources seriously. Explains Paul's actual argument in Galatians 2. Connects Paul to real 1st-century debates rather than 16th-century ones.

## Critiques
Dunn and Wright differ significantly on details. Some argue it under-reads Paul's concern for individual sin and forgiveness (John Piper, Tom Schreiner). Post-NPP scholars (Bird, Gathercole) attempt synthesis.
