## Historical Context
13th century Europe was experiencing a university revolution — Paris, Bologna, Oxford. Aristotle's works had just been re-introduced to the West through Arabic translations (Averroes, Avicenna). Many church authorities wanted to ban Aristotle as dangerous. Aquinas's project was to show that Aristotle and Christianity were not enemies but could be synthesized. The Crusades were ongoing — Islam was a sophisticated intellectual presence, not just a military one.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Born into the Norman nobility — his family kidnapped him for a year when he tried to join the Dominicans, considering it beneath their station. They reportedly sent a prostitute to his room; he drove her away with a burning torch. After his release, studied under Albert the Great in Cologne and Paris. So quiet as a student that classmates called him the 'Dumb Ox' — Albert said 'this dumb ox will one day fill the world with his bellowing.'

## Written Where & Why
*Summa Theologica* — left unfinished. After a mystical experience at Mass in December 1273, he stopped writing entirely: 'All I have written seems to me like straw compared to what I have seen.' Died 3 months later. Written as a teaching tool for Dominican students — structured as question, objections, reply, response to objections. *Summa Contra Gentiles* written possibly as a manual for missionaries to Muslims and Jews.

## Contemporaries & Intellectual World
Bonaventure (Franciscan counterpart — they received their doctoral degrees the same day in Paris, rode through the city together), Albertus Magnus (teacher), Roger Bacon (Oxford Franciscan), Ramon Llull (missionary philosopher).

## Writing Style & Influence on Others
The scholastic method: relentlessly organized, dialectical, question-objection-reply. No wasted words. Every objection to his position is stated in its strongest form before being answered. His Latin is precise to the point of being technical. Directly influenced the structure of all Western systematic theology and philosophy.

## Pressures & Controversies
Some of his positions were condemned by the Bishop of Paris (Étienne Tempier) in 1277 — three years after his death. The Franciscan-Dominican rivalry was fierce. Managing the integration of Aristotle without compromising Christian doctrine was his life's tightrope.

## Multi-Causal Connections


## Reacted Against
- [[Latin Averroism (Siger of Brabant)]] — *The Averroists claimed Aristotle taught the eternity of the world and denied individual immortality — Aquinas refuted them while adopting their Aristotle*
- [[Augustinian Anti-Aristotelianism]] — *Many Franciscans and conservatives wanted to ban Aristotle as dangerous. Aquinas's project is precisely to show Aristotle is not the enemy*

## Enabled By (Technology / Context / Person)
- [[Ibn Rushd (Averroes)]] — *Without the Arabic transmission of Aristotle via Averroes, Aquinas has no Aristotle to Christianize. The Summa is built on a debt to Islamic philosophy*
- [[Albertus Magnus]] — *Aquinas's teacher at Cologne and Paris — the only man of the era whose engagement with Aristotle matched what Aquinas would do. He recognized Aquinas's genius immediately*
- [[University of Paris (13th century)]] — *The institutional context that made the synthesis possible — the first research university, where Aristotle was being read, debated, and partially banned simultaneously*

## Forced By (Crisis / Event / Controversy)
- [[Condemnation of 1277 (Bishop Tempier)]] — *219 propositions condemned — some of them Aquinas's. Three years after his death. Shows the Thomistic synthesis was not immediately accepted as orthodox*
- [[Cathar  Albigensian Dualism]] — *The Cathars' rejection of material creation as evil required a robust theology of creation's goodness — the Dominicans (including Aquinas) were founded partly to combat this*

## Translation / Textual Causes
- [[William of Moerbeke (translator)]] — *Moerbeke translated Aristotle's complete works directly from Greek into Latin for Aquinas — Aquinas was working from better translations than anyone before him*

## Biographical Causes (Personal Experience → Theology)
- [[The Dumb Ox — Contemplative Personality]] — *His famous silence in class was mistaken for stupidity. Actually a man who thought before speaking. His theology has the same quality — every objection honored, every answer measured*
- [[Mystical Experience at Mass (December 1273)]] — *After this experience he stopped writing entirely: 'All I have written seems like straw.' The *Summa* is literally unfinished. The man who wrote the most systematic theology in history decided at the end that it was insufficient*
