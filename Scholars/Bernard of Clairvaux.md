# Bernard of Clairvaux
*1090–1153 · Catholic · Affective/Bridal Mysticism*

> **Why he's here:** The most influential spiritual writer of the 12th century and the father of affective mysticism. His 86 sermons on the Song of Songs shaped Western Christian spirituality for 800 years. The idea that love — not just intellect — is the organ of union with God flows from Bernard through the entire Western mystical tradition.

## Historical Context
12th-century Burgundy. The Cistercian reform movement sought to recover Benedictine simplicity against the opulence of Cluny. Bernard founded Clairvaux at age 25 and eventually led 68 daughter houses. He was simultaneously monk, papal adviser, crusade preacher, and the era's dominant theological voice.

## Biography
Born to Burgundian nobility. Entered Cîteaux in 1112, bringing 30 relatives with him. Founded Clairvaux 1115. His personality was immense — he condemned Abelard, preached the Second Crusade, settled papal schisms. Died having changed the face of Western monasticism.

## Key Concepts
- **Amor ipse intellectus est** — "Love itself is understanding"; against purely intellectual approaches to God
- **Four degrees of love** — love of self for self's sake → love of God for self's sake → love of God for God's sake → love of self for God's sake
- **Bridal mysticism** — the soul as bride; Song of Songs as map of mystical union
- **Humility** — foundational to all ascent; 12 degrees of humility from the Rule of Benedict

## Writing Style
Eloquent, passionate, psychologically acute. His Song of Songs sermons never finished (stopped at chapter 3 verse 1 after 86 sermons). Dense but rewarding.
