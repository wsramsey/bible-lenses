# Walker Percy
*1916–1990 · Catholic · Existentialist Fiction*

> **Why he's here:** The Catholic existentialist novelist of the American South. A physician turned writer who converted to Catholicism after tuberculosis and reading Kierkegaard and Dostoevsky. His fiction maps the spiritual malaise of modern secular America; his essays (*Lost in the Cosmos*, *The Message in the Bottle*) are brilliant diagnoses of why humanity is the only species that doesn't know what it is.

## Key Concepts
- **The wayfarer** (*Homo viator*) — the human person as fundamentally a traveler, in search of something; homelessness as the human condition
- **The malaise** — the numbing boredom and dislocation of modern consumer life; his protagonists experience this as the symptom of a spiritual emergency
- **Semiotics and the self** — his non-fiction explores how language shapes and distorts self-understanding; humans are the symbol-using animal
- **Lost in the cosmos** — the irony that humanity can explain everything in the cosmos except the explainer
