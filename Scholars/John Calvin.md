## Historical Context
Calvin came a generation after Luther in a France traumatized by religious war. The Protestant movement had been violently suppressed — the Affair of the Placards (1534) led to mass executions of French Protestants. Calvin fled Paris and eventually landed in Geneva, a city-state trying to establish itself as a Protestant republic. He was expelled, recalled, and spent most of his ministry trying to build a disciplined Christian city-state while managing constant political opposition from Geneva's noble families ('the Libertines').

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Trained as a lawyer — this shapes everything. His theology is systematic, logical, organized like a legal brief. His conversion was what he called 'sudden' — God subdued his 'obstinate heart.' Had no patience for mystical vagueness. Lost his wife Idelette after nine years of marriage. Their son died in infancy. Suffered from migraines, stomach ailments, and kidney stones throughout his ministry, often dictating from his sickbed.

## Written Where & Why
*Institutes of the Christian Religion* — first edition 1536 (age 26!), expanded through his life to its final form in 1559. Started as a defense of French Protestants to King Francis I. Became the systematic theology of the Reformation. Written in Geneva under constant revision. His *Commentaries* cover nearly the whole Bible — he preached through entire books consecutively and the sermons were transcribed by refugees who had fled to Geneva from France and England.

## Contemporaries & Intellectual World
Martin Luther (never met, deeply respected), Melanchthon (corresponded extensively), Heinrich Bullinger (Zurich — key ally), Michael Servetus (anti-Trinitarian whom Calvin controversially had executed for heresy in Geneva, 1553 — haunts his legacy), Sebastian Castellio (opponent on religious tolerance after Servetus execution).

## Writing Style & Influence on Others
Lucid, ordered, relentless logic. Less fire than Luther, more architecture. His Latin is elegant; his French is foundational to the French literary tradition. Preached without notes. The *Institutes* reads like a lawyer building an irrefutable case. His style became the model for Reformed theological writing — clear propositions, thorough objection-handling, heavy Scripture citation.

## Pressures & Controversies
The Servetus execution is the permanent shadow over his legacy — he believed heresy threatened the whole city. The Libertine faction in Geneva challenged his authority for years. Refugees poured into Geneva from persecuted Protestant communities across Europe — he was running a city, a seminary, an international reform movement simultaneously, while in chronic pain.
