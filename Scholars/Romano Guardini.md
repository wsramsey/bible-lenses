# Romano Guardini
*1885–1968 · Catholic · Cultural Theology*

> **Why he's here:** The theologian of the liturgical renewal movement and one of the deepest influences on [[Joseph Ratzinger]] / Benedict XVI. His *The Lord* remains one of the finest Christological meditations of the 20th century. His *The Spirit of the Liturgy* (1918) launched the modern liturgical movement. His *The End of the Modern World* (1950) anticipated postmodernity with remarkable precision.

## Historical Context
Weimar and postwar Germany. Guardini was a beloved professor at Berlin whose chair was abolished by the Nazis in 1939. Returned to Munich after the war; his lectures drew thousands.

## Key Concepts
- **Living form** — reality has an irreducible polarity; neither rationalism nor irrationalism; truth lives in the tension between opposites
- **The Lord** — Christ known through contemplative encounter with the Gospel texts; not historical-critical reconstruction
- **The spirit of the liturgy** — liturgy as the church at play before God; not primarily didactic but doxological
- **End of the modern world** — the modern project (autonomous reason, progress, humanism) has collapsed; Christians must now be a conscious minority
