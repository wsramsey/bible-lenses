# Krister Stendahl
*1921–2008 · Lutheran · New Perspective Grandfather*

> **Why he's here:** His 1963 essay "The Apostle Paul and the Introspective Conscience of the West" is the most consequential short piece in 20th century Pauline studies. Stendahl argued that Luther's reading of Paul through the lens of guilty conscience was a misreading — Paul's problem was not individual guilt but the question of how Gentiles fit into God's plan for Israel. This launched the New Perspective on Paul ([[E.P. Sanders]], [[N.T. Wright]], [[James D.G. Dunn]]).

## Key Concepts
- **Introspective conscience** — Western Christianity (especially Lutheran) reads Paul through Augustine's and Luther's agonized conscience; this is anachronistic; Paul didn't have a guilty conscience before Damascus
- **"What it meant" vs. "what it means"** — biblical scholarship's task is first descriptive (what did this mean in its original context?) before normative (what does it mean for us?); these must be kept separate
- **Paul's robust conscience** — "I have lived before God in all good conscience to this day" (Acts 23:1); Paul as a Jew was Torah-observant without existential crisis
- **Covenantal nomism ante litteram** — anticipated [[E.P. Sanders]]'s thesis
