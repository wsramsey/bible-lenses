## Historical Context
1517 Germany: the Holy Roman Empire was a patchwork of 300+ territories. The papacy was running a massive indulgence campaign (the Jubilee St. Peter's Basilica fundraiser) managed by the Dominican friar Johann Tetzel, who reportedly said 'as soon as the coin in the coffer rings, the soul from purgatory springs.' Corruption at Rome was an open scandal — Pope Alexander VI had openly kept mistresses and murdered rivals. The **printing press** (Gutenberg, 1450) had existed for 67 years — Luther's 95 Theses spread across Germany in two weeks, something literally impossible in any previous century.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Son of a copper miner who scraped together money to send Luther to law school. Caught in a violent thunderstorm in 1505, lightning struck near him and he vowed to become a monk if he survived. Entered the Augustinian monastery — his father never forgave him. Plagued by crippling scrupulosity (obsessive confession, terror of God's wrath). The 'tower experience' — reading Romans 1:17, suddenly understanding that 'the righteousness of God' is not a demand but a gift — broke the psychological and theological knot. Called this his 'second birth.'

## Written Where & Why
*95 Theses* (1517) — nailed or posted to the Wittenberg church door (a standard academic practice), intended as an academic debate, went viral. *Freedom of a Christian* (1520) — written while under threat of excommunication, a conciliatory gesture to the Pope that still demolished indulgences. *Address to the Christian Nobility* (1520) — called German princes to reform the church without Rome. German Bible translation (1522-1534) — done while hiding in Wartburg Castle after being declared an outlaw at the Diet of Worms (1521). The German Bible created modern standard German language.

## Contemporaries & Intellectual World
Erasmus (humanist reformer — initially supportive, then broke with Luther over free will in *On Free Will* vs Luther's *Bondage of the Will* — the most important theological debate of the era), Ulrich Zwingli (Swiss reformer — agreed on most things, broke famously over the Eucharist at Marburg 1529), Philip Melanchthon (closest colleague, systematizer), Thomas Münzer (radical reformer Luther opposed violently), Thomas More (Catholic opponent, later executed by Henry VIII), Cardinal Cajetan, Johann Eck.

## Writing Style & Influence on Others
Volcanic, earthy, polemical, brilliant. Wrote in German for ordinary people — the first major European intellectual to deliberately bypass Latin and address the vernacular public. Pamphlets, sermons, catechisms, hymns. Used woodcuts and satirical images as media (essentially the memes of 1517). His *Table Talk* (recorded by students at dinner) shows a man of enormous humor, rage, and vulnerability. His style influenced German prose for centuries.

## Pressures & Controversies
Declared a heretic and outlaw by Pope Leo X (1520) and Emperor Charles V (1521). Protected only by Frederick the Elector of Saxony, who hid him in Wartburg Castle. Faced enemies on two sides: Rome wanting him dead and radical reformers (Anabaptists, Münzer) going further than he wanted. The Peasants' War (1524-25) — peasants used his ideas to justify revolt; Luther's response condemning them lost him enormous popular support. Tormented by depression, physical illness, and periods of spiritual darkness his whole life.

## Multi-Causal Connections


## Reacted Against
- [[Johann Tetzel  Indulgence Trade]] — *Tetzel's hawking of indulgences for St. Peter's Basilica was the immediate trigger for the 95 Theses*
- [[Scholastic Theology (via Biel)]] — *Gabriel Biel's semi-Pelagian 'doing what is in you' (facere quod in se est) was Luther's target — human effort earning grace*
- [[Pope Leo X]] — *Excommunicated Luther 1520; Luther burned the papal bull publicly*

## Enabled By (Technology / Context / Person)
- [[Printing Press (Gutenberg, 1450)]] — *Without the press, the 95 Theses stay on a Wittenberg door. With it, they're across Germany in two weeks and across Europe in months. The Reformation is technologically impossible without it*
- [[Erasmus's Greek New Testament (1516)]] — *Erasmus published the Greek NT one year before the 95 Theses. Luther used it to show that metanoia means 'repent' (change of mind), not 'do penance' — destroying the sacramental basis for indulgences*
- [[Frederick the Wise (Elector of Saxony)]] — *Protected Luther from execution after the Diet of Worms (1521). Without Frederick, Luther is Jan Hus — burned at the stake*
- [[Wartburg Castle (Exile 1521-22)]] — *Hidden here as 'Junker Jörg' after being declared an outlaw. The isolation produced his German Bible translation — which created modern German language*

## Forced By (Crisis / Event / Controversy)
- [[Council of Constance (Jan Hus, 1415)]] — *Hus was burned despite a safe-conduct promise. When the same demand came to Luther at Worms, he knew exactly what compliance meant*
- [[Avignon Papacy & Great Western Schism]] — *100 years of visible papal corruption meant Luther's challenge landed in pre-loosened ground — no serious person in 1517 thought the papacy was incorrupt*

## Biographical Causes (Personal Experience → Theology)
- [[Thunderstorm Vow (1505)]] — *Lightning strikes near him; he vows to become a monk if he survives. Enters the Augustinian monastery against his father's will*
- [[Monastic Terror  Scrupulosity]] — *Years of crushing guilt that no amount of confession could resolve — the psychological engine that made justification by faith a matter of life and death, not doctrine*
- [[Tower Experience — Romans 117]] — *The 'righteousness of God' is not a demand but a gift — this reading broke the psychological and theological knot. Luther called it his 'second birth'*

## Translation / Textual Causes
- [[Latin Vulgate]] — *poenitentiam agite (do penance) for metanoeite (repent/change your mind) — Luther's German Bible corrected this, removing the textual basis for the sacrament of penance*

## Parallel Convergent (Independent Similar Conclusions)
- [[Jan Hus]] — *Burned 100 years before Luther for essentially the same positions. Luther only discovered the parallel mid-career and was shocked: 'We are all Hussites without knowing it'*
- [[Ulrich Zwingli]] — *Independent parallel reformer in Zurich — reached similar conclusions from the same sources without direct contact with Luther*

## Shared Sources
- [[Augustine of Hippo]] — *Both Luther and Calvin claim Augustine against scholasticism — but they read different Augustines. Luther's Augustine is the anti-Pelagian one on grace and bondage of will*
