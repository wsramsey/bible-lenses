# Gregory the Great
*540–604 · Catholic · Pastoral Theology*

> **Why he's here:** The hinge between the ancient and medieval church. Gregory the Great consolidated Augustinian theology into practical pastoral form, defined the papacy as a servant office, sent Augustine of Canterbury to England, and shaped Western Christian piety for 600 years. His *Pastoral Rule* was the handbook of bishops from the 7th century through the Reformation.

## Historical Context
Late 6th-century Rome — the Western Empire had collapsed (476), Lombards were sacking Italy, plague was endemic. Gregory governed Rome as effectively its civil ruler while serving as bishop. His context forced theology to become practical.

## Biography
Born to Roman senatorial nobility. Became prefect of Rome, then converted his family estate into a monastery. Sent as papal ambassador to Constantinople. Became pope reluctantly in 590 and served 14 years, transforming the institution into a pastoral and administrative powerhouse.

## Key Concepts
- **Servant of the servants of God** (*Servus servorum Dei*) — his self-title; leadership as servanthood
- **Pastoral Rule** — the bishop must diagnose each person's spiritual condition and apply the right medicine; the first systematic pastoral theology
- **Active and contemplative life** — both necessary; Gregory synthesized their tension for Western monasticism
- **Moral allegory** — Scripture's literal sense opens to moral application; his *Moralia* on Job is 35 books of this

## Shaped By

- [[Benedict of Nursia]] (Benedictine monasticism)

## Influenced
