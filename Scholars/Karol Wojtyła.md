# Karol Wojtyła (John Paul II)
*1920–2005 · Catholic · Theology of the Body*

> **Why he's here:** The philosopher-pope whose *Theology of the Body* (129 Wednesday audiences, 1979–1984) constitutes the most ambitious Catholic theology of human sexuality and personhood since Augustine. His *The Acting Person* bridges Thomistic metaphysics and Husserlian phenomenology. Canonized 2014.

## Historical Context
Poland under Nazi occupation, then Soviet communism. Wojtyła was a poet, playwright, actor, philosopher, and priest simultaneously. His experience of totalitarianism — which sought to define the person instrumentally — drove his personalist philosophy.

## Key Concepts
- **Theology of the Body** — the human body is a theology; sexual difference and nuptial union reveal Trinitarian love; the body "makes visible what is invisible"
- **Gift** — the fundamental structure of creation is gift; the person is fulfilled not by self-assertion but by self-donation
- **Personalism** — the person as irreducible subject; Kant's formula "never merely as means" elevated into theological anthropology
- **Spousal analogy** — the relationship of Christ to the Church is the ultimate referent for understanding human sexuality and gender
