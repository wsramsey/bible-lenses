# Sandra Schneiders
*1936– · Catholic · Theological Hermeneutics*

> **Why she's here:** The most rigorous Catholic feminist biblical hermeneutician of her generation. Her *The Revelatory Text* (1991) offered a full theological hermeneutics drawing on [[Hans-Georg Gadamer]], [[Paul Ricoeur]], and feminist criticism to argue that Scripture is revelatory precisely in its capacity to transform the reader — and that feminist critique is a legitimate mode of that transformation.

## Key Concepts
- **The revelatory text** — Scripture is not an information source but a medium of encounter with the living God; its meaning is transformative, not merely cognitive
- **World behind / of / in front of the text** — following Ricoeur; the text creates a "world" the reader can inhabit; meaning is in front of the text, not locked behind it
- **Feminist hermeneutics of suspicion** — the text has been produced and interpreted in patriarchal contexts; a feminist reading exposes this and recovers suppressed voices
- **Religious life** — her other major contribution; wrote the definitive theological account of women's religious life in the post-Vatican II church
