# Friedrich Schleiermacher
*1768–1834 · Protestant · Father of Modern Hermeneutics*

> **Why he's here:** The founder of modern hermeneutics as a systematic discipline and the father of liberal Protestant theology. Every subsequent discussion of how we interpret texts — biblical or otherwise — begins here. His two contributions are distinct but related: (1) hermeneutics as the general art of understanding any text, and (2) theology grounded in religious experience (*Gefühl*) rather than doctrine or metaphysics.

## Historical Context
Post-Kantian Germany. Kant had limited knowledge to phenomena and made metaphysics impossible. Schleiermacher salvaged religion by relocating it in feeling/experience rather than cognition — a move that haunts Protestant theology to this day.

## Key Concepts
- **General hermeneutics** — interpretation is not specific to Scripture or law but a general human activity; the same principles apply to any text
- **The hermeneutical circle** — understanding the whole requires understanding the parts, which requires understanding the whole; the interpreter must move between them
- **Psychological/grammatical interpretation** — interpretation involves both understanding the language (grammatical) and recreating the mental world of the author (psychological/divinatory)
- **Feeling of absolute dependence** (*schlechthinniges Abhängigkeitsgefühl*) — the essence of religion; not doctrine, not ethics, but a prereflective sense of utter dependence on the infinite
- **Divination** — the interpreter "divines" the author's intention; almost empathetic reconstruction

## Shaped By
- Kant (epistemological crisis he responded to)
- Romanticism (emphasis on feeling, individuality, genius)
- Moravian Brethren (his early formation; piety he never fully left)

## Influenced
- **[[Wilhelm Dilthey]]** (extended his hermeneutics to all human sciences)
- **[[Hans-Georg Gadamer]]** (critiqued him but built on him)
- **[[Rudolf Bultmann]]** (existential hermeneutics)
- All liberal Protestant theology
- Modern pastoral counseling (empathy as method)

## Primary Works
- *On Religion: Speeches to Its Cultured Despisers* (1799)
- *The Christian Faith* (*Glaubenslehre*, 1821–22)
- *Hermeneutics and Criticism* (lectures, pub. posthumously)
