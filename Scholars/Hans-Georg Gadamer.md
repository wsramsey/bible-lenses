# Hans-Georg Gadamer
*1900–2002 · Philosopher · Truth and Method*

> **Why he's here:** The most important hermeneutical philosopher of the 20th century and the framework within which all serious biblical interpretation now operates — whether scholars acknowledge it or not. His *Truth and Method* (1960) demolished the Enlightenment ideal of the objective, presuppositionless interpreter and replaced it with a model of understanding as dialogue between horizons. Every claim to "just read the text" is an argument with Gadamer.

## Historical Context
Post-Heidegger Germany. Gadamer was Heidegger's student and took his phenomenology of *Dasein* (being-in-the-world) in a hermeneutical direction. Lived to 102; his longevity meant he engaged postmodern thinkers (Derrida, Habermas) directly.

## Key Concepts
- **Fusion of horizons** (*Horizontverschmelzung*) — the interpreter's horizon (historical situation, prejudices, questions) and the text's horizon meet and fuse; understanding is not the interpreter vanishing into the text but two horizons merging into a new understanding
- **Effective history** (*Wirkungsgeschichte*) — every text has a history of effects; we always read texts through their reception history whether we know it or not; you cannot read Romans as if Augustine, Luther, and Barth never existed
- **Prejudice** (*Vorurteil*) — pre-judgments are not obstacles to understanding but its conditions; the Enlightenment prejudice against prejudice is itself a prejudice
- **The classic** — texts that prove inexhaustible; they always have more to say; Scripture as the paradigm case
- **Dialogue** — understanding as conversation; the text "speaks" and the interpreter responds; gadamer is anti-method

## Shaped By
- Martin Heidegger (direct student; phenomenology)
- [[Friedrich Schleiermacher]] and [[Wilhelm Dilthey]] (hermeneutical tradition he critiqued)
- Plato (dialogue as the model of thinking)
- Aristotle (practical wisdom/phronesis)

## Influenced
- **[[Anthony Thiselton]]** — brought Gadamer into evangelical biblical studies
- **[[Paul Ricoeur]]** — engaged him throughout his career
- **[[N.T. Wright]]** — Critical Realism is partly a response to Gadamer
- **[[Kevin Vanhoozer]]** — Speech Act theory partly as evangelical answer to Gadamer
- All contemporary hermeneutics and literary theory

## Primary Works
- *Truth and Method* (*Wahrheit und Methode*, 1960)
- *Philosophical Hermeneutics* (essays, 1976)
- *The Relevance of the Beautiful* (1977)
