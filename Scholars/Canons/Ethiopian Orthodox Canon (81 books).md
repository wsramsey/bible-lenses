# Ethiopian Orthodox Canon (81 books)
*81 books · Ethiopian Orthodox (Tewahedo) · Finalized: 4th century AD — but reflects ancient usage*

> The largest Christian biblical canon. Includes all Catholic deuterocanon plus: 1 Enoch (Henok), Book of Jubilees (Kufale), 1-3 Meqabyan (different from 1-3 Maccabees), Paralipomena of Baruch, and others. Ethiopia received Christianity in the 4th century and preserved texts lost elsewhere. 1 Enoch survived complete ONLY in Ethiopic (Ge'ez) — the Dead Sea Scrolls fragments confirm its antiquity.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthodox_Tewahedo_biblical_canon)

## Disputed Books
1 Enoch is canonical here — it is quoted in Jude 14-15 in the NT but rejected everywhere else. This makes the Ethiopian canon uniquely important for understanding the NT's own sources.

## Theological Impact
The Ethiopian church's canon reveals that the Western Protestant 66-book canon is a historically late and geographically limited decision. The church that may have the oldest continuous Christian tradition (Queen of Sheba, Ethiopian eunuch in Acts 8) has a much larger canon.

## Key Canon Decisions
- **Frumentius (c. 330 AD)** — First bishop of Ethiopia — brought Alexandrian Christianity and its canon
- **Council of Nicaea's influence** — Ethiopia aligned with Alexandria, not Rome
