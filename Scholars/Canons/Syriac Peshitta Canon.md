# Syriac Peshitta Canon
*Originally 22 NT books (no Revelation, 2 Peter, 2-3 John, Jude) books · Syriac Christianity · Finalized: c. 200 AD (OT); 5th century AD (NT)*

> The standard Bible of Syriac-speaking Christians. OT translated from Hebrew (not LXX). NT originally lacked the 4 shorter Catholic Epistles and Revelation — these were added later under Western influence. The Diatessaron (Tatian's harmony of the four Gospels, c. 170 AD) was used in Syriac churches instead of four separate Gospels for two centuries.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peshitta)

## Disputed Books
Revelation was absent from Syriac canon until 508 AD. 2 Peter, 2-3 John, Jude absent from original Peshitta NT.

## Theological Impact
Syriac Christianity developed largely independently of Greek Christianity — different theological vocabulary, different liturgical tradition (Aramaic, the language closest to Jesus's own). Thomas Christianity in India traces to this tradition.

## Key Canon Decisions
- **Tatian's Diatessaron (c. 170 AD)** — Gospel harmony used in place of four separate gospels — shows early canon fluidity
- **Rabbula of Edessa (d. 435)** — Replaced Diatessaron with four separate Gospels
