# Eastern Orthodox Canon
*varies: 49-51 OT books + 27 NT books · Eastern Orthodox · Finalized: Quinisext Council (692) — but practice varies by church*

> Generally follows the LXX tradition with deuterocanonical books, but the exact list varies by Orthodox church. Greek Orthodox includes 1 Esdras, Prayer of Manasseh, Psalm 151. Russian Orthodox follows slightly different list. All 27 NT books. The Orthodox never had a single authoritative council define the canon like Trent did for Catholics.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biblical_canon#Eastern_Orthodox_Church)

## Disputed Books
1 Esdras, 3 Maccabees, 4 Maccabees (some churches), Psalm 151, Prayer of Manasseh — vary by tradition

## Theological Impact
The absence of a dogmatic canon definition means Orthodox theology is less defined by explicit canon boundaries and more by living tradition and liturgy. Scripture is encountered primarily through worship.

## Key Canon Decisions
- **Quinisext Council / Council in Trullo (692)** — Referenced multiple canon lists without choosing one — reflects Orthodox comfort with diversity
- **Council of Jerusalem (1672)** — Affirmed deuterocanonicals against Protestant influence
