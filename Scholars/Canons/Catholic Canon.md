# Catholic Canon
*73 books · Roman Catholic · Finalized: Council of Trent (1546) — defined dogmatically*

> 46 OT books (including 7 deuterocanonicals: Tobit, Judith, 1-2 Maccabees, Wisdom, Sirach, Baruch) + 27 NT books. The deuterocanonicals are called 'deuterocanonical' by Catholics (second canon — added later) not 'apocryphal' (hidden/spurious). Council of Trent formally defined this in response to Luther removing these books.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biblical_canon#Catholic_Church)

## Disputed Books
The 7 deuterocanonical books removed by Protestants; Jerome himself doubted some of them

## Theological Impact
2 Maccabees 12:43-45 (prayers for the dead, offering for sin) is the primary Catholic text for purgatory. Its removal from Protestant Bibles is directly tied to the doctrinal difference.

## Key Canon Decisions
- **Council of Hippo (393)** — First formal list including deuterocanon
- **Council of Carthage (397)** — Ratified Hippo's list
- **Council of Florence (1442)** — Reaffirmed the full list
- **Council of Trent (1546)** — Dogmatic definition in response to Reformation
