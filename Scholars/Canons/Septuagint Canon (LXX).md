# Septuagint Canon (LXX)
*39 + 15 deuterocanonical (varies by manuscript) books · Hellenistic Jewish / Early Christian · Finalized: c. 100–50 BC (core) + additions through 1st century AD*

> Greek translation of Hebrew Scriptures plus additional books not in the Hebrew Bible. This was THE Bible of the early church and of most NT authors — Paul, Matthew, and others quote LXX not Hebrew. Includes Tobit, Judith, 1-2 Maccabees, Wisdom, Sirach, Baruch, additions to Daniel and Esther.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Septuagint)

## Disputed Books
The deuterocanonical books — accepted by Catholics and Orthodox, rejected by most Protestants

## Theological Impact
Because the NT was written in Greek and quotes LXX, several key NT theological words (parthenos=virgin in Isa 7:14; hilasterion=mercy seat/propitiation) derive from LXX translation choices that differ from the Hebrew.

## Key Canon Decisions
- **Letter of Aristeas (legendary)** — Claims 72 scholars translated Torah in 72 days with divine inspiration
- **Council of Hippo (393 AD)** — Affirmed LXX-based canon including deuterocanon
- **Jerome's Vulgate (382-405 AD)** — Translated from Hebrew for OT, creating tension with LXX-based tradition
