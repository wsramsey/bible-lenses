# Armenian Canon
*Includes 3 Corinthians books · Armenian Apostolic · Finalized: 5th century AD*

> The Armenian Bible (translated in the 5th century, called the 'Queen of Translations') included 3 Corinthians as canonical for centuries. First nation officially to adopt Christianity (301 AD). The Armenian canon also includes the Letter of the Corinthians to Paul and the History of Joseph the Carpenter in some manuscripts.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Bible)

## Disputed Books
3 Corinthians — an anti-Gnostic letter written as if from Paul, defending bodily resurrection. Canonical in Armenia until the modern era.

## Theological Impact
Shows that the NT canon was not universally settled until much later than usually assumed. 3 Corinthians influenced Armenian Christology.

## Key Canon Decisions
- **Gregory the Illuminator (c. 301 AD)** — Christianization of Armenia — canon received from Syrian/Caesarean sources
- **Mesrop Mashtots (c. 405 AD)** — Created Armenian alphabet specifically to translate the Bible
