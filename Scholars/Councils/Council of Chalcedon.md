# Council of Chalcedon
*451 AD · Ecumenical Council*

> Defined Christ as one person in two natures (divine and human) without confusion, change, division, or separation. The Chalcedonian definition remains the standard for Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant Christology. Rejected by Oriental Orthodox (Coptic, Ethiopian, Armenian) who remain 'miaphysite.' The great Christological dividing line.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Chalcedon)

## Doctrine Resolved
**Two natures of Christ — against both Nestorianism and Eutychianism**

## Key Participants
- Leo the Great (Tome of Leo)
- Marcian (Emperor)
