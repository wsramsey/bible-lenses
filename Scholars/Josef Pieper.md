# Josef Pieper
*1904–1997 · Catholic · Thomistic Philosophy*

> **Why he's here:** The writer who made Aquinas readable for the 20th century. Pieper translated Thomistic philosophy into luminous short essays on leisure, hope, love, the virtues, and the meaning of the university. C.S. Lewis praised *Leisure the Basis of Culture* as one of the most important books of his time.

## Key Concepts
- **Leisure** — not idleness but receptive openness; the capacity to receive reality as gift; the basis of culture and contemplation
- **The four cardinal virtues** — each treated in separate short volumes: prudence, justice, fortitude, temperance
- **Hope** — the virtue directed toward the not-yet; against both presumption and despair
- **Receptivity** — knowing as receiving, not just constructing; the Thomistic alternative to Kant
