# Arius of Alexandria
*256–336 AD · Suppressed: 325 AD (Nicaea), though Arianism survived for 50+ years after · Method: council-condemnation / exile / writings destroyed*

> ⚠️ This voice was suppressed. Its inclusion here does not imply endorsement — it reflects the historical reality that the shape of orthodoxy was partly determined by who had institutional power to silence dissent.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arius)

## Their Theology
The Son is the first and greatest of God's creatures — 'there was a time when he was not.' God is absolutely one and cannot share divinity. The Son is divine but subordinate and created. Scripture passages about Jesus's ignorance, weakness, and subordination (Mark 13:32, John 14:28) are his evidence.

## Suppressed By
- [[Athanasius of Alexandria]]
- [[Council of Nicaea I (325)]]

## What Survived Despite Suppression
Survived for decades after Nicaea — most Germanic barbarian tribes converted to Arian Christianity. Jehovah's Witnesses, Christadelphians, and some Unitarians hold broadly Arian positions today.

## Why It Matters
The Council of Nicaea was called specifically to answer Arius. The Nicene Creed (homoousios — same substance) is written against him. Every time the Creed is recited, Arius is being refuted. His view was plausible and scripturally argued — it took 50+ years and significant imperial power to suppress it.

## Unexpected Connection (The idea didn't die)
The Jehovah's Witnesses' theology is the largest Arian movement in history. Their New World Translation of John 1:1 ('the Word was a god') is a direct descendant of Arius's reading.
