# Nestorius of Constantinople
*386–451 AD · Suppressed: 431 AD (Council of Ephesus) · Method: council-condemnation / exile*

> ⚠️ This voice was suppressed. Its inclusion here does not imply endorsement — it reflects the historical reality that the shape of orthodoxy was partly determined by who had institutional power to silence dissent.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nestorius)

## Their Theology
Mary should be called Christotokos (Christ-bearer) not Theotokos (God-bearer) — she bore the human Christ, not God as such. Christ has two persons (or at least two prosopa) not one. The divine Logos is not the subject of Christ's human experiences (he didn't really suffer or die as God).

## Suppressed By
- [[Cyril of Alexandria]]
- [[Council of Ephesus (431)]]

## What Survived Despite Suppression
The Church of the East (Assyrian Church) — often called Nestorian though they dispute the label. Spread to Persia, India, China, Mongolia. At its peak in the 9th century was the largest Christian church geographically. Still exists as the Assyrian Church of the East.

## Why It Matters
Nestorius's condemnation at Ephesus was as much political as theological — Cyril of Alexandria used it to consolidate Alexandrian power over Constantinople. The 'Nestorian' church that spread to Asia is one of the great untold stories of Christian history.

## Unexpected Connection (The idea didn't die)
There are more Christians in India today who trace to the Thomas/Nestorian tradition than there are in most European countries. The history of Christianity in Asia is almost entirely absent from Western textbooks.
