# Jan Hus
*1369–1415 AD · Suppressed: 1415 AD (burned at Constance despite safe-conduct promise) · Method: burning / writings condemned*

> ⚠️ This voice was suppressed. Its inclusion here does not imply endorsement — it reflects the historical reality that the shape of orthodoxy was partly determined by who had institutional power to silence dissent.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jan_Hus)

## Their Theology
Scripture above papal authority. Communion in both kinds for laity (not just priests). Church as the body of the elect, not the institutional hierarchy. Simony and clerical immorality disqualify priests from their office. Drew on Wycliffe's ideas.

## Suppressed By
- [[Council of Constance]]
- [[Pope Martin V]]

## What Survived Despite Suppression
Hussite movement (survived in Bohemia as semi-independent church). Unitas Fratrum (Bohemian Brethren) → Moravians under Zinzendorf → influenced Wesley. His martyrdom radicalized a generation — the Hussite Wars (1419-1434) were the first religious war of the Reformation era, 100 years before Luther.

## Why It Matters
Luther discovered mid-career that he was essentially a Hussite: 'We are all Hussites without knowing it.' Hus proved the Council was willing to break its promises to burn heretics — when Luther stood at Worms (1521), he knew exactly what refusing to recant could mean.

## Unexpected Connection (The idea didn't die)
The Moravian church (direct Hussite descendant) sent missionaries to Jamaica in 1754. Their converts became leaders in the abolitionist movement. Hus → Zinzendorf → Moravians → Jamaican Christianity → Caribbean liberation theology. A chain from a Czech bonfire in 1415 to Caribbean abolition.
