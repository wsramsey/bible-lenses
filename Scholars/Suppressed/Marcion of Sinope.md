# Marcion of Sinope
*85–160 AD · Suppressed: c. 144 AD (excommunicated from Rome) · Method: excommunication / writings destroyed*

> ⚠️ This voice was suppressed. Its inclusion here does not imply endorsement — it reflects the historical reality that the shape of orthodoxy was partly determined by who had institutional power to silence dissent.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcion_of_Sinope)

## Their Theology
Rejected the OT entirely. The God of the OT (creator, judge) is different from the God of Jesus (loving father). Kept only edited Luke + 10 Pauline letters as his canon. Christ came to free humans from the creator God. Deeply Paulinist — arguably more consistent with Paul than most orthodoxy.

## Suppressed By
- [[Irenaeus of Lyon]]
- [[Tertullian]]
- [[Origen of Alexandria]]

## What Survived Despite Suppression
His influence survived in Manichaeism, some Gnostic groups, and continues to surface in every generation that struggles with OT violence.

## Why It Matters
Marcion forced the orthodox church to define its own canon and articulate why the OT belonged in Christian Scripture. Without Marcion, there may have been no Irenaeus's Against Heresies and no formal biblical canon. The thing that was destroyed shaped what survived.

## Unexpected Connection (The idea didn't die)
Every contemporary Christian who finds the OT God troubling is asking Marcion's question. He didn't disappear — he went underground into every sermon that skips the conquest narratives.
