# Michael Servetus
*1511–1553 AD · Suppressed: 1553 AD (burned in Geneva) · Method: burning (by Protestant Geneva, not Catholic Inquisition)*

> ⚠️ This voice was suppressed. Its inclusion here does not imply endorsement — it reflects the historical reality that the shape of orthodoxy was partly determined by who had institutional power to silence dissent.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Servetus)

## Their Theology
Rejected the Trinity as unbiblical — the word Trinity never appears in Scripture. Christ is the Son of God but not the eternal second person of a Trinity. Also rejected infant baptism. His Christianismi Restitutio tried to restore original Christianity against both Catholic and Protestant corruptions.

## Suppressed By
- [[John Calvin]]
- [[Geneva City Council]]

## What Survived Despite Suppression
Unitarianism, modern anti-Trinitarian movements, Jehovah's Witnesses. His burning became the founding symbol of the case for religious toleration — Sebastian Castellio's response to his execution was the first systematic argument for freedom of conscience in the modern era.

## Why It Matters
He was burned by Protestants — the people who said Scripture alone was the authority — for holding positions derived from Scripture. The irony was not lost on contemporaries. His execution haunts Calvin's legacy and permanently associates the Reformation with its own exercise of the power it claimed to oppose.

## Unexpected Connection (The idea didn't die)
Castellio's response to Servetus's burning ('to burn a man is not to prove a doctrine') is the direct intellectual ancestor of John Locke's Letter Concerning Toleration, which shaped Jefferson and the American First Amendment. Religious liberty as a political principle was born from Servetus's ashes.
