# Marguerite Porete
*1250–1310 AD · Suppressed: 1310 AD (burned in Paris) · Method: burning / writings destroyed (mostly)*

> ⚠️ This voice was suppressed. Its inclusion here does not imply endorsement — it reflects the historical reality that the shape of orthodoxy was partly determined by who had institutional power to silence dissent.

[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marguerite_Porete)

## Their Theology
Mirror of Simple Souls — the soul that has been annihilated into God no longer needs the church, sacraments, virtues, or external spiritual practices. Pure love transcends all institutional mediation. The Free Spirit tradition. Her book was burned twice before she was.

## Suppressed By
- [[Bishop of Cambrai]]
- [[Inquisition]]
- [[University of Paris theologians]]

## What Survived Despite Suppression
Her book survived anonymously, was copied and read for centuries without her name attached. Meister Eckhart's theology is closely parallel — he was posthumously condemned partly for similar ideas. Thomas Merton and contemporary mystics engage her tradition.

## Why It Matters
She was the first woman burned for heresy in France. Her theology challenges institutional Christianity at its root — the church is not the mediator of God. Her book is now considered a classic of Christian mysticism.

## Unexpected Connection (The idea didn't die)
Her Mirror was read in England as an anonymous 'holy woman's' work for 200 years. It influenced the Cloud of Unknowing and the entire English mystical tradition — without anyone knowing a condemned woman had written it.
