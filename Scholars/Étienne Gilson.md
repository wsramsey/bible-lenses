# Étienne Gilson
*1884–1978 · Catholic · Neo-Thomism*

> **Why he's here:** The historian of medieval philosophy who recovered Aquinas as a genuinely original philosopher — not just a baptizer of Aristotle. His central thesis: the Christian revelation of God as "I AM WHO I AM" (Exod 3:14) transformed philosophy permanently by introducing existence (*esse*) as philosophically primary.

## Key Concepts
- **Christian philosophy** — revelation doesn't just supplement reason; it redirects it toward questions reason alone wouldn't have reached
- **Esse** — Aquinas's unique contribution: being as act of existing, not just essence; God as *ipsum esse subsistens* (subsistent being itself)
- **History of philosophy as philosophy** — understanding a thinker's historical context is necessary for understanding their thought
