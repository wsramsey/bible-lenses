# Maurice Blondel
*1861–1949 · Catholic · Philosophy of Action*

> **Why he's here:** The philosopher who gave the *nouvelle théologie* its philosophical foundations. Blondel's central argument in *L'Action* (1893): human willing always surpasses what it achieves; the will always reaches beyond any finite object toward something infinite; this infinite something is the supernatural. Therefore the supernatural is not an alien imposition on human nature but the inner logic of human willing itself. [[Henri de Lubac]]'s *Surnaturel* (1946) is a theological development of Blondel's philosophical argument.

## Key Concepts
- **Action** — the fundamental category of human existence; not thinking or feeling but willing-and-acting
- **The logic of action** — every human action aims at something; achieving that something reveals a further insufficiency; the will is restless and insatiable; this restlessness is the trace of the supernatural
- **Immanence as apologetic method** — start from within human experience and show that it necessarily opens toward the transcendent; against both extrinsicism (supernatural as external imposition) and pure immanentism
- **The option** — at the end of the analysis, a choice is required: acknowledge the supernatural or refuse it; philosophy brings you to the threshold but cannot cross it
