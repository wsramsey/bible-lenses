# Karl Rahner
*1904–1984 · Catholic · Transcendental Thomism*

> **Why he's here:** The most influential Catholic theologian of the 20th century by sheer output and institutional reach. Rahner bridged Aquinas and Heidegger, served as a *peritus* at Vatican II, and produced 23 volumes of *Theological Investigations*. His concept of the "anonymous Christian" and his transcendental method remain among the most debated ideas in modern Catholic theology.

## Historical Context
20th-century Germany. Studied under Heidegger at Freiburg. A Jesuit theologian who became the dominant voice of progressive Catholicism at Vatican II and after. His influence on the Council's theological tone was massive.

## Key Concepts
- **Anonymous Christianity** — persons who respond authentically to the transcendental horizon of their existence (i.e., God) are in some sense oriented toward Christ, even without explicit faith
- **Supernatural existential** — every human person is always-already addressed by God's self-communication; grace is not an addition but the ground of human existence
- **Transcendental method** — theological reflection begins with the conditions of possibility in the human subject; Aquinas via Kant and Heidegger
- **The Holy Mystery** — God as the incomprehensible horizon toward which all knowledge and love reach; negative theology integrated into systematic theology
