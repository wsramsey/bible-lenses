# Maximus the Confessor
*580–662 · Eastern Orthodox · Cosmic Mysticism*

> **Why he's here:** The architect of Orthodox mystical theology. His concept of *theosis* (deification) is the backbone of Eastern Christian spirituality and the deep grammar behind every Orthodox mystic in this vault.

## Historical Context
Seventh-century Constantinople. Maximus wrote during the Monothelite controversy and was ultimately mutilated and exiled for defending two wills in Christ. He died in exile, vindicated posthumously at the Third Council of Constantinople (681).

## Biography
Born to Constantinople nobility. Became imperial secretary, then abandoned court for a monastery. His suffering for orthodoxy gives his mystical theology a martyr's credibility.

## Key Concepts
- **Theosis** — participation in divine nature (2 Pet 1:4); the goal of all Christian life
- **Logos/logoi** — each created thing has a divine reason in the one Logos (Christ)
- **Epektasis** — eternal striving toward God (from Gregory of Nyssa)
- **Cosmic Christology** — Christ as center and recapitulation of all creation
