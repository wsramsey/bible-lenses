# Thérèse of Lisieux
*1873–1897 · Catholic · The Little Way*

> **Why she's here:** The most influential Catholic saint of the 20th century, died at 24. Her *Story of a Soul* sold millions and her "Little Way" — the spirituality of small things done with great love — became a corrective to both spiritual heroism and spiritual despair. She is a Doctor of the Church. Pope Pius X called her "the greatest saint of modern times."

## Historical Context
19th-century Normandy, France — post-Revolution Catholicism, Jansenist rigorism still haunting French piety. Thérèse's Little Way was partly a reaction against a God of fear; she re-centered on merciful love.

## Biography
Born in Alençon to a devout bourgeois family (both parents are saints). Lost her mother at 4; the loss marked her. Entered the Carmelite convent at Lisieux at 15 after lobbying the Pope directly for permission. Died of tuberculosis at 24. Her autobiography was published posthumously by her prioress sister and became a global phenomenon. Declared Doctor of the Church in 1997.

## Key Concepts
- **The Little Way** — holiness through small, ordinary actions done with great love; the elevator vs. the great staircase
- **Spiritual childhood** — total dependence on God as a child on a parent; not infantile but radical trust
- **Merciful love** — her vocation was to be "love in the heart of the Church"; she leaned into divine mercy against Jansenist fear
- **Dark night** — her final 18 months were lived in profound spiritual darkness (not consolation); her faith was not feeling-based

## Writing Style
Intimate, warm, deceptively simple. Her autobiography reads easily; its theological depth only emerges on reflection.
