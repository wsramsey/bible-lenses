# Origen of Alexandria

> 🌍 Origen was **African** — born and educated in Alexandria, Egypt, the greatest intellectual city of the ancient world. The Alexandrian school he led was African Christianity's intellectual heart. His African context (the Library of Alexandria, the cosmopolitan mixing of Jewish, Greek, and Christian thought) produced the most systematic and speculative theology of the early church.

*184–253 · Christian · Patristic / Alexandrian*

> **Theological lean:** Allegorical Exegesis, Universal Restoration (apokatastasis), Pre-existence of souls

## Historical Context
Alexandria under the Severan dynasty and then the chaos of the 3rd century. The greatest library in the ancient world was nearby. Philosophically the most sophisticated city on earth — Platonism, Gnosticism, Judaism, and Christianity competed openly in lecture halls. Roman persecution was episodic and real — Origen's father Leonidas was martyred when Origen was 17.

## Biography — What Shaped This Thinker
Origen reportedly wanted to turn himself in for martyrdom with his father, but his mother hid his clothes so he couldn't leave. Became head of the catechetical school at 18. According to Eusebius, castrated himself based on a literal reading of Matthew 19:12 (later regretted and theologized away). This extreme asceticism shaped his entire approach — the body as something to be disciplined toward spiritual ascent.

## Written Where & Why
Wrote *De Principiis* (first systematic Christian theology) in Alexandria, pushing Platonic categories into every doctrine. Moved to Caesarea Maritima after conflict with Bishop Demetrius of Alexandria. *Hexapla* — massive six-column parallel Bible — done at Caesarea, showing his commitment to textual scholarship. Commentaries written to train students in the spiritual sense of Scripture.

## Contemporaries & Intellectual World
Clement of Alexandria (predecessor and teacher), Plotinus (Neoplatonist — they may have studied under the same teacher, Ammonius Saccas), Tertullian (Latin contemporary — very different method), Hippolytus of Rome.

## Writing Style & Influence on Others
Prodigious — ancient sources claim he wrote more than any man could read. Employed a team of stenographers paid for by his patron Ambrose. First to develop a systematic allegorical method: every text has a literal, moral, and spiritual (allegorical) sense. His commentaries are discursive, exploratory, willing to raise unanswerable questions.

## Pressures & Controversies
Navigating the tension between rigorous philosophical inquiry and church authority. His speculation on pre-existence of souls and universal salvation got him condemned posthumously (553 AD, Second Council of Constantinople) — 300 years after his death. He was tortured in the Decian persecution (250 AD) and died shortly after from the effects.
