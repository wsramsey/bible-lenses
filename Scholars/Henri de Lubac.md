# Henri de Lubac
*1896–1991 · Catholic · Nouvelle Théologie*

> **Why he's here:** The most important Catholic theologian of the 20th century alongside Rahner and Balthasar — and their theological opposite in key ways. De Lubac's *Surnaturel* (1946) blew up the neo-Scholastic two-tier nature/grace system by arguing that humanity has a natural desire for the supernatural. This moved the whole theological center of gravity. His *Catholicism* (1938) recovered the social and cosmic dimensions of Christian doctrine.

## Historical Context
20th-century France. The *nouvelle théologie* movement (de Lubac, Daniélou, Congar, Bouyer) sought to recover patristic and medieval sources against the sterility of late Scholasticism. Temporarily silenced by Rome (1950) for *Surnaturel*; rehabilitated at Vatican II; made cardinal in 1983.

## Key Concepts
- **Desiderium naturale** — humanity has a natural desire for the beatific vision; grace is not a foreign addition but the fulfillment of what we most deeply are
- **Surnaturel** controversy — against the two-story neo-Scholastic system (pure nature + supernatural addition); human nature is always already oriented toward God
- **Spiritual interpretation** — Scripture has multiple senses; the spiritual sense is not optional; his four-volume *Medieval Exegesis* is the major study
- **Catholicism** — the church is not a collection of individuals saved separately but the sacrament of human unity in Christ
