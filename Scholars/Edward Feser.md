# Edward Feser
*1968– · Catholic · Thomistic Philosophy*

> **Why he's here:** The leading analytic Thomist apologist of the contemporary period. Feser translates classical Aristotelian-Thomistic metaphysics into the idiom of analytic philosophy, making rigorous Thomistic arguments for God's existence accessible to philosophically trained readers who find popular apologetics unconvincing.

## Key Concepts
- **Aristotelian-Thomistic cosmological argument** — not the "first cause" strawman but the argument from actuality and potentiality to Pure Act; God as the ground of existence now, not just the first event
- **Formal and final causality** — recovery of Aristotle's four causes against the modern reduction to efficient causality; final causality (teleology) as necessary for ethics and philosophy of mind
- **Philosophy of mind** — argues that materialism about the mind is self-refuting; intellectual soul requires immaterial intellect
- **Capital punishment** — controversial: argued the Catholic tradition supports it against recent magisterial tendency
