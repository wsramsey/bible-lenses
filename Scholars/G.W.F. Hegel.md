# G.W.F. Hegel
*1770–1831 · German Idealism · Absolute Spirit*

> **Why he's here:** Every major theologian from the 19th century onward is in conversation with Hegel — either building on him or reacting against him. His claim that Christianity is the "absolute religion" (the religion in which Spirit knows itself as Spirit) gave theology an immense philosophical boost; his absorption of the Trinity into a dialectical process of self-knowing raised the question of whether his God was the Christian God or a philosophical abstraction. [[Karl Barth]] said you could either follow Hegel or follow the Reformation; you couldn't do both.

## Key Concepts
- **Dialectic** — thesis → antithesis → synthesis (*Aufhebung*, sublation); history moves through contradiction toward higher unity
- **Absolute Spirit** — reality is ultimately mind/spirit knowing itself; the universe is God's self-consciousness developing through history
- **The Trinity as dialectic** — Father (universal, abstract), Son (particular, incarnate), Spirit (the return to unity); Christianity is the philosophical truth in pictorial form
- **History as theodicy** — history is the unfolding of freedom; suffering and evil are necessary moments in Spirit's self-realization
- **The end of history** — Spirit reaches full self-knowledge; for Hegel this happened approximately with the Prussian state and Hegel's own philosophy

## Shaped By
- Kant (reacted against; overcame the thing-in-itself)
- [[Friedrich Schleiermacher]] (contemporary; they disliked each other)
- Lutheran Christianity (formation; never left it formally)

## Influenced
- **[[Karl Marx]]** (inverted Hegel; material dialectic instead of ideal)
- **[[Rudolf Bultmann]]** (existential dialectic)
- **[[Paul Tillich]]** (theology of culture)
- **[[Jürgen Moltmann]]** (dialectical theology of the cross)
- **[[Hans Urs von Balthasar]]** (engaged and refuted)
- Every 19th and 20th century theologian

## Primary Works
- *Phenomenology of Spirit* (1807)
- *Science of Logic* (1812–1816)
- *Elements of the Philosophy of Right* (1820)
- *Lectures on the Philosophy of Religion* (posthumous)
