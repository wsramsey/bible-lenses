# Edith Stein (Teresa Benedicta of the Cross)
*1891–1942 · Catholic · Phenomenology & Carmelite Mysticism*

> **Why she's here:** One of the most extraordinary intellectual-spiritual trajectories of the 20th century. Jewish atheist philosopher → student of Husserl → Catholic convert → Carmelite nun → Auschwitz martyr. Her *Finite and Eternal Being* bridges phenomenology and Scholasticism. Her *Science of the Cross* (written as the Nazis closed in) reframes John of the Cross's dark night through the lens of her own impending death.

## Historical Context
Weimar and Nazi Germany. Stein was Edmund Husserl's star student and assistant. Read [[Teresa of Ávila]]'s autobiography in one night and said "This is the truth." Converted in 1922. Entered Carmel in 1934. Arrested with her sister Rosa in 1942 and murdered at Auschwitz. Canonized 1998; co-patron of Europe.

## Key Concepts
- **Science of the Cross** — the cross not as doctrine but as existential form; to be Christian is to be configured to cruciform existence
- **Empathy** (*Einfühlung*) — her doctoral thesis; phenomenological account of how we know other persons
- **Finite and eternal being** — bridge between Husserl's phenomenology and Aquinas's metaphysics
- **Feminine genius** — her essays on women argue for complementarity and the unique contribution of feminine receptivity
