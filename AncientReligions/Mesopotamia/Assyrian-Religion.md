# Assyrian Religion and the Biblical Prophets
*The empire that destroyed the Northern Kingdom — and the God who used it*

## The Assyrian Empire
The first true empire in history — at its height (c. 700 BC) controlling territory from Egypt to Iran. The Assyrians were feared throughout the ancient world for:
- Systematic brutality as a terror weapon (flaying enemies alive, impaling, deportation of entire populations)
- Sophisticated propaganda machine (the palace reliefs are government propaganda in stone)
- Extraordinary cultural achievement (Ashurbanipal's library at Nineveh — 30,000 cuneiform tablets)

## Assyrian Religion
- **Ashur**: the national god; the divine warrior; the king fights in Ashur's name
- **Ishtar** (Inanna): goddess of love AND war; a paradox that runs through Mesopotamian religion
- **Enlil, Anu, Ea, Sin, Shamash, Adad**: the major divine council members
- The **King as divine mediator**: the Assyrian king is Ashur's representative; wars are sacred duty; conquest is theological

## Jonah and Nineveh
The Book of Jonah sends an Israelite prophet to **Nineveh** — Assyria's capital — to preach repentance. This is theologically explosive:
- Nineveh had recently destroyed the Northern Kingdom (722 BC)
- Assyrian religious propaganda said their god Ashur was supreme
- Jonah's message: the God of Israel cares about Nineveh's repentance
- Nineveh repents; God relents; Jonah is furious

**The theological point**: YHWH's concern extends to Israel's worst enemy. This directly confronts the ANE assumption that national gods care only about their nation.

## Nahum — The Counter-Jonah
Written c. 612 BC — the year Nineveh fell:
"Woe to the city of blood, full of plunder, never without victims!" 

Nahum is the raw celebration of Nineveh's destruction. Both Jonah and Nahum are in the canon — the tension between them is deliberate: God's mercy is real AND God's justice is real.

## Sennacherib's Invasion (701 BC) — Confirmed
Sennacherib's own annals confirm his invasion of Judah:
> "As for Hezekiah the Judean, I shut him up like a caged bird in his royal city Jerusalem. I imposed tribute..."

He doesn't claim to have taken Jerusalem — and indeed he didn't ([[Isaiah]]'s prophecy; the plague that stopped the army). This is one of the most remarkable convergences of biblical and non-biblical history.

## Ashurbanipal's Library — The World's First Library
**30,000 cuneiform tablets** collected from across the empire — the first systematic library in history. Rediscovered by British archaeologist Austen Henry Layard (1849–1876). Contains:
- The Enuma Elish (multiple copies)
- The Epic of Gilgamesh (including the Flood tablet)
- Medical texts, astronomical observations, omen texts
- Letters and administrative records

This library is the source of most of what we know about ancient Mesopotamian religion.
