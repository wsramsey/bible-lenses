# Sumerian Mythology and Its Biblical Connections
*The world's oldest literature — and Abraham's homeland*

## The Sumerians
The world's first literate civilization (c. 3500 BC). They invented:
- Writing (cuneiform on clay tablets)
- The wheel
- Urban civilization
- The first schools
- The first legal codes

Their capital cities — Ur, Eridu, Nippur, Lagash — are the landscape of early Genesis and Abraham's background.

## Enki and Ninhursag — The Garden of Eden Parallel
A Sumerian myth about Dilmun (a pure, primeval land) where:
- There is no disease, death, or suffering
- Enki (the water god) eats forbidden plants and becomes ill
- Ninhursag curses him; he suffers in 8 body parts
- Eventually Ninhursag is persuaded to heal him; creates 8 healing deities, one for each body part
- One of these deities, **Ninti** ("Lady of the Rib"), heals Enki's rib

**The word *Ninti*** means both "Lady of the Rib" AND "Lady who makes live" in Sumerian. Samuel Noah Kramer observed: Genesis may preserve a wordplay that only makes sense in Sumerian — Eve (*Hawwah* = living) was created from a rib (*tsela*). The Sumerian pun connects life and rib in ways the Hebrew doesn't explain.

## The Tower of Babel — Ziggurat Technology
**Genesis 11:1–9**: The people build a city with a tower to the heavens; God confuses their language.

The historical background: **Ziggurats** — stepped pyramidal temples with a shrine at the top — were the defining architectural feature of Mesopotamian cities. The tallest was **Etemenanki** in Babylon (the probable model for Babel's tower):
- 7 stories; perhaps 91 meters high
- Construction began under Nebuchadnezzar II (c. 605–562 BC) — during the Babylonian Exile
- The Jewish exiles would have seen it

The **confusión of languages** may reflect the actual linguistic diversity of the international workforce that built these structures — workers from across the empire, speaking dozens of languages.

## The Flood in Sumerian Literature
The **Eridu Genesis** (c. 1600 BC) is the oldest flood text:
- **Ziusudra** (= Utnapishtim = Noah) is warned by the god Enki
- Survives the flood in a boat
- Receives eternal life as reward

The Sumerian King List (c. 2100 BC) lists kings who reigned for fantastically long periods *before* the Flood (one king reigned 43,200 years) — then "the Flood swept over" and reigns became shorter. This is the background for the long lifespans in Genesis 5.

## Ur of the Chaldees — Abraham's Hometown
**Genesis 11:28–31**: Abraham's father Terah took him from **Ur of the Chaldees** toward Canaan.

Ur has been extensively excavated (Leonard Woolley, 1922–1934):
- Major Sumerian city; population 65,000 at its height
- **The Royal Cemetery** (c. 2600–2500 BC): 1,800 graves; 16 royal tombs with extraordinary gold objects; servants voluntarily killed to accompany royalty in death
- **The Ziggurat of Ur** (c. 2100 BC, Ur-Nammu): still partially standing; 15m high today; originally perhaps 30m

Abraham left this world of human sacrifice, polytheism, and royal theology for Canaan.
