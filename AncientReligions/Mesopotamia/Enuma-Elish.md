# Enuma Elish — The Babylonian Creation Epic
*"When on high..." — the story Genesis is answering*

## What It Is
The primary Babylonian creation myth, named for its opening words: *Enuma elish la nabu shamamu* — "When on high the heavens had not been named." Seven tablets; recited at the Akitu (New Year) festival in Babylon.

## The Story
1. **Before creation**: two primordial waters — **Apsu** (fresh water, male) and **Tiamat** (salt water, female) — mingle and generate the first gods
2. **The younger gods** make noise; Apsu is annoyed and wants to destroy them; Ea kills Apsu first
3. **Tiamat's revenge**: she raises an army of monsters; promotes **Kingu** to lead them
4. **Marduk's offer**: the young warrior god agrees to fight Tiamat in exchange for becoming king of the gods
5. **The battle**: Marduk nets Tiamat, splits her body in two; from her upper half he makes the sky, from her lower half the earth
6. **Human creation**: Marduk creates humans from the blood of the slain Kingu — to serve the gods and do their labor
7. **Marduk's temple** (Esagila) and city (Babylon) established; Marduk proclaimed king of the gods

## The Theology (What It Claims)
- **Creation is the byproduct of divine conflict** — the world is made from a dead monster
- **Humans exist to serve the gods** — to do their menial labor; humanity is a slave race
- **Power is the ultimate reality** — Marduk wins by strength; morality is irrelevant
- **Babylon is the center of the cosmos** — Marduk's temple is the navel of the earth
- **The king is divine** — Babylon's king is Marduk's earthly representative

## How Genesis 1 Responds
Genesis 1 uses the same literary *form* (structured creation narrative) but makes opposite theological claims:

| Enuma Elish | Genesis 1 |
|-------------|-----------|
| Creation from conflict/violence | Creation by peaceful word |
| Tiamat (chaos water) defeated | God separates the waters by command — no conflict |
| Humans made from blood of slain god | Humans made in God's image |
| Humans created as divine slaves | Humans created as God's royal stewards |
| Sun, moon = great gods | Sun, moon = lights (not even named — demoted) |
| Marduk = supreme god | One God, unnamed until Exodus |

**[[John Walton]]** (*The Lost World of Genesis One*): Genesis 1 is a **temple inauguration text** using the ANE genre to say: this entire cosmos is YHWH's temple; humans are his image (idol) placed in his temple to represent his rule.

## The Discovery and Its Impact
When George Smith of the British Museum deciphered the Flood tablet from Ashurbanipal's library (1872), he reportedly jumped up, ran around the room, and began undressing. The similarity to Genesis was immediately obvious and caused enormous controversy — was the Bible borrowing from Babylon?
