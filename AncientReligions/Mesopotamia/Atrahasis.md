# The Atrahasis Epic
*The Babylonian account of creation AND flood — the closest parallel to Genesis 1–9*

## What It Is
The most complete Babylonian creation-and-flood text; covers roughly the same ground as Genesis 1–9:
- Creation of humans (from clay + divine blood)
- Human multiplication and divine annoyance
- Attempts to reduce human population (plague, drought, famine)
- The Flood as final solution
- Atrahasis (= "Exceedingly Wise") survives on a boat

## The Creation of Humanity
Humans are made when a god is **slaughtered** and his flesh and blood mixed with clay. Humans are created to:
- Do the labor the gods were tired of doing
- Serve the gods with food offerings

**The key theological difference from Genesis**: In Atrahasis, humans are slaves created from divine violence. In Genesis, humans are made in God's *image* and given dominion — rulers, not slaves; gardeners, not conscripts.

## The Population Problem
The gods created humans to do their work — but humans multiplied too much and made too much noise. The gods tried:
- Plague (Namtar sent)
- Drought (Adad withheld rain)
- Famine

Each time, Atrahasis prayed and the relevant god relented. Finally **Enlil** proposed the Flood to eliminate humanity entirely.

## The Moral Vacuum
There is no sin in Atrahasis. The humans have done nothing wrong — they simply exist in too large numbers and make too much noise. The gods' motivation is self-interested, not moral.

This illuminates what Genesis is doing: the same literary form, the same narrative structure — but Genesis inserts *morality* as the reason for the Flood. YHWH is not annoyed by noise; He is responding to wickedness.
