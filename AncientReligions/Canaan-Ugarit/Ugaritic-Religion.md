# Ugaritic Religion — The Canaanite Pantheon
*The religion Israel was always fighting — and partly shaped by*

## The Discovery
In 1929, a Syrian farmer's plow hit an ancient tomb near Ras Shamra on the Syrian coast. French archaeologists excavated what proved to be **Ugarit** — a major Bronze Age city destroyed c. 1185 BC (possibly by the Sea Peoples). The find included a royal archive with **1,400 clay tablets** in a previously unknown alphabetic script. Deciphered within months.

The tablets revealed the fullest picture of Canaanite religion ever found — and immediately explained dozens of obscure biblical passages.

## The Ugaritic Pantheon

### El — The Father God
The head of the divine council; called:
- "Bull El, my father"
- "El, the creator of creatures"
- "Father of humanity"
- "The Kindly One, El the Compassionate"

**Biblical connection**: Israel's God is called *El* (generic Semitic word for God), *El Shaddai*, *El Elyon*, *Elohim* (plural of El). The God of Genesis 14 is "El Elyon, Creator of heaven and earth" — precisely the Ugaritic title. Early Israel appears to have identified YHWH *with* El.

### Baal — The Storm God / Elijah's Enemy
The son of El (or sometimes Dagan); the active, warrior god; the god of rain and fertility:
- "Rider of the clouds" (*rkb 'rpt*) — the exact phrase used of YHWH in Psalm 68:4
- Battles Yam (Sea) and Mot (Death) — the chaos and underworld forces

**Biblical connection**: 
- **Elijah vs. Baal** (1 Kings 18): the contest on Mount Carmel is explicit — YHWH or Baal, who sends rain?
- **Psalm 29**: scholarship now recognizes this as a direct adaptation of a Baal hymn — YHWH replaces Baal as the storm god; "the voice of the LORD" (7x) = Baal's thunder
- **"Rider of the clouds"**: YHWH inherits Baal's storm imagery but without Baal's vulnerability and death

### Asherah — El's Consort / Controversial
The mother goddess; El's wife; "Creatress of the gods."

**Controversial biblical connection**: An inscription from **Kuntillet Ajrud** (c. 800 BC) mentions "YHWH of Samaria and his Asherah" — suggesting some Israelites worshiped YHWH *with* a female consort. This is exactly what the prophets condemn. The struggle against Asherah poles (*asherim*) in the OT reflects this background.

### Mot (Death) and Yam (Sea)
The cosmic opponents:
- **Yam** (sea/chaos) battles Baal; connected to the biblical Leviathan, Rahab, the tehom (deep) of Genesis 1
- **Mot** (death) kills and is killed by Baal; the cycle of seasons; connected to Sheol

**Job 41** — the description of Leviathan — makes sense against this background: YHWH controls the sea-monster that in Ugaritic religion defeated the storm god.

## What the Psalms Reveal
Multiple Psalms preserve imagery that only makes sense against Ugaritic background:
- **Psalm 82**: "God presides in the great assembly; he renders judgment among the gods" — the *divine council* of Ugaritic myth, repurposed: Israel's God presides over the divine beings and judges them for failing to execute justice
- **Psalm 29**: the storm theophany; directly parallel to Baal hymns
- **Psalm 89**: "You crushed Rahab like a corpse; you scattered your enemies with your mighty arm" — YHWH's defeat of the chaos monster, paralleling Baal's defeat of Yam
