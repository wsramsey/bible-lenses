# The Baal Cycle
*The closest thing Canaan had to an epic — and the background to half the Psalms*

## The Story (roughly)
The major Ugaritic narrative texts tell the story of Baal's rise to kingship:

**Part 1: Baal vs. Yam** (Sea)
- Yam (the sea god, also called Prince Sea and Judge River) demands Baal be handed over to him
- The divine council submits; Baal doesn't
- The craftsman god Kothar-wa-Khasis makes two magic clubs for Baal
- Baal defeats Yam and takes the kingship

**Part 2: Baal's Palace**
- Baal demands a palace (temple) to mark his kingship
- El gives permission; Kothar builds it in silver and gold
- Baal opens windows and roars — the thunder; rain falls on the earth

**Part 3: Baal vs. Mot** (Death)
- Mot (Death) demands Baal come to the underworld
- Baal descends; vegetation dies; the earth mourns
- Baal's sister Anat finds Mot and kills him (grinds him, sows him)
- Baal returns; the earth revives

## Why This Matters for Reading the Bible

### The Chaos Conflict Motif
YHWH's conflict with the sea/chaos runs throughout the OT:
- **Exodus 14**: YHWH defeats the sea (and Pharaoh, the chaos power) to free Israel — structured as the divine warrior defeating chaos
- **Psalm 74:13–14**: "It was you who split open the sea by your power; you broke the heads of the monster in the waters; you crushed the heads of Leviathan"
- **Isaiah 51:9**: "Was it not you who cut Rahab to pieces, who pierced that monster through?"
- **Job 38**: YHWH's speech from the whirlwind references controlling the sea with gates and bars

**The transformation**: In Ugarit, Baal nearly loses this fight; it's an ongoing cosmic struggle. In the Bible, YHWH commands and the sea obeys (Genesis 1:9; Psalm 93; Job 38:8–11). The chaos is not a co-equal opponent but a servant.

### The Divine Council
Baal's world involves a council of gods meeting on "the mount of assembly" — identical language to Isaiah 14:13 (the king of Babylon's pride: "I will ascend to the mount of assembly") and Psalm 82.

**[[Mark Smith]]** (*The Origins of Biblical Monotheism*): Israel's monotheism developed gradually through a process of **differentiation** — YHWH began as one of the divine beings, then absorbed the functions of El and Baal, then the other divine beings became angels or were demoted.
