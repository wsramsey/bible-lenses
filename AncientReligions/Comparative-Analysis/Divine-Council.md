# The Divine Council — Gods Among Gods
*The biblical texts that assume a heavenly court of divine beings*

## The Phenomenon
Multiple biblical texts assume a **council of divine beings** that meets in heaven, from which YHWH presides:
- **Psalm 82:1** — "God presides in the great assembly; he renders judgment among the 'gods'"
- **Job 1–2** — "The sons of God came to present themselves before YHWH, and Satan also came"
- **1 Kings 22:19** — Micaiah's vision: "I saw YHWH sitting on his throne with all the multitudes of heaven standing around him on his right and on his left"
- **Isaiah 6** — The seraphim crying "holy, holy, holy"; the heavenly court
- **Genesis 6:1–4** — "The sons of God saw that the daughters of humans were beautiful... their children were the heroes of old, the men of renown"
- **Genesis 1:26** — "Let *us* make mankind in our image" — who is the "us"?

## The Ugaritic Background
The Ugaritic texts describe a divine council (*phr ilm* — "assembly of the gods") presided over by El, with various divine beings serving roles. Baal, Yam, Mot, Asherah, and others are members.

The Hebrew word *elohim* (plural of *el*) is used both for "God" (YHWH) and for these council members — which is why Psalm 82 can say "God judges among the *elohim*."

## The Theological Development
**Michael Heiser** (*The Unseen Realm*, *Supernatural*): The biblical picture includes a genuine divine council — not polytheism but a structured heavenly court with YHWH at the apex and divine beings serving beneath him. This is "cosmic geography" — different divine beings assigned to different nations (Deuteronomy 32:8 in the Dead Sea Scrolls version: "according to the number of the sons of God"; MT: "sons of Israel").

The **Second Temple development**: these lesser divine beings become "angels" — messengers of YHWH with no independent divine status. The move from divine council to angelic hierarchy is the internal development of Jewish monotheism, possibly accelerated by contact with Zoroastrianism.

## The Genesis 6 Problem
"The sons of God saw that the daughters of humans were beautiful, and they married any of them they chose... The Nephilim were on the earth in those days."

Three interpretations:
1. **Sethites and Cainites** (Augustine): the "sons of God" = righteous descendants of Seth; "daughters of men" = Cainite women. Avoids the problem.
2. **Divine beings** (early Jewish interpretation, 1 Enoch): divine beings violated creation order by mating with humans; their giant offspring = the Nephilim; this sin provoked the Flood. 1 Enoch elaborates this extensively; the NT references it (Jude 6–7; 2 Peter 2:4)
3. **Kings as divine beings** (some modern scholars): "sons of God" = kings who claimed divine status and abused their power through polygamy

## 1 Enoch and the Watchers
The **Book of 1 Enoch** (2nd–1st c. BC; preserved complete only in Ethiopian canon) develops the divine council mythology extensively:
- 200 "Watchers" (divine beings) descended and took human wives
- Their offspring were the Nephilim (giants)
- They taught humanity forbidden knowledge — metallurgy, magic, cosmetics
- God imprisoned them in the underworld
- The Flood was partly to destroy the Nephilim

1 Enoch is **quoted by Jude 14–15** in the NT — the only direct OT apocryphal quotation in the NT. It was apparently authoritative to early Christians even if not canonical.
