## The Connection Types

### 1. Typological Connections (OT → NT)
Every OT figure or event that points forward to Christ:
- Adam → Christ (second Adam)
- Passover Lamb → Christ the Lamb
- Temple → Christ's body
- Exodus → New Exodus in Christ
- David → Son of David / Messiah

### 2. Influence Connections (Scholar → Scholar)
Who shaped whom:
- Plato → Augustine → Medieval theology
- Aristotle → Aquinas → Scholasticism
- Luther → Calvin → Reformed tradition
- Schleiermacher → Liberal theology
- Barth → Bonhoeffer → Post-liberal theology

### 3. Controversy Connections (Figure ↔ Event)
Who was involved in which debates:
- Arius + Athanasius → Council of Nicaea
- Pelagius + Augustine → Pelagian Controversy
- Luther + Pope Leo X → The Reformation
- Wellhausen + Childs → JEDP vs. Canonical Criticism

### 4. World Context Connections (Scholar → World Event)
What was happening when they wrote:
- Barth → World War I → Romans commentary
- Aquinas → Aristotle's return → Summa Theologiae
- Desert Fathers → Constantine's conversion → monasticism as protest
- Liberation theologians → Latin American poverty → preferential option

### 5. Hermeneutical Lineage (Scholar → Method)
Which interpretive approach they use:
- Origen → Allegorical
- Luther → Grammatical-Historical + Law/Gospel
- Wellhausen → Historical-Critical / Source Criticism
- Childs → Canonical Criticism
- N.T. Wright → Critical Realism + New Perspective

### 6. Tradition Connections (Scholar → Tradition)
Which theological stream they belong to:
- Aquinas → Catholic / Dominican / Thomistic
- Calvin → Reformed / Presbyterian
- Wesley → Methodist / Arminian
- Palamas → Eastern Orthodox / Hesychast

### 7. Fruit Connections (Event/Scholar → Cultural Impact)
What they produced in the world:
- Benedict → Western monasticism → universities, hospitals, literacy
- Wilberforce → abolition → human rights law
- Crusades → Christian-Muslim relations → 1,000 years of conflict
- Gutenberg + Luther → printing press + vernacular Bible → mass literacy

## How to Read a Node
Every note in the vault has a **Connections** section listing:
- What it points TO (influences, typological fulfillments)
- What points TO IT (what it produced, who it influenced)
- What WORLD EVENT it belongs to
- What SCHOLARS wrote about it
- What THEMES it touches

## The Graph in Words
If you could see all the connections at once, you would see:
- **Jesus** at the center, with lines going out to every OT figure (typology) and every NT and post-NT event (fulfillment)
- **Paul** as the second hub — his letters connect to every major theological debate
- **Augustine** as the hinge of ancient/medieval — everything before him feeds in; everything after feeds out
- **Luther** as the hinge of medieval/modern — the great rupture
- **Barth** as the hinge of modern/contemporary — the neo-orthodox response to liberal collapse
- **The Cross** as the event that every tradition, every scholar, every hermeneutical method must somehow account for
