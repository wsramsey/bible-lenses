# Theosis - Development History

## Earliest Form
The seeds lie in NT participation language and patristic reflection on incarnation and salvation.

## Historical Catalysts
- patristic debates about Christ and salvation
- Greek metaphysical and participatory assumptions
- monastic and liturgical practice

## Major Turning Points
- Irenaeus and Athanasius
- Cappadocian development
- Byzantine articulation of divine-human participation
- modern ressourcement and ecumenical retrieval

## Key Controversies
- grace and nature
- essence/energies distinctions
- Protestant misunderstandings of deification

## Denominational / Traditional Mutation Over Time
Theosis remained central in the East, more implicit in the West, and is now increasingly revisited ecumenically.

## Practical Consequences Over Time
It shaped ascetic practice, sacramental realism, holiness, and communal spiritual formation.
