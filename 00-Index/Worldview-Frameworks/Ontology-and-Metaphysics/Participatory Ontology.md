# Participatory Ontology

## Summary
A worldview in which beings share in realities greater than themselves and salvation is understood as communion, participation, transformation, and union.

## Background Assumptions
- reality is layered and participatory
- union with God is a real transformation
- symbols and sacraments participate in what they signify

## What This Framework Makes Plausible
- [[Theosis]]
- sacramental realism
- figural and typological reading
- recapitulation and participation in Christ

## What It Tends To Resist
- reduction of salvation to legal bookkeeping
- flat materialism
- purely individualistic religion

## Key Beliefs Shaped By It
- [[Atonement]]
- [[Eucharist]]
- [[Mariology]]
- [[Saints]]

## Lived World It Produces
A world of sacrament, holiness, ascetic transformation, communion, and dense symbolic meaning.
