# Forensic Salvation Framework

## Summary
A salvation framework that foregrounds guilt, judgment, acquittal, righteousness, and legal standing before God.

## Background Assumptions
- moral and legal culpability are central
- divine judgment is a key interpretive lens
- salvation is intelligible in judicial categories

## What This Framework Makes Plausible
- justification as declaration
- strong concern for assurance
- close attention to guilt and pardon

## What It Tends To Resist
- purely therapeutic accounts of salvation
- accounts that minimize judgment language

## Key Beliefs Shaped By It
- [[Justification]]
- [[Predestination]]
- [[Grace]]

## Lived World It Produces
A world attentive to conscience, guilt, pardon, and confidence before God, often with strong pastoral concern for assurance.
