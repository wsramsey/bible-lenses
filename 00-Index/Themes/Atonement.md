# The Atonement
*Every major theory of what the cross accomplished — and who argued for it*

## The Biblical Texts
- [[Isaiah 53]] — "he was pierced for our transgressions... by his wounds we are healed"
- [[Mark 10:45]] — "the Son of Man came to give his life as a ransom for many"
- [[Romans 3:21–26]] — "God presented Christ as a sacrifice of atonement (*hilasterion*)"
- [[2 Corinthians 5:21]] — "God made him who had no sin to be sin for us"
- [[Hebrews 9–10]] — the high priest typology; Christ as both priest and sacrifice
- [[Revelation 5]] — the Lamb who was slain; cosmic victory

## The Major Theories

### Christus Victor (Gustaf Aulén, 1931)
Christ defeats the powers of sin, death, and evil through the cross and resurrection.
- Key scholars: [[Origen]] · [[Irenaeus]] · [[Gustaf Aulén]] · [[N.T. Wright]] · [[Greg Boyd]]

### Satisfaction Theory (Anselm, 1098)
Humanity owes God an infinite debt of honor; only God-man can pay it.
- Key scholars: [[Anselm of Canterbury]] · [[Thomas Aquinas]]

### Penal Substitution
Christ bears the punishment due to sinners; God's justice is satisfied.
- Key scholars: [[Calvin]] · [[Owen]] · [[Packer]] · [[Grudem]]

### Moral Influence Theory (Abelard)
The cross demonstrates God's love and motivates our response.
- Key scholars: [[Peter Abelard]] · [[Schleiermacher]] · [[Liberal Protestantism]]

### Scapegoat / Revelation Theory (Girard)
The cross exposes the scapegoat mechanism; God is on the side of the victim.
- Key scholars: [[René Girard]] · [[James Alison]]

### Governmental Theory (Grotius)
God relaxes the strict requirement of punishment to demonstrate his governance.
- Key scholars: Hugo Grotius · [[Arminius]] · [[Wesley]]

### Participation / Theosis
Christ takes on our humanity to give us his divinity; salvation as deification.
- Key scholars: [[Athanasius]] · [[Maximus the Confessor]] · [[Gregory Palamas]] · Eastern Orthodoxy

## Key Debates
- **Is penal substitution central or one image among many?** [[Packer]]/[[Carson]] vs. [[Chalke]] (*The Lost Message of Jesus* controversy, 2003)
- **Does God the Father punish the Son?** Trinitarian objections to penal substitution
- **Christus Victor vs. penal substitution**: complementary or competing?
