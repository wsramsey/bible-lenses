# Theodicy and Suffering
*Where to find every major voice on why suffering exists and what to do with it*

## Bible Figures Who Suffered
- [[Job]] — the paradigm case; "Where were you when I laid the foundations of the earth?"
- [[Jeremiah]] — the weeping prophet; the confessions (Jer 11–20)
- [[David]] — Psalms 22, 88 (lament); Absalom's death
- [[Jesus]] — Gethsemane · the cry of dereliction (Psalm 22 / Matthew 27:46) · the cross

## Scholars on Suffering
- **[[C.S. Lewis]]** — *The Problem of Pain* (intellectual) + *A Grief Observed* (personal, after Joy's death) — the same thinker before and after
- **[[Nicholas Wolterstorff]]** — *Lament for a Son* — his son Eric died; the most honest Christian grief memoir
- **[[Jürgen Moltmann]]** — *The Crucified God* — God suffers in Christ; God is not the unmoved mover; the cross is in God
- **[[Simone Weil]]** — *Waiting for God*; affliction (*malheur*) as the point where God is closest and most absent
- **[[Dorothee Sölle]]** — *Suffering*; against passive acceptance; suffering demands response
- **[[Thomas Aquinas]]** — *Summa* III, qq. 46–49; the passion as the most fitting means of redemption; suffering as participation in Christ's
- **[[Julian of Norwich]]** — *Revelations of Divine Love*; "all shall be well"; visions received during near-death illness

## Church Events Where Suffering Was Forced to Yield an Answer
- [[The Black Death (1347)]] — mass death; the context for the Rhineland mystics
- [[The Holocaust]] — where was God at Auschwitz? Elie Wiesel vs. Frankl vs. theological responses
- [[WWI]] — shattered liberal optimism about progress; context for Barth
- [[The Crucifixion]] — the defining suffering; every other suffering is read through this

## Hermeneutical Approaches
- **Psalms of Lament** — the genre that gives permission to complain to God
- **[[Walter Brueggemann]]** — *The Message of the Psalms*; orientation → disorientation → new orientation
- **[[Lament]]** as a practice; the church lost it; recovering it
