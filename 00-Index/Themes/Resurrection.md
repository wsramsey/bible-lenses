# The Resurrection
*The most disputed event in history — every major voice*

## The Primary Texts
- [[Mark 16]] — the empty tomb; the women's fear
- [[Matthew 28]] — the guard at the tomb; the Great Commission
- [[Luke 24]] — Emmaus road; "Did not our hearts burn within us?"
- [[John 20–21]] — Mary Magdalene; Thomas; the Beloved Disciple; the restoration of Peter
- [[1 Corinthians 15]] — Paul's earliest account (c. 54 AD); the tradition received; 500 witnesses; "if Christ is not raised, your faith is futile"
- [[Acts 2]] — Peter's Pentecost sermon; the resurrection as the foundation

## The Historical Arguments

### For the Resurrection
- **[[N.T. Wright]]** — *The Resurrection of the Son of God* (800pp); the most rigorous historical defense; the empty tomb + appearances + emergence of early Christianity require the resurrection as the most plausible explanation
- **[[Gary Habermas]]** — the "minimal facts" approach; five facts all critical scholars accept; the resurrection is the best explanation
- **[[William Lane Craig]]** — philosophical and historical arguments; the resurrection as the foundation of Christian apologetics

### Against / Skeptical
- **[[Bart Ehrman]]** — the resurrection is a post-Easter theological development; we cannot verify historical claims about miraculous events
- **[[John Dominic Crossan]]** — Jesus's body was likely eaten by dogs; the resurrection is a metaphor for the continuation of his movement
- **[[Rudolf Bultmann]]** — the resurrection is not a historical event but an existential claim; "demythologizing" required

### Theological Interpretations
- **[[Karl Barth]]** — the resurrection is the vindication of Jesus's person and the foundation of all theology; not merely historical but eschatological
- **[[Wolfhart Pannenberg]]** — the resurrection is a real historical event; it is the proleptic anticipation of the end; all theology must be grounded here
- **[[Jürgen Moltmann]]** — the resurrection as protest against death; the theology of hope; the future is open

## The Empty Tomb Debate
- **N.T. Wright**: the empty tomb is historically credible; Jewish resurrection expectation was always bodily; the disciples would not have preached resurrection in Jerusalem if the tomb were occupied
- **Crossan**: the empty tomb stories are legendary; Paul doesn't mention it
- **Ehrman**: the empty tomb accounts have discrepancies; women were first witnesses (criterion of embarrassment = historicity)
