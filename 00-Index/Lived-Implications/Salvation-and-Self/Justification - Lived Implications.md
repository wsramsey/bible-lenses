# Justification - Lived Implications

## Daily Spirituality
Can produce either deep assurance or persistent anxiety depending on how assurance is taught.

## Moral Psychology
Shapes how people relate guilt, conscience, forgiveness, and self-understanding.

## Family / Community / Authority
Often influences how communities talk about conversion, testimony, discipline, and belonging.

## Work / Money / Material Life
Can decouple worth from achievement, though in some settings it coexists with strong work-discipline cultures.

## Body / Gender / Sexuality
Indirectly influences purity, conscience, and moral self-scrutiny.

## Politics / Social Order
May produce strong concern for law, judgment, freedom of conscience, and institutional reform.

## Suffering / Death / Hope
Provides confidence before judgment and consolation in death.

## Practical Tensions
- assurance vs scrupulosity
- grace vs moral laxity fears
- inward confidence vs anti-sacramental individualism
