# Theosis - Lived Implications

## Daily Spirituality
Encourages prayer, ascetic practice, sacramental participation, and gradual transformation.

## Moral Psychology
Frames growth as healing and transfiguration more than mere legal acquittal.

## Family / Community / Authority
Often lives inside liturgical, communal, and sacramental structures.

## Work / Money / Material Life
Can sacralize ordinary life as a site of transformation.

## Body / Gender / Sexuality
Usually treats the body as capable of sanctification rather than as merely a liability.

## Politics / Social Order
May foster a less purely legal imagination and a stronger emphasis on communion and holiness.

## Suffering / Death / Hope
Frames suffering as capable of transfiguration and death as a defeated enemy in Christ.

## Practical Tensions
- mysticism vs vagueness
- transformation vs moral passivity
- participation vs precision
