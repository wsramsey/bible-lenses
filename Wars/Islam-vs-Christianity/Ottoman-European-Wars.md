# Ottoman-European Wars (1354–1699)
*Islam at the gates of Vienna — twice*

## The Ottoman Advance into Europe

**1354**: Ottomans cross into Europe at Gallipoli
**1389**: **Battle of Kosovo** — Ottomans defeat Serbian coalition; Serbia becomes a vassal state; Saint Prince Lazar martyred; Kosovo becomes the sacred wound of Serbian identity
**1396**: **Battle of Nicopolis** — Crusade against Ottomans defeated; Bulgaria falls
**1453**: **Fall of Constantinople** — see Byzantine-Arab Wars
**1462–1478**: Vlad the Impaler of Wallachia resists the Ottomans (the historical Dracula — his impaling of Ottoman prisoners was a psychological weapon)
**1521**: Belgrade falls to Suleiman the Magnificent
**1526**: **Battle of Mohács** — Hungary crushed; Louis II of Hungary dies; Ottoman Hungary established
**1529**: **First Siege of Vienna** — Suleiman's armies reach Vienna; siege fails due to supply lines and weather; the high-water mark of Ottoman advance
**1571**: **Battle of Lepanto** — combined fleet of Spain, Venice, and the Papacy (the Holy League) defeats the Ottoman fleet; the largest naval battle since antiquity; 400+ ships, 100,000+ men; stopped Ottoman naval expansion in the Mediterranean; Pope Pius V attributed the victory to the Rosary
**1683**: **Second Siege of Vienna** — Grand Vizier Kara Mustafa with 140,000–200,000 troops; Jan III Sobieski of Poland arrives with the "Winged Hussars"; the relief of Vienna (September 12, 1683); one of the most dramatic military engagements in European history
**1699**: **Treaty of Karlowitz** — Ottomans cede Hungary; the first major Ottoman territorial retreat

## The Theological Framing
Both sides framed these conflicts religiously:
- The Ottomans used the concept of *jihad* (holy war)
- The Papacy declared crusades; the Holy League of Lepanto was explicitly religious
- The Habsburgs called themselves "Defenders of Christendom"
- This framing was real but also served political purposes — religious language legitimized what were also imperial ambitions

## The Legacy
- The Ottoman wars shaped the identity of Eastern European Catholic countries (Poland, Hungary, Croatia, Slovakia) — the memory of fighting Islam for centuries
- The Battle of Kosovo is the founding myth of Serbian national identity
- The 1683 Siege of Vienna is commemorated in Austrian culture
- These memories shape contemporary European politics around Muslim immigration

## The Croissant Question
Legend says the croissant was invented in Vienna to celebrate the defeat of 1683 — baked in the shape of the Ottoman crescent. The legend is probably apocryphal but culturally significant.
