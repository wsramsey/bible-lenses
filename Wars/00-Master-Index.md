## The Honest Summary
Christianity has been involved in:
- Defensive wars against persecution (Roman era)
- Wars of conquest justified by mission (Reconquista, Americas)
- Wars against heresy (Albigensian, Hussite)
- Wars between Protestant and Catholic Christians (France, Germany, England, Ireland)
- Wars against Islam (Crusades, Ottoman wars)
- Colonial wars with religious justification (Americas, Africa)
- Modern ethnic conflicts with religious dimensions (Balkans, Sudan, Nigeria)

None of these can be separated from their political, economic, and ethnic contexts.
None of them can be excused simply by that context.
The pattern is consistent: when Christianity aligns with political and military power, violence follows.
The counter-pattern is equally consistent: the prophetic voices against that violence (Las Casas, Wilberforce, Bonhoeffer, King) also came from within Christianity.
