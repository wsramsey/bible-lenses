# The Albigensian Crusade (1209–1229)
*The crusade against Christian heresy — the most destructive internal Christian conflict*

## Background
→ See [[Cathars and Waldensians]] for theological background

Pope Innocent III declared a crusade against the Cathars of southern France (Languedoc). Northern French nobility — who would gain territory — enthusiastically participated.

## The Massacre of Béziers (July 22, 1209)
The crusading army's first major engagement. The city of Béziers refused to surrender its Cathar population.

**The alleged command**: The papal legate Arnaud Amalric was allegedly asked how to distinguish Cathars from Catholics. His response: *"Caedite eos. Novit enim Dominus qui sunt eius."* ("Kill them all; God will know his own.")

**The result**: The entire population of Béziers — estimated 20,000 — was killed. The city was burned.

Whether Arnaud actually said this is disputed. That it happened — that Catholics massacred a city full of people including many Catholics — is not disputed.

## The Campaign
- Simon de Montfort led the military campaign
- City after city taken; Cathar strongholds systematically reduced
- The great castle of **Montségur** fell in 1244 after a 10-month siege; 220 Cathar perfecti were burned at the stake rather than recant — the largest mass burning in the Crusade

## The Destruction of Occitan Culture
Languedoc was one of the most sophisticated cultures in medieval Europe:
- The **troubadours** — the creators of courtly love poetry; the tradition of romantic love in Western literature
- Religious tolerance — Jews, Christians, Cathars lived relatively peacefully
- Political independence from northern France

All of this was destroyed. The Albigensian Crusade was as much a cultural and political conquest as a religious one.

## The Legacy
- The Inquisition was established in part to complete what the Crusade had started
- The last Cathars were hunted down and burned into the early 14th century
- The troubadour tradition was extinguished
- Languedoc was absorbed into the French kingdom
- The event remains a sore memory in southern French identity (Occitan nationalism)
