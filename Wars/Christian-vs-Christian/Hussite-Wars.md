# The Hussite Wars (1419–1434)
*The first Protestant victory — a century before Luther*

## Background
Jan Hus was burned at the Council of Constance (1415). The Czechs rose in fury. **Hussite theology**: Scripture over tradition; communion in both kinds (bread and wine) for laity; the vernacular in worship; church reform.

## Five Crusades — All Failed
The Pope declared crusades against the Hussites. Five separate crusading armies invaded Bohemia:
- 1420, 1421, 1422, 1427, 1431 — all defeated

**Jan Žižka (1360–1424)**: The Hussite military commander — one of the greatest generals in medieval history. Blind in one eye, then both eyes — commanded brilliantly while totally blind. Invented the *Wagenburg* — a mobile fortification of armed wagons that defeated armored knights.

**The Hussite hymn**: "Ye Warriors of God" (*Ktož jsú boží bojovníci*) — one of the first battle hymns of a Protestant movement.

## The Significance
- The Hussites proved that a motivated religious movement could defeat professional crusading armies
- They pioneered the use of firearms and mobile tactics
- They created a proto-Protestant state that lasted until the Battle of White Mountain (1620) — 200 years
- Luther knew the story; Hus's prophecy ("You may roast the goose...") inspired him

## The Utraquists
The moderate Hussites who accepted the **Compactata of Basel (1436)** — communion in both kinds for laity, in exchange for recognizing Catholic authority. The first time Rome made a theological concession to a reform movement.
