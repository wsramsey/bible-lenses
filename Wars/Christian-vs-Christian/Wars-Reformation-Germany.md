# The German Reformation Wars (1524–1555)

## The Peasants' War (1524–1525)
The largest popular uprising in European history before the French Revolution.

**Cause**: The Reformation unleashed radical ideas of freedom and equality. Peasants applied them literally — abolition of serfdom, tithe reduction, common lands restored.

**Luther's response**: Initially sympathetic, then appalled. His tract *Against the Murderous, Thieving Hordes of Peasants* (1525) urged the princes to slaughter the rebels "like mad dogs." This severely damaged Luther's credibility with the poor.

**Deaths**: 100,000–130,000 peasants killed in the suppression.

**Thomas Müntzer**: Radical reformer who led peasant armies; captured and executed 1525; became a hero to Marxists 400 years later (East German stamps featured him).

## The Schmalkaldic War (1546–1547)
The **Schmalkaldic League** — an alliance of Protestant princes — was crushed by Emperor Charles V at the **Battle of Mühlberg (1547)**. Charles V seemed to have won.

## The Peace of Augsburg (1555)
Charles V's victory proved hollow. The **Peace of Augsburg** established:
- **Cuius regio, eius religio**: "Whose realm, his religion" — the prince determines the religion of his territory
- Lutheranism recognized alongside Catholicism
- Calvinism NOT recognized — this exclusion eventually contributed to the Thirty Years' War

The Peace of Augsburg was a temporary settlement that lasted 63 years. It froze the religious map of Germany without resolving the underlying tensions.
