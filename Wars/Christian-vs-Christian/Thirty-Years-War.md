# The Thirty Years' War (1618–1648)
*The most destructive European war before the 20th century — fought largely in the name of God*

## The Origin: The Defenestration of Prague (1618)
Protestant Bohemian nobles threw three Catholic Habsburg officials out of a window of Prague Castle — into a manure pile 17 meters below (they survived). This act of defiance against the Habsburg Emperor Ferdinand II began the war.

## The Four Phases

### Bohemian Phase (1618–1625)
Bohemian Protestant revolt against Habsburg Catholic rule. **Battle of White Mountain (1620)**: Catholic League crushed the Bohemian revolt; Bohemia forcibly recatholicized; 30,000 Protestant families expelled; Czech culture suppressed for 300 years.

### Danish Phase (1625–1629)
Denmark (Lutheran) intervened to help German Protestants. Crushed by the Habsburg general Wallenstein.

### Swedish Phase (1630–1635)
**Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden** — the "Lion of the North" — intervened with a superb army. Won major victories; was killed at the **Battle of Lützen (1632)**. Sweden emerged as a great power.

**The Sack of Magdeburg (1631)**: Catholic League forces under Tilly sacked and burned the Protestant city; 20,000 civilians killed. One of the worst atrocities of the war.

### French Phase (1635–1648)
**The great irony**: Catholic France, under Cardinal Richelieu, allied with Protestant Sweden and German princes against Catholic Habsburg Spain and Austria. This phase abandoned all pretense of religious motivation — it was pure geopolitics. France emerged as Europe's dominant power.

## The Peace of Westphalia (1648)
The founding document of modern international relations:
- **Religious tolerance** for Catholics, Lutherans, and Calvinists
- Territorial sovereignty — states have the right to govern their own territory without outside interference
- The birth of the modern state system
- Germany's religious map was frozen: Protestant north, Catholic south (roughly)

## The Devastation
- Germany lost 25–40% of its population
- Some regions lost 60–70% of their people
- It took Germany a century to recover demographically
- The phrase "ravaged like the Thirty Years' War" entered German language as the worst possible description

## The Theological Lesson
The Thirty Years' War proved that religious wars resolved nothing — both sides were Christian, both prayed to the same God, and the result was 8 million dead and Germany in ruins. The Enlightenment's argument for separating church and state was powered by this experience.
