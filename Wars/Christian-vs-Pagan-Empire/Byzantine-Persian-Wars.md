# Byzantine-Persian Wars (502–628 AD)
*The war that bled both empires and left them open to the Islamic conquest*

## The Conflict
A century of intermittent warfare between the Christian Byzantine Empire and the Zoroastrian Sasanian Persian Empire. These were the two superpowers of the ancient world — between them they controlled the entire Middle East.

## The Religious Dimension
- Persia had a large Christian minority (the Church of the East); sometimes protected, sometimes persecuted
- Byzantium had a Zoroastrian minority; sometimes tolerated
- The wars were primarily geopolitical but were framed in religious terms by both sides

## The Holy Cross Incident (614)
The Persians under Khosrow II sacked Jerusalem (614):
- 60,000+ Christians killed or enslaved
- The **True Cross** (the relic believed to be the cross of Christ) captured and taken to Persia
- The Patriarch Zacharias taken captive
- This was a massive psychological blow to Christendom

## Heraclius's Counterattack (622–628)
Emperor Heraclius launched what is sometimes called "the first Crusade" — a specifically religious war to recover the True Cross:
- Invaded Persia; devastating campaigns
- Recovered the True Cross in 628
- Heraclius was greeted as a hero in Jerusalem

## The Catastrophic Aftermath
Both empires were exhausted. Within 15 years:
- Arab armies under the banner of Islam had conquered Syria, Palestine, Egypt, and Persia
- The entire Middle East changed hands permanently
- The great Christian cities of Alexandria, Antioch, Jerusalem all became Muslim-majority
