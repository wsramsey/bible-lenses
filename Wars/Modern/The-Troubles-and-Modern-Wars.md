# Modern Religious Conflicts Involving Christianity

## The Balkans (1990s)
The Yugoslav Wars (1991–2001) had a significant religious dimension:
- **Catholic Croats** vs. **Orthodox Serbs** vs. **Muslim Bosniaks** vs. **Muslim Albanians**
- The Srebrenica massacre (1995): 8,000 Bosniak Muslim men and boys killed by Serb forces
- The Kosovo War (1998–1999): NATO intervened; largely Orthodox Serbs vs. Muslim Albanians
- Religious identity was a marker for ethnic identity; the wars were simultaneously ethnic and religious

The Serbian Orthodox Church's role: complicated; some clergy blessed Serb paramilitaries; others spoke out against violence.

## Sudan (1955–2005, 2011–present)
The Sudanese civil wars had a religious dimension:
- **First civil war (1955–1972)**: Arab Muslim north vs. African Christian/animist south
- **Second civil war (1983–2005)**: 2 million dead; Sharia law imposed on non-Muslims; one of the deadliest conflicts since WWII
- **South Sudan independence (2011)**: the predominantly Christian south seceded; then fell into its own civil war

## Nigeria
Nigeria is roughly split between a Muslim north and a Christian south. Ongoing violence:
- Boko Haram: Islamist insurgency; attacks on Christian communities in the north
- Fulani herdsmen vs. farmers in the "Middle Belt": complex conflict with religious and ethnic dimensions
- Estimated 50,000+ Christians killed in northern Nigeria since 2000

## Central African Republic (2012–present)
Conflict between predominantly Muslim Séléka rebels and Christian/animist anti-Balaka militias. Pope Francis visited (2015) and opened a Holy Door there — a dramatic gesture in an active war zone.

## Lebanon
The Lebanese Civil War (1975–1990) involved:
- Maronite Catholic militias
- Sunni and Shia Muslim factions
- Palestinian groups
- Israeli intervention
- Syrian intervention
15 years; 120,000–150,000 dead; the country still lives with the consequences

## The Philippines
The **Moro insurgency** in Mindanao: Muslim minority fighting for autonomy/independence from the predominantly Catholic Philippines. Ongoing since the 1970s; peace agreements periodically achieved and broken.

## Nagorno-Karabakh
**Armenian Christians vs. Azerbaijani Muslims**: the conflict over Nagorno-Karabakh (1988–2020, 2023). Ethnic conflict with religious dimension; Armenia sees itself as a Christian nation; Azerbaijan is majority Muslim.

## The Islamic State and Christians
ISIS (Islamic State, 2013–2019) specifically targeted Christian communities:
- Ancient Christian communities in Iraq and Syria nearly destroyed
- The Nineveh Plain (Iraq) — Christians fled or were massacred; communities going back to the 1st century AD displaced
- The "ISIS genocide" of Christians and Yazidis recognized by the US government

## The Honest Assessment
Contemporary conflicts involving Christianity and Islam are complex:
- They are rarely *purely* religious — ethnic, economic, and political factors are always present
- But religious identity matters — it shapes who sides with whom and how violence is justified
- The worst violence often occurs at the intersection of religious and ethnic identity
- Peacemaking often requires specifically Christian or interreligious actors (the Sant'Egidio Community; the Acholi Religious Leaders Peace Initiative in Uganda)
