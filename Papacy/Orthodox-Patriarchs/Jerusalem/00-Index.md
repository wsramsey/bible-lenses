# Patriarchs of Jerusalem
*The holy city — contested across traditions*

Jerusalem is recognized as a patriarchate in the ancient Pentarchy (Rome, Constantinople, Alexandria, Antioch, Jerusalem). Three patriarchal lines claim Jerusalem:

## Greek Orthodox Patriarchs of Jerusalem
*(Key figures only — full list: 45+ patriarchs)*

**James the Just** (c. 44–62 AD) — The Lord's brother; first bishop of Jerusalem; martyred by stoning; the *Liturgy of St. James* bears his name

**Simeon** (c. 62–107) — Second bishop; cousin of Jesus; fled to Pella before the Roman siege; martyred under Trajan

**Macarius I** (314–333) — Present at Nicaea; oversaw building of Church of the Holy Sepulchre under Constantine

**Cyril of Jerusalem** (350–386) — Great catechetical teacher; *Catechetical Lectures*; disputes with Arians

**Juvenal** (422–458) — Secured patriarchal status for Jerusalem at Chalcedon

**Sophronius** (634–638) — Surrendered Jerusalem to Caliph Omar; wrote on the Holy City; the Arab conquest

**Modestus** (630–634) — Rebuilt churches destroyed by Persians (614)

**Theophilus III** (2005–present) — Current Greek Orthodox Patriarch

## Latin Patriarchs of Jerusalem
*(Crusader period, 1099–1291, and restored 1847)*

**Arnulf of Chocques** (1099) — First Latin Patriarch; controversial election during Crusade

**Daimbert of Pisa** (1099–1102) — Established Latin hierarchy

**William of Tyre** (1175–1186) — Greatest historian of the Crusader states; *Historia rerum in partibus transmarinis gestarum*

## Armenian Patriarchs of Jerusalem
The Armenian Apostolic Church has maintained a patriarchate in Jerusalem since the 4th century.
