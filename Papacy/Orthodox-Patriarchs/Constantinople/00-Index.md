# Ecumenical Patriarchs of Constantinople
*The 'first among equals' in Eastern Orthodoxy — c. 270 patriarchs from 306 AD to present*

The Ecumenical Patriarch of Constantinople holds the title "first among equals" (*primus inter pares*) among the bishops of Eastern Orthodoxy. The see was founded by the Apostle Andrew (tradition) and became the primary Eastern see after Constantine moved the capital to Constantinople in 330 AD.

## The Full List
*(Key patriarchs are linked to individual notes; see Wikipedia for the complete chronological list)*

### Early Period (306–1054)
- [[Metrophanes I]] (306–314) — First patriarch
- [[Alexander of Constantinople]] (314–337) — Nicaea
- [[Paul I of Constantinople]] (337–351) — Arian controversy; martyred
- [[Gregory of Nazianzus]] (379–381) — The Theologian; Council of Constantinople
- [[John Chrysostom]] (398–404) — The Golden-Mouthed; exiled
- [[Nestorius]] (428–431) — Condemned at Ephesus
- [[Flavian of Constantinople]] (446–449) — Robber Synod; Chalcedon
- [[Photius I]] (858–867, 877–886) — Photian Schism; greatest Byzantine theologian

### The Great Schism Period
- [[Michael Cerularius]] (1043–1058) — **The Great Schism of 1054**

### Under Ottoman Rule (1453–1923)
- [[Gennadius II Scholarius]] (1454) — First patriarch under Ottomans
- [[Cyril Loukaris]] (1620–1638) — Calvinist-leaning; martyred

### Modern Period
- [[Bartholomew I]] (1991–present) — Current patriarch; ecumenical leadership

## The Role of the Ecumenical Patriarch
- **First among equals** — not a "pope" with jurisdictional supremacy but a presider among the autocephalous Orthodox churches
- **Ecumenical Councils** — traditionally convened by the emperor/patriarch together
- **The Filioque** — the primary theological dispute with Rome
- **Pan-Orthodox gatherings** — the 2016 Holy and Great Council on Crete
