# Patriarchs of Alexandria
*Two parallel traditions since the Council of Chalcedon (451)*

After Chalcedon (451), Alexandria split into two patriarchates:
- **Coptic Orthodox Patriarchs** — rejected Chalcedon (*Miaphysite*); still exists; Pope of Alexandria and Patriarch of All Africa
- **Greek Orthodox Patriarchs** — accepted Chalcedon; Greek/Melkite tradition; still exists

## Coptic Orthodox Popes of Alexandria (Key Figures)
*(Full list: 118 popes from St. Mark to the present)*

1. **St. Mark the Evangelist** (c. 42–68 AD) — Tradition holds he founded the church in Alexandria
2. **Demetrius I** (188–230) — Organized the Catechetical School; opposed Origen
3. **Dionysius the Great** (248–264) — The Great; persecutions of Decius and Valerian
4. **Athanasius I** (328–373) — *Athanasius contra mundum*; defender of Nicaea; exiled five times
5. **Cyril I** (412–444) — Council of Ephesus; *Theotokos* controversy; Nestorius condemned; controversy over Hypatia's death
6. **Dioscorus I** (444–451) — Rejected Chalcedon; exiled; became the patriarch of the Coptic Church's separation
7. **Benjamin I** (622–661) — Arab conquest of Egypt; church survived under Islam
8. **Shenouda III** (1971–2012) — Great modern Coptic patriarch; ecumenical engagement; imprisoned by Sadat
9. **Tawadros II** (2012–present) — Current Coptic Pope
