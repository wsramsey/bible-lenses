# Patriarchs of Antioch
*Where followers of Jesus were first called 'Christians' (Acts 11:26)*

Antioch (modern Antakya, Turkey) was the third great see of early Christianity after Rome and Alexandria. It split into multiple patriarchates:

- **Greek Orthodox Patriarchate of Antioch** — Chalcedonian; currently based in Damascus
- **Syriac Orthodox Patriarchate** — rejected Chalcedon; *Miaphysite*; uses Syriac liturgy
- **Melkite Greek Catholic Patriarchate** — in communion with Rome
- **Maronite Patriarchate** — always in communion with Rome

## Key Patriarchs

**Ignatius of Antioch** (c. 107–108) — Martyr; wrote seven letters on his way to martyrdom in Rome; the epistles are foundational for early ecclesiology (*Let me be food for the wild beasts*)

**Eustathius** (c. 324–330) — First Patriarch of Antioch after Nicaea; opponent of Arianism

**Meletius** (360–381) — Presided at Council of Constantinople (381); the Meletian Schism

**John Chrysostom** — Patriarch of Antioch before Constantinople; [[see Constantinople notes]]

**Severus of Antioch** (512–518) — The greatest Miaphysite theologian; exiled after Chalcedonian emperor took over; 3,000+ hymns; foundational for Syriac Orthodox theology
