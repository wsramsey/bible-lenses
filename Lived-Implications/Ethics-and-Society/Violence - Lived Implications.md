# Violence - Lived Implications

## Daily Spirituality
Shapes whether discipleship is imagined as nonviolence, just-war discernment, purification through force, or spiritual warfare.

## Moral Psychology
Forms instincts around enemies, punishment, honor, fear, and forgiveness.

## Family / Community / Authority
Influences parenting, discipline, political obedience, and responses to threat.

## Work / Money / Material Life
Can justify military vocation, coercive order, or conscientious resistance.

## Body / Gender / Sexuality
Often intertwines with ideas of masculinity, honor, purity, and vulnerability.

## Politics / Social Order
Has obvious effects on state power, nationalism, policing, and revolutionary resistance.

## Suffering / Death / Hope
Changes whether suffering is endured, resisted, redemptively interpreted, or militarized.

## Practical Tensions
- pacifism vs just war
- coercion vs protection
- martyrdom vs militancy
