# Santiago de Compostela — The Camino
*The most walked pilgrimage route in the world — still*

## What It Is
The **Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela** is the traditional burial site of the Apostle James (son of Zebedee, brother of John) — the only apostle whose tomb is in Western Europe.

The **Camino de Santiago** (Way of Saint James) is a network of pilgrimage routes across Europe converging at Santiago. The most famous: the **Camino Francés** (French Way), 800km across northern Spain from the Pyrenees.

## The Numbers
- In 2019 (pre-COVID): **347,578 pilgrims** completed the Camino Francés
- In 2023: **446,000 pilgrims** — a record
- The pilgrimage has existed since at least the 9th century
- In 1987, the Council of Europe named it the "First European Cultural Route"

## The Cathedral

### The Pórtico de la Gloria (1168–1188)
The greatest Romanesque sculptural program in Spain:
- **Master Mateo**: the sculptor; his self-portrait is on the back side of the central pillar (facing the altar) — pilgrims touch his forehead "to receive wisdom"; the marble is worn smooth
- **Central figure**: Christ in Majesty; the four Evangelists; 24 elders of the Apocalypse playing musical instruments
- **The foot of the central pillar**: five finger-shaped grooves worn into the marble by centuries of pilgrims placing their hands

### The Botafumeiro
The largest censer (incense burner) in the world: 1.5m tall, 80kg. Eight *tiraboleiros* (rope-pullers) swing it on a 65m rope across the transept — originally to fumigate the pilgrims. Still used at major feasts.

## The Theology of Pilgrimage
The Camino is one of the few surviving practices of **embodied theology** in secular Europe:
- The body walks; the spirit is formed by the walking
- Difficulty (blisters, weather, weight of the pack) is the pedagogy
- Community forms along the road — strangers become companions (*compañeros* — those who share bread)
- The arrival at the cathedral and the embrace of the statue of James is the culmination

Modern pilgrims include many non-Christians — the Camino functions as a secular spiritual practice for millions who have left institutional religion but seek the form
