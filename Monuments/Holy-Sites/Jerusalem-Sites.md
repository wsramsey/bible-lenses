# Jerusalem — The Holy Sites
*The most contested city in human history — sacred to three religions*

## The Old City: Four Quarters
Jerusalem's Old City (1.2 sq km) contains the holiest sites of three religions within walking distance of each other:
- **Jewish Quarter**: Western Wall, Jewish Quarter synagogues
- **Muslim Quarter**: Via Dolorosa (partially), Haram al-Sharif
- **Christian Quarter**: Church of the Holy Sepulchre, Via Dolorosa
- **Armenian Quarter**: Cathedral of St. James (Armenian Apostolic)

## The Temple Mount (Haram al-Sharif)
**Jewish significance**: The site of Solomon's Temple and Herod's Temple — the center of biblical Israel's religion; the place where the Ark of the Covenant rested; the direction of Jewish prayer

**Islamic significance**: Al-Aqsa Mosque (c. 705 AD) and the Dome of the Rock (691–692 AD) — the third-holiest site in Islam; Muhammad's night journey

**Christian significance**: Where Jesus taught, where the money-changers were driven out, where the disciples gathered; the Temple Jesus predicted would be destroyed (fulfilled 70 AD); the site from which the earliest church was born

**The Most Contested 35 Acres on Earth**: Jews cannot pray there (political arrangement since 1967); Israeli sovereignty vs. Islamic Waqf administration; any change risks war

## The Via Dolorosa
The traditional route Jesus walked to Calvary — 14 Stations of the Cross, marked by chapels and plaques through the Muslim and Christian quarters.

**Archaeological note**: The route was established in the medieval period and likely doesn't follow the actual historical path (disputed); but it has been the devotional route for 600+ years.

## The Garden of Gethsemane
At the foot of the Mount of Olives — olive trees that may be 2,000+ years old (DNA evidence supports first-century dating of the root systems).

**[[Franciscan]] custody**: The Church of All Nations (1924) covers the traditional rock where Jesus prayed; international donations funded it; the windows deliberately have no white glass to maintain a dark, nocturnal atmosphere

**[[Russian Orthodox]] Gethsemane**: Mary's Tomb nearby (Church of Mary Magdalene, 1888)

## The Mount of Olives
- **The Ascension site** (Chapel of the Ascension, now a mosque)
- **The Jewish Cemetery**: 150,000 graves; Jews have buried here for 3,000 years; the oldest Jewish cemetery in continuous use
- **The view**: The most photographed view of Jerusalem; the Dome of the Rock and the Old City walls

## The City of David
**Archaeological significance**: The excavations beneath Silwan village are revealing the actual Iron Age Jerusalem of David and Solomon — an area outside the current Old City walls.

The **Siloam Tunnel** (c. 701 BC, King Hezekiah) is still intact and walkable — 533m through living rock; the **Siloam Inscription** (celebrating its completion) is in the Istanbul Archaeological Museum.
