# Church of the Holy Sepulchre
*The holiest site in Christianity — shared (contentiously) by six traditions*

## What It Marks
The traditional site of both the **Crucifixion** (Golgotha/Calvary) and the **Resurrection** (the empty tomb). Archaeological evidence supports that this was outside the city walls in the 1st century (as the Gospel accounts require) and that it was used as a burial site.

## The Six Custodian Traditions
The church is shared — often contentiously — by six Christian traditions:
1. **Greek Orthodox** (largest share; controls the Katholikon, the main rotunda)
2. **Roman Catholic / Franciscan** (since 1342; Calvary chapel)
3. **Armenian Apostolic** (St. Helena Chapel, part of the rotunda)
4. **Coptic Orthodox** (tiny chapel on the roof — the Copts' claim is the smallest)
5. **Ethiopian Orthodox** (monks live on the roof in huts; chapels in the outer courtyard)
6. **Syriac Orthodox** (access certain chapels on Sundays)

**The Status Quo**: Since 1852 (Ottoman firman), no changes may be made to the building or its practices without agreement of all parties. A wooden ladder on a window ledge (put there in the 18th century) remains because no one can agree to remove it — the "Immovable Ladder."

## The Architecture
- **The Rotunda / Anastasis**: Contains the **Aedicule** — the small building housing the tomb; the current Aedicule dates from 1810 (after a fire); restoration completed 2017
- **The Stone of Unction**: The slab where Jesus's body was anointed before burial; pilgrims prostrate and pray on it
- **Golgotha (Calvary)**: A rocky outcrop now enclosed in the church; 5 meters above the main floor; Greek Orthodox altar on the right, Catholic altar on the left
- **The Chapel of St. Helena**: Where Constantine's mother allegedly found the True Cross

## The Key Controversies
- **Authenticity**: Is this the real site? Most archaeologists believe yes; a minority argue the Garden Tomb (19th-century Protestant alternative) is more plausible
- **The keys**: A Muslim family (Joudeh) has held the key to the church since Saladin's time; a second Muslim family (Nuseibeh) opens the door — a medieval arrangement to prevent the Christian factions from fighting over who controls the entrance
