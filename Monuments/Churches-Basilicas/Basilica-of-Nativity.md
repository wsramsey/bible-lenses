# Church of the Nativity, Bethlehem (339/565 AD)
*The oldest continuously operating church in the world*

## The Site
Built over the grotto traditionally identified as the birthplace of Jesus. The Constantinian basilica (339 AD) was replaced by Justinian's larger church (565 AD) — the one substantially present today.

## The Door of Humility
The entrance was reduced to 1.2m high in the medieval period — too small for anyone to enter standing upright. Historical reasons: prevent horses from being ridden in; prevent Ottomans from desecrating it. Theological reading: you must bow to enter the place of Christ's birth.

## The Grotto
Beneath the main church: the Grotto of the Nativity.
- A **14-pointed silver star** set into the marble floor marks the traditional spot of Jesus's birth; inscription: *Hic de Virgine Maria Jesus Christus natus est* ("Here Jesus Christ was born of the Virgin Mary")
- The star was removed in 1847 by Ottoman authorities (Greek-Catholic dispute); France's objection led to the Crimean War (1853–1856) — one of the strangest diplomatic precipitations in history

## The Mosaics
The nave floor preserves remnants of the original 4th-century mosaic floor — one of the oldest surviving church floor mosaics.

The walls retain 12th-century Crusader mosaics and 6th-century mosaic fragments — a palimpsest of Christian history.
