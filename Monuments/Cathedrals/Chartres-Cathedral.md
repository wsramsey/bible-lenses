# Chartres Cathedral (1194–1220)
*The definitive Gothic cathedral — the entire Bible in stone and glass*

## Why It Matters
Chartres is the most complete surviving Gothic cathedral — retaining its original stained glass windows (176 windows, 2,600 sq meters), its sculptural programs, and its labyrinth. It is the supreme example of Gothic theology made architectural.

## The Gothic System
Three innovations enabled Gothic architecture:
1. **The pointed arch**: transfers weight diagonally; allows taller, thinner walls
2. **The flying buttress**: transfers lateral thrust to external piers; frees the wall from structural role
3. **The ribbed vault**: distributes weight to specific points; enables the window-wall

**Result**: walls dissolved into glass; the building becomes light

## The Theological Program

### The West Façade (Royal Portal, c. 1145)
The oldest surviving part. Three portals:
- **Left**: Incarnation — Annunciation, Nativity, Presentation
- **Center**: Second Coming — Christ in Majesty, the Apocalypse
- **Right**: Ascension and the signs of the Zodiac (cosmic time sanctified)

The tympana (semicircular relief areas above doors) are the most complete surviving 12th-century sculptural theology.

### The Stained Glass
176 windows encoding:
- The complete Old Testament (north rose window: creation to Abraham)
- The complete New Testament (south rose window: Christ in glory)
- The lives of the saints (narrative windows in nave)
- The guilds who donated windows (bakers, butchers, water carriers depicted at the bottom)

**The Blue Virgin** (Notre-Dame de la Belle Verrière, c. 1150): One of the oldest surviving Gothic windows; the Virgin in her characteristic "Chartres blue"

### The Labyrinth
An 11-circuit labyrinth inlaid in the nave floor (13.7m diameter, c. 1215):
- A pilgrimage in miniature — the medieval pilgrims who couldn't reach Jerusalem walked it on their knees
- No branching paths — one way in, one way out; represents the Christian life

## The Marian Dedication
Chartres is dedicated to the Virgin Mary and houses the *Sancta Camisia* — allegedly the tunic Mary wore during the Annunciation. The cathedral was the most important Marian pilgrimage site in medieval France.
