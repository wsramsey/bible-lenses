# Hagia Sophia (537 AD)
*The most significant building in Christian history*

## What It Is
"Holy Wisdom" (*Hagia Sophia*) — dedicated to Christ as the divine Wisdom (Sophia) of God. The seat of the Ecumenical Patriarch of Constantinople for nearly a thousand years.

## The Architecture
- **Dome**: 31.87m diameter, 55.6m high — appeared to float, supported by light flooding from 40 windows around the base; "suspended from heaven by a golden chain" (Procopius)
- **Total interior**: 7,570 sq meters
- **Construction time**: 5 years, 10 months — an engineering miracle of antiquity
- When completed, Justinian reportedly said: **"Solomon, I have surpassed thee"**

## The Theological Program
The building is a theology in stone:
- The **dome** = heaven; the square base = earth; the building mediates between them
- **Gold mosaics** turn the interior into light — Pseudo-Dionysius's light theology made spatial
- The **apse mosaic** of Christ Pantocrator (restored after iconoclasm): the ruler of all, present in his church
- **Mary with Christ Child** (apse): the *Theotokos* — the Ephesian definition made visual
- The building enacted the theology that in worship, heaven and earth meet

## The Conversions
- **537**: Christian cathedral (Eastern Orthodox)
- **1054**: The Great Schism; Rome and Constantinople separate — the Hagia Sophia is the central symbol
- **1204**: Fourth Crusade sacks Constantinople; Latin Mass said here for 57 years
- **1453**: Mehmed II converts it to a mosque; minarets added; mosaics plastered
- **1934**: Atatürk converts it to a museum
- **2020**: Erdoğan converts it back to a mosque

## The Mosaics (Surviving)
- **The Deësis (c. 1261)**: Christ enthroned between Mary and John the Baptist; one of the greatest Byzantine mosaic panels
- **Emperor John II Komnenos and Empress Eirene with the Virgin** (c. 1122): south gallery
- **Emperor Constantine IX and Empress Zoe** (c. 1042): south gallery
