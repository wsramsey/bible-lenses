# Sagrada Família (1882–present)
*The most visited building in Spain — a living theology in stone*

## What It Is
A Roman Catholic church designed as an **expiation temple** — built entirely by public donation (no government funding), still under construction after 140+ years.

Antoni Gaudí took over as architect in 1883 and devoted the last 43 years of his life to it exclusively. He is buried in the crypt. He was struck by a tram in 1926 and died; the building continues under architects interpreting his plans.

## The Theological Program
Three façades, each dedicated to one mystery:
- **Nativity Façade** (east, begun Gaudí): the Incarnation; joy; life bursting from every surface; natural forms (plants, animals, insects) crowding around the Holy Family
- **Passion Façade** (west, Josep Maria Subirachs): stark, angular, brutal; the suffering and death of Christ; deliberately harsh contrast with the Nativity façade
- **Glory Façade** (south, under construction): the Last Judgment; the ultimate destination of humanity

## Gaudí's Nature Theology
Gaudí drew all his architectural forms from nature:
- **Columns** branch like trees; the interior is a forest
- **Vaults** follow the structural logic of bones and shells
- **Light** floods through translucent stone and colored glass
- **Gaudí**: "Nothing is art if it does not come from nature, which is the source of all life"

His theology: creation reveals the Creator; the building should feel like the inside of a natural organism, because natural organisms express divine artistry.

## Timeline
- 1882: Foundation stone laid
- 1926: Gaudí dies; only the crypt and part of the apse complete
- 1936: Anarchists burn the workshop and destroy Gaudí's original models
- 1940s–2010s: Reconstruction from photographs and partial drawings
- 2026: Target completion date (140th anniversary) — though the spires will be complete; full program 2030s
