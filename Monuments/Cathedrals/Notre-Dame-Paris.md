# Notre-Dame de Paris (1163–1345)
*The most recognized cathedral in the world*

## Significance
Notre-Dame is not the finest Gothic cathedral (Chartres holds that title) but it is the most symbolically central — the heart of Paris, the seat of the Archbishop, the site of Napoleon's coronation, the witness to the French Revolution's de-Christianization, the place where Victor Hugo set his novel that saved medieval Gothic from demolition.

## The Architecture
- **Length**: 128 meters; **height**: 69 meters (towers)
- **Flying buttresses**: among the most elegant in Gothic architecture — the apse flying buttresses span 15 meters
- **Rose windows**: three (west, north, south); the south rose (c. 1260, 13m diameter) is largely original glass
- **The chimeras and gargoyles**: 19th-century restorations by Viollet-le-Duc; the gargoyles are drainage spouts given monstrous form; the chimeras are purely decorative

## Historical Moments
- **1163**: Bishop Maurice de Sully lays the foundation stone; Pope Alexander III present
- **1239**: Louis IX brings the Crown of Thorns to Notre-Dame (now in the treasury)
- **1431**: Henry VI of England crowned King of France here
- **1804**: Napoleon crowns himself Emperor (famously taking the crown from Pope Pius VII's hands to crown himself)
- **1789–1799**: During the Revolution, declared a "Temple of Reason"; statues of kings beheaded; gold stripped
- **1844**: Victor Hugo's *The Hunchback of Notre-Dame* (1831) had sparked a restoration campaign; Viollet-le-Duc begins 25-year restoration
- **1944**: Liberation of Paris — Te Deum sung while snipers still active in the cathedral
- **2019**: Fire destroys the spire and part of the roof; the Rose windows and walls survive

## The 2019 Fire
April 15, 2019. 400 firefighters; the spire collapsed at 7:50 PM. The vaulted stone ceiling largely survived. Emmanuel Macron pledged to rebuild in 5 years. Reopened December 7, 2024.
