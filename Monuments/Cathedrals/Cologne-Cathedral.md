# Cologne Cathedral (1248–1880)
*632 years in construction — the world's largest Gothic cathedral*

## The Construction
Begun 1248; the choir was consecrated 1322. Then construction stalled — the funds ran out; the cathedral was incomplete with a massive crane on the south tower for 300 years (a Cologne landmark). Construction resumed 1842 (the neo-Gothic movement, spurred by Romanticism and nationalism) and was finally completed 1880.

**Height**: 157.38 meters — was the world's tallest structure 1880–1884.

## The Shrine of the Three Magi
The primary reason Cologne became a pilgrimage center: the **Shrine of the Three Magi** (Dreikönigenschrein, c. 1180–1225) — the reliquary containing what Frederick Barbarossa brought from Milan in 1164, allegedly the bones of the Magi (Matthew 2).

The shrine is one of the greatest works of medieval goldsmithing:
- 2.2m long, 1.5m high
- Gold and silver; enamel; gems
- Images of the Three Magi, the Baptism of Christ, prophets, apostles

The pilgrimage to Cologne to venerate the Magi was one of the major medieval pilgrimages — pilgrims still receive the Magi blessing at Epiphany.

## Theological Connections
- **Albertus Magnus** and **Thomas Aquinas** both taught in Cologne — the greatest medieval theological university; the cathedral was their intellectual home
- The **Rhineland Mystics** ([[Meister Eckhart]], [[Johannes Tauler]], [[Henry Suso]]) worked in Cologne and surrounding area
- The Gothic cathedral as the theology of light — Pseudo-Dionysius made architectural
