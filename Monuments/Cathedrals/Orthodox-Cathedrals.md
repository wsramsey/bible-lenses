# Major Orthodox Cathedrals

## Russia

### Cathedral of Christ the Saviour, Moscow
- **Built**: 1839–1883 (original); 1995–2000 (rebuilt after Stalin demolished it)
- **Significance**: Tallest Orthodox church; seat of the Patriarch of Moscow; symbol of Russian Orthodox revival post-Soviet
- **History**: Stalin demolished it in 1931 to build a "Palace of Soviets" (never built); became a swimming pool; rebuilt after communism fell

### St. Basil's Cathedral, Moscow (Cathedral of the Intercession)
- **Built**: 1555–1561 by Ivan the Terrible (to commemorate the conquest of Kazan)
- **Architects**: Postnik Yakovlev and Barma
- **Theology**: The tent-like domes represent the flames of a bonfire ascending to heaven; each of the 9 chapels dedicated to a different saint
- **Legend**: Ivan the Terrible blinded the architects so they could never build anything more beautiful

### Cathedral of the Dormition (Assumption), Moscow Kremlin
- **Built**: 1475–1479 (by Italian architect Aristotle Fioravanti for Ivan III)
- **Significance**: Every Russian Tsar crowned here; every Patriarch of Moscow enthroned here; the Kremlin's spiritual center

### Saint Isaac's Cathedral, Saint Petersburg
- **Built**: 1818–1858 under Alexander I
- **Architects**: Auguste de Montferrand
- **Significance**: One of the largest domed cathedrals in the world; 101.5m high; gilded dome visible from 40km away

## Greece and the Orthodox World

### Church of the Holy Apostles, Thessalonica (c. 1312–1315)
- Late Byzantine; Palamite theology in architectural form; hesychast frescoes

### Monastery of the Great Lavra, Mount Athos (963 AD)
- The oldest and most senior monastery on the Holy Mountain; founded by Saint Athanasius of Athos
- Never been sacked or destroyed; library holds priceless Byzantine manuscripts

## Serbia

### Temple of Saint Sava, Belgrade
- **Built**: 1935–ongoing (exterior complete; interior under construction)
- Largest Orthodox church in the world; built on site where the Ottomans burned the relics of Saint Sava (1595)
- The burning of his relics was meant to break Serbian Christian identity; the church is the response — 400 years later

## Ethiopia

### Church of Our Lady Mary of Zion, Aksum
- **Significance**: Believed to house the Ark of the Covenant (Ethiopia's most sacred claim)
- Guards it: a single monk chosen for life who never leaves
- The old church (17th century) and a new church (1960s) stand side by side; only men may enter the old church
