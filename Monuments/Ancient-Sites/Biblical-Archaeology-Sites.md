# Biblical Archaeology — Major Sites
*The physical evidence for the biblical world*

## Qumran and the Dead Sea Scrolls
**Location**: West Bank, near the Dead Sea

The **Dead Sea Scrolls** — 981 manuscripts found in 11 caves (1947–1956) — are the most important archaeological discovery in biblical studies:
- Written c. 300 BC – 68 AD
- Include the oldest known manuscripts of every OT book except Esther
- The Great Isaiah Scroll (1QIsa-a): the oldest complete manuscript of any biblical book; essentially identical to the Masoretic text — confirming that the text was faithfully transmitted
- Community Rule, War Scroll, and other sectarian documents — describing the Essene community
- **The site**: Khirbet Qumran; the settlement's ruins include a scriptorium where the scrolls may have been copied

**Museum**: Shrine of the Book, Israel Museum, Jerusalem (the most significant DSS); the rest in the Israel Antiquities Authority and various international collections

## Tel Megiddo (Armageddon)
**Location**: Jezreel Valley, Israel

The site of **Har Megiddo** — the hill of Megiddo — from which "Armageddon" derives (Revelation 16:16).

Archaeologically, one of the most excavated sites in Israel — 20+ layers of civilization; controlled the Via Maris (the ancient highway between Egypt and Mesopotamia). Whoever controlled Megiddo controlled the traffic of empires.

The **ancient stables** here were long attributed to Solomon (1 Kings 9:15–19); current archaeology dates them to Ahab.

## Masada
**Location**: Judean Desert, overlooking the Dead Sea

The fortress where 960 Jewish rebels held out against the Roman siege (73–74 AD) — the last stronghold of the Sicarii after Jerusalem's fall. According to Josephus, the defenders chose mass suicide rather than Roman captivity.

**The Masada Complex**: Israeli national identity — "Masada shall not fall again." The Israeli Defense Forces hold induction ceremonies here.

**Archaeological note**: The suicide account may be Josephus's dramatization; the archaeology reveals a more complex story.

## Caesarea Maritima
**Location**: Mediterranean coast, Israel

Herod the Great's artificial harbor city — one of the largest construction projects of the ancient world. The **Praetorium** (governor's palace) where Paul was imprisoned (Acts 23–24) and **Felix and Festus** held his trial.

The **Pilate Stone** was found here (1961) — a dedicatory inscription mentioning "Pontius Pilate, Prefect of Judea" — the only archaeological evidence of Pilate outside literary sources.

**Museum**: Caesarea National Park and Israel Antiquities Authority

## Hazor, Megiddo, and Gezer
The three cities Solomon supposedly built (1 Kings 9:15) — each with an identical 6-chambered gate structure. **Yigael Yadin's discovery** (1955) confirmed that the same architect built all three; physical evidence for Solomonic building.

## Lachish
**Location**: Shephelah, Israel

The second-most important city in Judah after Jerusalem. **The Lachish Letters** (c. 587 BC) — 21 ostraca (pottery shards with ink writing) — are Hebrew correspondence during the Babylonian siege, providing the closest we have to "real-time" documentation of the events of Jeremiah's time.

The **Assyrian reliefs** (British Museum, London) from Sennacherib's palace at Nineveh depict the conquest of Lachish in extraordinary detail — matching the archaeological destruction layer.

## Jericho (Tell es-Sultan)
**Location**: West Bank

One of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities (~10,000 years). The site of Joshua's conquest (Joshua 6). Kathleen Kenyon's excavations (1952–1958) found that Jericho was largely unoccupied in the Late Bronze Age (when the biblical Exodus would have placed the conquest) — a major archaeological problem for historical reconstruction. Debates continue.

## Shiloh
**Location**: West Bank (Khirbet Seilun)

Where the Tabernacle rested for approximately 300 years; where Samuel grew up; where the Ark of the Covenant was kept. Recent Israeli/evangelical excavations have identified what appears to be the Tabernacle's footprint — a large open plaza consistent with the biblical description.
