# Atonement - Development History

## Earliest Form
The NT presents multiple overlapping patterns: sacrifice, victory, ransom, reconciliation, participation, and example.

## Historical Catalysts
- martyrdom and persecution contexts
- patristic battle with death and corruption
- medieval legal and feudal social imagination
- Reformation focus on guilt and judgment

## Major Turning Points
- Irenaean recapitulation
- Anselm's satisfaction theory
- Reformation penal/forensic sharpening
- modern moral influence and political readings

## Key Controversies
- satisfaction vs victory
- penal substitution debates
- divine child abuse critique
- cross as exemplar vs objective accomplishment

## Denominational / Traditional Mutation Over Time
The balance of models changes depending on metaphysics, social order, and views of justice.

## Practical Consequences Over Time
It shapes preaching, emotional life, views of God, repentance, violence, and forgiveness.
