# Justification - Development History

## Earliest Form
In Paul, justification language emerges within disputes over law, Gentile inclusion, covenant identity, and righteousness before God.

## Historical Catalysts
- Pauline debates over law and Gentiles
- Augustine/Pelagius controversies on grace
- medieval penitential and sacramental systems
- Luther's reading of Romans and Galatians

## Major Turning Points
- Augustine's grace-centered anti-Pelagian development
- scholastic integration into sacramental theology
- Luther and Calvin's forensic stress
- Trent's Catholic clarification
- New Perspective revision of covenantal context

## Key Controversies
- faith vs works
- infused vs imputed righteousness
- relation to sanctification
- Paul within Second Temple Judaism

## Denominational / Traditional Mutation Over Time
The doctrine moved from Pauline polemic into anti-Pelagian grace debates, then into medieval sacramental structures, then into Reformation protest and confessional identity.

## Practical Consequences Over Time
It shaped assurance, penitence, sacramental dependence, pastoral comfort, and modern Protestant identity.
