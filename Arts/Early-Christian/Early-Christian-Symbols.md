# Early Christian Symbols — A Theological Dictionary

## The Problem These Symbols Solved
Early Christians needed to identify each other under persecution *and* encode their theology in non-explicitly-Christian forms. Each symbol is a compressed theological statement.

## The Major Symbols

### ICHTHYS (Fish) ✦
**Greek acrostic**: Ἰ(ησοῦς) Χ(ριστὸς) Θ(εοῦ) Υ(ἱὸς) Σ(ωτήρ) — "Jesus Christ, Son of God, Savior"
**Theology**: The complete Christological confession in one word; the five terms summarize the Gospel
**Usage**: Carved on tombs; painted in catacombs; scratched on walls; worn as jewelry; modern Christian bumper stickers

### Chi-Rho (☧) ✦
**Greek letters**: X (Chi) + P (Rho) = first two letters of ΧΡΙΣΤΟΣ (Christos)
**History**: Used by Christians before Constantine; after his 312 vision "in this sign conquer," became the official Roman Christian symbol; placed on military standards and coins
**The Hinton St Mary Mosaic** (c. 350 AD): The Chi-Rho with the face of Christ — the earliest certain image of Christ in British art; Christ identified with the symbol

### The Anchor ✦
**Scripture**: Hebrews 6:19 — "We have this hope as an anchor for the soul, firm and secure"
**Visual**: An anchor contains a cross hidden in its shape — the cross disguised as a nautical object
**Theology**: Hope as the foundation; Christ as the one who holds us secure

### The Vine and Grapes ✦
**Scripture**: John 15:1 — "I am the true vine"
**Theology**: Union with Christ; the Eucharist; the abundant life
**Ambiguity**: Vines were standard Roman decoration; easy to paint in a catacomb without detection

### The Peacock ✦
**Theology**: Incorruptibility (ancient belief that peacock flesh did not decay); the resurrection; eternal life
**Usage**: Common in early Christian funerary art

### The Palm Branch ✦
**Scripture**: Revelation 7:9 — the redeemed holding palm branches
**Theology**: Victory; martyrdom; eternal life

### Alpha and Omega (Α Ω) ✦
**Scripture**: Revelation 1:8 — "I am the Alpha and the Omega"
**Theology**: Christ as the beginning and end of all things; his cosmic lordship

### The Lamb (*Agnus Dei*) ✦
**Scripture**: John 1:29 — "Behold, the Lamb of God"; Revelation 5 — the Lamb on the throne
**Theology**: Christ's sacrificial death and his cosmic reign; the paradox of power through weakness
