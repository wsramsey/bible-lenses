# David (Michelangelo, 1501–1504)

## The Subject
Michelangelo depicts David *before* the battle — not the triumphant victor but the moment of decision. David looks toward the unseen Goliath; his sling is over his shoulder; his brow is furrowed; his body is tensed.

**The theological moment**: Not triumph but courage in the face of overwhelming odds; faith before the outcome is known.

## The Renaissance Humanism
The nude David was deliberately placed outside the Palazzo della Signoria (Florence's civic building) — not in a church. David as the symbol of Florentine civic virtue: the small republic (Florence) confident against larger powers (Milan, Rome). The biblical figure became a political icon — a common Renaissance move.
