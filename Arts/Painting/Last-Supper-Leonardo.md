# The Last Supper (Leonardo da Vinci, 1495–1498)
*The most reproduced religious painting in history*

## The Moment Depicted
Jesus has just said: "One of you will betray me" (John 13:21). The painting captures the instant of maximum psychological tension — each apostle reacts to the announcement in a way that reveals his character.

## The Composition
Leonardo organized the 13 figures in four groups of three, with Christ as the still center:
- **Left to right**: Bartholomew, James Minor, Andrew — then Judas, Peter, John — then Christ — then Thomas, James Major, Philip — then Matthew, Thaddaeus, Simon
- **Judas** (fourth from left): reaches for bread while pulling back; his face in shadow; clutching the money bag — a subtle condemnation without distortion
- **John** beside Jesus: androgynous; swooning; traditionally identified as "the Beloved Disciple"
- **Peter** whispers to John; holds a knife (foreshadowing the Garden)
- **Thomas** raises one finger — the same finger that will probe the wounds of the Risen Christ

## The Eucharistic Theology
The painting encodes a specific Eucharistic interpretation:
- The **bread and wine** are present on the table
- Christ's hands frame them — blessing them
- This is the institution narrative of 1 Corinthians 11:23-26
- The painting was in the refectory (dining hall) of a Dominican monastery — the monks ate their meals "with" the disciples every day

## The Deterioration Crisis
Unlike fresco (painted on wet plaster), Leonardo used a mixed technique on dry plaster that began deteriorating within decades. It has been restored multiple times. What we see today is substantially a restoration.
