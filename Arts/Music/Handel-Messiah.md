# Handel's Messiah (1741)
*The most performed choral work in history*

## The Composition
Handel composed the *Messiah* in 24 days in August–September 1741. He reportedly said upon completing the "Hallelujah" chorus: "I did think I did see all heaven before me, and the great God himself."

## The Structure
Three parts:
1. **Part I**: Prophecy and Incarnation (Isaiah 40, 7, 9; Luke 2)
2. **Part II**: The Passion, Death, and Resurrection (Isaiah 53; John 1, 3; Revelation 5)
3. **Part III**: Resurrection of the Dead and Eternal Life (Job 19; 1 Corinthians 15; Revelation 5)

## The "Hallelujah" Chorus
Revelation 19:6, 11:15, 19:16 set to music. King George II reportedly stood during its first London performance (1743) — tradition holds this is why audiences still stand.

**The theology**: "The Kingdom of this world is become the kingdom of our Lord and of his Christ; and he shall reign for ever and ever." The *Hallelujah* is not primarily about Christmas but about the eschaton — the final victory of God.

## The "I Know That My Redeemer Liveth"
Job 19:25 and 1 Corinthians 15:20 combined. The soprano solo that opens Part III — simple, certain, personal faith in the resurrection stated as pure lyrical fact.
