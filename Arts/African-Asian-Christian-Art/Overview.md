# African and Asian Christian Art — Non-Western Traditions
*1,700 years of Christian art that never went through Rome*

## Coptic Art (Egypt, 4th century–present)

### Coptic Textiles
The **Copts preserved ancient Egyptian weaving traditions** and infused them with Christian imagery:
- **Nilotic scenes**: the Nile with fish and birds; Christ as the Nile that gives life
- **The Cross**: the **Coptic cross** with its distinctive equal arms and flared ends
- **Saints and angels**: in a distinctly Egyptian style — large frontal eyes, flat perspective, colorful patterning

The **Cleveland Museum of Art**, **Metropolitan Museum of Art**, and **Louvre** hold major Coptic textile collections — among the finest early Christian artifacts surviving.

### Coptic Icons
Developed independently of Byzantine icon tradition:
- Brighter, flatter colors
- Egyptian facial types (dark skin, large eyes)
- Direct, frontal gaze
- The **Eye of Horus** transformed — the large eye in Coptic art connects ancient Egyptian protection symbolism with Christian perception

## Ethiopian Christian Art

### The Distinctly Ethiopian Style
Developing independently for 1,700 years, Ethiopian Christian art has:
- **Large, distinctive eyes** (often showing both eyes in profile faces — defying naturalistic convention)
- **Flat, patterned backgrounds** — gold or decorative
- **Rich colors**: deep reds, blues, golds
- **African facial features** — Christ, Mary, and the saints as African people

The oldest examples: **Ethiopian illuminated manuscripts** from the 14th–15th century (Walters Art Museum; British Library; Ethiopian monasteries). **Gospel books** were the most sacred objects — elaborately decorated, kept in special sanctuaries.

### The Kebra Nagast Illustrations
Manuscripts illustrating the epic of Solomon, Sheba, and Menelik — the founding story of Ethiopian Christian identity. Unlike any other Christian art tradition.

## Asian Christian Art

### Korean Christian Art
South Korea's explosive Christian growth (1% in 1900 → 30%+ today) produced a flourishing of **Korean Christian painting** in the 20th century:
- Traditional Korean painting techniques (ink wash, silk) applied to biblical subjects
- **He Qi** (Chinese-Korean artist): bright colors; folk art influence; Christ and the disciples in East Asian robes; enormously popular globally
- The Last Supper with Korean table settings; the Nativity in a Korean village

### Indian Christian Art
The **Patna School** and other Indian Christian artists from the 19th–20th century:
- Christ and Mary in Indian dress
- Sari-clad Madonna
- The Sermon on the Mount in a tropical landscape
- The **Inculturation art** movement: deliberately presenting the Gospel in Indian visual forms

### Chinese Christian Art
The **Jingjiao art** (Church of the East in Tang China) — the Xi'an Stele is the primary artifact, but there are also:
- Nestarian crosses incorporating lotus motifs
- Painting fragments from Dunhuang (Silk Road) combining Buddhist and Christian visual languages

## Liberation Theology Art (Latin America)

**The murals of Oscar Romero's context**: The Church of the Beatitudes in El Salvador and numerous churches across Latin America were decorated in the 1970s–1980s with large-scale murals depicting:
- The Exodus as contemporary liberation
- Christ with indigenous facial features
- The Last Supper with campesinos (peasants) as the disciples
- Mary as a woman of the people

**Fernando Llort**: El Salvadoran artist; his naive, colorful style became the visual identity of Salvadoran Christianity; he decorated the Metropolitan Cathedral of San Salvador (dedicated to Romero).

## The Theological Significance
Non-Western Christian art is not "primitive Christianity" or "folk art" — it is:
- Christianity developing on its own terms without Western intermediation
- Proof that the Gospel transcended its cultural carriers
- A corrective to the unconscious assumption that Christian art = European art

When the face of Christ has Ethiopian features, or Korean robes, or Coptic large eyes — it is enacting the theological claim that **every human bears the Imago Dei** and that Christ's incarnation was for all peoples.
